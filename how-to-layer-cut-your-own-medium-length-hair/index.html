How To Layer Cut Your Own Medium Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Mar 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to add some texture and volume to your medium-length hair, layering is a great way to achieve that. But going to a salon can be expensive, and not everyone has the time or resources to do so. That's why we've created this step-by-step guide on how to layer cut your own medium length hair at home. With just a few tools and some patience, you can rock a fresh new look without breaking the bank. So, let's get started!

How to Layer Cut Your Own Medium Length Hair

Layer cutting your own hair might seem intimidating, but it's actually quite simple. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb or brush
  • Hair ties or clips

Now that you have your tools ready, here's how to layer cut your own medium length hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to cut and give you a better idea of how your hair falls naturally.

Step 2: Part Your Hair

Part your hair down the middle, creating two equal sections. Use hair ties or clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with one section, comb or brush your hair straight down. Take your scissors and cut the hair straight across, creating a blunt line. This will be your bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the top section of hair and comb it straight down. Take a small section of hair from the middle and hold it between your fingers. Point the scissors diagonally upwards and cut the hair at an angle. This will create a shorter layer on top of the bottom layer.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Release the last section of hair and comb it straight down. Take a small section of hair from the top and hold it between your fingers. Point the scissors diagonally upwards and cut the hair at an angle. This will create the shortest layer on top of the other two layers.

Step 6: Check for Balance

Once you've finished layering your hair, check for balance. Make sure the layers are even on both sides and adjust if necessary.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

You're done! Style your hair as desired and enjoy your new layered look.

Tips for Layer Cutting Your Own Medium Length Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure clean cuts.
  • Keep your hair wet or damp to prevent flyaways.
  • Don't cut too much off at once â it's better to trim a little at a time.

FAQ About Layer Cutting Your Own Medium Length Hair

Here are some common questions about layer cutting your own hair:

Can I layer cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, but it's important to keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so be cautious when cutting to avoid taking off too much length.

How often should I layer cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to freshen up your look. Generally, every 3-6 months is a good timeframe.

Hair Care After Layer Cutting Your Own Medium Length Hair

After layer cutting your own hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to avoid tangles and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Layer cutting your own medium length hair can be a fun and affordable way to switch up your style. With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to create a layered look that's perfect for you. Happy cutting!
