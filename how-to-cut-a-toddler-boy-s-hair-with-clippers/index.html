How To Cut A Toddler Boy s Hair With Clippers

Written by William May 19, 2023 · 9 min read

If you're a parent of a toddler boy, you might be wondering how to cut his hair without having to take him to a salon or barber. Cutting a toddler's hair can be a challenging task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily at home. In this blog post, we will show you how to cut a toddler boy's hair with clippers.

How to Cut a Toddler Boy's Hair with Clippers

Cutting a toddler boy's hair with clippers is a simple process, but it requires some preparation and practice. You will need to purchase a good set of clippers, scissors, and a comb. Before you start cutting, make sure your child is comfortable and relaxed. Here's how to cut a toddler boy's hair with clippers:

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

The first step is to choose the right clippers for your child's hair. Look for clippers that are designed for kids, with a lower noise level and adjustable blade settings. You can also choose a cordless clipper for more flexibility and maneuverability.

Step 2: Prepare Your Child

Before you start cutting, make sure your child is comfortable and relaxed. You can distract him with a toy or a video, or give him a snack to keep him occupied. It's also a good idea to cover him with a towel or a cape to protect his clothes from hair clippings.

Step 3: Trim the Hair

Start by trimming the hair with scissors, cutting off any long or uneven strands. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it to the desired length. Then, attach the appropriate guard to the clippers and start trimming the hair. Start from the back of the head and move forward, using gentle strokes and following the natural shape of the hairline. Keep the clippers level and avoid pressing too hard on the scalp.

Step 4: Clean Up the Hair

After you finish cutting, use the scissors to trim any stray hairs and clean up the neckline and sideburns. Use a damp cloth or a brush to remove any hair clippings from your child's neck and shoulders.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide on how to cut a toddler boy's hair with clippers:

  1. Choose the right clippers for your child's hair.
  2. Prepare your child by making him comfortable and relaxed.
  3. Trim the hair with scissors, cutting off any long or uneven strands.
  4. Attach the appropriate guard to the clippers and start trimming the hair.
  5. Start from the back of the head and move forward, using gentle strokes.
  6. Follow the natural shape of the hairline and keep the clippers level.
  7. Avoid pressing too hard on the scalp.
  8. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs and clean up the neckline and sideburns.
  9. Remove any hair clippings from your child's neck and shoulders.
  10. Give your child a treat or reward for being a good sport.

Tips for Cutting Toddler's Hair

Here are some tips for cutting a toddler boy's hair with clippers:

  • Choose a time when your child is well-rested and fed.
  • Distract your child with a toy, a video, or a snack.
  • Use a towel or a cape to protect your child's clothes from hair clippings.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one.
  • Take breaks if your child gets restless or uncomfortable.
  • Don't rush and take your time to ensure an even cut.
  • Be patient and calm, and avoid getting frustrated or angry.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your child's head.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help hold your child still.
  • Practice makes perfect, so keep trying and learning.

FAQs

Can I cut my toddler boy's hair with scissors?

Yes, you can cut your toddler boy's hair with scissors, but it requires more skill and precision. Clippers are easier and faster to use, especially for beginners.

What is the best age to start cutting a toddler's hair?

The best age to start cutting a toddler's hair is around 12-18 months, when their hair starts to grow longer and thicker. You can also wait until they are 2-3 years old, when they can sit still for a longer time.

How often should I cut my toddler boy's hair?

You should cut your toddler boy's hair every 4-6 weeks, depending on how fast their hair grows and how long you want it to be.

Hair Care for Toddler Boys

After cutting your toddler boy's hair, you should take care of it by washing it with a mild shampoo and conditioner, and combing it gently. Avoid using hot tools or harsh chemicals on their hair, and protect their scalp from the sun and wind. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or a hair oil to keep their hair soft and shiny.

Now that you know how to cut a toddler boy's hair with clippers, you can save time and money by doing it at home. Remember to be patient, calm, and gentle, and your child will love his new haircut!
