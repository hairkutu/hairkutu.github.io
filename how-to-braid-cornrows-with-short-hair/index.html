How To Braid Cornrows With Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jun 27, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Cornrows are a popular and stylish protective hairstyle that can be worn by people of all ages and hair types. However, if you have short hair, you may be wondering if it's possible to achieve this look. The good news is that it is possible to braid cornrows with short hair! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to braid cornrows with short hair, as well as some tips and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Braiding Cornrows with Short Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Cornrows with Short Hair

How to Braid Cornrows with Short Hair

Braiding cornrows with short hair may seem intimidating, but it's actually quite simple. The key is to start with clean, detangled hair and to work in small sections. Here are the basic steps:

Step 1: Wash and Detangle Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to detangle your hair before braiding by using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. This will make the braiding process smoother.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Using a rat-tail comb, part your hair into small, even sections. The size of the sections will depend on the length of your hair and the thickness of the braids you want to create. For short hair, you may need to create smaller sections.

Step 3: Begin Braiding

Start by taking a small section of hair from the front of your head and dividing it into three strands. Cross the right strand over the middle strand, then cross the left strand over the middle strand. Continue braiding in this pattern, adding more hair to each strand as you go.

For short hair, you may need to use smaller sections of hair and braid more tightly to create the desired look.

Step 4: Continue Braiding

Once you have completed one row of braids, move on to the next section of hair and repeat the braiding process. Continue braiding until all sections of your hair have been braided.

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide for braiding cornrows with short hair:

Step 1: Wash and Detangle Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to detangle your hair before braiding by using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. This will make the braiding process smoother.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Using a rat-tail comb, part your hair into small, even sections. The size of the sections will depend on the length of your hair and the thickness of the braids you want to create. For short hair, you may need to create smaller sections.

Step 3: Prep Your Hair

Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair to help keep it moisturized and prevent breakage. This will also make it easier to braid.

Step 4: Begin Braiding

Start by taking a small section of hair from the front of your head and dividing it into three strands. Cross the right strand over the middle strand, then cross the left strand over the middle strand. Continue braiding in this pattern, adding more hair to each strand as you go.

For short hair, you may need to use smaller sections of hair and braid more tightly to create the desired look.

Step 5: Continue Braiding

Once you have completed one row of braids, move on to the next section of hair and repeat the braiding process. Continue braiding until all sections of your hair have been braided.

Step 6: Finish Your Braids

When you reach the end of each braid, secure it with a small elastic band or a braid clip. You can also use hair gel or edge control to smooth down any flyaways or frizz.

Tips for Braiding Cornrows with Short Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when braiding cornrows with short hair:

  • Start with clean, detangled hair to make the braiding process easier.
  • Use a rat-tail comb to create even sections of hair.
  • Work in small sections to make the braids more manageable.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Braid more tightly to create the illusion of longer hair.
  • Use hair gel or edge control to smooth down flyaways and frizz.

FAQs

Can I braid cornrows with short hair?

Yes, you can braid cornrows with short hair. The key is to work in small sections and braid tightly to create the illusion of longer hair.

How long does it take to braid cornrows with short hair?

The time it takes to braid cornrows with short hair will depend on the length of your hair and the thickness of the braids you want to create. It may take anywhere from 1-3 hours.

How long can I wear cornrows with short hair?

You can wear cornrows with short hair for up to 2-3 weeks, depending on how well you take care of them.

Hair Care for Cornrows with Short Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair while wearing cornrows:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Cleanse your scalp with a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent buildup.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your braids, as this can cause breakage.
  • Wear a satin bonnet or scarf at night to prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your braids.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy and looking great while wearing cornrows with short hair.

Conclusion

Braiding cornrows with short hair may seem daunting, but with a little practice and patience, it's definitely achievable. By following the steps and tips outlined in this article, you can achieve a stylish and protective hairstyle that will keep your hair healthy and happy.
