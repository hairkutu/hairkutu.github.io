How To Go Back To Natural Hair Without Cutting

Written by Oliver Mar 28, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you want to embrace your natural curls, but don't want to cut off your relaxed hair, there is a way to transition without doing the big chop. In this article, we will show you how to go back to natural hair without cutting. We'll cover everything from why you might want to go natural, to step-by-step instructions on how to transition, and tips for maintaining healthy hair during the process.

Why Go Natural?

There are many reasons why someone might choose to go back to their natural hair, whether it's to embrace their heritage and culture, to avoid the harmful chemicals in relaxers, or simply to try something new. Whatever your reason, transitioning to natural hair can be a rewarding and empowering journey.

How to Transition

The key to transitioning to natural hair without cutting is to gradually let your natural hair grow out while continuing to care for your relaxed hair. Here's how:

Step 1: Start with a Plan

Before you begin, it's essential to have a plan for how you want to transition. You may choose to do a big chop eventually, but if you're not ready for that, you can start by trimming your relaxed ends regularly to gradually remove them over time.

Step 2: Protect Your Hair

Protective styles such as braids, twists, and weaves can help protect your hair from damage and promote growth. Be sure to keep your scalp clean and moisturized while wearing these styles.

Step 3: Moisturize and Condition

Moisture is key to keeping your hair healthy during the transition. Use a deep conditioner once a week and a leave-in conditioner daily to keep your hair hydrated.

Step 4: Use Natural Products

Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals that can damage your hair. Instead, opt for natural products that will nourish and strengthen your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy during the transition:

Tip 1: Be Patient

Transitioning to natural hair takes time, so be patient and don't get discouraged if you don't see immediate results.

Tip 2: Protect Your Hair at Night

Use a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to protect your hair from breakage while you sleep.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat can damage your hair, so avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons. Instead, opt for styles that don't require heat.

FAQs

Q: Can I transition to natural hair without cutting?

A: Yes, you can transition to natural hair without cutting by gradually trimming your relaxed ends over time.

Q: How long does it take to transition to natural hair?

A: It can take anywhere from six months to two years to fully transition to natural hair, depending on how fast your hair grows.

Q: What products should I use to transition to natural hair?

A: Use natural products that are gentle and nourishing, such as coconut oil, shea butter, and castor oil.

Hair Care During Transition

During your transition, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent breakage and promote growth. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair once a week to keep your scalp clean and healthy.

Tip 2: Deep Condition Weekly

Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

Tip 3: Trim Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to remove damaged ends and promote growth.

Transitioning to natural hair can be a challenging but rewarding journey. With the right plan and care, you can successfully transition without cutting your hair. Remember to be patient, protect your hair, and use natural products to keep your hair healthy and strong.
