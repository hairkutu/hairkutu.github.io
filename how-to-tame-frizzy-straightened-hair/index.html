How To Tame Frizzy Straightened Hair A Complete Guide

Written by William Apr 17, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizzy hair after straightening it? You're not alone! Frizz is a common problem, especially for those with naturally wavy or curly hair. But don't worry, there are ways to tame frizzy straightened hair and achieve a smooth, sleek look. In this article, we'll share our top tips and tricks for taming frizz and maintaining healthy, beautiful hair.

How to Tame Frizzy Straightened Hair

The key to taming frizzy straightened hair is to use the right products and styling techniques. Here are some simple steps you can take to achieve a smooth, sleek look:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Before you start styling your hair, make sure it's clean and completely dry. Wet or damp hair is more prone to frizz, so wait until your hair is fully dry before straightening it.

2. Use a heat protectant

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. To protect your hair from heat damage, use a heat protectant spray or serum before using any heat styling tools.

3. Use a good quality flat iron

The quality of your flat iron can make a big difference in how your hair looks and feels. Look for a flat iron with ceramic plates, which will help distribute heat evenly and reduce the risk of damage to your hair.

4. Section your hair

To ensure that you straighten every strand of hair, divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Work on one section at a time, starting from the bottom and working your way up to the top.

5. Straighten your hair in small sections

When straightening your hair, use small sections of hair (about 1-2 inches wide) at a time. This will help ensure that every strand of hair is straightened evenly and reduce the risk of frizz.

6. Finish with a smoothing serum

After straightening your hair, apply a smoothing serum to help tame any remaining frizz and add shine. Be sure to use a serum that's specifically designed for your hair type.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to tame frizzy straightened hair:

1. Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing your hair with a hydrating shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair and let it air dry until it's completely dry.

2. Apply a heat protectant

Spray a heat protectant evenly throughout your hair to protect it from heat damage.

3. Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties.

4. Straighten your hair

Use a flat iron to straighten each section of hair, working from the bottom up.

5. Apply a smoothing serum

Finish by applying a smoothing serum to your hair, focusing on the ends.

Tips for Taming Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips for taming frizzy hair:

1. Use a deep conditioning treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

2. Avoid over-washing your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it more prone to frizz. Try to wash your hair every other day or every few days.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can cause breakage and frizz.

4. Sleep on silk

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase or wearing a silk scarf can help reduce frizz and protect your hair while you sleep.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about taming frizzy straightened hair:

Q: Can I use hairspray to tame frizz?

A: Hairspray can help keep your hair in place, but it won't necessarily tame frizz. Instead, try using a smoothing serum or oil to tame frizz.

Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It's best to limit heat styling to once or twice a week to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and beautiful:

1. Trim your hair regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

2. Eat a healthy diet

Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth.

3. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it more prone to damage and frizz. Use a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

4. Protect your hair from the sun

UV rays can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

By following these tips and tricks, you can tame frizzy straightened hair and achieve a sleek, smooth look. Remember to take care of your hair by using the right products and techniques, and you'll be rewarded with healthy, beautiful hair.
