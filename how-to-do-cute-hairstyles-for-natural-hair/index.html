How To Do Cute Hairstyles For Natural Hair

Written by Daniel Apr 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of wearing the same old boring hairstyles for your natural hair? Do you want to learn how to do cute hairstyles for natural hair that will make you stand out from the crowd? Well, you've come to the right place! In this article, we will show you step-by-step how to create some of the cutest hairstyles for natural hair. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents:

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to:

Before we get started, there are a few things you need to know. First, you need to make sure that your hair is clean and moisturized. Second, you will need some styling products such as gel, mousse, or hair butter to help hold your style in place. Lastly, you will need some hair accessories such as hairpins, headbands, or hair ties to add a little extra something to your hairstyle.

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

The first step in creating cute hairstyles for natural hair is to make sure your hair is clean and conditioned. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Don't forget to detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers while you're conditioning!

Step 2: Apply Your Styling Products

Once your hair is clean and conditioned, it's time to apply your styling products. If you're going for a sleek look, use a gel or hair butter to smooth down your edges and define your curls. If you're going for a more voluminous look, use a mousse or leave-in conditioner to add some extra bounce to your curls.

Step 3: Choose your Hairstyle

Now it's time to choose your hairstyle. There are so many cute hairstyles for natural hair to choose from, so pick one that fits your personal style and the occasion you're dressing up for. Some popular hairstyles include:

  • Bantu Knots
  • Twist-Outs
  • Braid-Outs
  • Pineapple Updo
  • Frohawk

Step by Step:

Bantu Knots

  1. Part your hair into small sections.
  2. Apply a styling product to each section.
  3. Twist each section to create a small knot.
  4. Repeat until all of your hair is in knots.
  5. Allow your hair to dry completely.
  6. Untwist each knot and fluff your hair to create volume.

Twist-Outs

  1. Part your hair into small sections.
  2. Apply a styling product to each section.
  3. Twist each section to create a small twist.
  4. Allow your hair to dry completely.
  5. Untwist each twist and fluff your hair to create volume.

Braid-Outs

  1. Part your hair into small sections.
  2. Apply a styling product to each section.
  3. Braid each section to create a small braid.
  4. Allow your hair to dry completely.
  5. Untwist each braid and fluff your hair to create volume.

Pineapple Updo

  1. Gather your hair at the top of your head.
  2. Secure your hair with a hair tie.
  3. Fluff your hair to create volume.

Frohawk

  1. Part your hair down the middle.
  2. Twist each section to create a small twist.
  3. Pin each twist to the side of your head.
  4. Fluff your hair to create volume.

Tips:

  • Experiment with different styling products to find what works best for your hair.
  • Try using different hair accessories to add a little extra something to your hairstyle.
  • Don't be afraid to try new hairstyles!

FAQ:

Q: How often should I wash my natural hair?

A: It's recommended that you wash your natural hair once a week to keep it clean and moisturized.

Q: Can I use heat on my natural hair?

A: Yes, you can use heat on your natural hair, but it's important to use a heat protectant to prevent damage.

Q: How do I keep my natural hair moisturized?

A: Use a moisturizing conditioner and a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. You can also use a hair oil or hair butter to seal in moisture.

Hair Care:

It's important to take care of your natural hair to keep it healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for natural hair care:

  • Wash your hair once a week with a sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Deep condition your hair once a month.
  • Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  • Protect your hair at night by sleeping with a satin or silk bonnet.

Now that you know how to do cute hairstyles for natural hair, it's time to try them out for yourself! Don't be afraid to experiment and have fun with your hair. Remember, your natural hair is beautiful just the way it is!
