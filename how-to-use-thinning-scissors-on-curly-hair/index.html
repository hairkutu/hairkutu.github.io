How To Use Thinning Scissors On Curly Hair

Written by Christine Aug 16, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how important it is to have the right tools to maintain your curls. Thinning scissors are a great tool to have in your arsenal, but using them on curly hair can be tricky. In this article, we will guide you through the process of using thinning scissors on curly hair step by step, and provide you with some tips and tricks to get the best results.

Description

Thinning scissors are special scissors that have one serrated blade and one straight blade. They are used to thin out hair, creating a softer look and reducing volume. Thinning scissors are especially useful for people with curly hair, as they can help to remove some of the bulk and weight of the hair, making it easier to manage and style.

How to

Step 1: Choose the Right Scissors

When it comes to thinning scissors, there are many different types and styles to choose from. For curly hair, it is best to choose a pair of scissors with a serrated blade that has wider teeth. This will allow you to remove some of the bulk without creating frizz or disrupting the curl pattern.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Before you start using thinning scissors, it is important to prep your hair. Start by washing and conditioning your hair, then detangle it using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. Once your hair is detangled, you can either air dry it or use a diffuser to dry it on low heat. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start using the thinning scissors.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, you will need to section your hair into smaller sections. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you are thinning out your hair evenly. Use hair clips to hold each section in place, starting with the bottom layer and working your way up.

Step 4: Start Thinning

Once your hair is sectioned, you can start using the thinning scissors. Start at the bottom of each section and work your way up, using the thinning scissors to remove some of the bulk. Hold the scissors at a slight angle and use short, quick cuts to thin out your hair. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can create uneven layers and disrupt your curl pattern.

Step 5: Blend Your Hair

Once you have thinned out each section, it is important to blend your hair to ensure that it looks natural. Use a regular pair of scissors or your thinning scissors to blend any harsh lines or uneven layers. Be gentle and take your time, making sure that you are not cutting too much hair at once.

Tips

  • Use a light touch when using thinning scissors on curly hair, as too much pressure can create frizz and disrupt the curl pattern.
  • Always start with a little bit of hair and work your way up, as it is easier to remove more hair than to add it back.
  • Be patient and take your time, as rushing can lead to mistakes and uneven layers.
  • Don't use thinning scissors on wet hair, as this can cause the hair to look uneven when it dries.

FAQ

Can thinning scissors be used on all types of curly hair?

Thinning scissors can be used on all types of curly hair, but it is important to choose the right type of scissors based on your hair texture and thickness.

Can thinning scissors damage curly hair?

Thinning scissors can damage curly hair if they are not used properly. It is important to use a light touch and to avoid cutting too much hair at once.

Hair Care

Using thinning scissors on curly hair can be a great way to maintain your curls and reduce bulk. However, it is important to take care of your hair to ensure that it stays healthy and strong. Make sure to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and deep condition your hair regularly. Avoid using heat styling tools and harsh chemicals, and always protect your hair from the sun and wind. With proper care and maintenance, your curly hair will look beautiful and healthy for years to come.
