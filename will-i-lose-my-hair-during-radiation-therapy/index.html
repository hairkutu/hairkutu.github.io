Will I Lose My Hair During Radiation Therapy

Written by Petter May 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

When undergoing radiation therapy, many cancer patients may wonder whether they will lose their hair. This is a common concern since hair loss is a well-known side effect of chemotherapy. However, the answer to the question is not always straightforward. In this article, we will explore the topic of hair loss during radiation therapy and provide some tips on how to take care of your hair during treatment.

What is Radiation Therapy?

Radiation therapy is a cancer treatment that uses high-energy radiation to kill cancer cells. It is usually delivered externally through a machine that directs the radiation to the cancerous area. The goal of radiation therapy is to destroy cancer cells while minimizing damage to healthy cells.

Why Does Radiation Therapy Cause Hair Loss?

Radiation therapy can cause hair loss when it is directed at the scalp or other areas with hair. The radiation damages the hair follicles, which can cause the hair to fall out. However, not all types of radiation therapy cause hair loss. It depends on the type and dose of radiation used and the area being treated.

Will I Lose My Hair During Radiation Therapy?

The answer to this question depends on several factors, including the type of radiation therapy you receive and the area being treated. If you are receiving radiation therapy to the head, you are more likely to experience hair loss. If you are receiving radiation therapy to other parts of the body, such as the chest or abdomen, you may not experience hair loss.

How Can I Prepare for Hair Loss?

If you are concerned about losing your hair during radiation therapy, there are several things you can do to prepare. First, talk to your doctor about the type of radiation therapy you will be receiving and whether it is likely to cause hair loss. If it is, ask your doctor for advice on how to prepare.

Here are some tips:

  • Cut your hair short before starting treatment
  • Invest in a good wig or hairpiece
  • Consider shaving your head before hair loss begins
  • Protect your scalp from the sun with a hat or scarf

How Can I Take Care of My Hair During Radiation Therapy?

If you do experience hair loss during radiation therapy, there are several things you can do to take care of your hair and scalp. First, be gentle when washing your hair. Use a mild shampoo and avoid rubbing your scalp too vigorously. You may also want to avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals.

Here are some tips:

  • Use a soft brush or comb
  • Avoid using hair dryers or hot rollers
  • Protect your scalp from the cold with a hat or scarf
  • Moisturize your scalp with a gentle lotion

FAQs

Will my hair grow back after radiation therapy?

Yes, in most cases, hair will grow back after radiation therapy. However, it may take several months for your hair to grow back fully.

Is there anything I can do to prevent hair loss during radiation therapy?

Unfortunately, there is no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during radiation therapy. However, there are some things you can do to minimize your risk, such as avoiding radiation therapy to the scalp if possible.

Are there any medications that can prevent hair loss during radiation therapy?

There are no medications that can guarantee the prevention of hair loss during radiation therapy. However, some studies suggest that scalp cooling may help reduce the risk of hair loss.

Conclusion

Hair loss is a potential side effect of radiation therapy, but it is not inevitable. The likelihood of hair loss depends on several factors, including the type of radiation therapy and the area being treated. If you do experience hair loss, there are several things you can do to take care of your hair and scalp. Talk to your doctor about your concerns and ask for advice on how to prepare for and manage hair loss during radiation therapy.
