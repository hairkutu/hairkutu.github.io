How To Give Yourself A Layered Haircut Women s

Written by Christine Mar 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you want to give yourself a new look without spending a lot of money, consider giving yourself a layered haircut. This style is versatile, easy to maintain, and can be done right at home. In this article, we'll give you step-by-step instructions on how to give yourself a layered haircut women's.

Table of Content

  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also helpful to have a handheld mirror, so you can see the back of your head.

Next, decide on the length of your hair. If you want a drastic change, start with longer layers and work your way up. If you're just looking for a trim or a refresh, start with shorter layers.

Once you have your tools and know the length you want, it's time to start cutting.

Step by step

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Wash and dry your hair as you normally would. Make sure it's completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section your hair

Section your hair by parting it down the middle and then from ear to ear. Clip the top section up and out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the bottom layer

Start by cutting the bottom layer. Take a small section of hair, comb it straight down, and cut it at the desired length. Continue taking small sections and cutting them to the same length. Make sure to check the length by holding the hair up and looking in the mirror.

Step 4: Cut the middle layer

Release the middle section of hair and cut it to the desired length, using the same technique as before. Make sure the layers blend together by using a comb to blend the ends.

Step 5: Cut the top layer

Finally, release the top section of hair and cut it to the desired length. Use the same technique as before and make sure to blend the layers together.

Tips

  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair. It's easier to cut more hair off than to add it back on.
  • Take breaks if you need them. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're struggling, ask a friend or family member for assistance.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain the length. On average, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! Hair grows back, and mistakes can be fixed. If you make a mistake, stop cutting and assess the situation. You may need to visit a professional stylist to fix any major mistakes.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.

Now that you know how to give yourself a layered haircut women's, you can save money and have fun experimenting with your new look. Remember to take your time, start with small sections, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
