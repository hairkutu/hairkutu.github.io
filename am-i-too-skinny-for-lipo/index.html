Am I Too Skinny For Lipo

Description

Liposuction, or lipo, is a cosmetic surgery procedure that removes excess fat from various parts of the body. It is a popular procedure for people who have trouble losing fat through diet and exercise alone. However, there is a common misconception that only overweight or obese people can undergo lipo. This is not true. People who are considered "skinny" or have a low body fat percentage can also undergo lipo, but there are certain factors to consider.

How to

If you are considering lipo and are worried about being "too skinny," there are a few things you can do to determine if you are a good candidate for the procedure:

  1. Consult with a board-certified plastic surgeon: A qualified plastic surgeon will be able to evaluate your body type and assess whether or not you are a good candidate for lipo.
  2. Consider your body fat percentage: While being skinny doesn't necessarily disqualify you from lipo, having a low body fat percentage can make the procedure more difficult. Your surgeon will need to determine if there is enough fat to remove without causing complications.
  3. Think about your overall health: Lipo is a surgical procedure, so it is important to be in good health before undergoing the procedure. If you have any underlying health conditions, you may not be a good candidate for lipo.

Step by step

If you decide to move forward with lipo, here is what you can expect during the procedure:

  1. Anesthesia: You will be given either local or general anesthesia, depending on the extent of the procedure.
  2. Incision: Your surgeon will make small incisions in the areas where fat will be removed.
  3. Fat removal: Your surgeon will use a cannula (a thin tube) to suction out the excess fat.
  4. Closure: Your surgeon will close the incisions with sutures or surgical tape.

Tips

If you are considering lipo, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose a qualified plastic surgeon: Make sure your surgeon is board-certified and has experience performing lipo on people with your body type.
  • Follow your surgeon's post-operative instructions: This will help ensure a smooth recovery and optimal results.
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle: Lipo is not a substitute for a healthy diet and exercise. To maintain your results, it is important to continue living a healthy lifestyle.

FAQ

Q: Can lipo be done on any part of the body?

A: Lipo can be performed on many different areas of the body, including the abdomen, thighs, arms, and back.

Q: Is lipo painful?

A: There will be some discomfort and swelling after the procedure, but your surgeon will prescribe pain medication to help manage any pain.

Q: Will lipo leave scars?

A: The incisions made during lipo are small and will likely fade over time. However, there may be some scarring.

Hair Care

While lipo is a cosmetic procedure that focuses on the body, it is important to take care of your hair as well. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can cause hair breakage and damage.
  • Use a gentle shampoo: Look for a shampoo that is free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals.
  • Condition regularly: Conditioning your hair regularly will help keep it moisturized and healthy.

Overall, being "too skinny" does not necessarily disqualify you from lipo. If you are considering the procedure, it is important to consult with a qualified plastic surgeon and consider your overall health and body fat percentage.
