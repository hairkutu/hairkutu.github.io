Short Hairstyles For Fine Hair Over Sixty A Guide To Flattering Cuts

Written by Christine Jul 20, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As we age, our hair starts to lose its thickness and volume. For those with fine hair, this can be especially challenging. But just because you have fine hair, doesn't mean you can't rock a fabulous short hairstyle. In fact, a shorter cut can add volume and depth to your locks. Here are our top picks for short hairstyles for fine hair over sixty.

How to Choose the Right Cut

Before we dive into specific cuts, let's talk about how to choose the right cut for your face shape and hair type. As we age, our face shape can change, so it's important to reassess what styles look best on us. Here are some general guidelines:

  • If you have a round face, opt for a cut that adds height to the crown, like a pixie cut with longer layers on top.
  • If you have a square face, choose a cut that softens your features, like a layered bob or a side-swept pixie.
  • If you have an oval face, you're in luck - most cuts will look great on you! Try a short bob or a blunt cut for a modern look.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, go for a cut that balances out your features, like a chin-length bob or a side-parted pixie.

When it comes to hair type, fine hair can benefit from shorter cuts that add volume and texture. Avoid heavy, blunt cuts that can make hair appear flat and lifeless.

The Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a classic short hairstyle that can look chic and sophisticated on women of all ages. For fine hair, opt for a side-swept pixie with longer layers on top. This will add volume and dimension to your hair while still keeping it short and low-maintenance.

To style, apply a volumizing mousse to damp hair and blow dry with a round brush. Use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks, and finish with a texturizing spray for hold.

The Bob Cut

The bob cut is another timeless style that can work wonders for fine hair. For a modern twist, try a chin-length bob with choppy layers. This will add texture and movement to your hair while still keeping it short and easy to manage.

To style, apply a volumizing spray to damp hair and blow dry with a round brush. Use a flat iron to smooth out any frizz, and finish with a light-hold hairspray for shine.

The Layered Cut

The layered cut is perfect for adding volume and texture to fine hair. For a short layered cut, try a bob with choppy layers or a shaggy pixie cut. This will add depth and dimension to your hair while still keeping it short and stylish.

To style, apply a volumizing mousse to damp hair and blow dry with a diffuser. Use a curling iron to add soft waves, and finish with a texturizing spray for hold.

Tips for Styling Fine Hair

Styling fine hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a fabulous look. Here are some tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add thickness and body to your hair.
  • Avoid heavy styling products that can weigh down fine hair. Opt for lightweight mousses and sprays instead.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots.
  • Use a round brush to add lift and volume while blow drying.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and cuts until you find what works best for you.

FAQs About Short Hairstyles for Fine Hair Over Sixty

What is the best short hairstyle for fine hair over sixty?

There are many great short hairstyles for fine hair over sixty, including the pixie cut, bob cut, and layered cut. The best cut for you will depend on your face shape and hair type.

How can I add volume to my fine hair?

You can add volume to fine hair by using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, blow drying your hair upside down, and using lightweight styling products like mousses and sprays. A short cut with layers can also add volume and texture.

Hair Care for Fine Hair

Caring for fine hair is important to keep it looking healthy and full. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every other day to avoid stripping it of natural oils.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner formulated for fine hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage fine hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

With the right cut, styling techniques, and hair care, you can rock a fabulous short hairstyle at any age. Whether you prefer a pixie cut, bob cut, or layered cut, there's a short style out there that will flatter your fine hair over sixty.
