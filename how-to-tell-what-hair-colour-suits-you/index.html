How To Tell What Hair Colour Suits You

Written by Robby Jul 24, 2023 · 9 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to tell what hair colour suits you. Choosing the right hair colour can be a daunting task, especially with so many options available. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will discuss how to determine what hair colour suits you and provide some helpful tips to ensure your new hair colour looks great.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Skin Tone
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Right Hair Colour
  • Helpful Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your New Hair Colour

How to Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in choosing the right hair colour is to determine your skin tone. Your skin tone can be warm, cool, or neutral. Here's how to tell which one you have:

Warm Skin Tone

If you have a warm skin tone, your skin will have a yellow, peachy, or golden undertone. Your veins will appear greenish, and gold jewellery will look better on you than silver. Some celebrities with warm skin tones include Beyonce, Jennifer Lopez, and Jessica Alba.

Cool Skin Tone

If you have a cool skin tone, your skin will have a pink, red, or bluish undertone. Your veins will appear blue, and silver jewellery will look better on you than gold. Some celebrities with cool skin tones include Taylor Swift, Emma Stone, and Anne Hathaway.

Neutral Skin Tone

If you have a neutral skin tone, your skin will have a mix of warm and cool undertones. Your veins will appear blue-green, and both gold and silver jewellery will look good on you. Some celebrities with neutral skin tones include Angelina Jolie, Emma Watson, and Jennifer Aniston.

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Right Hair Colour

Now that you know your skin tone, it's time to choose the right hair colour. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Look for hair colours that complement your skin tone. If you have a warm skin tone, look for hair colours with golden, caramel, or honey tones. If you have a cool skin tone, look for hair colours with ashy or cool undertones, such as platinum blonde or ash brown. If you have a neutral skin tone, you can go for either warm or cool hair colours.
  2. Consider your eye colour. If you have blue or green eyes, cool hair colours like platinum blonde or ash brown will look great on you. If you have brown or hazel eyes, warm hair colours like caramel or honey will complement your eyes.
  3. Think about your natural hair colour. If you're not looking for a drastic change, choose a hair colour that's only a few shades lighter or darker than your natural hair colour.
  4. Consider your lifestyle. If you have a busy schedule, choose a hair colour that's low maintenance and doesn't require frequent touch-ups.
  5. Consult with a professional hairstylist. They can recommend hair colours that will suit your skin tone and help you achieve your desired look.

Helpful Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour

Here are some additional tips to help you choose the right hair colour:

  • Don't choose a hair colour based solely on what's trending. Trends come and go, but a hair colour that suits you will always look great.
  • Consider your personality. If you're outgoing and love to stand out, a bold hair colour like blue or pink might be perfect for you. If you're more reserved, a natural-looking hair colour like brown or blonde might be a better fit.
  • Take care of your hair. Once you've chosen a hair colour, make sure to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about choosing the right hair colour:

What is the most flattering hair colour?

The most flattering hair colour is one that complements your skin tone and eye colour. For warm skin tones, golden or caramel hair colours are flattering. For cool skin tones, ashy or cool-toned hair colours like platinum blonde or ash brown are flattering.

Can I dye my hair a different colour than my skin tone?

Yes, you can dye your hair a different colour than your skin tone. However, certain hair colours may clash with your skin tone and make you look washed out or unflattering.

How often should I touch up my hair colour?

It depends on the hair colour and how quickly your hair grows. Generally, you should touch up your hair colour every 4-6 weeks to maintain its vibrancy.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your New Hair Colour

Here are some hair care tips for maintaining your new hair colour:

  • Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair colour from fading.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause your hair colour to fade.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant hair product.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause your hair colour to fade.

Now that you know how to tell what hair colour suits you, it's time to choose your new hair colour and rock your new look!
