How To Cut Your Medium Length Hair In Layers

Written by Robby Aug 01, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new look, consider cutting your medium length hair in layers. Layers can add volume and dimension to your hair, making it look fuller and more dynamic. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut your medium length hair in layers, including tips and hair care advice to keep your new style looking great.

How to Cut Your Medium Length Hair in Layers

What You'll Need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips
  • Styling products (optional)

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and free of any tangles. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prep your hair for cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips. Start by separating the top section of your hair from the bottom section, using a horizontal parting above your ears. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, use your comb to separate a section of hair. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends, making sure to keep the scissors straight. Repeat this process for all sections of hair in the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the top section of your hair and use your comb to separate a section of hair from the middle layer. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends, making sure to blend with the bottom layer. Repeat this process for all sections of hair in the middle layer.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top section of your hair and use your comb to separate a section of hair from the top layer. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends, making sure to blend with the middle layer. Repeat this process for all sections of hair in the top layer.

Step 6: Check for Balance

After cutting each layer, check for balance by comparing each side of your hair. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure your layers are even.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, style it as desired using your favorite products. Layers can add volume and texture to your hair, so experiment with different styles to see what works best for you.

Tips for Cutting Your Medium Length Hair in Layers

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure an even cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Blend each layer to avoid harsh lines between sections.
  • Check for balance as you go to avoid any unevenness.

FAQs

How often should I cut my medium length hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own hair in layers?

While it's possible to cut your own hair in layers, it's recommended to visit a professional stylist for the best results.

Will layers work for any hair type?

Layers can work for most hair types, but it's important to consider your hair texture and face shape when deciding on a layered style.

Hair Care for Your New Layered Style

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid heat styling tools to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid breakage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.

Cutting your medium length hair in layers can be a fun and exciting way to switch up your look. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a fresh new style that you'll love. Remember to take care of your hair with gentle products and regular trims to keep your layers looking great.
