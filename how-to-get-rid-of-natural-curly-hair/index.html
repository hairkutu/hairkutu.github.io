How To Get Rid Of Natural Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of dealing with your natural curly hair? Do you want to change up your look and go for a straighter style? If so, you're in luck. In this post, we'll go over a step-by-step guide on how to get rid of natural curly hair. From preparation to aftercare, we've got you covered. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • Straightening
  • Aftercare
  • Tips for Maintaining Straight Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Straight Hair

Preparation

Before you begin the process of getting rid of your natural curly hair, it's important to prepare your hair properly. Here are a few steps to follow:

1. Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or product residue. Follow up with a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.

2. Blow dry your hair

Blow dry your hair straight using a round brush. This will help to smooth out your curls and make the straightening process easier.

3. Apply heat protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the straightening process. Make sure to apply it evenly throughout your hair.

Straightening

Now that your hair is prepped and ready to go, it's time to start straightening. Here's how:

1. Divide your hair into sections

Divide your hair into several sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to straighten each section evenly.

2. Use a straightening iron

Using a straightening iron, start at the roots of each section and slowly work your way down to the ends. Be sure to go over each section several times to ensure that it's straight.

3. Finish with a serum

Finish off the straightening process by applying a serum to your hair. This will help to add shine and keep your hair looking sleek and smooth.

Aftercare

After you've straightened your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its new look. Here are a few tips:

1. Avoid getting your hair wet

Avoid getting your hair wet for at least 72 hours after straightening it. This will give your hair time to set and prevent it from reverting back to its natural curly state.

2. Don't use any heavy products

Avoid using any heavy products on your hair, as they can weigh it down and make it look greasy. Stick to lightweight products like serums and sprays.

3. Touch up as needed

If you notice any curls coming back, touch up your hair with a straightening iron as needed. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray each time you straighten your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Straight Hair

Here are a few additional tips for maintaining your straight hair:

1. Sleep with your hair in a loose bun

Sleeping with your hair in a loose bun can help to prevent it from getting tangled and maintain its straightness.

2. Avoid using hot tools too often

Avoid using hot tools like straightening irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

FAQs

Here are a few frequently asked questions about getting rid of natural curly hair:

1. Will getting rid of my natural curly hair damage it?

Straightening your hair can cause damage if you don't do it correctly. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using hot tools too often.

2. Can I still wear my hair curly after straightening it?

Absolutely! You can still wear your hair curly after straightening it. Just be sure to use a curl-enhancing product to bring your curls back to life.

3. How often should I straighten my hair?

You should avoid straightening your hair too often, as it can cause damage. Stick to once a week or less.

Hair Care for Straight Hair

Here are a few tips for taking care of your straight hair:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Use a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your hair from becoming too dry or damaged.

2. Use a leave-in conditioner

Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

3. Trim your hair regularly

Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to get rid of your natural curly hair and achieve a sleek, straight style. Just be sure to take care of your hair properly to maintain its new look. Good luck!
