How To Have A Bob With Thick Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Jul 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of dealing with your thick, heavy hair? Do you want to try out a new hairstyle that is both trendy and manageable? Look no further than the bob haircut! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to have a bob with thick hair. From choosing the right length to styling tips, we've got you covered. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Choosing the Right Length
  • Finding the Right Stylist
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Styling Your Bob
  • Maintaining Your Bob
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Choosing the Right Length

The first step to having a bob with thick hair is choosing the right length. For those with thick hair, we recommend going for a longer bob, also known as a "lob". This will help to prevent your hair from looking too bulky and unmanageable. A lob typically falls between the collarbone and the shoulders, but you can adjust the length to suit your preferences.

Finding the Right Stylist

Once you have decided on the length of your bob, it's time to find the right stylist. Look for someone who has experience working with thick hair and has a good reputation. You can ask for recommendations from friends or family members, or search for reviews online. It's important to find someone who you feel comfortable communicating with and who understands your hair type.

Preparing Your Hair

Before getting your bob haircut, it's important to prepare your hair. This means washing and conditioning your hair thoroughly to remove any buildup or residue. You should also detangle your hair using a wide-toothed comb to prevent any knots or tangles. If you have particularly thick hair, you may want to consider blow-drying your hair beforehand to make it more manageable for your stylist.

Cutting Your Hair

When it comes to cutting your hair, it's important to trust your stylist and communicate your preferences clearly. You can bring in photos of bob haircuts that you like to give your stylist an idea of what you're looking for. They will likely start by cutting your hair in sections, taking into account your face shape and hair texture. Once the initial cut is done, they may use thinning shears to remove any excess bulk and create a more textured look.

Styling Your Bob

After getting your bob haircut, it's important to learn how to style it properly. For those with thick hair, we recommend using a lightweight, volumizing mousse or spray to add some texture and volume to your hair. You can also use a round brush and blow-dryer to create a sleek, polished look. If you prefer a more tousled look, you can use a curling iron or wand to add some loose waves to your hair.

Maintaining Your Bob

To keep your bob looking its best, it's important to maintain it regularly. This means getting regular trims every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your haircut. You should also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Finally, avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and make your hair look dry and brittle.

FAQs

Q: Will a bob haircut work for all face shapes?

A: Yes, a bob haircut can work for all face shapes! Your stylist will be able to adjust the length and shape of your bob to suit your individual features.

Q: How can I prevent my bob from looking too bulky?

A: To prevent your bob from looking too bulky, we recommend going for a longer length and using products that add texture and volume to your hair.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, here are some additional hair care tips for those with thick hair:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Consider using a hair mask to moisturize your hair
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to have a beautiful, manageable bob with thick hair. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you. Good luck!
