How To Cut The Back Of Your Hair In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby May 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune to get your hair cut in a salon? Do you want to learn how to cut the back of your hair in layers? If you answered yes to either of these questions, then you have come to the right place. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your hair in layers, specifically focusing on the back.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Cut the Back of Your Hair in Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Cutting your hair in layers can make it look more voluminous and add texture to your locks. It is a great way to change up your look without sacrificing too much length. While cutting the back of your hair in layers may seem intimidating at first, with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily at home.

How to Cut the Back of Your Hair in Layers

Before we get into the step-by-step guide, let's go over the tools you will need to successfully cut the back of your hair in layers:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties
  • Clips
  • Large mirror

Now that you have your tools ready, let's get started with the step-by-step guide.

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will give you a better idea of how your hair falls and where the layers should be.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using hair ties and clips. This will make it easier to cut your hair in layers without cutting too much off at once.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it out straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends with the scissors at an angle. Make sure to cut the hair at a slight angle, as this will create a more natural-looking layer.

Step 4: Work Your Way Up

Continue working your way up in sections, cutting each layer slightly shorter than the previous one. Remember to cut at an angle and to not take off too much hair at once.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you have finished cutting all the layers, check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to even out the layers and ensure that they are all at the desired length.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair in Layers

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger ones to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Cut your hair at an angle to create a more natural-looking layer.
  • Don't be afraid to make mistakes - hair grows back!
  • If you are unsure about cutting your hair in layers, start with a small trim and work your way up to more layers.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my hair in layers?

A: It is recommended to cut your hair in layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain the style and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my hair in layers if it is curly?

A: Yes, you can cut curly hair in layers. However, it is important to cut each layer at the same length to avoid creating a choppy look.

Q: Should I wet or dry my hair before cutting it in layers?

A: It is recommended to cut your hair when it is dry, as wet hair can shrink and create an uneven cut.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

After cutting your hair in layers, it is important to take care of it properly to maintain the style and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, too often as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims every 6-8 weeks to maintain the style and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back too tightly, as this can cause breakage and damage.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut the back of your hair in layers at home and achieve a stylish and voluminous look. Remember to take your time, start with small sections, and don't be afraid to make mistakes - hair grows back!
