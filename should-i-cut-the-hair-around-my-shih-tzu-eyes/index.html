Should I Cut The Hair Around My Shih Tzu Eyes

Written by Aprilia Jul 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Shih Tzu dogs are known for their long, luxurious coats that require regular grooming. One question that many Shih Tzu owners ask is whether or not they should cut the hair around their dog's eyes. The answer is not a simple yes or no, and it depends on a number of factors. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of cutting the hair around your Shih Tzu's eyes, how to do it correctly, and some tips for maintaining your dog's coat.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut the Hair Around Your Shih Tzu's Eyes
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Shih Tzu's Coat
  • FAQs
  • Shih Tzu Hair Care

Description

The hair around a Shih Tzu's eyes can grow quite long, and if left untrimmed, it can obstruct their vision and cause eye irritation. Some owners prefer to leave the hair long, as it can give the dog a cute and fluffy appearance. However, if you're considering cutting the hair around your Shih Tzu's eyes, there are a few things to keep in mind.

How to Cut the Hair Around Your Shih Tzu's Eyes

If you've decided to cut the hair around your Shih Tzu's eyes, it's important to do it correctly to avoid injuring your dog. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Use Sharp Scissors

Make sure you have a pair of sharp, high-quality scissors that are designed for pet grooming. Dull scissors can pull on your dog's hair, causing discomfort and potentially leading to injury.

Ensure Your Dog is Calm

Before you start trimming, make sure your dog is calm and relaxed. If they're nervous or anxious, they may move around and make it difficult to trim the hair evenly.

Don't Cut Too Close to the Eye

When trimming the hair around your Shih Tzu's eyes, make sure you don't cut too close to the eye itself. This can cause injury and discomfort for your dog.

Trim Gradually

It's better to trim the hair gradually, in small sections, rather than trying to cut it all at once. This will help you achieve a more even trim and reduce the risk of injury.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting the hair around your Shih Tzu's eyes:

  1. Gather your supplies, including sharp scissors and a comb.
  2. Make sure your dog is calm and relaxed.
  3. Comb the hair around your dog's eyes, making sure it's free from tangles and knots.
  4. Using your scissors, trim the hair gradually, in small sections. Make sure you don't cut too close to the eye.
  5. Take breaks as needed to allow your dog to rest and relax.
  6. Once you've finished trimming, use a damp cloth to wipe away any loose hairs around your dog's eyes.

Tips for Maintaining Your Shih Tzu's Coat

In addition to trimming the hair around your Shih Tzu's eyes, there are a few other things you can do to maintain their coat:

Brush Regularly

Shih Tzu dogs need to be brushed regularly to prevent tangles and mats. Aim to brush your dog's coat at least once a day.

Keep Your Dog Clean

Bathing your Shih Tzu regularly can help keep their coat clean and free from dirt and debris. Aim to bathe your dog every 2-4 weeks.

Visit a Professional Groomer

If you're not comfortable grooming your Shih Tzu yourself, consider taking them to a professional groomer. They can help keep your dog's coat looking healthy and well-maintained.

FAQs

Q: What happens if I don't trim the hair around my Shih Tzu's eyes?

A: If you don't trim the hair around your Shih Tzu's eyes, it can obstruct their vision and cause eye irritation.

Q: How often should I trim the hair around my Shih Tzu's eyes?

A: You should trim the hair around your Shih Tzu's eyes as needed, depending on how quickly it grows.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to trim my Shih Tzu's hair?

A: No, you should use scissors that are designed for pet grooming to avoid injuring your dog.

Shih Tzu Hair Care

Maintaining a Shih Tzu's coat can be a lot of work, but it's essential for their health and well-being. In addition to trimming the hair around their eyes, make sure you're brushing their coat regularly, keeping them clean, and visiting a professional groomer as needed. With proper care and attention, your Shih Tzu can have a beautiful and healthy coat for years to come.
