How Do You Know If You Suit Blonde Or Brown Hair

Written by Aprilia Aug 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're considering changing your hair color, you may be wondering which shade would suit you best. Should you go blonde or brown? While there's no one-size-fits-all answer, there are some tips and tricks you can use to determine which color will complement your skin tone and features. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about choosing between blonde and brown hair.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Determine Your Ideal Hair Color
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing Your Color
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Color
  • FAQs About Blonde and Brown Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Healthy Locks

How to Determine Your Ideal Hair Color

The first step in choosing your hair color is to consider your skin undertones. Your skin can have warm, cool, or neutral undertones, which will affect how certain hair colors look on you. Here's how to determine your undertones:

  • If you have warm undertones, your skin will have a yellow or golden hue. You might look best in warm shades of blonde or brown, such as honey or caramel.
  • If you have cool undertones, your skin will have a pink or blue hue. You might look best in cool shades of blonde or brown, such as ash or platinum.
  • If you have neutral undertones, your skin will have a mix of warm and cool tones. You'll have more flexibility in choosing your hair color, as both warm and cool shades will likely look good on you.

Another factor to consider is your natural hair color. If you have very dark hair, going blonde might not be the best choice for you, as it can be damaging and require a lot of upkeep. On the other hand, if you have light hair, going dark might wash you out and make you look older.

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing Your Color

Now that you have an idea of your skin undertones and natural hair color, follow these steps to choose your ideal hair color:

  1. Research hair colors that complement your skin undertones. Look for celebrities or models with similar skin tones and see what shades they've worn.
  2. Consider your lifestyle and personality. Do you want a low-maintenance color or are you willing to spend time and money on upkeep?
  3. Consult with a professional stylist. They can help you choose a shade that will complement your features and provide advice on how to maintain your color.
  4. Try on wigs or use online hair color simulators to get a better idea of how different shades will look on you.
  5. Make the leap! Once you've decided on your color, schedule an appointment with your stylist and get ready to rock your new look.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Color

Whether you choose blonde or brown hair, here are some tips for keeping your color looking vibrant:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading and maintain shine.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip your color. Aim for every other day or every few days.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.
  • Get regular touch-ups to keep your color looking fresh.

FAQs About Blonde and Brown Hair

Here are some common questions about choosing between blonde and brown hair:

1. Is blonde hair more high-maintenance than brown hair?

Generally, yes. Blonde hair requires more frequent touch-ups and can be more damaging to your hair. However, the level of maintenance depends on the shade of blonde you choose and your natural hair color.

2. Can I go blonde if I have dark hair?

Yes, but it may take multiple sessions to achieve your desired shade and can be damaging to your hair. Talk to your stylist about the best approach for your hair type and color.

3. Will going blonde or brown make me look older?

Not necessarily. The right shade can actually make you look younger and more vibrant. It all depends on your skin tone and features.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Locks

No matter what color you choose, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips for keeping your locks healthy:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat tools too frequently, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of protein and vitamins, which are essential for healthy hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

Now that you know how to choose between blonde and brown hair, it's time to make a decision and rock your new look. Just remember to take good care of your hair and consult with a professional stylist if you have any questions or concerns. Happy coloring!
