Will Thick Hair Grow Long Tips And Tricks For Long Luscious Locks

Written by Petter Jul 14, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're one of the lucky ones with thick, voluminous hair, you may be wondering if your hair will grow long. While some people may have trouble growing their hair due to genetics, hair type, or hair care practices, having thick hair can actually be an advantage when it comes to growing your locks. In this article, we'll explore the question "will thick hair grow long?" and provide tips and tricks for achieving long, healthy hair.

Table of Contents

  2. Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Long Hair
  3. Tips for Growing Long, Thick Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Thick Hair

How to Grow Long Hair with Thick Hair

The good news is that thick hair is generally healthy and strong, which means it's more likely to grow long. However, there are a few things you can do to help your hair grow as long as possible:

  • Get regular trims: While it may seem counterintuitive, getting regular trims can actually help your hair grow longer. This is because split ends can travel up the hair shaft and cause breakage, which can make your hair appear shorter than it is. Getting a trim every 6-8 weeks can help prevent split ends and keep your hair healthy.
  • Stay hydrated: Drinking plenty of water is not only good for your overall health, but it can also help your hair grow. Your hair needs moisture to stay healthy and hydrated, so make sure you're drinking at least 8 glasses of water a day.
  • Eat a balanced diet: Your hair needs nutrients like protein, biotin, and vitamin E to grow strong and healthy. Make sure you're eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats.

Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Long Hair

While there's no magic formula for growing long hair, there are a few steps you can take to help your hair reach its full potential:

  1. Start with healthy hair: Before you can start growing your hair, you need to make sure it's healthy. This means addressing any issues like split ends, dryness, or damage before you start growing it out.
  2. Be patient: Hair grows at an average rate of about half an inch per month, so it can take a while to see significant growth. Be patient and don't get discouraged if your hair doesn't grow as quickly as you'd like.
  3. Protect your hair: To prevent damage and breakage, make sure you're protecting your hair when you sleep, exercise, or style it. Use a silk or satin pillowcase, wear a protective style when working out, and avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair.
  4. Get regular trims: As we mentioned earlier, getting regular trims can actually help your hair grow longer. Make sure you're getting a trim every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.
  5. Be gentle: Treat your hair with care and avoid harsh chemicals, heat styling tools, or rough handling. Use natural, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners, and avoid blow drying or flat ironing your hair too often.

Tips for Growing Long, Thick Hair

Now that we've covered the basics, here are a few additional tips for growing long, thick hair:

  • Massage your scalp: Massaging your scalp can help stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.
  • Use a hair growth serum: There are many hair growth serums on the market that can help boost hair growth. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, caffeine, or castor oil.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals: Chemical treatments like coloring, bleaching, or relaxing can damage your hair and make it harder to grow long. Avoid these treatments if possible, or use gentle, non-damaging alternatives.

FAQs

How long does it take to grow long hair?

On average, hair grows at a rate of about half an inch per month. However, this can vary depending on factors like genetics, age, and overall health. It may take several months or even years to grow your hair to your desired length.

Can I use hair extensions to make my hair look longer?

Yes, hair extensions can be a great way to add length and volume to your hair. However, it's important to choose high-quality extensions that match your natural hair color and texture, and to care for them properly to avoid damage.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Finally, here are a few tips for caring for your thick hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb: Thick hair can be prone to tangles, so use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Use a deep conditioner: Thick hair can be prone to dryness, so use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid heavy products: While thick hair can handle more product than fine hair, avoid using heavy, greasy products that can weigh your hair down or make it look greasy.

With these tips and tricks, you can help your thick hair grow long and healthy. Remember to be patient, take care of your hair, and enjoy the journey to long, luscious locks!
