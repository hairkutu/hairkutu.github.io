How To Cut Layers In Your Own Hair Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 09, 2023 · 6 min read

If you’ve been wanting to change up your hairstyle without breaking the bank, cutting layers in your own hair can be a great option. Not only is it cost-effective, but it can also be a fun DIY project to take on. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to cut layers in your own hair short.

How to Cut Layers in Your Own Hair Short: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, make sure you have the necessary tools on hand. You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

It’s important to start with clean, dry hair to ensure a more accurate cut. Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before beginning the cutting process.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into three parts: top, middle, and bottom. Clip each section out of the way, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers and cut the ends in a straight line. Repeat this process for the entire bottom layer.

Step 5: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the middle layer and repeat the same process as step 4, cutting the ends of each section in a straight line.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top layer and repeat the same process as step 4 and 5. However, for this layer, angle your scissors when cutting the ends to create more texture and volume.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve cut all three layers, use your scissors to blend the sections together by lightly snipping the ends. This will create a more natural and seamless look.

Step 8: Check for Evenness

After you’ve finished cutting, check for evenness by running your fingers through your hair and looking in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure a symmetrical look.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Your Own Hair Short

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you cut
  • When cutting the top layer, angle your scissors for a more layered look
  • Take your time and don’t rush the process

FAQs

Is it possible to cut layers in my own hair if I have long hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in your own hair regardless of the length. However, it may be more difficult to cut layers in longer hair since it can be harder to see the ends.

Can I cut layers in my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in your own curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so cut less than you think you need to.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers in Your Own Hair Short

After cutting layers in your own hair, it’s important to take good care of your hair to maintain the new style. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Additionally, get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and to prevent split ends.

By following these steps and tips, you can successfully cut layers in your own hair short. Remember to take your time, start with small sections, and check for evenness throughout the process. With a little practice, you may even become a pro at cutting your own hair!
