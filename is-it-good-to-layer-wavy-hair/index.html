Is It Good To Layer Wavy Hair

Written by Petter Aug 16, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you wondering if layering is good for your wavy hair? You've come to the right place! Layering can be a great way to add texture and volume to your wavy hair, but it's important to consider your hair type and personal preferences before making the cut. In this article, we'll explore the benefits and drawbacks of layering wavy hair, provide step-by-step instructions for layering at home, and offer tips for caring for your newly layered locks.

Table of Contents

  • Benefits of Layering Wavy Hair
  • Drawbacks of Layering Wavy Hair
  • How to Layer Wavy Hair
  • Tips for Layering Wavy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Layered Wavy Hair

Benefits of Layering Wavy Hair

Layering can be a great way to enhance your natural waves and add body and movement to your hair. By removing some of the weight from your hair, layers can help your waves bounce and curl more easily, especially if you have long or thick hair. Layering can also make your hair look more dimensional and flattering, especially if you have an oval or heart-shaped face.

Another benefit of layering wavy hair is that it can make your hair easier to style and manage. With the right layers, you can achieve a variety of looks, from beachy waves to sleek updos. And if you have fine or thin hair, layers can give the illusion of more volume and thickness.

Drawbacks of Layering Wavy Hair

While layering can be a great option for many wavy-haired people, it's not for everyone. If you have very tight or coiled curls, for example, layering may actually make your hair look frizzy or uneven. Similarly, if you have a lot of natural volume or your hair tends to pouf in humidity, layering may make your hair look too fluffy or unmanageable.

Another potential drawback of layering wavy hair is that it can lead to more maintenance and upkeep. Depending on the length and thickness of your hair, you may need to trim your layers every few months to keep them looking fresh and prevent split ends. You may also need to use specific styling products, such as curl creams or mousses, to enhance your waves and prevent frizz.

How to Layer Wavy Hair

If you've decided that layering is right for you, the next step is to decide how you want to get your layers. While you can certainly visit a salon and have a professional stylist cut your hair, you can also layer your hair at home with a few simple tools and techniques. Here's how:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start layering, you'll need a few basic tools, including:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb or brush
  • Hair clips or elastics to section your hair
  • A mirror or two to check your progress

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips or elastics to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin with one of the back sections, and take a small section of hair to start cutting. Hold the section straight up and away from your head, and use your scissors to cut a small amount of hair at a time, working from the bottom up. Be sure to cut at an angle to create soft, blended layers.

Step 4: Repeat on the Other Side

Once you've finished one side of your head, repeat the process on the other side, making sure to keep your layers even and balanced.

Step 5: Trim the Front

Finally, trim the front sections of your hair to frame your face and create a more flattering shape. You can either cut straight across or angle the scissors to create a softer, more textured look.

Tips for Layering Wavy Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when layering your wavy hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair, and cut a little bit at a time. You can always cut more, but it's hard to undo a bad cut!
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Be patient and take your time. Layering can be a time-consuming process, but the results are worth it.
  • Consider your face shape and hair texture when deciding how to layer your hair.
  • Experiment with different lengths and angles to find the right layers for your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I layer my wavy hair if it's already short?

A: Yes! Layering can work for any length of hair, including short and medium styles. Your stylist may use different techniques to create layers on shorter hair, such as razor cutting or point cutting.

Q: Will layering damage my hair?

A: Properly layered hair should not cause any damage to your hair. However, if you cut your hair too often or use dull scissors, you may end up with split ends or uneven layers.

Q: Do I need to use specific products on my layered wavy hair?

A: While you don't necessarily need to use specific products on your layered wavy hair, using curl-enhancing products can help enhance your waves and prevent frizz. Consider using a leave-in conditioner, curl cream, or mousse to keep your hair looking its best.

Hair Care for Layered Wavy Hair

Once you've layered your wavy hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are a few tips:

  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair and undo your layers.
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you do use hot tools.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it look dry and frizzy.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

Now that you know the benefits and drawbacks of layering wavy hair, as well as how to do it yourself at home, you can decide if it's the right choice for you. With the right layers and care, you can achieve beautiful, bouncy waves that will turn heads wherever you go!
