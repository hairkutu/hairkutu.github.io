How To Cut The Back Of Your Hair Straight Across By Yourself

Written by William Apr 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to save some money on haircuts or simply prefer to style your own hair, you may be wondering how to cut the back of your hair straight across by yourself. While it may seem challenging at first, with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know to achieve a straight and even cut.

Description

Cutting the back of your hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're not a professional hairstylist. It's important to have the right tools on hand and to take your time to ensure a precise cut. By following these steps, you'll be able to achieve a straight across cut that looks like it was done by a professional.

How to Cut the Back of Your Hair Straight Across by Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section off your hair. Make sure your scissors are sharp to ensure a smooth and precise cut.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's important to start with clean and dry hair to ensure an even cut. Wash your hair as you normally would and allow it to air dry or blow-dry it until it's completely dry.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into two parts, making a part down the middle of your head. Clip one section out of the way, leaving the other section down.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer of your hair, comb your hair straight down and use your scissors to cut across in a straight line. Be sure to cut small sections at a time and check your work frequently to ensure an even cut.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, release the top layer of hair and comb it straight down. Use the bottom layer as a guide and cut across in a straight line. Again, be sure to cut small sections at a time and check your work frequently.

Step 6: Check for Any Unevenness

After you've finished cutting your hair straight across, use your comb to check for any unevenness. If you notice any areas that need to be trimmed, use your scissors to make small adjustments.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your cut, style your hair as you normally would. Whether you prefer to wear your hair straight or styled in curls, your new straight across cut will look great.

Tips

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a smooth and precise cut.
  • Cut small sections at a time to ensure an even cut.
  • Check your work frequently to avoid any mistakes.

FAQ

Can I cut the back of my hair by myself?

Yes, with the right tools and technique, you can cut the back of your hair by yourself. It may take some practice to achieve a professional-looking cut, but with patience and attention to detail, you can do it.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. Take a step back and assess the situation. You can always make small adjustments to fix any mistakes. If you're unsure how to fix the mistake, it's best to seek the advice of a professional hairstylist.

How often should I cut the back of my hair?

The frequency of haircuts depends on the individual's hair type and desired style. However, it's recommended to get a trim every six to eight weeks to keep your hair healthy and looking its best.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. Avoid using heat tools too often and use a heat protectant spray when styling with heat. Lastly, consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair nourished and strong.
