How To Thin Short Hair Without Thinning Shears

Description

Thinning shears are a popular tool for hairstylists when it comes to removing bulk from thick hair or creating texture in long hair. However, for those with short hair, thinning shears may not be the best option. Thinning shears can easily make short hair look choppy or uneven. Thankfully, there are other ways to thin short hair without using thinning shears. In this blog post, we will walk you through how to do just that.

How to Thin Short Hair Without Thinning Shears

Before we dive into the steps of thinning short hair without thinning shears, it is important to note that this method is most suitable for those with hair that is at least shoulder length. If your hair is shorter than this, it may be more difficult to thin without thinning shears.

The best way to thin short hair without thinning shears is by using a razor comb. A razor comb is a comb that has a razor blade built into it. This allows you to thin your hair as you comb through it.

Step by Step Instructions

Here are the steps to follow when using a razor comb to thin short hair:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. It is important to have clean and dry hair before you begin.
  2. Choose a razor comb that has a blade width that matches your desired hair thickness. A wider blade will remove more hair with each pass, while a narrower blade will remove less.
  3. Starting at the back of your head, comb a section of hair straight up. Hold the razor comb at a 45-degree angle and comb through the section of hair from top to bottom.
  4. Continue combing through small sections of hair until you have achieved your desired level of thinness.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 on the sides and front of your head.

Tips for Thinning Short Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning short hair:

  • Start with a wider blade and work your way down to a narrower blade. This will allow you to control the amount of hair you are removing.
  • Only thin the ends of your hair. Avoid thinning the roots, as this can lead to frizz and breakage.
  • Be gentle when combing through your hair with the razor comb. Pulling too hard can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you are thinning your hair.

FAQs

Can I use regular scissors to thin short hair?

While you can use regular scissors to thin short hair, they are not as effective as a razor comb. Regular scissors can make short hair look choppy or uneven.

Will thinning my hair make it look thinner?

Thinning your hair will not make it look thinner in terms of volume. Thinning your hair will remove bulk and create texture, making your hair look more voluminous.

Hair Care After Thinning

After thinning your hair, it is important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for hair care after thinning:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair for at least a week after thinning. This will allow your hair to recover from the thinning process.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as alcohol can dry out your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the health of your hair.

Thinning short hair without thinning shears may seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily and effectively. Follow the steps outlined in this blog post and you will be on your way to achieving the perfect level of hair thinness.
