How To Cut A High Fade At Home Guide For Beginners

How to Cut a High Fade at Home

A high fade is a popular hairstyle that involves cutting hair on the sides and back of the head very short and gradually tapering it upwards towards the top of the head. The top hair can be left longer or cut to the same length as the sides. Here are the basic steps for cutting a high fade at home:

1. Gather the Right Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Clippers with different guard sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clip or hair tie
  • Mirror

2. Decide on the Length and Style

Decide on the length and style you want for the top of your head. You can leave the top hair longer, or you can cut it to the same length as the sides. You can also choose to style the top hair in a particular way, such as a pompadour or quiff.

3. Cut the Top Hair

If you're leaving the top hair longer, comb it and cut it to the desired length. If you're cutting it to the same length as the sides, use a clip or hair tie to separate the top hair from the sides.

4. Cut the Sides and Back

Start with the longest guard size and cut the hair on the sides and back of the head. Gradually switch to shorter guard sizes to create a tapered effect. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that weren't cut by the clippers.

5. Blend the Top and Sides

Blend the top and sides by using a clipper without a guard to taper the hair around the edges. Use scissors to blend any uneven areas.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to cut a high fade at home:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Decide on the Length and Style

Decide on the length and style you want for the top of your head. Comb your hair in the direction you want it to fall.

Step 3: Cut the Top Hair

If you're leaving the top hair longer, comb it and cut it to the desired length. If you're cutting it to the same length as the sides, use a clip or hair tie to separate the top hair from the sides.

Step 4: Cut the Sides and Back

Start with the longest guard size and cut the hair on the sides and back of the head. Gradually switch to shorter guard sizes to create a tapered effect. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that weren't cut by the clippers.

Step 5: Blend the Top and Sides

Blend the top and sides by using a clipper without a guard to taper the hair around the edges. Use scissors to blend any uneven areas.

Step 6: Refine the Cut

Use a mirror to check for any uneven areas and refine the cut as necessary. You can also use the clippers to create a line around the edges of the hairline.

Tips for Cutting a High Fade at Home

Here are some tips to help you achieve a great high fade at home:

  • Start with a longer guard size and gradually switch to shorter ones to create a natural-looking fade.
  • Make sure your clippers are sharp and well-maintained for a clean cut.
  • Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that weren't cut by the clippers.
  • Use a mirror to check for any uneven areas and refine the cut as necessary.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.

FAQs

What is a high fade?

A high fade is a popular hairstyle that involves cutting hair on the sides and back of the head very short and gradually tapering it upwards towards the top of the head.

Can I cut a high fade at home?

Yes, you can cut a high fade at home. However, cutting hair can be intimidating, especially if you're doing it for the first time. Make sure you have the necessary tools and take your time.

What tools do I need to cut a high fade?

You will need clippers with different guard sizes, scissors, a comb, a clip or hair tie, and a mirror.

Hair Care After Cutting a High Fade

After cutting a high fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Style your hair with a pomade or hair wax to keep it in place.
  • Visit the barber regularly to maintain the style.

Cutting a high fade at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and a little patience, you can achieve a great look without spending a lot of money. Remember to take your time and don't rush the process.
