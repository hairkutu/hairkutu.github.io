How To Check What Hairstyle Suits Me A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 21, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Choosing the right hairstyle can be a daunting task, especially with so many options available. You want a hairstyle that flatters your face shape, suits your lifestyle, and makes you feel confident. But how do you know which hairstyle is right for you? In this article, we'll walk you through a step-by-step guide on how to check what hairstyle suits you.

Table of Contents:

  • Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape
  • Step 2: Identify Your Hair Type
  • Step 3: Consider Your Lifestyle
  • Step 4: Look for Inspiration
  • Step 5: Consult with a Professional
  • Tips for Choosing the Right Hairstyle
  • FAQs

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing the right hairstyle is to determine your face shape. Your face shape will help you determine which hairstyles will flatter your features and which ones may not work for you. There are several face shapes, including round, oval, square, heart, and diamond. To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and examine your face in the mirror.

If you have a round face, you'll want to choose a hairstyle that adds height and lengthens your face. A layered or textured cut works well for round faces. If you have an oval face, you can pull off most hairstyles, but a long layered cut or a blunt bob are flattering options. For square faces, choose a style that softens your angles, like a long bob or side-swept bangs. Heart-shaped faces look great with a side-parted bob or layered cut, and diamond faces can pull off a short pixie cut or a chin-length bob.

Step 2: Identify Your Hair Type

Identifying your hair type is crucial in choosing the right hairstyle. Your hair type will determine which cuts, textures, and styles will work best for you. There are several hair types, including straight, wavy, curly, and coily. If you have straight hair, a blunt cut or a long layered cut works well. Wavy hair looks great with a shoulder-length cut or textured layers. For curly hair, a long bob or a layered cut with bangs can be flattering. Coily hair looks great in a short pixie cut or a long layered cut.

Step 3: Consider Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle is an important factor in choosing the right hairstyle. If you're always on the go, you'll want a low-maintenance style that doesn't require a lot of styling time. If you work in a professional environment, you'll want a style that looks polished and put-together. Consider how much time you're willing to spend on your hair each day and choose a style that fits your lifestyle.

Step 4: Look for Inspiration

Once you've determined your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle, it's time to look for inspiration. Browse through magazines, search online, or look at celebrity hairstyles for inspiration. Make a list of styles you like and bring them with you to your hair appointment.

Step 5: Consult with a Professional

Finally, it's essential to consult with a professional hairstylist. They can help you determine which hairstyles will work best for your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle. They can also make recommendations based on your hair's texture, thickness, and condition. Be open to their suggestions and ask questions if you're unsure about anything.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hairstyle

  • Consider your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle when choosing a hairstyle.
  • Look for inspiration in magazines, online, or from celebrity hairstyles.
  • Consult with a professional hairstylist to determine which styles will work best for you.
  • Be open to trying new styles and don't be afraid to ask questions.

FAQs

How often should I get my hair cut?

It depends on your hair type and the style you choose. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I pull off a short hairstyle?

Short hairstyles can be flattering on many face shapes and hair types. Consult with a professional hairstylist to determine which short styles will work best for you.

Hair Care Tips

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools, like flat irons and curling irons, too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Choosing the right hairstyle can be a fun and exciting process. By following these steps, you can find a style that flatters your features, suits your lifestyle, and makes you feel confident. Remember to consult with a professional hairstylist and be open to trying new styles. Happy styling!
