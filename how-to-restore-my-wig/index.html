How To Restore My Wig Tips For Reviving Your Favorite Hairpiece

Written by Bardi Apr 16, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you wondering how to restore your wig to its former glory? Whether you wear wigs for fashion, convenience, or medical reasons, it's important to take care of them properly to extend their lifespan and keep them looking good. In this post, we'll share some tips and step-by-step instructions for restoring your wig and bringing it back to life.

How to Restore My Wig: Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to restore your wig in a few easy steps:

Step 1: Detangle Your Wig

The first step in restoring your wig is to detangle it. Use a wide-tooth comb or a wig brush to gently separate any knots or tangles. Start at the ends of the hair and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to pull too hard or damage the wig fibers. If your wig is especially tangled, you can use a detangling spray or a mixture of water and fabric softener to make it easier to comb through.

Step 2: Wash Your Wig

Once your wig is detangled, it's time to wash it. Fill a sink or basin with cool water and add a small amount of wig shampoo. Gently swish the wig in the water for a few minutes, taking care not to rub or scrub the fibers too hard. Rinse the wig thoroughly with cool water and gently squeeze out the excess water.

Step 3: Condition Your Wig

After washing your wig, it's important to condition it to keep the fibers soft and smooth. Apply a small amount of wig conditioner to the hair, avoiding the roots and cap. Let the conditioner sit on the hair for a few minutes, then rinse it out with cool water.

Step 4: Dry Your Wig

The next step in restoring your wig is to dry it. Gently blot the wig with a towel to remove excess water, then place it on a wig stand or mannequin head to air dry. Avoid using a hair dryer or other heat source, as this can damage the wig fibers and cause them to become frizzy or tangled.

Step 5: Style Your Wig

Once your wig is dry, you can style it as desired. Use a wig brush or comb to create your desired look, and use heat tools sparingly to avoid damaging the fibers. If your wig has lost its shape or volume, you can use a wig styling spray or foam to add body and fullness.

Tips for Restoring Your Wig

Here are some additional tips to help you restore your wig:

  • Be gentle when detangling your wig to avoid damaging the fibers
  • Use cool water when washing and conditioning your wig
  • Avoid using hot tools or excessive heat when styling your wig
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape and prevent tangling
  • Consider using a wig cap or liner to protect your wig from sweat and oil buildup

FAQ about Restoring Wigs

Here are some frequently asked questions about restoring wigs:

How often should I wash my wig?

It depends on how often you wear your wig and how much product you use. As a general rule, you should wash your wig every 6-8 wears to keep it clean and fresh.

Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my wig?

No, you should use special wig shampoo and conditioner that is formulated specifically for wig fibers. Regular shampoo and conditioner can damage the fibers and cause them to become tangled or frizzy.

Can I dye or bleach my wig?

It depends on the type of wig you have. Synthetic wigs cannot be dyed or bleached, as the fibers are not designed to withstand the chemicals. Human hair wigs can be dyed or bleached, but it's best to have it done by a professional stylist to avoid damaging the wig.

Hair Care for Your Wigs

Finally, it's important to take care of your wigs to keep them looking and feeling their best. Here are some tips for hair care for your wigs:

  • Use a wig brush or comb to detangle your wig before and after wearing it
  • Avoid using heat tools or excessive heat when styling your wig
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape and prevent tangling
  • Avoid wearing your wig in the shower or while swimming
  • Use a wig cap or liner to protect your wig from sweat and oil buildup

By following these tips and taking proper care of your wig, you can enjoy wearing it for years to come. Whether you're restoring an old favorite or taking care of a new purchase, these steps will help you keep your wig looking and feeling great.
