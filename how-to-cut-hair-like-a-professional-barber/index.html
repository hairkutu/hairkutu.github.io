How To Cut Hair Like A Professional Barber A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Cutting your hair at home can save you time and money, but it can also be intimidating. With the right tools, techniques, and a little bit of practice, you can learn how to cut hair like a professional barber. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to achieve a professional-looking haircut at home.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You Will Need
  • How to Cut Hair Like a Professional Barber
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Hair Like a Pro
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting

Tools You Will Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you will need to gather the necessary tools. Here are the tools you will need:

  • Clippers with various guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Cape or towel to cover your clothes
  • Mirror or two mirrors to see the back of your head
  • Spray bottle filled with water

How to Cut Hair Like a Professional Barber

Cutting hair like a professional barber requires some basic knowledge of hair cutting techniques. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Choose a hairstyle that suits your face shape and hair type
  • Start with clean and dry hair
  • Use the right tools for the job
  • Work in a well-lit area
  • Take your time and be patient

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve a professional-looking haircut at home:

  1. Start with the sides and back. Use the clippers with a guard to cut the hair to the desired length. Start with the longest guard and work your way down to the shortest guard.
  2. Blend the sides and back into the top. Use the clippers without a guard to create a fade. Start from the bottom and work your way up, blending the hair as you go.
  3. Cut the top of the hair. Use the scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Start with small sections and work your way through the hair.
  4. Blend the top into the sides and back. Use the scissors to blend the top into the sides and back. Use a comb to help blend the hair.
  5. Cut the bangs. Use the scissors to cut the bangs to the desired length. Cut vertically to create texture.
  6. Trim the neckline. Use the clippers with no guard to trim the hair on the neckline. Follow the natural hairline.
  7. Check for evenness. Use the mirrors to check for any uneven spots. Make any necessary adjustments.
  8. Clean up. Use the clippers to clean up any stray hairs around the ears and neck.

Tips for Cutting Hair Like a Pro

These tips will help you achieve a professional-looking haircut:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair at once
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen the hair before cutting to make it easier to work with
  • Use a comb to help guide the scissors and create even lines
  • Cut vertically to create texture and avoid blunt lines
  • Take breaks to rest your hands and avoid making mistakes due to fatigue

FAQs

Can I cut my hair if it is wet?

It is best to cut hair when it is dry to avoid cutting too much or unevenly. However, if you must cut your hair when it is wet, make sure to cut less than you normally would and use a longer guard on the clippers.

How do I cut the back of my head?

Use two mirrors to see the back of your head. Hold one mirror in front of you and the other behind you to get a clear view of the back of your head while cutting.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on the style and how fast your hair grows. Generally, hair should be trimmed every 4-6 weeks to maintain the style and keep it looking healthy.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it is important to take care of it to maintain the style and keep it looking healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine
  • Use products that are suitable for your hair type
  • Get regular trims to maintain the style

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
