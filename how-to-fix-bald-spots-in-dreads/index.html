How To Fix Bald Spots In Dreads A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter May 20, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have dreadlocks, you know how frustrating it can be to develop bald spots. Bald spots can occur for a variety of reasons, such as tight braiding, excessive tension on the hair, or improper maintenance. However, there are ways to fix bald spots in dreads without having to cut them off completely. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to fix bald spots in dreads and provide tips on how to prevent them from happening in the future.

Description

Bald spots in dreads occur when the hair falls out, leaving a visible patch of scalp. This can be caused by a variety of reasons, such as tight braiding, excessive tension on the hair, or improper maintenance. When this happens, it can be frustrating and embarrassing, especially if you have been growing your dreads for a long time. However, there are ways to fix bald spots in dreads, and with proper maintenance, you can prevent them from happening in the future.

How to Fix Bald Spots in Dreads

If you have noticed bald spots in your dreads, don't panic. There are ways to fix them without having to cut off your entire dreadlock. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to fix bald spots in dreads:

Step 1: Identify the Cause of the Bald Spot

The first step in fixing bald spots in dreads is to identify the cause of the bald spot. If the bald spot is due to tight braiding, you may need to loosen the braid to allow for better blood circulation. If the bald spot is due to excessive tension on the hair, you may need to change your hairstyle to avoid putting too much pressure on the hair. If the bald spot is due to improper maintenance, you may need to adjust your hair care routine.

Step 2: Cleanse Your Scalp

Before fixing the bald spot, it's important to cleanse your scalp. Use a gentle shampoo to remove any buildup or residue on your scalp. Make sure to massage your scalp gently to improve blood circulation.

Step 3: Apply Hair Growth Products

Apply hair growth products to the bald spot to promote hair growth. Look for products that contain biotin, keratin, and other hair-boosting ingredients. These products can help stimulate hair growth and prevent future bald spots.

Step 4: Moisturize Your Hair

Moisturize your hair to prevent future breakage and bald spots. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Dry hair is more prone to breakage, which can lead to bald spots.

Step 5: Reattach the Loose Hair

If the bald spot is due to loose hair, reattach the hair to the dreadlock. Use a crochet hook to pull the hair through the dreadlock and secure it in place. Make sure to do this gently to avoid causing further damage to the hair.

Step 6: Cover the Bald Spot

If the bald spot is still visible, you can cover it up with a head wrap or scarf. This can help protect the hair and scalp while promoting hair growth.

Tips for Preventing Bald Spots in Dreads

Preventing bald spots in dreads requires proper maintenance and care. Here are some tips to help prevent bald spots:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that put pressure on the hair and scalp
  • Use a gentle shampoo to cleanse your scalp regularly
  • Moisturize your hair to prevent dryness and breakage
  • Use hair growth products to promote healthy hair growth
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage

FAQs

Can you fix bald spots in dreads?

Yes, you can fix bald spots in dreads with proper maintenance and care. Follow the steps outlined in this article to fix bald spots in dreads.

What causes bald spots in dreads?

Bald spots in dreads can be caused by tight braiding, excessive tension on the hair, or improper maintenance. These factors can lead to hair breakage and bald spots.

How can I prevent bald spots in dreads?

To prevent bald spots in dreads, avoid tight hairstyles, use a gentle shampoo, moisturize your hair, use hair growth products, avoid heavy products, and get regular trims.

Hair Care for Dreads

Dreadlocks require proper maintenance and care to prevent breakage and bald spots. Here are some tips for taking care of your dreadlocks:

  • Use a gentle shampoo to cleanse your scalp
  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage
  • Use hair growth products to promote healthy hair growth
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage

By following these tips, you can keep your dreadlocks healthy and prevent bald spots from occurring.
