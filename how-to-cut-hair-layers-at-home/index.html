How To Cut Hair Layers At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Apr 06, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of your same old hairstyle? Want to try something new without going to the salon? Cutting layers at home is a great way to add some dimension and texture to your hair. In this article, we'll go over how to cut hair layers at home in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language. From how to section your hair to what tools you'll need, we've got you covered. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Hair Layers at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Hair Layers
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Layers

How to Cut Hair Layers at Home

Cutting hair layers at home can be intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it's totally achievable. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle with water

Once you have your tools ready, it's time to section your hair. This will make it easier to cut the layers evenly. Here's how:

Sectioning Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Comb through any tangles and make sure it's completely dry. Then, using a comb, part your hair down the middle from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Clip each side out of the way.

Next, take a small section of hair from the top of your head and clip the rest out of the way. This will be your guide section for the rest of your layers. Use your comb to comb it straight up and hold it between your fingers at the length you want to cut it. Make sure it's even on both sides and cut straight across with your scissors. This will be your shortest layer.

After you've cut your guide section, take another small section of hair from just below it and clip the rest away. Use your guide section as a reference and cut this section at the same length. Continue working your way down your head, taking small sections of hair and cutting them at the same length as your guide section.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you've sectioned your hair, it's time to start cutting your layers. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Take a small section of hair from the top of your head and hold it between your fingers at the length you want to cut it. Use your guide section as a reference.
  2. Cut the section straight across with your scissors.
  3. Repeat this process with another small section of hair just below the first, using the first section as a reference.
  4. Continue working your way down your head, taking small sections of hair and cutting them at the same length as the previous sections.
  5. Once you've finished cutting all of your layers, take a look in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Hair Layers

Here are some tips to keep in mind while cutting your hair layers:

  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to cut a little bit at a time and check your progress as you go.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut evenly.
  • Use your fingers as a guide. Holding your hair between your fingers can help you cut it at the length you want.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so it's okay to take breaks and come back to it later.

FAQs

Can I cut layers into curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers into curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair can shrink up when it dries, so be careful not to cut too much off.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go back and fix it later. It's better to cut a little bit at a time than to cut too much off at once.

Do I need to wash my hair before cutting layers?

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry. This will make it easier to see what you're doing and ensure that your layers are even.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

Once you've cut your layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep your layers looking fresh.

Now that you know how to cut hair layers at home, you can experiment with different styles and give yourself a fresh new look. Just remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
