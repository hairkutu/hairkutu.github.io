Why Does The Back Of My Head Feel Dry

Written by William Mar 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Have you ever experienced a dry, itchy sensation on the back of your head? It can be a frustrating and uncomfortable feeling, especially if it persists for an extended period. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why the back of your head might feel dry and provide some tips on how to alleviate the discomfort.

What Causes Dryness on the Back of the Head?

There are several reasons why the back of your head might feel dry:

1. Dandruff

Dandruff is a common condition characterized by flaky, itchy skin on the scalp. While it usually affects the scalp itself, it can also extend to the back of the head. Dandruff is caused by a combination of factors, including a yeast that lives on the scalp, excess oil production, and sensitivity to certain hair products.

2. Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that causes skin cells to grow too quickly, resulting in scaly patches on the skin. It can affect any part of the body, including the scalp and the back of the head.

3. Eczema

Eczema is a condition that causes dry, itchy, and inflamed skin. Like psoriasis, it can affect any part of the body, including the scalp and the back of the head.

4. Dry Scalp

Just like the skin on the rest of your body, your scalp can also become dry and flaky. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including cold weather, low humidity, and harsh hair products.

How to Alleviate Dryness on the Back of the Head

If you're experiencing dryness on the back of your head, there are several things you can do to alleviate the discomfort:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Switch to a gentle, fragrance-free shampoo that won't irritate your scalp. Look for products that are specifically designed for dry or sensitive scalps.

2. Moisturize Your Scalp

Apply a moisturizing scalp treatment or oil to the affected area. This will help to soothe the dryness and prevent further irritation. Avoid using heavy oils or products that can clog your pores.

3. Avoid Hot Water

Hot water can strip your scalp of its natural oils, worsening the dryness. Wash your hair with lukewarm water instead.

4. Use a Humidifier

If you live in a dry climate or are experiencing low humidity, use a humidifier to add moisture to the air. This can help to prevent your scalp from becoming too dry.

FAQs

1. Can stress cause dryness on the back of the head?

Yes, stress can contribute to a variety of skin conditions, including dandruff and eczema.

2. Should I see a doctor if my dryness persists?

If your dryness persists despite home remedies, you should see a dermatologist. They can help to diagnose the underlying cause of your dryness and recommend appropriate treatment.

Hair Care Tips

Proper hair care can go a long way in preventing dryness on the back of the head. Here are some tips:

1. Don't Overwash

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days.

2. Use a Conditioner

Conditioner can help to hydrate your hair and prevent dryness. Apply it to the mid-lengths and ends of your hair, avoiding your scalp.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Elements

Wear a hat or scarf in cold weather to protect your scalp from the elements.

4. Choose the Right Hair Products

Avoid products that contain alcohol or other harsh ingredients that can dry out your scalp. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type.

In conclusion, dryness on the back of the head can be caused by a variety of factors, including dandruff, psoriasis, eczema, and dry scalp. By using gentle hair care products, moisturizing your scalp, and avoiding hot water, you can alleviate the discomfort and prevent further dryness. If your dryness persists, see a dermatologist for diagnosis and treatment.
