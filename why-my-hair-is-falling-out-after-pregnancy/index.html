Why Is My Hair Falling Out After Pregnancy

Written by Robby Jul 09, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you've recently given birth, you may be experiencing hair loss. This is a common postpartum symptom that affects many new mothers. In this article, we'll discuss why your hair is falling out after pregnancy and what you can do to prevent it.

Description

Postpartum hair loss, also known as telogen effluvium, is a common condition that affects many women after giving birth. During pregnancy, your body experiences hormonal changes that can cause your hair to grow thicker and healthier. However, after giving birth, your hormone levels return to normal, and your hair may start to shed excessively.

How to identify postpartum hair loss?

Postpartum hair loss is characterized by excessive shedding that occurs several weeks after giving birth. You may notice clumps of hair falling out while brushing or washing your hair. This shedding can last for several months before your hair growth returns to normal.

Step by step guide on how to prevent postpartum hair loss

1. Take care of your hair

To prevent postpartum hair loss, you should take good care of your hair. Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your hair. Instead, use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners that are formulated for postpartum hair loss.

2. Eat a healthy diet

Eating a healthy diet can also help prevent postpartum hair loss. Your hair needs essential nutrients like protein, iron, and biotin to grow and stay healthy. Make sure to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein sources.

3. Stay hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can also help prevent postpartum hair loss. Your hair needs moisture to stay healthy and hydrated, so make sure to drink at least eight glasses of water a day.

4. Manage stress

Stress can also contribute to postpartum hair loss. Try to manage your stress levels by practicing relaxation techniques like yoga or meditation. You can also talk to a therapist or counselor if you're feeling overwhelmed.

Tips to care for your hair during postpartum hair loss

1. Use a wide-toothed comb

Using a wide-toothed comb can help prevent further hair loss by avoiding tangles and breakage. Comb your hair gently, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

2. Avoid tight hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, or buns that can pull on your hair and cause breakage. Instead, try loose, comfortable hairstyles like a messy bun or a simple braid.

3. Consider using hair growth supplements

Hair growth supplements like biotin, folic acid, and omega-3 fatty acids can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. Talk to your doctor before taking any supplements to make sure they're safe for you and your baby.

FAQs

1. How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss can last for several months, but it usually stops on its own after about six to twelve months.

2. Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

Postpartum hair loss can be prevented by taking good care of your hair, eating a healthy diet, staying hydrated, and managing stress levels. However, it's a common condition that affects many women after giving birth.

Hair care during postpartum hair loss

Postpartum hair loss can be frustrating, but it's a common condition that affects many women after giving birth. To prevent further hair loss, take good care of your hair, eat a healthy diet, and manage your stress levels. If you're concerned about your hair loss, talk to your doctor or a hair care professional for advice.
