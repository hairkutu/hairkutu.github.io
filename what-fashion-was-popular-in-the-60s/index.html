What Fashion Was Popular In The 60S

Written by Robby Aug 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

The 1960s was a decade of change, rebellion, and experimentation. It was a time when fashion took a turn towards the unconventional, and people began to express themselves through their clothing. In this blog post, we will take a look at what fashion was popular in the 60s, and how it has influenced fashion trends today.

The Miniskirt

The miniskirt was one of the most iconic pieces of clothing from the 60s. It was first introduced by British designer Mary Quant, and quickly became a popular trend. The miniskirt was a symbol of female liberation and rebellion, as it challenged traditional notions of modesty and femininity.

The miniskirt was typically worn with knee-high boots, which helped to elongate the legs and create a more streamlined silhouette. It was also often paired with bold patterns and colors, which were also popular during this decade.

The Hippie Movement

The hippie movement was a cultural revolution that emerged in the mid-1960s. It was a rejection of mainstream values and a celebration of peace, love, and freedom. The fashion of the hippie movement was characterized by loose-fitting, flowy clothing, and a focus on natural materials.

Tie-dye, floral prints, and fringe were all popular elements of hippie fashion. Bell-bottoms, which were wide-legged pants that flared out at the bottom, were also a popular trend during this time.

Bold Patterns and Colors

The 60s was a time of experimentation and self-expression, which was reflected in the bold patterns and colors that were popular during this decade. Paisley, polka dots, and geometric prints were all common patterns, while bright colors like orange, pink, and yellow were popular choices.

These patterns and colors were often used in combination with each other, creating a vibrant and eclectic look. This trend has continued to influence fashion today, with bold patterns and colors still being popular choices for many people.

Mod Style

The mod style was a fashion movement that emerged in the early 1960s. It was characterized by clean lines, simple shapes, and a focus on bold colors and graphic patterns. The mod style was heavily influenced by youth culture, and was a rejection of the more conservative styles of the past.

Mod fashion was often associated with British culture, and was popularized by bands like The Beatles and The Who. The mod style has continued to influence fashion trends, with many designers today drawing inspiration from this iconic decade.

FAQ

What were popular fabrics in the 60s?

Natural fabrics like cotton, wool, and silk were popular choices during the 60s. Synthetic fabrics like polyester and nylon were also introduced during this decade, and were used to create more affordable clothing options.

What accessories were popular in the 60s?

Accessories like large sunglasses, hoop earrings, and headbands were all popular choices during the 60s. Scarves were also a popular accessory, and were often worn around the neck or tied around the head.

What were popular hairstyles in the 60s?

The bouffant, beehive, and pixie cut were all popular hairstyles during the 60s. Long, straight hair was also a popular choice, as were side-swept bangs.

Hair Care

If you want to recreate the iconic hairstyles of the 60s, there are a few things you can do to care for your hair. First, make sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. You can also use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, which will help prevent breakage.

If you want to add volume to your hair, try using a volumizing spray or mousse. You can also use a round brush to create a bouffant or beehive hairstyle. And if you're feeling adventurous, you can always try adding some temporary color with a wash-out hair dye.

Overall, the fashion of the 60s was all about experimentation and self-expression. It was a decade of change and rebellion, and its influence can still be seen in fashion trends today. Whether you're a fan of the miniskirt, the hippie style, or the mod look, there's something for everyone in the fashion of the 60s.
