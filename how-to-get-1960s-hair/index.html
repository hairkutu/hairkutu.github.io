How To Get 1960S Hair The Ultimate Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

The 1960s were a time of great style and fashion. From the mod looks of London to the bohemian styles of San Francisco, the 1960s were a decade of experimentation and change. One of the most iconic looks of the era was the hair. From voluminous bouffants to sleek, straight styles, 1960s hair was all about making a statement. In this guide, we'll show you how to get 1960s hair for a retro look that's perfect for any occasion.

Description: What is 1960s hair?

1960s hair was characterized by a number of different styles. One of the most popular was the bouffant, which was a high, voluminous style that was often worn with a headband or scarf. Another popular style was the beehive, which was similar to the bouffant but with more height at the crown. Straight, sleek styles were also popular, as were curly looks that were achieved with rollers or a curling iron.

How to Get 1960s Hair

Step 1: Start with the Right Cut

The first step to getting 1960s hair is to start with the right cut. Look for a style that has plenty of volume and movement, such as a layered cut or a shaggy bob. You may also want to consider getting bangs, as they were a popular feature of many 1960s hairstyles.

Step 2: Get the Right Products

To achieve the perfect 1960s hair look, you'll need the right products. Look for volumizing shampoos and conditioners, as well as styling products like mousse, hairspray, and pomade. You may also need a straightening iron or curling iron, depending on the style you're going for.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Once you've washed and conditioned your hair, it's time to blow dry it. Use a round brush to add volume and curl to your hair, working in small sections from the roots to the ends. If you're going for a straight style, use a flat brush instead.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Depending on the style you're going for, you may need to use a curling iron or straightening iron to achieve the right look. For a bouffant or beehive, use a teasing comb to add volume at the crown, then smooth the top layer over with a brush. For a straight style, use a flat iron to smooth your hair from the roots to the ends.

Tips for Getting 1960s Hair

Here are some tips for getting the perfect 1960s hair:

  • Use a volumizing product like mousse or hairspray to add height and body to your hair.
  • For a sleek, straight style, use a flat iron to smooth your hair from the roots to the ends.
  • For a curly style, use a curling iron or rollers to create soft, bouncy curls.
  • Accessorize your hair with headbands, scarves, or barrettes for a retro look.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products until you find the perfect look for you.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I get 1960s hair if I have short hair?

A: Yes! There are plenty of 1960s hairstyles that work well with short hair, such as the pixie cut or the shaggy bob.

Q: What kind of products do I need to get 1960s hair?

A: You'll need volumizing shampoos and conditioners, as well as styling products like mousse, hairspray, and pomade.

Q: Can I get 1960s hair if I have curly hair?

A: Absolutely! You can use a curling iron or rollers to create soft, bouncy curls that are perfect for a retro look.

Hair Care Tips for 1960s Hair

Here are some tips for keeping your 1960s hair looking its best:

  • Wash your hair every other day to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools to protect your hair from damage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and keep it looking its best.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be well on your way to achieving the perfect 1960s hair look. Whether you're going for a mod style or a bohemian vibe, these hairstyles are sure to turn heads and make a statement. So embrace your inner flower child and give these retro looks a try!
