How To Decide Which Haircut Suits Me A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 07, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're thinking about getting a new haircut but aren't sure what style to choose, you're not alone. With so many options to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide which haircut suits you best. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of choosing a haircut that complements your face shape, hair type, and personal style.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing a haircut that suits you is to determine your face shape. This is important because different face shapes are better suited to different haircuts. There are six main face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong.

To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and examine your face in the mirror. Look at the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Take note of the length of your face as well. Once you have a good idea of your face shape, you can move on to choosing a haircut that will flatter your features.

Considering Your Hair Type

Your hair type is another important factor to consider when choosing a haircut. Different hair types have different textures, thicknesses, and densities, and each type is better suited to certain haircuts.

If you have fine or thin hair, you may want to consider a layered haircut that adds volume and movement to your hair. If you have thick or coarse hair, a blunt cut may be a better option to help control the volume. If you have curly or wavy hair, you may want to consider a haircut that enhances your natural texture.

Taking Your Lifestyle into Account

Another factor to consider when choosing a haircut is your lifestyle. Ask yourself how much time you're willing to spend styling your hair each day. If you're always on the go and don't have a lot of time to style your hair, a low-maintenance haircut may be a better option. On the other hand, if you enjoy experimenting with different hairstyles and have the time to do so, a more complex haircut may be a good choice.

Reflecting Your Personal Style

Your personal style is also an important consideration when choosing a haircut. Think about the clothes you like to wear and the overall image you want to project. If you have a classic or conservative style, a sleek, polished haircut may be a good choice. If you have a more bohemian or eclectic style, a more free-spirited haircut may be a better option.

Choosing a Salon and Stylist

Once you have a general idea of the type of haircut you want, it's time to choose a salon and stylist. Look for a salon that specializes in the type of haircut you're interested in, and read reviews from other customers to get an idea of the quality of service. When choosing a stylist, look for someone who has experience working with your hair type and face shape.

Getting a Consultation

Before committing to a haircut, it's a good idea to schedule a consultation with your stylist. During the consultation, you can discuss your hair goals and get feedback from the stylist. They may be able to offer suggestions or modifications to ensure that the haircut is a good fit for your face shape, hair type, and personal style.

Maintenance and Upkeep

Finally, it's important to consider the maintenance and upkeep involved with your chosen haircut. Some haircuts require frequent trims or touch-ups to maintain their shape, while others may require more styling time each day. Be honest with yourself about how much time and effort you're willing to put into maintaining your new haircut.

Additional Tips

  • Bring pictures to your consultation to give your stylist a clear idea of the type of haircut you're interested in.
  • Consider the climate in your area when choosing a haircut. If you live in a humid area, for example, you may want to choose a haircut that won't frizz or wilt in the heat.
  • Don't be afraid to ask your stylist for advice on styling products and techniques that will help you maintain your new haircut.

FAQs

How often should I get a haircut?

The frequency of haircuts depends on your hair type and the style of your haircut. Generally, it's a good idea to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

What if I don't like my new haircut?

If you're not happy with your new haircut, talk to your stylist about what changes can be made. They may be able to make adjustments to the cut to better suit your preferences.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've chosen a new haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner that's suited to your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your haircut.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes that can damage your hair.

By following these tips and taking the time to choose a haircut that suits you, you can achieve a look that makes you feel confident and stylish.
