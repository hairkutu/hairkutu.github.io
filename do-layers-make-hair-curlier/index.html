Do Layers Make Hair Curlier The Ultimate Guide

Written by Bardi Mar 19, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you looking to add some bounce and volume to your hair? Layers might be the perfect solution for you. But, do layers make hair curlier? This is a common question among those with straight or wavy hair who are looking to add some texture to their locks. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at whether or not layers can make your hair curlier, and provide some tips for achieving the look you want.

Description

Layers are a popular hair cutting technique that involves cutting different lengths of hair throughout the length of your hair, creating a layered effect. This technique can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair, as well as create a more flattering shape for your face. But, can layers make your hair curlier?

How to Make Layers Work for You

The answer to whether or not layers make hair curlier is: it depends. Layered hair can create the illusion of curls or waves, but it won’t necessarily make your hair curlier on its own. Here are some tips for making layers work for you:

1. Consider your hair type

Those with naturally curly or wavy hair will likely see more defined curls with layers, while those with straight hair may only see a slight wave or bend. If you have straight hair and want to add more curl or wave, you may need to use styling tools and products in addition to layers.

2. Work with your stylist

Your stylist can help determine the best length and placement of layers for your hair type and desired look. Be sure to communicate your goals and expectations clearly to avoid any misunderstandings.

3. Use styling products

Using products designed for curly or wavy hair can help enhance the texture created by layers. Try using a curl-enhancing mousse, gel, or cream to define and hold your curls or waves.

Step-by-Step Guide to Layered Hair

If you’re ready to give layered hair a try, here’s a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Decide on the length of your layers

Consider your face shape, hair type, and desired look when deciding on the length of your layers. Shorter layers will create more volume and movement, while longer layers will create a more subtle effect.

Step 2: Choose your cutting technique

There are several cutting techniques that can be used to create layers, including point cutting, slide cutting, and razoring. Your stylist can help determine the best technique for your hair type and desired look.

Step 3: Wash and dry your hair

Start with clean, dry hair to ensure an accurate cut. If you have curly or wavy hair, avoid brushing or combing it out before cutting.

Step 4: Start cutting

Your stylist will likely start by cutting the longest layers first, then gradually work their way up to shorter layers. Be sure to communicate any concerns or preferences during the cutting process.

Step 5: Style your hair

Once your hair is cut, use styling products and tools to enhance the look of your layers. Try using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to create more defined curls or waves.

Tips for Caring for Layered Hair

Here are some tips for keeping your layered hair looking its best:

1. Use a wide-tooth comb

Avoid using a brush on your curly or wavy hair to prevent frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.

2. Use a deep conditioner

Curly and wavy hair tends to be more prone to dryness and damage, so using a deep conditioner once a week can help keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

3. Avoid heat styling

Excessive heat styling can damage your hair, so try to limit your use of hot tools. If you do use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

FAQs

Do layers make hair curlier?

Layers can create the illusion of curls or waves, but they won’t necessarily make your hair curlier on their own. Those with naturally curly or wavy hair will likely see more defined curls with layers, while those with straight hair may only see a slight wave or bend.

What products should I use for layered hair?

Using products designed for curly or wavy hair can help enhance the texture created by layers. Try using a curl-enhancing mousse, gel, or cream to define and hold your curls or waves.

How often should I cut my layered hair?

How often you cut your layered hair will depend on your hair type and desired look. Typically, you should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

In conclusion, while layers can create the illusion of curls or waves, they won’t necessarily make your hair curlier on their own. Those with naturally curly or wavy hair will likely see more defined curls with layers, while those with straight hair may only see a slight wave or bend. By working with your stylist and using the right products and tools, you can achieve the look you want with layered hair.
