How To Cut Toddler Boy Hair With Clippers And Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

As a parent, cutting your toddler's hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're doing it for the first time. However, with the right tools and a bit of practice, you can save time and money by trimming your little one's hair at home. In this article, we'll show you how to cut toddler boy hair with clippers and scissors, providing you with a step-by-step guide, tips, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • How to Cut Toddler Boy Hair with Clippers and Scissors
  • Tips for Cutting Toddler Boy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Advice for Toddler Boys

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your toddler's hair, you'll need to gather the following tools:

  • Clippers with guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair spray or water spray bottle
  • Cape or towel to protect your child's clothes
  • Clip or hair tie to hold hair in place

Once you have all the tools, you're ready to start cutting. Follow the steps below to give your toddler a fresh new haircut.

How to Cut Toddler Boy Hair with Clippers and Scissors

Step 1: Dampen the Hair

Before you start cutting, dampen your toddler's hair with a water spray bottle or hair spray. This will make the hair easier to cut and prevent it from flying around.

Step 2: Choose the Right Guard Size

Select the guard size you want to use for the clippers. For a longer cut, use a larger size guard; for a shorter cut, use a smaller size guard. Start with a larger guard and gradually work your way down to a smaller one if you want a shorter cut.

Step 3: Cut the Hair with Clippers

Starting at the back of the head, run the clippers with the selected guard through the hair. Move the clippers upwards, against the direction of hair growth, and work your way around the head. Use a comb to lift the hair and trim any excess hair that the clippers missed.

Step 4: Use Scissors to Trim

Use scissors to trim the hair around the ears, neck, and hairline. Comb the hair downward and trim any hair that extends below the comb. Use scissors to create texture and blend the cut.

Step 5: Check for Uneven Spots

Check for any uneven spots and make any necessary adjustments. Use the clippers and scissors to blend any uneven areas and create a smooth, even cut.

Step 6: Clean Up

Once you finish cutting, use a brush to remove any hair from your child's head and clothes. Clean the clippers and scissors with a brush and oil to keep them in good condition.

Tips for Cutting Toddler Boy Hair

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen the hair before cutting to prevent flyaways.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair when cutting with scissors to create a more even cut.
  • Take breaks if your child becomes fussy or uncomfortable.
  • Watch online tutorials or ask a professional for advice if you're unsure about cutting.

FAQs

Can I Cut My Toddler's Hair with Regular Scissors?

You can use regular scissors to cut your toddler's hair, but it's recommended to use hair cutting scissors for a better cut. Hair cutting scissors are sharper and designed to create a more even cut.

How Often Should I Cut My Toddler's Hair?

It depends on how fast your toddler's hair grows and the type of haircut you want. Generally, it's recommended to cut hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the length and style.

How Do I Get My Toddler to Sit Still During Haircuts?

Try distracting your toddler with toys, snacks, or a favorite video. You can also enlist the help of another person to hold your child steady during the haircut.

Hair Care Advice for Toddler Boys

After cutting your toddler's hair, it's important to take care of their hair to keep it healthy and soft. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your child's hair once or twice a week with a mild shampoo.
  • Use a detangler or leave-in conditioner to make combing easier and prevent tangles.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers, as they can damage the hair.
  • Trim the hair regularly to maintain the shape and style.

With these tips and a bit of practice, you can confidently cut your toddler boy's hair with clippers and scissors. Remember to be patient and take breaks if needed. Happy cutting!
