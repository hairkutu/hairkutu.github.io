How To Cut Volume Into Curly Hair

Written by Frank Jul 12, 2023 · 7 min read

Curly hair is beautiful, but sometimes it can be hard to manage. One of the biggest challenges with curly hair is finding the right balance between volume and frizz. If you have curly hair and want to add volume without frizz, you’ve come to the right place. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut volume into curly hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Volume into Curly Hair
  2. Step by Step
  3. Tips
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care

How to Cut Volume into Curly Hair

The first step to cutting volume into curly hair is to choose the right haircut. Curly hair tends to be thicker than straight hair, so you need to choose a haircut that will thin out your hair without sacrificing length. A layered haircut is a great option, as it will remove some of the weight from your hair while still leaving you with plenty of length.

Another important factor to consider when cutting volume into curly hair is the length of your hair. If you have long curly hair, you may want to consider a shorter haircut to help reduce its weight. Shorter haircuts can also help to create more defined curls and add volume to your hair.

Step by Step

Here’s how to cut volume into curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help prevent frizz.
  2. Blow dry your hair using a diffuser attachment to help create volume. Use a low heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.
  3. Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back.
  4. Starting with the back sections, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use a pair of sharp scissors to cut the ends of the hair at an angle.
  5. Repeat this process on each section of hair, working from the back to the front.
  6. Once you’ve finished cutting all of your hair, use a curling iron or wand to add volume and definition to your curls.
  7. Finish off with a lightweight hair spray to help hold your curls in place.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you cut volume into your curly hair:

  • Start with a small section of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as it will be harder to see how much you’re cutting.
  • Use a diffuser attachment when blow drying your hair to help create volume.
  • Don’t be afraid to experiment with different haircuts and styles to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting volume into curly hair:

  • Can I cut volume into my curly hair without going to a salon? Yes, you can cut volume into your curly hair at home, as long as you have the right tools, such as sharp scissors and a diffuser attachment for your blow dryer.
  • What’s the best haircut for adding volume to curly hair? A layered haircut is a great option for adding volume to curly hair, as it removes some of the weight from your hair while still leaving you with plenty of length.
  • How often should I cut my curly hair to maintain volume? It’s recommended to cut your curly hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain volume and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help prevent frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it’s wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

By following these tips and steps, you can cut volume into your curly hair and achieve beautiful, voluminous curls without frizz. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
