What s The Difference Between Thickening And Volumizing Shampoo

Written by Aprilia Jun 14, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're someone who loves experimenting with different hair products, then you must have come across the terms "thickening" and "volumizing" shampoos. These two types of shampoos are designed to give your hair a fuller and thicker look. But what's the difference between them? In this article, we'll explore the key differences between thickening and volumizing shampoos, and which one is best suited for your hair type.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Choose the Right Shampoo
  • Step by Step Guide to Using Thickening and Volumizing Shampoos
  • Tips for Getting the Best Results
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thickening and Volumizing Shampoos

Description

Thickening and volumizing shampoos are designed to make your hair look fuller and thicker, but they achieve this in different ways. Thickening shampoos work by coating each hair strand with a layer of protein or keratin, which thickens the hair shaft and makes it appear fuller. Volumizing shampoos, on the other hand, work by lifting the hair from the scalp, creating more space between each strand, and giving the hair a fuller look.

So, while both shampoos may give you the appearance of fuller hair, they achieve this through different mechanisms.

How to Choose the Right Shampoo

Choosing the right shampoo depends on your hair type and the look you want to achieve. If you have thin, fine hair and want to add volume to your hair, then a volumizing shampoo may be the best choice for you. Volumizing shampoos are also great for people with oily hair, as they help to keep the hair looking fresh and clean for longer.

If you have thick hair and want to make it look fuller, then a thickening shampoo may be the better choice. Thickening shampoos are also great for people with dry or damaged hair, as they help to strengthen and protect the hair.

It's important to note that using the wrong type of shampoo can actually make your hair look worse, so be sure to choose the right one for your hair type.

Step by Step Guide to Using Thickening and Volumizing Shampoos

Using thickening and volumizing shampoos is easy, but there are a few things you can do to get the best results:

Step 1: Wet Your Hair

Start by wetting your hair with warm water. This will help to open up the hair cuticles and make it easier for the shampoo to penetrate your hair.

Step 2: Apply Shampoo

Apply a small amount of shampoo to your hair and massage it into your scalp. Be sure to focus on the roots of your hair, as this is where the shampoo will have the most impact.

Step 3: Rinse Thoroughly

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water, making sure to remove all of the shampoo. Leaving shampoo in your hair can make it look dull and greasy.

Step 4: Condition (Optional)

Depending on your hair type, you may want to use a conditioner after shampooing. If you do, be sure to apply it to the ends of your hair, rather than the roots, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.

Step 5: Dry and Style

Dry and style your hair as usual. You should notice a fuller and thicker look to your hair.

Tips for Getting the Best Results

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results from your thickening or volumizing shampoo:

  • Use the right shampoo for your hair type
  • Massage the shampoo into your scalp for at least a minute
  • Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water
  • Avoid using too much shampoo, as this can make your hair look greasy
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh down your hair
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add more volume
  • Avoid using hot tools, as these can damage your hair and make it look thin

FAQs

Q: Can I use both thickening and volumizing shampoos?

A: Yes, you can use both types of shampoos, but it's important to use them on different days. Using both shampoos on the same day can make your hair look weighed down and greasy.

Q: Can thickening shampoos cause hair loss?

A: No, thickening shampoos do not cause hair loss. In fact, they can help to strengthen and protect your hair.

Q: How often should I use a thickening or volumizing shampoo?

A: It depends on your hair type and how often you wash your hair. If you have oily hair, you may want to use a volumizing shampoo every day. If you have dry or damaged hair, you may want to use a thickening shampoo once or twice a week.

Hair Care Tips for Thickening and Volumizing Shampoos

Using the right shampoo is just one part of a healthy hair care routine. Here are a few more tips to help you keep your hair looking its best:

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Avoid using hair ties that can damage your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as these can weigh down your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray

In conclusion, choosing the right shampoo for your hair type is important if you want to achieve a fuller and thicker look. Whether you choose a thickening shampoo or a volumizing shampoo, be sure to use it correctly and follow a healthy hair care routine to get the best results.
