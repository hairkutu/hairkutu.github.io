What To Do For Thinning Hair On Top Of Head A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 25, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Thinning hair on top of the head can be a distressing experience, but it's not uncommon. In fact, according to the American Hair Loss Association, two-thirds of men experience some form of hair loss by the age of 35. Women can also experience hair loss due to various reasons, such as hormonal changes, stress, and genetics. Nonetheless, there are several ways to address thinning hair on top of the head. This article covers everything you need to know about what to do for thinning hair on top of the head.

Description

In this article, we will discuss the causes of hair loss, the different types of hair loss, and the various methods to prevent, slow down, or treat thinning hair on top of the head. We will also provide practical tips and hair care advice to help you maintain healthy hair.

How to

Before we dive into the different ways to address thinning hair on top of the head, it's essential to understand what causes hair loss. There are many reasons why people experience hair loss. Some of the most common causes include:

  • Genetics
  • Hormonal changes
  • Stress
  • Poor nutrition
  • Medical conditions
  • Medications

Step by Step

Once you've identified the cause of your hair loss, you can take steps to prevent or treat it. Here are some ways to address thinning hair on top of the head:

1. Improve your diet

Eating a healthy, balanced diet can help promote hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, and E. These nutrients are essential for healthy hair.

2. Avoid harsh styling products and treatments

Avoid using harsh chemicals, hair dyes, and heat styling tools on your hair. These can damage your hair and lead to hair loss.

3. Try hair loss treatments

There are several over-the-counter and prescription hair loss treatments available. These include minoxidil, finasteride, and corticosteroids. Consult with your doctor or dermatologist to determine which treatment is best for you.

4. Consider hair transplant surgery

If you have severe hair loss, hair transplant surgery may be an option. During this procedure, hair follicles are taken from a donor area and transplanted to the balding area.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you maintain healthy hair:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption
  • Get enough sleep and manage stress
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid brushing wet hair

FAQ

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss. When you're stressed, your body produces more cortisol, a hormone that can lead to hair loss.

Q: Are there any natural remedies for hair loss?

A: Some natural remedies that may help prevent hair loss include using essential oils, such as lavender and rosemary oil, and taking supplements like biotin and saw palmetto. However, there's not enough scientific evidence to support the effectiveness of these remedies.

Q: Will wearing a hat cause hair loss?

A: No, wearing a hat will not cause hair loss. However, wearing a hat that is too tight or not clean can lead to scalp irritation, which may lead to hair loss.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential to prevent hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid excessive hair brushing or combing
  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow
  • Avoid exposing your hair to excessive heat and UV rays

By following these tips and methods, you can prevent or address thinning hair on top of the head. If you're concerned about your hair loss, consult with your doctor or dermatologist to determine the underlying cause and the most effective treatment options for you.
