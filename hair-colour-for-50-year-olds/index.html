Hair Colour For 50 Year Olds A Guide To Looking Fabulous

Written by Aprilia Jun 08, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you in your 50s and looking to add some colour to your hair? You’re not alone. More and more women and men are opting to colour their hair, and age shouldn’t be a factor in your decision. In fact, hair colour can actually make you look younger and feel more confident. Here’s a guide to finding the perfect hair colour for 50 year olds.

Description

As we age, our hair changes. It may become thinner, drier, and greyer. However, this doesn’t mean you have to stick with your natural hair colour. In fact, hair colour can add depth, shine, and texture to your hair. Plus, it can make you feel more youthful and vibrant. But with so many options available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right hair colour. That’s where this guide comes in.

How to

The first step in choosing a hair colour is to consider your skin tone. If you have cool undertones, you’ll look best in cool colours like ash blonde, platinum, or cool brown. If you have warm undertones, opt for warm colours like golden blonde, honey, or chestnut. If you’re not sure what your undertone is, look at the veins on your wrist. If they’re blue, you have cool undertones. If they’re green, you have warm undertones.

Next, consider your natural hair colour. If you have dark hair, it may be difficult to achieve a lighter colour without damaging your hair. In this case, consider adding highlights or lowlights to add dimension. If you have lighter hair, you have more options for dramatic colour changes.

Finally, consider your lifestyle and maintenance preferences. If you’re low-maintenance, opt for a colour that’s close to your natural hair colour so regrowth is less noticeable. If you’re willing to make regular trips to the salon, you can experiment with bolder colours and more frequent touch-ups.

Step by Step

1. Determine your skin tone by looking at the veins on your wrist.

2. Consider your natural hair colour and how much change you’re willing to make.

3. Think about your lifestyle and maintenance preferences.

4. Consult with a professional hair stylist to determine the best colour for you.

5. Schedule regular touch-ups to maintain your new hair colour.

Tips

- Consider adding highlights or lowlights for a subtle change.

- Choose a colour that complements your skin tone.

- Don’t be afraid to experiment with bold colours.

- Use colour-safe shampoo and conditioner to maintain your new hair colour.

FAQ

What is the best hair colour for 50 year olds?

There’s no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. The best hair colour for you depends on your skin tone, natural hair colour, and lifestyle. Consult with a professional hair stylist to determine the best colour for you.

Can I colour my hair at home?

While you can colour your hair at home, it’s best to consult with a professional hair stylist. They can help you achieve the desired colour without damaging your hair.

How often should I touch up my hair colour?

This depends on the type of colour and how quickly your hair grows. Consult with your hair stylist to determine the best touch-up schedule for you.

Hair Care

Once you’ve coloured your hair, it’s important to take care of it. Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner to maintain your new hair colour. Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can cause the colour to fade faster. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. If you do use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

By following these tips, you can find the perfect hair colour for 50 year olds and enjoy a more youthful and vibrant look.
