How To Make Fine Hair Look Thicker With Colour

Written by Daniel Jun 13, 2023 · 7 min read

Do you have fine hair that lacks volume and thickness? Are you tired of trying different hairstyles to make your hair look fuller? Well, the solution to your problem might be simpler than you think. By adding colour to your hair, you can create an illusion of thickness and volume. In this article, we will discuss how to make fine hair look thicker with colour, step by step.

Description

Thin, fine hair can be difficult to style and manage. It can also make you feel self-conscious about your appearance. Fortunately, there are ways to add volume and thickness to your hair without using expensive hair products or treatments. One of the most effective methods is to add colour to your hair. By choosing the right shade and technique, you can create an illusion of fuller, thicker hair.

How to

Step 1: Choose the Right Shade

The first step to making fine hair look thicker with colour is to choose the right shade. Darker colours tend to make hair appear thicker, while lighter colours can make hair look thinner. So, if you have fine hair, you should opt for darker shades such as chocolate brown or black. These shades will make your hair look fuller and thicker.

Step 2: Use Highlights

Another way to make fine hair look thicker with colour is to use highlights. Adding highlights to your hair creates depth and dimension, which can make your hair look thicker. You can use a few lighter shades of your natural hair colour to create highlights. Alternatively, you can use a contrasting colour such as blonde or red to create a more dramatic effect.

Step 3: Try Ombre Colouring

Ombre colouring is a technique that involves gradually fading the colour from dark to light. This technique can make fine hair look thicker and fuller by creating the illusion of depth and dimension. Ombre colouring is also a low-maintenance option, as it allows for your natural hair colour to blend in seamlessly with the dyed sections.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when trying to make fine hair look thicker with colour:

  • Avoid over-processing your hair with colour, as this can lead to damage and breakage.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and thickness to your hair.
  • Blow-dry your hair upside down to create volume at the roots.
  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.

FAQ

Q: Can I dye my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair at home. However, it is recommended that you seek professional help if you are not familiar with the process. Using the wrong products or techniques can lead to damage and breakage.

Q: Will colouring my hair damage it?

A: Colouring your hair can cause damage if it is not done properly. However, if you use the right products and techniques, you can minimize damage and keep your hair healthy.

Q: How often should I colour my hair?

A: It is recommended that you colour your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the colour and avoid damage.

Hair Care

It is important to take good care of your hair after colouring it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner to protect your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.

By following these tips and techniques, you can make your fine hair look thicker and fuller with colour. Experiment with different shades and techniques to find what works best for you. With a little bit of creativity, you can achieve the hair of your dreams.
