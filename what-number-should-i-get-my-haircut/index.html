What Number Should I Get My Haircut

Written by Petter Jun 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you wondering what number you should get your haircut? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will go over everything you need to know about hair clipper guard sizes and how to choose the right one for your desired haircut. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

When it comes to choosing the right hair clipper guard size, it all depends on the length of hair you want to achieve. Hair clipper guards come in different sizes, ranging from 0 to 8. The higher the number, the longer the hair will be left. For example, if you want a buzz cut, you would go with a size 0 or 1. If you want a longer haircut, you would go with a higher number, such as 5 or 6.

How To

Choosing the right hair clipper guard size is important to achieving the haircut you want. Here's how to choose the right one:

  1. Decide on the length of hair you want to achieve.
  2. Look at the hair clipper guard size chart and choose the one that corresponds to your desired length.
  3. Attach the chosen guard to your clippers.
  4. Start cutting your hair, making sure to go against the grain for a smoother finish.
  5. Blend the sides and back with a shorter guard size, such as a 1 or 2.
  6. Finish with trimming any excess hair using scissors or a trimmer.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to choose the right hair clipper guard size:

Step 1: Decide on the Length of Hair You Want to Achieve

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to decide on the length you want to achieve. Do you want a buzz cut, a crew cut, or a longer hairstyle? Once you have decided on the length, you can move on to choosing the right guard size.

Step 2: Look at the Hair Clipper Guard Size Chart and Choose the Right One

Once you know the length of hair you want to achieve, look at the hair clipper guard size chart and choose the one that corresponds to your desired length. Remember, the higher the number, the longer the hair will be left.

Step 3: Attach the Chosen Guard to Your Clippers

Attach the chosen guard to your clippers, making sure it is securely in place. You don't want it to come off while you're cutting your hair.

Step 4: Start Cutting Your Hair

Start cutting your hair, making sure to go against the grain for a smoother finish. Take your time and make sure you are cutting evenly.

Step 5: Blend the Sides and Back with a Shorter Guard Size

Once you have achieved the desired length on the top, it's time to blend the sides and back. Use a shorter guard size, such as a 1 or 2, to blend the sides and back with the top.

Step 6: Finish with Trimming Any Excess Hair

Finish with trimming any excess hair using scissors or a trimmer. Make sure to check for any uneven spots and trim accordingly.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when choosing the right hair clipper guard size:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down if you want a shorter length.
  • Use a mirror to check for any uneven spots.
  • If you're not sure which guard size to choose, start with a 4 or 5 and adjust accordingly.
  • Don't forget to clean your clippers and guards after each use.

FAQ

What is the difference between a buzz cut and a crew cut?

A buzz cut is a very short hairstyle, typically achieved with a size 0 or 1 guard. A crew cut is slightly longer, usually achieved with a size 3 or 4 guard, and is tapered on the sides and back.

Can I use the same guard size all over my head?

It depends on the length you want to achieve. If you want an even length all over your head, you can use the same guard size. However, if you want a tapered or layered look, you will need to use different guard sizes.

Hair Care

After you have finished your haircut, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Shampoo and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a hairbrush or comb to keep your hair looking neat.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and straighteners.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the desired length.

Now that you know what number you should get your haircut, you can confidently choose the right hair clipper guard size for your desired length. Remember to take your time, use a mirror to check for any uneven spots, and take care of your hair after your haircut.
