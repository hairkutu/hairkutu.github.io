What To Do With Curly Hair While Working Out Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Apr 26, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse, especially when it comes to working out. While you want to look good and feel confident while exercising, you also don't want to damage your curls or have them get in the way of your workout. So, what should you do with your curly hair while working out? Read on to find out.

Description

Working out with curly hair can be a challenge, but it doesn't have to be. With the right techniques and products, you can keep your curls looking great while you exercise. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for managing curly hair during workouts, as well as answer some common questions about curly hair care.

How to

If you have curly hair, you know that it can be easily damaged by sweat, friction, and heat. That's why it's important to take extra care when working out. Here are some steps you can take to protect your curls:

1. Tie Your Hair Back

The simplest way to keep your curly hair out of your face while working out is to tie it back. Use a soft scrunchie or hair tie to avoid damaging your hair. A high ponytail or bun is a great option for keeping your hair off your neck and shoulders.

2. Use a Headband

If you have shorter curly hair, a headband can help keep it in place while you exercise. Look for a wide, stretchy headband that won't slip off your head during your workout.

3. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Before you work out, apply a leave-in conditioner to your curls. This will help protect your hair from sweat and friction, as well as keep your curls hydrated. Use a lightweight, non-greasy formula that won't weigh your curls down.

4. Avoid Heat

If you're going to be working out in a hot environment, like a sauna or steam room, try to avoid direct heat on your curls. This can cause them to become frizzy and dry. Instead, tie your hair back or wear a headband to keep it off your face, and avoid touching your hair with your hands.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to managing your curly hair during your workout:

1. Start with Clean Hair

Before you work out, make sure your hair is clean and free of product buildup. Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

2. Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your curls, focusing on the ends. You can also use a detangling spray to make it easier to comb through your hair.

3. Tie Your Hair Back

Tie your hair back in a high ponytail or bun, using a soft scrunchie or hair tie to avoid damaging your hair. If you have shorter hair, use a wide, stretchy headband to keep it in place.

4. Start Your Workout

Begin your workout, making sure to avoid touching your hair with your hands. If you need to wipe sweat off your face, use a towel or a clean cloth.

5. Post-Workout Care

After your workout, take care when removing your hair tie or headband. Gently detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers, and reapply leave-in conditioner if necessary. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser on low heat to avoid damaging your curls.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for managing your curly hair during your workout:

  • Avoid wearing tight hats or caps that can flatten your curls.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to protect your curls while you sleep.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your curls, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Don't wash your hair too often, as this can strip your curls of their natural oils.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about curly hair care:

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have dry or coarse hair, you may only need to wash it once or twice a week. If you exercise frequently or have oily hair, you may need to wash it more often.

Q: What products should I use on my curly hair?

A: Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as leave-in conditioners, detangling sprays, and curl enhancers. Avoid products that contain sulfates, alcohol, or silicone, as these can dry out your hair.

Q: How do I prevent frizz in my curly hair?

A: To prevent frizz, avoid using heat styling tools, and use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair. Apply a leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum to your hair before styling, and avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day.

Hair Care

Curly hair requires extra care and attention, but with the right techniques and products, you can keep your curls looking beautiful and healthy. Remember to be gentle when handling your curls, and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools. With a little patience and TLC, you can achieve the perfect curls for your workout and beyond.
