How To Figure Out What Kind Of Hair Type You Have

Written by Eveline Aug 06, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you struggling to find hair products that work for your hair? Do you constantly wonder why your hair doesn't look like the models in hair commercials? The answer to these questions might lie in understanding your hair type. In this article, we will explore how to figure out what kind of hair type you have, and give you tips and tricks to care for your hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Hair Type
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Determine Your Hair Type
  • Tips for Hair Care Based on Your Hair Type

How to Determine Your Hair Type

There are generally four hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Within each hair type, there are subcategories based on the texture, thickness, and density of your hair. The best way to determine your hair type is through the hair typing system developed by Andre Walker, Oprah Winfrey's hairstylist. The hair typing system is divided into four categories: type 1, type 2, type 3, and type 4.

Type 1 hair is straight, type 2 hair is wavy, type 3 hair is curly, and type 4 hair is coily. Within each hair type, there are subcategories based on the texture, thickness, and density of your hair.

Type 1 Hair

Type 1 hair is straight and typically lacks volume and body. It is usually thin and can be easily styled. There are three subcategories of type 1 hair:

  • Type 1A - very fine and straight
  • Type 1B - medium texture and straight
  • Type 1C - coarse and straight

Type 2 Hair

Type 2 hair is wavy and has more volume than type 1 hair. It can be easily straightened or curled. There are three subcategories of type 2 hair:

  • Type 2A - loose waves
  • Type 2B - medium waves
  • Type 2C - tight waves

Type 3 Hair

Type 3 hair is curly and usually has more body than type 1 and 2 hair. It is prone to frizz and can be difficult to manage. There are three subcategories of type 3 hair:

  • Type 3A - loose curls
  • Type 3B - springy curls
  • Type 3C - tight curls

Type 4 Hair

Type 4 hair is coily and tightly curled. It is the most fragile hair type and requires extra care. There are three subcategories of type 4 hair:

  • Type 4A - tightly coiled with an S pattern
  • Type 4B - tightly coiled with a Z pattern
  • Type 4C - tightly coiled with no defined pattern

Step-by-Step Guide to Determine Your Hair Type

Now that you know the different hair types, it's time to determine your hair type. Follow these steps:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Allow your hair to air dry without any products.
  3. Examine your hair in a mirror and determine the texture, thickness, and density of your hair.
  4. Use the hair typing system to determine your hair type and subcategory.

Remember, your hair type can change over time due to factors such as hormones, age, and heat damage. It's important to reassess your hair type periodically to ensure you are using the right products and techniques for your hair.

Tips for Hair Care Based on Your Hair Type

Once you have determined your hair type, it's important to care for your hair accordingly. Here are some tips:

  • For type 1 hair, use lightweight products that won't weigh down your hair. Avoid heavy oils and creams.
  • For type 2 hair, use products that enhance your waves without weighing them down. Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair.
  • For type 3 hair, use products that define your curls and reduce frizz. Avoid using a brush on your hair, and instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle.
  • For type 4 hair, use products that provide moisture and nourishment to your hair. Avoid using heat on your hair, and instead, use protective styles such as braids and twists.

FAQ about Hair Types

Q: Can my hair type change over time?

A: Yes, your hair type can change over time due to factors such as hormones, age, and heat damage. It's important to reassess your hair type periodically to ensure you are using the right products and techniques for your hair.

Q: Can I have more than one hair type?

A: Yes, it's common to have more than one hair type. For example, you might have type 2C hair on the top of your head and type 3A hair towards the bottom.

Q: What's the best way to care for my hair?

A: The best way to care for your hair is to use products and techniques that are tailored to your hair type. Follow the tips outlined in this article, and consider consulting with a hairstylist or trichologist for personalized advice.

Now that you know how to figure out what kind of hair type you have, it's time to start caring for your hair in the best way possible. Remember, every hair type is unique and beautiful in its own way, and with the right care, your hair can look and feel its best.
