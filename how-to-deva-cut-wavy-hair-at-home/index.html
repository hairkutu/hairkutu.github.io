How To Deva Cut Wavy Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 17, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Deva cutting is a technique that is specifically designed for curly or wavy hair. It is a method that involves cutting the hair while it is dry, in its natural state, and without using any styling tools. If you have wavy hair and you want to try deva cutting at home, this guide will show you how to do it.

Description

Deva cutting is a method of cutting curly or wavy hair that was developed by the Devachan Salon in New York City. This technique is designed to enhance the natural curl or wave pattern of the hair and create a more personalized haircut. Deva cutting involves cutting the hair while it is dry and without using any styling tools or products. This allows the stylist to see the natural pattern of the hair and create a cut that works with the hair's texture and movement.

How to Deva Cut Wavy Hair at Home

Before you begin deva cutting your wavy hair at home, there are a few things you will need:

  • A pair of sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A comb or hair pick
  • A mirror

Once you have gathered your supplies, follow these steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry completely. Do not use any styling products or tools.
  2. Part your hair in the middle and divide it into four sections, using clips to secure each section.
  3. Starting with one section, use the spray bottle to wet your hair. You want your hair to be damp, but not soaking wet.
  4. Use the comb or hair pick to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  5. Take a small section of hair and twist it around your finger, creating a curl. Use the scissors to cut the curl at the point where you want the hair to fall. Be sure to cut the hair in a straight line, perpendicular to the hair shaft.
  6. Repeat this process with each section of hair, working your way around your head.
  7. Once you have finished cutting all of your hair, use the spray bottle to wet your hair again and scrunch your hair with your hands to encourage your natural wave pattern.
  8. Let your hair air dry completely.
  9. Once your hair is dry, use the comb or hair pick to gently separate any curls that are stuck together.
  10. Your deva cut is complete!

Tips for Deva Cutting Wavy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the best results when deva cutting your wavy hair:

  • Make sure you are cutting each curl individually, rather than cutting through multiple curls at once.
  • Don't be afraid to cut less hair than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more later if needed.
  • If you have thicker hair, you may want to consider cutting your hair in smaller sections to ensure that you are getting each curl.
  • Be patient and take your time. Deva cutting can take longer than traditional hair cutting, but the results are worth it.

FAQs

What type of scissors should I use for deva cutting?

You should use sharp hair cutting scissors, rather than regular household scissors. Dull scissors can damage the hair and create split ends.

Can I deva cut my hair if it is straight?

Deva cutting is specifically designed for curly or wavy hair. If your hair is straight, deva cutting may not be the best option for you.

Do I need to use any products after I deva cut my hair?

You do not need to use any products after you deva cut your hair, but you may want to use a leave-in conditioner or styling product to enhance your natural wave pattern.

Hair Care for Wavy Hair

To keep your wavy hair looking its best after deva cutting, here are some tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and disrupt your natural wave pattern.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is sulfate-free to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to damp hair to help enhance your natural wave pattern.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as this can create frizz and disrupt your natural wave pattern.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using the deva cutting technique, you can achieve beautiful, natural-looking waves that are personalized to your unique hair texture and movement.
