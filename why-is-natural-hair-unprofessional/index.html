Why Is Natural Hair Unprofessional

Description

For years, natural hair has been deemed unprofessional in many workplace settings. This is a phenomenon that has been experienced by many black women who choose to wear their hair in its natural state. This issue stems from the notion that natural hair is unkempt and unprofessional, while straightened hair is seen as more acceptable and polished. This belief has caused many women to feel pressured to conform to Eurocentric beauty standards and to alter their natural hair in order to appear more professional. This article will explore the reasons behind why natural hair is considered unprofessional and provide tips on how to embrace your natural hair in the workplace.

How to

In order to understand why natural hair is considered unprofessional, we must first examine the history of black hair in America. Black hair has been a source of controversy and discrimination for centuries. During slavery, black women were forced to cover their hair as a means of suppressing their identity and culture. Later on, during the Civil Rights Movement, black women began to embrace their natural hair as a form of resistance and empowerment. However, in modern times, natural hair has once again become a target of discrimination in the workplace.

Step by Step

Step 1: Recognize that natural hair is beautiful and professional.

Step 2: Educate yourself on the history of black hair in America.

Step 3: Speak out against discrimination in the workplace.

Step 4: Embrace your natural hair and wear it proudly.

Tips

Tip 1: Find a natural hairstyle that makes you feel confident and professional.

Tip 2: Use natural hair products that work well with your hair type.

Tip 3: Surround yourself with a supportive community of people who embrace their natural hair.

Tip 4: Speak to your employer about the company's hair policy and advocate for change if necessary.

FAQ

Q: Is it legal for employers to discriminate against natural hair?

A: No, it is not legal for employers to discriminate against natural hair. The CROWN Act (Creating a Respectful and Open World for Natural Hair) has been passed in several states to protect individuals from hair discrimination in the workplace.

Q: Can I still straighten my hair if I choose to?

A: Yes, you can still straighten your hair if you choose to. The important thing is to feel comfortable and confident in your natural hair.

Hair Care

It is important to take care of your natural hair in order to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips for natural hair care:

- Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.

- Use a deep conditioner to nourish and moisturize your hair.

- Avoid using heat styling tools too often.

- Protect your hair at night with a silk or satin bonnet or pillowcase.

In conclusion, natural hair is not unprofessional. It is a beautiful and natural expression of black culture and identity. It is important for individuals and employers to recognize and celebrate the diversity of natural hair in the workplace. By embracing natural hair and advocating for change, we can work towards a more inclusive and accepting society.
