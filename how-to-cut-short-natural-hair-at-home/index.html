How To Cut Short Natural Hair At Home

Are you tired of spending money at the salon every time you need a haircut? Or maybe you just want to experiment with a new look without breaking the bank. Whatever your reasons are, cutting your short natural hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. In this article, we will guide you step by step on how to cut short natural hair at home.

How to Cut Short Natural Hair at Home

Before you start cutting your short natural hair, it is important to have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. It is also a good idea to have a mirror or two to help you see the back of your head.

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Start with clean and dry hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before you start cutting. This will give you a better idea of how your hair looks and feels. It will also make it easier to see the natural shape of your hair.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using a comb. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then divide each side into two or three sections. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Start cutting

Start with the back of your head and work your way to the front. Use your comb to pull each section of hair straight and then cut the ends with your scissors. Make sure to cut small amounts at a time and keep checking to make sure the length is even.

Step 4: Trim the sides and front

Once you have finished cutting the back, move on to the sides and front. Use your comb to lift each section of hair and then trim the ends with your scissors. Again, make sure to cut small amounts at a time.

Step 5: Check for any uneven spots

After you have finished cutting, check your hair for any uneven spots. If you find any, trim them with your scissors until you are satisfied with the length.

Step by Step Guide

If you're more of a visual learner, here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut short natural hair at home:

  1. Start with clean and dry hair
  2. Section your hair
  3. Start cutting from the back of your head to the front
  4. Trim the sides and front
  5. Check for any uneven spots

Tips for Cutting Short Natural Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting your short natural hair at home:

  • Cut small amounts at a time to avoid cutting too much off
  • Use a mirror or two to help you see the back of your head
  • Take breaks if you need to
  • Be patient and don't rush the process
  • Watch tutorials or read articles to learn more about cutting short natural hair

FAQs

Can I cut my short natural hair if I have never done it before?

Yes, you can. Just make sure to do your research and take your time. Start with small cuts and work your way up.

What if I make a mistake?

It's okay if you make a mistake. Hair grows back, and you can always go to a salon to fix it if needed.

How often should I cut my short natural hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to trim it. Generally, it is recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Short Natural Hair at Home

After cutting your short natural hair at home, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly
  • Avoid using heat tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends

Cutting your short natural hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools, a little patience, and some practice, you can achieve the perfect look that you've been dreaming of. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
