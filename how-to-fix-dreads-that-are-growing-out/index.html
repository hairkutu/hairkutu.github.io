How To Fix Dreads That Are Growing Out A Step By Step Guide

Are you struggling with dreads that are growing out and causing you frustration? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people with dreadlocks experience this issue, but luckily, there are solutions. In this blog post, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to fix dreads that are growing out. We'll also give you some tips and answer frequently asked questions to help you maintain your dreads properly. Let's get started!

How to Fix Dreads That Are Growing Out

When dreads are growing out, they can become loose at the roots and lose their shape. This can make them look messy and unkempt. To fix this issue, you'll need to retwist your dreads at the roots. This will help to tighten them up and give them a more defined shape.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fix your dreads that are growing out:

  1. Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo to remove any buildup.
  2. Section your hair into manageable sections.
  3. Take a section of hair and apply a small amount of locking gel to the roots.
  4. Using a comb, twist the hair in a clockwise direction.
  5. Continue twisting until you reach the end of the dread.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 for each section of hair.
  7. Wrap each twisted section with a rubber band to hold it in place.
  8. Leave the rubber bands in for a few hours or overnight to allow the dreads to set.
  9. Remove the rubber bands and gently separate any twisted sections that have merged together.

After following these steps, you should notice that your dreads are tighter at the roots and have a more defined shape. Repeat this process as needed to maintain your dreads.

Tips for Maintaining Dreads

Here are some tips to help you maintain your dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup.
  • Avoid using regular conditioner, as it can loosen your dreads.
  • Retwist your dreads at the roots every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape.
  • Avoid using excessive heat on your hair, as it can damage your dreads.
  • Use a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to protect your dreads while you sleep.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing dreads that are growing out:

Can I fix my dreads myself?

Yes, you can fix your dreads yourself by following the steps outlined in this blog post.

Do I need special products to fix my dreads?

You'll need locking gel and rubber bands to retwist your dreads. You can purchase these products at most beauty supply stores.

How often should I retwist my dreads?

You should retwist your dreads every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape.

Hair Care for Dreads

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and beautiful dreads. Here are some tips to help you care for your dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup.
  • Avoid using regular conditioner, as it can loosen your dreads.
  • Avoid using excessive heat on your hair, as it can damage your dreads.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.
  • Use a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to protect your dreads while you sleep.

By following these tips and regularly retwisting your dreads, you can maintain a beautiful and healthy head of hair. Remember, with a little patience and effort, you can fix your dreads that are growing out and keep them looking great for years to come.
