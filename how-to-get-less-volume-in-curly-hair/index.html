How to Get Less Volume in Curly Hair - Tips and Tricks

If you have naturally curly hair, you know how hard it can be to control the volume. While some people love the fullness that comes with curly hair, others prefer a more relaxed, low-volume look. Luckily, there are several ways to get less volume in curly hair without sacrificing the curls themselves. In this article, we'll discuss some tips and tricks to help you achieve a more controlled, low-volume style.

How to Get Less Volume in Curly Hair

There are several ways to tame the volume in curly hair:

  • Blow-drying with a diffuser
  • Using a hair straightener
  • Applying a styling product
  • Getting a haircut

Blow-Drying with a Diffuser

Blow-drying your hair with a diffuser can help reduce volume and define your curls. A diffuser attachment on your blow dryer disperses the air more evenly, reducing the amount of frizz and puffiness. To use a diffuser:

  1. Wash your hair and apply a curl-enhancing product.
  2. Flip your head upside down and place sections of your hair onto the diffuser.
  3. Turn on the blow dryer and use a low heat setting.
  4. Hold the diffuser in place for a few seconds before moving on to the next section.
  5. Once your hair is dry, flip your head back up and gently scrunch your curls.

Using a Hair Straightener

Using a hair straightener can help smooth out your curls and reduce volume. To do this:

  1. Wash your hair and apply a heat protectant.
  2. Divide your hair into sections.
  3. Starting at the roots, clamp a small section of hair with the straightener.
  4. Slowly glide the straightener down to the ends, pulling the hair taut as you go.
  5. Repeat on all sections of your hair.

Applying a Styling Product

Applying a styling product can help weigh down your curls and reduce volume. Choose a product that is specifically designed for curly hair, such as a curl-defining cream or mousse. To use:

  1. Wash your hair and towel-dry it.
  2. Apply a small amount of the styling product to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  3. Use your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.
  4. Let your hair air-dry or blow-dry it with a diffuser.

Getting a Haircut

A haircut can also help reduce volume in curly hair. Ask your stylist for a layered cut, which can remove some of the bulk and weight from your hair. You can also opt for longer layers, which will help keep your curls intact while reducing volume.

Tips for Maintaining Low-Volume Curls

Here are some tips for maintaining your low-volume curls:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your curls and make them look greasy.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz and prevent breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I reduce frizz in my curly hair?

To reduce frizz, try using a microfiber towel to dry your hair instead of a regular towel. You can also apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner or oil to your hair to help smooth down the cuticle.

Can I still have volume in my hair with low-volume curls?

Absolutely! Low-volume curls can still have plenty of bounce and body. If you want to add some volume, try using a volumizing spray or mousse before blow-drying your hair.

Hair Care for Low-Volume Curls

It's important to take care of your hair to maintain your low-volume curls. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve a more controlled, low-volume look for your curly hair. Experiment with different methods to find what works best for you, and remember to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
