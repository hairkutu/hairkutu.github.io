How To Cut Short Wavy Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 17, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon every time you need a trim? Do you have short wavy hair that needs a little TLC? If you answered yes to either of these questions, this guide is for you! In this article, we’ll walk you through how to cut short wavy hair yourself. We’ll cover all the steps, tips, and tricks you need to know to achieve the perfect cut.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Short Wavy Hair Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Short wavy hair can be tricky to cut, especially if you’re doing it yourself. However, with a little practice and the right tools, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut your hair in layers, trim your ends, and shape your hair to suit your face shape.

How to Cut Short Wavy Hair Yourself

If you’re new to cutting your own hair, the process can seem daunting. However, with the right preparation and approach, you can achieve a great result. Here are the basic steps you need to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see where your hair falls naturally and make it easier to cut.
  2. Section your hair. Use clips to divide your hair into sections, starting at the back and working your way forward. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much off.
  3. Start with the back. Begin by cutting the back of your hair first, using a mirror to check your progress. Use a comb to separate your hair into sections and cut each section to the desired length.
  4. Move to the sides. Once you’ve finished cutting the back, move on to the sides. Again, use a mirror to check your progress and make sure you’re cutting your hair evenly.
  5. Trim the ends. Once you’ve finished cutting the length of your hair, use scissors to trim any split ends or uneven pieces. Take your time and work carefully to avoid cutting off too much.
  6. Shape your hair. If you want to add layers or shape your hair to suit your face shape, use scissors to cut small sections of hair at a time. Work slowly and carefully, checking your progress in the mirror as you go.
  7. Finishing touches. Once you’re happy with your cut, use a comb and scissors to blend any choppy sections and make sure your hair is even.

Tips and Tricks

Whether you’re new to cutting your own hair or you’re a seasoned pro, there are a few tips and tricks that can help you achieve a great result:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. This will make it easier to achieve a clean, even cut.
  • Start small. If you’re new to cutting your own hair, start with a small trim and work your way up to a bigger cut.
  • Use a mirror. Make sure you have a mirror in front of you and behind you so you can see your hair from all angles.
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair can take time, especially if you’re working with short, wavy hair. Take breaks if you need to and don’t rush the process.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short wavy hair:

Q: Can I cut my short wavy hair myself?
A: Yes, you can cut your own short wavy hair with the right preparation and tools. Q: What tools do I need to cut my short wavy hair?
A: You’ll need a good pair of hair cutting scissors, clips to section your hair, and a comb. Q: How often should I cut my short wavy hair?
A: It’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Once you’ve cut your short wavy hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you can cut your short wavy hair yourself and achieve a great result. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
