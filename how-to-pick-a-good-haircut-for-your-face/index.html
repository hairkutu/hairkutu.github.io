How To Pick A Good Haircut For Your Face A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 06, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Choosing the right haircut can be overwhelming, especially when there are so many options available. However, picking a haircut that complements your face shape and features can make a world of difference in your overall appearance. In this article, we'll break down how to pick a good haircut for your face, step by step, and provide you with some tips and hair care advice to ensure you get the most out of your new 'do.

  • Considering Your Hair Texture
  • Analyzing Your Hair Type
  • Choosing the Right Length
  • Enhancing Your Features
  • Maintaining Your Haircut
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in picking a good haircut for your face is determining your face shape. There are six different face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Once you know your face shape, you can choose a haircut that accentuates your best features and softens any less desirable ones.

To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back. Look at the overall shape of your face, paying attention to the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. You can also trace the outline of your face on a piece of paper or take a selfie to help you visualize your face shape.

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, congratulations! You can rock just about any haircut. Your face is longer than it is wide, with a gently rounded hairline and jawline. To highlight your balanced proportions, try a layered, shoulder-length cut or a short, textured pixie.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, you'll want to elongate your face with a haircut that adds height and volume on top. A layered, shoulder-length cut with side-swept bangs or a sleek, high ponytail can help give the illusion of a longer face.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, you'll want to soften your angular features with a haircut that has soft layers and movement. A long, layered cut with face-framing layers or a wavy bob can help soften your jawline and balance your features.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, you'll want to draw attention to your cheekbones and eyes with a haircut that has volume and texture around the crown of your head. A long, layered cut with side-swept bangs or a tousled, chin-length bob can help balance your features.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, you'll want to soften your angular features with a haircut that has layers and movement. A long, layered cut with face-framing layers or a side-swept pixie can help balance your features and accentuate your cheekbones.

Oblong Face Shape

If you have an oblong face shape, you'll want to add width to your face with a haircut that has volume and texture around the sides of your head. A chin-length bob with choppy layers or a curly pixie can help balance your features and soften your jawline.

Considering Your Hair Texture

In addition to your face shape, you'll also want to consider your hair texture when picking a good haircut. There are four main types of hair texture: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Each texture will react differently to different haircuts and styling techniques, so it's important to choose a haircut that works with your natural texture.

Analyzing Your Hair Type

Another factor to consider when picking a good haircut is your hair type. There are four main types of hair: fine, medium, coarse, and thick. Each hair type has its own unique characteristics and requires different approaches when it comes to cutting and styling.

Choosing the Right Length

The length of your haircut is another important consideration. Your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle will all play a role in determining the best length for your hair. Some general guidelines include:

  • Short haircuts work well for round, heart, and oval face shapes.
  • Medium-length haircuts are versatile and work well for all face shapes and hair textures.
  • Long haircuts can be flattering for oval and oblong face shapes, but may overwhelm round and heart-shaped faces.

Enhancing Your Features

Your haircut should enhance your best features and draw attention away from any less desirable ones. For example, if you have a prominent nose, you may want to avoid a center part that draws attention to your nose. Instead, you could try a side part or bangs that help balance your features.

Maintaining Your Haircut

Finally, when picking a good haircut, it's important to consider how much time and effort you're willing to put into styling and maintaining your hair. Some haircuts require daily styling and regular trims, while others can be left to air dry and require minimal upkeep.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I can't decide on a haircut?

If you're having trouble deciding on a haircut, consider consulting with a professional hairstylist. They can offer personalized recommendations based on your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle.

How often should I get a haircut?

How often you should get a haircut depends on the length of your hair and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-12 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

How do I style my new haircut?

The best way to style your new haircut will depend on your hair texture and the specific cut you choose. Your hairstylist can offer styling tips and product recommendations to help you achieve your desired look.

How can I keep my hair healthy?

To keep your hair healthy, it's important to wash and condition it regularly, protect it from heat damage, and avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products. Eating a balanced diet and staying hydrated can also help promote healthy hair growth.

The Bottom Line

Picking a good haircut doesn't have to be stressful. By considering your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle, you can choose a haircut that enhances your best features and complements your personal style. Remember to communicate with your hairstylist and ask questions if you're unsure about anything. With the right haircut and proper hair care, you'll be able to show off your best self every day.
