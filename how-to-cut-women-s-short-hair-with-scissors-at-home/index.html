How To Cut Women s Short Hair With Scissors At Home

Written by Christine Apr 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are looking for a way to save money and time on haircuts, cutting your own short hair with scissors at home is a great option to consider. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut that suits your style and personality. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting women's short hair with scissors at home, step by step.

Table of Content:

  • Tools
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning
  • Cutting
  • Blending
  • Finishing
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Tools

The first thing you need to do is make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hairdressing scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends. You can purchase hairdressing scissors online or at a beauty supply store.

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Wet hair can be difficult to cut accurately, so it's best to work with dry hair. Comb your hair thoroughly to remove any tangles or knots.

Sectioning

Once your hair is prepared, use the comb to section your hair into layers. Start by creating a horizontal parting at the crown of your head, and clip the top section away. Then, create a parting from ear to ear, dividing the hair into two sections. Clip the bottom section away.

Now, create another horizontal parting just below the crown, and clip the top section away. You should have three sections of hair: the top, middle, and bottom.

Cutting

Starting with the bottom section, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and slide your fingers down to the desired length. Use the scissors to cut the hair in a straight line. Continue cutting small sections of hair in this manner until you've completed the bottom section.

Then, move on to the middle section and repeat the process. Finally, cut the top section in the same way.

Blending

After you've cut all the sections of your hair, you'll need to blend them together for a seamless look. Comb your hair straight down and use the scissors to cut into the ends of your hair, creating soft, feathered edges.

Finishing

Once you've finished cutting and blending your hair, use the mirror to check the back and sides of your hair for any uneven spots. Make any necessary adjustments by cutting small sections of hair as needed.

Finally, style your hair as desired using a blow dryer, straightener, or curling iron.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to the desired length.
  • Take breaks if you need to, and step away from the mirror occasionally to look at your hair from a different angle.
  • Don't cut your hair too short, as it will be harder to fix mistakes.
  • Invest in high-quality scissors that will last for years.

FAQ

  • Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

    • Yes, you can cut curly hair with scissors. Just make sure to stretch the curls out before cutting to ensure an even cut.

  • How often should I cut my hair?

    • It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

  • What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

    • If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to fix it, or wait for it to grow out and start over.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends, and consider using hair masks or oils to keep your hair moisturized.

Cutting women's short hair with scissors at home is a great way to save money and time while achieving a professional-looking haircut. With the right tools and technique, you can create a style that suits your personality and enhances your natural beauty. Remember to take your time, be patient, and enjoy the process!
