How To Cut My Short Layered Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 03, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon every time you need a trim? Do you want to take control of your hair and try cutting it yourself? If you have short layered hair, you're in luck! With a little practice and patience, you can easily cut your own hair at home. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your short layered hair yourself, with step-by-step instructions, tips, and FAQs.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut My Short Layered Hair Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Short Layered Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Short Layered Hair

How to Cut My Short Layered Hair Yourself

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to gather the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle with water

Once you have your tools ready, it's time to get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing and conditioning your hair, and let it air dry completely.

Step 2: Comb your hair to remove any tangles.

Step 3: Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. Start with the bottom layer, and clip the rest of your hair on top.

Step 4: Take a small section of your hair, and comb it straight down. Hold the end of the section with your fingers, and cut it straight across with the scissors. Repeat this process for the rest of the bottom layer.

Step 5: Release the next layer of hair from the clip, and repeat step 4. Make sure to cut each section slightly shorter than the previous layer, to create the layered effect.

Step 6: Continue releasing and cutting each layer until you reach the top layer of your hair.

Step 7: Cut the top layer of your hair using the same technique, but be careful not to cut too much as this layer is the shortest and most visible.

Step 8: Once you've finished cutting all the layers, comb your hair to see if there are any uneven spots. If you find any, trim them with the scissors.

Step 9: Style your hair as desired, and enjoy your new look!

Tips for Cutting Your Short Layered Hair

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Start with small sections of hair, and cut slowly to avoid making mistakes.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as it's easier to trim more later than to fix a mistake.
  • Make sure to cut each layer slightly shorter than the previous one, to create the layered effect.
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or frustrated, and come back to it later.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my short layered hair myself if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can! Just make sure to cut your hair when it's dry, so you can see the natural curls and avoid cutting too much.

Q: How often should I cut my short layered hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain your style. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Q: What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

A: Don't panic! If you make a mistake, try to even it out by trimming the surrounding hair. If it's a major mistake, you may need to see a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Short Layered Hair

After cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too many styling products.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it when it's wet.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your short layered style.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before styling with hot tools.

Now that you know how to cut your short layered hair yourself, you can save time and money while still looking fabulous. With practice and patience, you'll become a pro at cutting your own hair in no time!
