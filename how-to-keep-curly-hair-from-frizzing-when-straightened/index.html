How To Keep Curly Hair From Frizzing When Straightened

Written by Luffy Aug 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how difficult it can be to keep it looking sleek and straight. Even after straightening, your hair may still frizz up, leaving you feeling frustrated and defeated. But don't worry, there are ways to keep your curly hair from frizzing when straightened. In this article, we'll provide you with step-by-step instructions, tips, and FAQs to help you achieve smooth, straight locks.

Table of Contents

How to Keep Curly Hair from Frizzing When Straightened

There are a few things you can do before, during, and after straightening your curly hair to prevent frizz.

1. Shampoo and Conditioner

Start with a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for curly hair. Look for products that contain hydrating ingredients like coconut oil or shea butter, which can help to combat frizz.

2. Towel Dry

After washing your hair, gently towel dry your curls. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with the towel, as this can cause damage and frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to blot your hair dry.

3. Apply Heat Protectant

Before straightening your hair, apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your locks. This will help to protect your hair from heat damage and frizz.

4. Use a High-Quality Straightener

Invest in a high-quality straightener with adjustable heat settings. This will allow you to customize the temperature to your hair type and minimize the risk of heat damage.

5. Straighten in Small Sections

To prevent frizz, straighten your hair in small sections. This will allow you to achieve a smooth, sleek finish without having to go over the same section multiple times.

6. Finish with a Serum

Once you've finished straightening your hair, apply a serum or oil to the ends of your hair. This will help to seal in moisture and prevent frizz.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you keep your curly hair from frizzing when straightened.

Step 1: Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair. Rinse thoroughly and gently towel dry your hair.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair, focusing on the ends and any areas that tend to frizz.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using hair clips or elastics. This will make it easier to straighten your hair without missing any sections.

Step 4: Straighten Your Hair

Take one section of hair and clamp your straightener at the roots. Slowly glide the straightener down the length of your hair, making sure to keep the heat moving. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 5: Apply Serum

Once you've finished straightening your hair, apply a serum or oil to the ends of your hair. This will help to seal in moisture and prevent frizz.

Tips for Preventing Frizz

Here are a few additional tips to help you prevent frizz when straightening your curly hair.

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Before washing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls. This will help to prevent breakage and frizz.

2. Dry Your Hair Completely

Make sure your hair is completely dry before straightening. Wet hair is more prone to frizz and heat damage.

3. Avoid Touching Your Hair

Once you've straightened your hair, avoid touching it as much as possible. This can disrupt the hair cuticle and cause frizz.

FAQs

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair every day?

A: It's not recommended to straighten your hair every day, as this can cause heat damage and breakage. Try to limit straightening to once or twice a week.

Q: What should I do if my hair still frizzes after straightening?

A: If your hair still frizzes after straightening, try applying a smoothing serum or oil to the affected areas.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are a few additional tips to help you care for your curly hair and prevent frizz.

1. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help to reduce frizz and prevent breakage.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help to nourish and hydrate your curls, reducing the risk of frizz.

3. Avoid Over-Washing

Over-washing your hair can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz. Try to wash your hair every two to three days, and use a dry shampoo in between washes if necessary.

With these tips and tricks, you can keep your curly hair looking sleek and straight without the frizz. Remember to use a heat protectant, straighten in small sections, and finish with a smoothing serum or oil for best results.
