How To Cut And Thin Out Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 03, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’re thinking about cutting and thinning out your own bangs, you’ve come to the right place. In this guide, we’ll show you how to do it yourself, step-by-step. We’ll also give you tips on how to care for your new bangs and answer some frequently asked questions about cutting and thinning out bangs.

Table of Contents

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut and Thin Out Bangs

Cutting and thinning out your own bangs can be intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips

Once you have your tools, follow these steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with dry, clean hair. Comb your hair straight down over your forehead, creating a triangle-shaped section of hair that you want to cut into bangs.
  2. Using your comb, separate the triangle section of hair into two equal parts.
  3. Clip one half of the triangle section of hair to the side.
  4. Take the other half of the triangle section of hair and comb it straight down in front of your face, making sure it’s even.
  5. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Start with small cuts and work your way up to your desired length. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can’t add hair back.
  6. Unclip the other half of the triangle section of hair and repeat steps 3-5.
  7. Once you’ve cut both sides, compare them and make any necessary adjustments to ensure they are even.
  8. To thin out your bangs, hold the hair between your fingers and make small vertical snips into the hair. This will remove some of the bulk and create a wispy, textured look.
  9. Repeat step 8 until you’ve achieved your desired level of thinness.
  10. Style your bangs as desired.

Tips for Cutting and Thinning Out Bangs

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting and thinning out your bangs:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can’t add hair back.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Make sure your hair is dry and clean before you start cutting. Wet hair can be deceptive and may result in uneven bangs.
  • When thinning out your bangs, make small vertical snips to avoid creating choppy or uneven layers.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help. If you’re unsure about cutting and thinning out your own bangs, ask a friend or family member for assistance.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting and thinning out bangs:

How often should I cut my bangs?
It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. On average, most people need to trim their bangs every 4-6 weeks.
What if I make a mistake?
If you make a mistake, don’t panic. Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may be able to fix it yourself or seek professional help. If it’s a minor mistake, try to blend it in with the rest of your hair or wear a headband or hat until it grows out.
Can I thin out my bangs with regular scissors?
No, regular scissors are not designed for thinning out hair. You’ll need to use special thinning shears to achieve the desired effect.

Hair Care Tips for Bangs

Once you’ve cut and thinned out your bangs, it’s important to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some hair care tips for bangs:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your bangs clean and oil-free.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can transfer oil from your hands to your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Blow dry your bangs straight down to avoid creating cowlicks or unwanted texture.
  • Use a small amount of hair oil or serum to smooth down any flyaways or frizz.
  • Consider using dry shampoo in between washes to keep your bangs looking fresh.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut and thin out your own bangs like a pro. Good luck!
