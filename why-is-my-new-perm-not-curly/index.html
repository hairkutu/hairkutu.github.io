Why Is My New Perm Not Curly

Written by Luffy Mar 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you recently got a perm and expected to have beautiful, bouncy curls, you may be disappointed to find that your hair is not as curly as you hoped. This can be frustrating, especially if you spent a lot of time and money on the perm. In this article, we will explore some of the reasons why your new perm may not be as curly as you expected and what you can do about it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get a Perm
  • Step by Step Guide to Getting a Perm
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Perm
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Permed Hair

How to Get a Perm

A perm is a chemical treatment that is used to add curls or waves to hair that is naturally straight. The process involves wrapping hair around curling rods and applying a chemical solution that breaks down the hair's natural bonds. Once the bonds are broken down, the hair is rinsed and neutralized, which sets the curls in place.

Step by Step Guide to Getting a Perm

Here is a step-by-step guide to getting a perm:

  1. Consult with a professional stylist: Before getting a perm, it is important to consult with a professional stylist who can assess your hair's condition and determine if it is suitable for a perm.
  2. Choose the right perm solution: There are different types of perm solutions available, and your stylist can help you choose the right one for your hair type and texture.
  3. Prepare your hair: Your stylist will shampoo your hair and towel dry it. Your hair should be damp, but not too wet.
  4. Wrap your hair around the curling rods: Your stylist will wrap small sections of your hair around the curling rods, starting at the nape of your neck and working their way up to the top of your head.
  5. Apply the perm solution: Once your hair is wrapped around the curling rods, your stylist will apply the perm solution to your hair.
  6. Wait for the perm solution to set: The perm solution needs time to break down your hair's natural bonds. Your stylist will leave it on your hair for a specific amount of time, depending on your hair type and texture.
  7. Rinse your hair: After the perm solution has set, your stylist will rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  8. Neutralize your hair: To set the curls in place, your stylist will apply a neutralizing solution to your hair.
  9. Remove the curling rods: Finally, your stylist will remove the curling rods and style your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Your Perm

Here are some tips for maintaining your new perm:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 24-48 hours after getting a perm to allow the curls to set.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and cause the curls to loosen.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

FAQs

Why is my new perm not curly?

There are several reasons why your new perm may not be as curly as you expected:

  • Your hair may be too long or heavy to hold the curls.
  • Your hair may be too damaged or over-processed to hold the curls.
  • The perm solution may not have been left on your hair long enough.
  • The curling rods may have been too big or too small for your hair type and texture.
  • Your hair may not have been wrapped tightly enough around the curling rods.

Can I fix my perm if it's not curly enough?

If your perm is not curly enough, you can try getting a second perm. However, it is important to wait at least 6-8 weeks before getting another perm to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Permed Hair

Here are some hair care tips for permed hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes and bleaches, as they can damage your hair and cause the curls to loosen.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using hair elastics and clips that can cause breakage and frizz.

In conclusion, if your new perm is not as curly as you expected, there could be several reasons why. It is important to consult with a professional stylist and follow the proper hair care tips to maintain your new look.
