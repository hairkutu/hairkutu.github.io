Why Is My Hair Going From Curly To Straight

Written by Petter May 29, 2023 Â· 11 min read

If you've noticed that your once-curly hair is starting to lose its texture and become straighter over time, you're not alone. This is a common hair concern that many people experience, and there are a number of factors that can contribute to this change in hair texture. In this article, we'll explore some of the most common reasons why hair goes from curly to straight, and what you can do to help maintain your natural curl pattern.

Genetics

One of the most common reasons why hair goes from curly to straight is genetics. Some people are simply born with naturally curly hair that gradually becomes straighter over time. This can be due to changes in the hair follicles themselves, which can affect the texture, thickness, and curl pattern of the hair. While there's not much you can do to change your genetics, there are ways to help maintain your natural curl pattern (which we'll cover later in this article).

Heat Damage

Another common culprit behind curly-to-straight hair is heat damage. Curl patterns can be easily damaged by excessive heat from styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. This can cause the hair to lose its natural curl pattern and become straighter over time. To prevent heat damage, it's important to use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools, and to limit the amount of heat you expose your hair to as much as possible.

Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments such as relaxers and perms can also cause hair to lose its natural curl pattern over time. Relaxers work by breaking down the protein bonds in the hair, which can permanently straighten the hair. Perms, on the other hand, work by restructuring the protein bonds in the hair to create curls. While these treatments can be effective at achieving the desired look, they can also cause damage to the hair and alter its natural texture. If you're considering a chemical treatment, it's important to consult with a professional stylist who can advise you on the best options for your hair type and texture.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can also play a role in hair texture changes. For example, during pregnancy, many women experience changes in their hair texture, often resulting in thicker, fuller hair. However, after giving birth, some women may notice that their hair becomes straighter and thinner. Hormonal changes such as menopause can also cause changes in hair texture, as can certain medical conditions such as thyroid disorders.

Aging

As we age, our hair goes through a number of changes, including changes in texture. Many people find that their hair becomes straighter and thinner as they get older, due to changes in the hair follicles themselves. However, this doesn't mean that you can't maintain your natural curl pattern as you age. With the right care and attention, you can help keep your curls looking healthy and defined.

How to Maintain Your Curls

If you're looking to maintain your natural curl pattern, there are a few things you can do to help keep your curls looking healthy and defined:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that's specifically formulated for curly hair.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or curl-defining cream to help enhance your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling, and use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.

Step-by-Step: Caring for Curly Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide to caring for curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Gently blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel or cotton T-shirt.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-defining cream to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  5. Allow your hair to air-dry as much as possible, or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on low heat.
  6. Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying, as this can cause frizz.
  7. Once your hair is dry, use a small amount of oil or serum to help reduce frizz and add shine.

Tips for Curly Hair

Here are a few additional tips for maintaining healthy, defined curls:

  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, which can dry out your hair and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach and hair dyes.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to help keep it healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Invest in a satin or silk pillowcase to help prevent tangles and reduce frizz.

FAQs

Here are a few commonly asked questions about hair texture changes:

Can stress cause hair to become straight?

While stress can certainly cause hair loss, it's unlikely to cause hair to become straight. However, stress can contribute to a number of other hair problems, such as dryness, breakage, and split ends.

Can I reverse hair texture changes?

While you can't necessarily reverse hair texture changes, you can take steps to help maintain your natural curl pattern and keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Can pregnancy cause hair to become straight?

While every woman's experience is different, some women do report changes in their hair texture during and after pregnancy. This can be due to hormonal changes, and may result in hair that's straighter, thicker, or thinner than before.

Hair Care Products for Curly Hair

If you're looking for products that are specifically formulated for curly hair, here are a few options to consider:

  • DevaCurl: This brand offers a range of sulfate-free shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that are designed to enhance and define curly hair.
  • Ouidad: Ouidad specializes in products for curly and kinky hair, including shampoos, conditioners, and styling gels.
  • Cantu: Cantu offers a range of affordable products for curly hair, including leave-in conditioners, curl creams, and styling gels.

By understanding the common reasons why hair goes from curly to straight, and taking steps to maintain your natural curl pattern, you can help keep your hair healthy, hydrated, and defined. Whether you're dealing with genetics, heat damage, chemical treatments, hormonal changes, or aging, there are plenty of ways to help keep your curls looking their best.
