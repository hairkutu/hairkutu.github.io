How To Cut Hair So It s Not Straight Across

Cutting hair straight across can give a blunt and simple look. However, if you want to add some texture and movement to your hair, you can try cutting it in a different way. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut hair so it's not straight across. Follow these tips and tricks to achieve a chic and stylish haircut.

Description

Many people think that cutting hair straight across is the only way to achieve a clean and uniform look. However, this style can sometimes look too simple and boring. Cutting hair in different angles and layers can add texture, volume, and movement to the hair. This technique is perfect for people who want to add some personality to their hair without sacrificing its length.

How to Cut Hair So It's Not Straight Across

Cutting hair in different angles and layers can seem intimidating at first. However, with the right tools and techniques, anyone can achieve a chic and stylish haircut. Here are some steps you can follow:

1. Prepare your tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also important to work in a well-lit area and have a mirror to check your progress.

2. Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. This will make it easier for you to cut your hair in layers. Start by sectioning your hair from the crown of your head to your ears. Clip the top section away and focus on the bottom section first.

3. Cut your hair in layers

Take a small section of your hair at the back and hold it vertically. Hold your scissors at a slight angle and cut the hair in a diagonal direction. This will create a soft and layered effect. Repeat this step for the rest of the sections in the bottom layer.

Once you're done with the bottom layer, unclip the top section and repeat the same process. Make sure to blend the top layer with the bottom layer to create a seamless look.

4. Trim the ends

After you're done cutting your hair in layers, it's time to trim the ends. Hold your scissors vertically and snip the ends of your hair. This will give your hair a clean and polished look.

Step by Step

If you're still not sure how to cut your hair in layers, here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. This will help you see the natural texture and movement of your hair.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. Start by sectioning your hair from the crown of your head to your ears. Clip the top section away and focus on the bottom section first.

Step 3: Cut your hair in layers

Take a small section of your hair at the back and hold it vertically. Hold your scissors at a slight angle and cut the hair in a diagonal direction. Repeat this step for the rest of the sections in the bottom layer.

Once you're done with the bottom layer, unclip the top section and repeat the same process. Make sure to blend the top layer with the bottom layer to create a seamless look.

Step 4: Trim the ends

After you're done cutting your hair in layers, it's time to trim the ends. Hold your scissors vertically and snip the ends of your hair. This will give your hair a clean and polished look.

Tips

Cutting hair in layers can be tricky, especially if you're doing it for the first time. Here are some tips to help you achieve a chic and stylish haircut:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Hold your scissors at a slight angle to create soft and feathered layers.
  • Blend the top layer with the bottom layer to create a seamless look.
  • Check your progress using a mirror to make sure your hair is even on both sides.

FAQ

1. Can I cut my hair in layers even if it's short?

Yes, you can still cut your hair in layers even if it's short. However, it's important to work with smaller sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.

2. Can I cut my hair in layers at home?

Yes, you can cut your hair in layers at home. However, it's important to use the right tools and techniques to avoid damaging your hair. If you're not confident, it's best to seek the help of a professional hairstylist.

3. How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6 to 8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair in layers, it's important to take care of it to maintain its shape and texture. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Visit a professional hairstylist for a trim or touch-up every 6 to 8 weeks.

With these tips and tricks, you can now cut your hair in layers and achieve a chic and stylish look. Remember to take care of your hair to maintain its health and texture. Good luck!
