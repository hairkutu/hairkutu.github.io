How To Thin Your Hair With Thinning Scissors

Written by Aprilia Jul 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning scissors are a great tool for those who want to reduce the volume of their hair without sacrificing length. They are especially useful for those with thick hair, as it can be difficult to manage and style. In this article, we will go over how to thin your hair using thinning scissors, step by step. We will also provide tips on how to care for your hair after thinning and answer some frequently asked questions.

How to Thin Your Hair

Before we get started, it's important to note that using thinning scissors incorrectly can damage your hair. If you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to have a professional do it for you. However, if you're up for the challenge, here's how to thin your hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see which areas of your hair need thinning. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type, and gently towel dry your hair before blow-drying it completely.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use clips to section your hair into manageable parts. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward. This will help you to thin your hair evenly.

Step 3: Hold the Thinning Scissors Correctly

It's important to hold the thinning scissors correctly to avoid cutting too much hair. Hold the scissors vertically and use the teeth to cut small sections of hair at a time.

Step 4: Cut Small Sections of Hair

Starting at the bottom of each section, use the thinning scissors to cut small sections of hair. Work your way up the hair section, cutting more hair as you go.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After each section, check your progress in a mirror. It's important to thin your hair evenly throughout your head. If you notice any uneven areas, go back and thin those sections some more.

Tips for Thinning Your Hair

Thinning your hair with thinning scissors can take some practice. Here are some tips to help you get the hang of it:

  • Use a comb to comb the hair you want to thin.
  • Use the scissors at an angle to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can create uneven areas.
  • Take breaks in between sections to avoid straining your hand.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning your hair using thinning scissors:

Is it safe to thin your hair with thinning scissors?

Thinning scissors can be safe if used correctly. However, if you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to have a professional do it for you.

How often should I thin my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to thin your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Can thinning scissors be used on any hair type?

Yes, thinning scissors can be used on any hair type. However, they are especially useful for those with thick hair.

Hair Care After Thinning

After thinning your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to avoid damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy.

Thinning your hair with thinning scissors can be a great way to reduce volume and make your hair more manageable. However, it's important to use them correctly to avoid damage. If you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to have a professional do it for you. With the right care, your hair will look and feel great after thinning.
