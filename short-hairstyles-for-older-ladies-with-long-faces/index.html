Short Hairstyles For Older Ladies With Long Faces

Written by William Jun 20, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're an older lady with a long face, finding the perfect hairstyle can be a challenge. Luckily, there are plenty of short hairstyles that can flatter your face shape and make you look and feel great. In this article, we'll explore some of the best short hairstyles for older ladies with long faces, including how to style them and how to care for your hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Right Short Hairstyle
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Short Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Short Hair
  • FAQs about Short Hairstyles for Older Ladies with Long Faces
  • Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

How to Choose the Right Short Hairstyle

When choosing a short hairstyle for a long face, you want to look for styles that add width and volume to the sides of your face. This will help balance out your features and create a more proportional look. Some great short hairstyles for this face shape include:

  • Pixie cut
  • Bob
  • Layered haircut
  • Short shag

When choosing a style, consider your hair texture and lifestyle. If you have fine hair, a layered haircut can add volume and movement. If you're always on the go, a low-maintenance bob may be the perfect fit.

Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a short, cropped style that can be customized to suit your face shape and hair texture. To add volume and width to a long face, consider a pixie cut with side-swept bangs or choppy layers. This will help create the illusion of a wider face and balance out your features.

Bob

The bob is a classic short hairstyle that can be customized in a variety of ways. For a long face, consider a chin-length bob with layers that add volume to the sides of your face. This will help create the illusion of a wider face and balance out your features.

Layered Haircut

A layered haircut can add volume and movement to fine hair, while also creating the illusion of a wider face. For a long face, consider a layered cut with long, face-framing layers that add width to the sides of your face.

Short Shag

The short shag is a textured, layered haircut that can add volume and movement to any hair type. For a long face, consider a shag with choppy layers and side-swept bangs. This will help create the illusion of a wider face and balance out your features.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Short Hair

Styling short hair can be quick and easy, once you know the right techniques. Follow these steps to create a beautiful, polished look:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a styling product, such as mousse or gel, to add volume and hold.
  3. Blow dry your hair using a round brush, lifting the roots to add volume.
  4. Use a flat iron or curling iron to add texture and movement.
  5. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

Remember, the key to styling short hair is to add volume and texture to create the illusion of a wider face.

Tips for Maintaining Short Hair

Maintaining short hair can be easy, once you know the right tips and tricks. Follow these tips to keep your short hair looking great:

  • Get regular trims to keep your style looking fresh.
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Experiment with different products and styling techniques to find what works best for you.

With these tips, you can keep your short hair looking great day after day.

FAQs about Short Hairstyles for Older Ladies with Long Faces

Q: Can older ladies with long faces wear bangs?

A: Yes! Bangs can be a great way to balance out a long face and add volume to the sides. Consider side-swept bangs or choppy, textured bangs that create the illusion of a wider face.

Q: What styling products are best for short hair?

A: The best styling products for short hair are those that add volume and hold, such as mousse, gel, and hairspray. Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type and styling needs.

Q: Can I still wear a ponytail with short hair?

A: Absolutely! While you may not be able to create a traditional ponytail with short hair, you can still create a half-up style or a small, textured ponytail using bobby pins or hair ties.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Keeping your short hair healthy and shiny is easy with these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping the natural oils.
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

With these tips, you can keep your short hair looking healthy and beautiful for years to come.

In conclusion, there are plenty of short hairstyles for older ladies with long faces that can flatter your features and make you feel confident and beautiful. Whether you choose a pixie cut, bob, layered haircut, or short shag, remember to add volume and texture to create the illusion of a wider face. With the right styling techniques and hair care tips, you can keep your short hair looking great day after day.
