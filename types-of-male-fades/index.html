Types Of Male Fades A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jun 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle that will give you a fresh and trendy look? Well, you're in luck because male fades are all the rage right now! In this post, we'll walk you through the different types of male fades so that you can choose the one that suits your style the best. Let's get started!

What is a Fade?

A fade is a short hairstyle that gradually fades from longer hair on top to shorter hair on the sides and back. The hair on the sides and back is usually shaved or cut very short, while the hair on top can vary in length. Fades are popular among men because they are easy to maintain and can be customized to fit any style.

Types of Male Fades

Low Fade

The low fade is a popular choice for those who want a subtle change. It starts above the ears and gradually fades into the hairline, creating a natural and clean look. This type of fade works well with longer hair on top and can be styled in many ways.

Mid Fade

The mid fade is a versatile hairstyle that works well with most hair types. It starts in the middle of the head and gradually fades down to the sides and back. This type of fade is perfect for those who want a more dramatic change without going too short.

High Fade

The high fade is a bold and edgy hairstyle that is perfect for those who want to make a statement. It starts at the temples and gradually fades into the hairline, leaving a lot of hair on top. This type of fade works well with short hair on top and can be styled in many ways.

Bald Fade

The bald fade is a popular choice for those who want a clean and sharp look. It starts at the skin and gradually fades up into the hairline. This type of fade works well with short hair on top and can be styled in many ways.

Taper Fade

The taper fade is a classic hairstyle that has been popular for decades. It starts at the hairline and gradually fades down to the sides and back. This type of fade works well with longer hair on top and can be styled in many ways.

How to Get a Fade

Getting a fade is easy, but it's important to go to a professional barber to ensure that it is done correctly. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Choose the Type of Fade

Choose the type of fade that you want based on your personal style and hair type.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length that you want for the hair on top and the sides and back.

Step 3: Visit a Barber

Visit a professional barber who is experienced in cutting fades. Show them a picture of the type of fade that you want and explain the length that you want.

Step 4: Maintain the Fade

Maintain the fade by visiting the barber regularly for touch-ups and by using the right hair products.

Tips for Maintaining a Fade

Here are some tips for maintaining your fade:

  • Visit the barber regularly for touch-ups
  • Use the right hair products for your hair type
  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors

FAQs

1. Can anyone get a fade?

Yes, anyone can get a fade. However, the type of fade that will work best for you will depend on your hair type and personal style.

2. How do I know which type of fade to get?

Choose the type of fade that you want based on your personal style and hair type. You can also ask your barber for recommendations.

3. How often should I get a touch-up?

You should visit the barber every 2-4 weeks for a touch-up, depending on how fast your hair grows.

4. Can I style my hair in different ways with a fade?

Yes, you can style your hair in many ways with a fade. Experiment with different hairstyles to find the one that suits you best.

Hair Care for Fades

It's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors
  • Use hair products that are designed for your hair type

Now that you know the different types of male fades, it's time to choose the one that suits your style the best. Remember to go to a professional barber to ensure that your fade is done correctly and to maintain your fade by visiting the barber regularly and using the right hair products. With the right care, your fade will look sharp and stylish for weeks to come!
