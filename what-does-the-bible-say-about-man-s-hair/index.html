What Does The Bible Say About Man s Hair

Written by Robby Mar 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

As men, we often take great pride in our hair. We spend hours styling it, washing it, and getting it cut just right. But what does the Bible say about man's hair? In this article, we will explore what the Bible has to say about men's hair and what it means for us today.

Description

The Bible has a lot to say about how we should live our lives, and that includes how we should present ourselves. When it comes to hair, the Bible has a few things to say about how men should wear their hair, but it is not an exhaustive list. It is important to remember that the Bible is a guide for how we should live, but it does not give us specific rules on every aspect of our lives. With that in mind, let's dive into what the Bible says about men's hair.

How to

When it comes to how men should wear their hair, the Bible gives us a few guidelines. First, we should not imitate the hairstyles of pagan cultures. In 1 Corinthians 11:14-15, it says, "Does not the very nature of things teach you that if a man has long hair, it is a disgrace to him, but that if a woman has long hair, it is her glory?" This verse is often interpreted to mean that men should not have long hair, but it is important to remember that the context of the verse is about imitating pagan cultures. So, if a particular hairstyle is associated with a pagan culture, it is best to avoid it.

Second, the Bible tells us that our hair is a symbol of our submission to authority. In 1 Corinthians 11:3-4, it says, "But I want you to understand that the head of every man is Christ, the head of a wife is her husband, and the head of Christ is God. Every man who prays or prophesies with his head covered dishonors his head." This verse is often interpreted to mean that men should not wear hats or cover their heads while praying, but it is also a reminder that our hair should reflect our submission to authority.

Step by Step

So, what does this mean for how we should wear our hair today? Here are a few step-by-step guidelines:

  1. Avoid hairstyles that are associated with pagan cultures.
  2. Keep your hair neat and tidy.
  3. Do not wear hats or cover your head while praying.
  4. Remember that your hair is a symbol of your submission to authority.

Tips

If you are looking for some tips on how to keep your hair looking great while still following these guidelines, here are a few:

  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair neat and tidy.
  • Use high-quality hair care products to keep your hair healthy.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find one that works for you.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about what the Bible says about men's hair:

Should men have short hair?

While the Bible does say that long hair is a disgrace to men, it is important to remember that the context of the verse is about imitating pagan cultures. So, it is not necessarily a sin to have long hair, but it is best to avoid hairstyles that are associated with pagan cultures.

Can men wear hats?

The Bible tells us that men should not wear hats or cover their heads while praying. This is a reminder that our hair should reflect our submission to authority. However, it is not a sin to wear hats in other situations.

Hair Care

If you want to keep your hair looking great while still following the guidelines in the Bible, here are a few hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a high-quality shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it.
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth and health.

In conclusion, the Bible has a few guidelines for how men should wear their hair, but it is not an exhaustive list. It is important to remember that our hair should reflect our submission to authority and should not imitate pagan cultures. By following these guidelines and taking good care of our hair, we can honor God in all aspects of our lives.
