Best Men s Hair Gel For Long Thick Hair

If you have long, thick hair, finding the right hair gel can be a challenge. You want a product that can hold your hair in place without making it feel stiff or crunchy. In this article, we'll explore the best men's hair gel for long thick hair, as well as some tips on how to use it effectively.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Hair Gel for Long Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

  • FAQs

Description

The best hair gel for long thick hair is one that can provide a strong hold without making your hair look greasy or weighed down. It should also be able to withstand humidity and last throughout the day.

When choosing a hair gel, look for products that are specifically designed for long thick hair. These gels are often thicker and more viscous than regular hair gels, which helps them hold your hair in place for longer periods of time.

Some of the best men's hair gels for long thick hair include:

1. American Crew Firm Hold Styling Gel

This gel is designed to provide a strong hold that lasts all day. It also adds shine to your hair without making it look greasy or oily. The formula is alcohol-free, which means it won't dry out your hair or scalp.

2. Redken Brews Stand Tough Extreme Gel

This gel is perfect for long thick hair that needs a little extra hold. It's designed to withstand humidity and last all day without flaking or leaving residue on your hair. The formula is also infused with protein to help strengthen your hair over time.

3. Baxter of California Hard Water Pomade

While technically not a gel, this pomade is a great alternative for long thick hair. It provides a strong hold and a matte finish, making it a great choice for more natural-looking hairstyles. The formula is also infused with natural ingredients like sage and chamomile to nourish your hair and scalp.

How to Use Hair Gel for Long Thick Hair

Using hair gel for long thick hair is easy, but it does require a little bit of technique. Here are some tips on how to use hair gel effectively:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Hair gel works best on clean, dry hair. Make sure you've washed and dried your hair thoroughly before applying any gel. You can also use a blow dryer to help dry your hair faster.

2. Apply the gel evenly

Squeeze a small amount of gel onto your fingers and rub them together to distribute the product evenly. Then, run your fingers through your hair, making sure to coat each strand evenly.

3. Style your hair

Once the gel is evenly distributed, you can begin styling your hair. Use a comb or your fingers to create the desired look. If you're going for a more natural-looking style, use your fingers to create texture and movement.

4. Let the gel dry completely

Allow the gel to dry completely before touching your hair. This will help the gel set and prevent it from becoming sticky or crunchy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to use hair gel for long thick hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair thoroughly
  2. Squeeze a small amount of gel onto your fingers
  3. Rub your fingers together to distribute the gel evenly
  4. Run your fingers through your hair, making sure to coat each strand evenly
  5. Begin styling your hair using a comb or your fingers
  6. Allow the gel to dry completely before touching your hair

Tips for Using Hair Gel on Long Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for using hair gel on long thick hair:

  • Use a small amount of gel to avoid making your hair look greasy or weighed down
  • Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you
  • Don't be afraid to use a blow dryer to help set your style
  • Try using a hair spray to help hold your style in place even longer

FAQs

1. Is hair gel bad for long thick hair?

No, hair gel is not bad for long thick hair as long as you use it properly. Avoid using too much gel, as this can make your hair look greasy or weighed down. Also, make sure to wash your hair thoroughly after using gel to prevent product buildup.

2. How often should I use hair gel on long thick hair?

You can use hair gel on long thick hair as often as you like, but it's best to limit your use to a few times a week. Overusing hair gel can dry out your hair and scalp, leading to breakage and other damage.

3. Can I use hair gel on wet hair?

While you can use hair gel on wet hair, it's best to use it on dry hair for the best results. Wet hair can dilute the gel and make it less effective at holding your style in place.

Hair Care Tips for Long Thick Hair

Finally, here are some additional hair care tips for long thick hair:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated and healthy
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons too often, as this can damage your hair
  • Get regular haircuts to prevent split ends and other damage

With the right hair gel and proper hair care, you can achieve any hairstyle you desire. Experiment with different styles and products to find the one that works best for you and your long thick hair.
