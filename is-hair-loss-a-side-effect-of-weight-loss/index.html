Is Hair Loss A Side Effect Of Weight Loss

Written by Luffy Sep 11, 2023 · 7 min read

When embarking on a weight loss journey, many people are concerned about potential side effects such as fatigue, mood swings, and muscle loss. However, one side effect that often goes unnoticed is hair loss. In this article, we will answer the question, "is hair loss a side effect of weight loss?" and provide tips for preventing hair loss while losing weight.

Table of Contents

  • What Causes Hair Loss During Weight Loss?
  • How to Prevent Hair Loss During Weight Loss
  • Step by Step Guide for Hair Care During Weight Loss
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair During Weight Loss
  • FAQs About Hair Loss and Weight Loss

What Causes Hair Loss During Weight Loss?

Hair loss during weight loss can be caused by a variety of factors. One common cause is a lack of essential nutrients such as protein, iron, and biotin. When the body is in a calorie deficit, it may not be getting enough of these nutrients to support healthy hair growth.

Another cause of hair loss during weight loss is hormonal changes. When the body is in a calorie deficit, it produces less estrogen and testosterone, which can lead to hair loss.

Finally, stress can also be a contributing factor. Losing weight can be a stressful experience, and stress can lead to hair loss.

How to Prevent Hair Loss During Weight Loss

The good news is that there are steps you can take to prevent hair loss while losing weight. First, make sure you are getting enough essential nutrients such as protein, iron, and biotin. You may need to supplement your diet with vitamins or protein shakes to ensure you are getting enough of these nutrients.

Second, try to minimize stress during your weight loss journey. Practice stress-reducing activities such as meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises.

Finally, consider using a hair growth shampoo or treatment to help promote healthy hair growth.

Step by Step Guide for Hair Care During Weight Loss

Here is a step-by-step guide for taking care of your hair during weight loss:

  1. Make sure you are getting enough essential nutrients such as protein, iron, and biotin.
  2. Consider taking a multivitamin or protein supplement to ensure you are getting enough of these nutrients.
  3. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  4. Avoid using harsh hair treatments such as bleach or perms.
  5. Use a hair growth shampoo or treatment to promote healthy hair growth.
  6. Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids or ponytails that can pull on your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair During Weight Loss

Here are some additional tips for maintaining healthy hair during weight loss:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools such as straighteners or curling irons.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it is wet, as wet hair is more prone to breakage.
  • Avoid wearing hats or tight headbands for extended periods of time.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Consider getting a scalp massage to promote healthy hair growth.

FAQs About Hair Loss and Weight Loss

Q: Is hair loss a common side effect of weight loss?

A: Yes, hair loss can be a common side effect of weight loss.

Q: Can hair loss during weight loss be permanent?

A: In most cases, hair loss during weight loss is temporary and will grow back once the body adjusts to the new weight.

Q: Can I prevent hair loss during weight loss?

A: Yes, you can prevent hair loss during weight loss by ensuring you are getting enough essential nutrients, minimizing stress, and using hair growth treatments.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after weight loss?

A: It can take several months for hair to grow back after weight loss, depending on the individual.


In conclusion, hair loss can be a side effect of weight loss, but it is not inevitable. By taking steps to ensure you are getting enough essential nutrients and minimizing stress, you can prevent hair loss while losing weight. Additionally, using hair growth treatments and practicing good hair care habits can help promote healthy hair growth.
