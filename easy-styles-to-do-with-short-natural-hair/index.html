Easy Styles To Do With Short Natural Hair

Are you looking for easy styles to do with short natural hair? Look no further! In this post, we will share some simple and stylish hairstyles that you can do on your short natural hair. Whether you have a TWA (teeny weeny afro) or a slightly longer cut, these hairstyles will work for you. Let's get started!

Description

Short natural hair can be fun and versatile to style. With the right techniques and products, you can achieve a variety of looks that will make you feel confident and beautiful. In this post, we will show you some easy styles that you can do on your short natural hair.

How to

Before we dive into the hairstyles, here are some general tips on how to style short natural hair:

  • Start with clean, conditioned hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a deep conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently detangle your hair.
  • Use bobby pins, hair ties, and hair clips to secure your styles.
  • Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair.

Step by Step

Now, let's get into the hairstyles! Here are step-by-step instructions for each style:

Style 1: Wash and Go

1. Start with clean, conditioned hair.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair.

3. Use your fingers to define your curls and reduce frizz.

4. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser on low heat.

5. Fluff your curls with your fingers to add volume.

Style 2: Twist Out

1. Start with clean, conditioned hair.

2. Section your hair into small sections.

3. Apply a twisting cream or gel to each section.

4. Twist each section tightly from root to tip.

5. Allow your hair to dry completely or sit under a hooded dryer.

6. Unravel each twist carefully and fluff your hair with your fingers.

Style 3: Bantu Knots

1. Start with clean, conditioned hair.

2. Section your hair into small sections.

3. Apply a twisting cream or gel to each section.

4. Twist each section tightly from root to tip.

5. Wrap each twist into a small knot and secure with a bobby pin or hair tie.

6. Allow your hair to dry completely or sit under a hooded dryer.

7. Carefully unravel each knot and fluff your hair with your fingers.

Style 4: Frohawk

1. Start with clean, conditioned hair.

2. Section the top of your hair into a small strip.

3. Use bobby pins or hair ties to secure the sides of your hair.

4. Use a comb or your fingers to tease the top section of your hair.

5. Apply a holding gel or pomade to the top section of your hair.

6. Smooth the top section of your hair back and secure with bobby pins or hair ties.

7. Fluff the sides of your hair with your fingers.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Experiment with different textures and lengths to find what works best for you.
  • Use a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to protect your hair while you sleep.
  • Don't be afraid to accessorize with headbands, scarves, or hair jewelry.
  • Be gentle when detangling your hair to avoid breakage.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about styling short natural hair:

Q: How often should I wash my short natural hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how active you are. As a general rule, you should wash your hair every 7-10 days to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Q: How can I prevent shrinkage on my short natural hair?

A: You can stretch your hair using methods such as banding, twists, or braids. You can also use a blow dryer on low heat to stretch your hair.

Hair Care

Here are some general tips for caring for your short natural hair:

  • Moisturize your hair daily with a water-based product.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant and avoiding excessive heat styling.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

With these easy styles and tips, you can rock your short natural hair with confidence and style. Whether you're going for a casual or a formal look, these hairstyles are sure to turn heads.
