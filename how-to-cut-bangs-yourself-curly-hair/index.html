How To Cut Bangs Yourself Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 12, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have curly hair, you may be hesitant to cut your own bangs. But with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a great look without spending a lot of money at the salon. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut bangs yourself curly hair in a few easy steps. Whether you're looking for a new style or just want to freshen up your look, this guide will help you achieve the perfect cut.

Description

Curly hair is unique and requires special care to maintain its shape and texture. When cutting bangs, it's important to take into account the natural curl pattern of your hair. This will help you achieve the best results and avoid any unwanted frizz or damage. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about cutting bangs yourself curly hair, including how to choose the right tools, how to prepare your hair, and how to style your new look.

How to Cut Bangs Yourself Curly Hair: Step by Step

Step 1: Choose the Right Tools

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few essential tools. These include:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clips or hair ties
  • A comb or brush

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean, as dull or dirty scissors can damage your hair. You may also want to invest in a pair of specialty shears designed specifically for cutting curly hair, as these can help you achieve a more precise cut.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. If you're planning to cut your bangs while your hair is wet, make sure to comb it out and remove any tangles. If you prefer to cut your bangs while your hair is dry, make sure to style it as you normally would.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use clips or hair ties to section off the hair you want to cut. For bangs, this will typically be the hair at the front of your head, just above your eyebrows. Make sure to leave enough hair for the length and thickness of bangs you want to achieve.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Using your scissors, start cutting the hair in small sections. Begin at the center of your bangs and work your way outwards, cutting small amounts at a time. Remember to cut your hair at an angle, rather than straight across, to achieve a more natural look. If you're not sure how much to cut, start with less and work your way up to your desired length.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've cut your bangs, take a step back and check your progress. Make sure both sides are even and adjust as needed. If you need to make any additional cuts, do so in small increments to avoid cutting too much at once.

Step 6: Style Your Bangs

Once you're happy with your cut, it's time to style your new look. Use a comb or brush to style your bangs as desired, and add any product you normally use to enhance your curls. You may also want to use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help set your new bangs.

Tips for Cutting Bangs Yourself Curly Hair

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to your desired length
  • Use sharp, clean scissors to avoid damaging your hair
  • Cut at an angle, rather than straight across, for a more natural look
  • Check your progress frequently to ensure evenness
  • Style your bangs as desired to achieve the perfect look

FAQs

Can I cut my own bangs with curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own bangs with curly hair. Just make sure to follow the steps outlined in this guide and use the right tools for the job.

How do I avoid damaging my hair when cutting bangs?

Use sharp, clean scissors and cut in small sections to avoid damaging your hair. You may also want to invest in specialty shears designed for cutting curly hair.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my bangs?

If you make a mistake while cutting your bangs, don't panic. You can always make adjustments and trim more hair as needed. If you're really unhappy with the results, you may want to visit a professional stylist to help you fix the problem.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair requires a different approach than straight hair. Here are a few tips to keep your curls looking their best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, which can cause damage and frizz
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to set your curls without causing frizz
  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil

Cutting your own bangs can be a fun and rewarding experience, especially when you have curly hair. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great look without spending a lot of money at the salon. Follow the steps outlined in this guide and use the tips and tricks provided to achieve the perfect cut. And remember, if you're not happy with the results, you can always make adjustments or visit a professional stylist for help.
