How Long Will My Hair Fall Out Postpartum

Written by William Apr 16, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're a new mom, you might be wondering how long it will take for your hair to stop falling out after giving birth. Hair loss after pregnancy is a common phenomenon, but it can be frustrating to deal with. In this article, we'll discuss why postpartum hair loss occurs, how long it lasts, and what you can do to manage it.

Table of Contents

  • Why Does Hair Loss Occur Postpartum?
  • How Long Does Postpartum Hair Loss Last?
  • Tips for Managing Postpartum Hair Loss
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Postpartum Hair Loss

Why Does Hair Loss Occur Postpartum?

During pregnancy, hormones can cause your hair to grow thicker and fuller. However, after giving birth, your hormone levels rapidly drop, which can trigger hair loss. This is because hair growth and shedding are controlled by hormones, and changes in hormone levels can disrupt the normal growth cycle of your hair.

Postpartum hair loss can be alarming, but it's a normal part of the postpartum experience. Most women will experience some degree of hair loss after giving birth, but the severity can vary from person to person.

How Long Does Postpartum Hair Loss Last?

Postpartum hair loss typically peaks around 3-4 months after giving birth, but it can last up to a year. The good news is that your hair should eventually return to its pre-pregnancy state, although it may take several months for this to happen.

If you're concerned about the amount of hair you're losing, or if your hair loss is accompanied by other symptoms such as itching or scalp tenderness, it's a good idea to talk to your doctor. They can help determine if there's an underlying condition that needs to be addressed.

Tips for Managing Postpartum Hair Loss

While you can't completely prevent postpartum hair loss, there are some things you can do to manage it:

  • Be gentle with your hair. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and use a wide-toothed comb to detangle it.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles. Tight ponytails, braids, and other styles that pull on your hair can exacerbate hair loss.
  • Eat a healthy diet. Foods rich in vitamins and minerals can help support healthy hair growth.
  • Consider a shorter haircut. Shorter hair can be easier to manage and may help disguise hair loss.

FAQ

Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

No, postpartum hair loss is not permanent. Your hair should eventually return to its pre-pregnancy state, although it may take several months for this to happen.

Can breastfeeding cause hair loss?

Yes, breastfeeding can contribute to postpartum hair loss. However, it's important to note that not all women who breastfeed will experience hair loss.

Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

While you can't completely prevent postpartum hair loss, you can take steps to manage it. See the tips section above for more information.

Hair Care for Postpartum Hair Loss

It's important to take good care of your hair during the postpartum period to help minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons.
  • Avoid chemical treatments such as perms and relaxers.
  • Consider using a volumizing shampoo or conditioner to help disguise hair loss.
  • If you're experiencing scalp tenderness or itching, try using a gentle scalp treatment such as a tea tree oil shampoo or aloe vera gel.

Remember, postpartum hair loss is a normal part of the postpartum experience, and it will eventually resolve on its own. In the meantime, take good care of your hair and be gentle with it, and don't hesitate to talk to your doctor if you have any concerns.
