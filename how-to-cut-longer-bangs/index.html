How To Cut Longer Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 04, 2023 · 6 min read

Description

Longer bangs can give you a fresh and stylish look. However, cutting them on your own can be a daunting task. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting longer bangs at home.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Cut Longer Bangs: Step-by-Step Guide

Before we begin, make sure you have a sharp pair of scissors and a comb. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Decide on the Length

The first step is to decide on the length of your bangs. If you are unsure, start with a longer length and trim gradually. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't add length once it's cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair from the rest of your hair. This will make it easier to focus on your bangs. Use hair clips to secure the rest of your hair.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start by cutting your bangs in the middle. Hold your scissors at a slight angle and cut in a straight line. Once you have cut the center, move on to the sides. Cut diagonally towards the center, making sure both sides are equal in length.

Step 4: Check Your Work

After cutting, comb your bangs and check if they are equal in length. If you notice any unevenness, trim as needed. Remember to cut a little at a time to avoid cutting too much.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Once you are happy with the length, style your bangs as desired. You can blow-dry them or use a straightener to give them a sleek look.

Tips for Cutting Bangs

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your bangs:

  • Start with a longer length and trim gradually.
  • Always use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Cut your bangs when your hair is dry for more accuracy.
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or frustrated.
  • Don't cut your bangs too short, as they will take longer to grow back.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my bangs when my hair is wet?

A: We recommend cutting your bangs when your hair is dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

Q: How often should I cut my bangs?

A: It depends on your hair growth and the length of your bangs. As a general rule, cut your bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their length.

Q: What if I mess up my bangs?

A: Don't panic! If you accidentally cut too much, wait for your hair to grow back or visit a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your bangs looking great, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can confidently cut your own longer bangs at home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun experimenting with your new look!
