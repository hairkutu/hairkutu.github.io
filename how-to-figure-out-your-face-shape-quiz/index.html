How To Figure Out Your Face Shape Quiz A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Apr 13, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Do you ever wonder what your face shape is? Figuring out your face shape can help you determine which hairstyles, makeup looks, and accessories suit you best. However, it can be challenging to determine your face shape without the help of a professional. That's why we've created this easy-to-follow guide on how to figure out your face shape quiz in the comfort of your own home.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Measure Your Face
  • Step 2: Analyze Your Measurements
  • Step 3: Take the Face Shape Quiz
  • Tips on Determining Your Face Shape
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Your Face Shape

Step 1: Measure Your Face

The first step is to measure your face. You'll need a measuring tape or ruler and a mirror. Here are the measurements you'll need to take:

  • Forehead: Measure across your forehead at the widest point (typically between your eyebrows and hairline).
  • Cheekbones: Measure across your cheekbones at the widest point (just below the outer corner of your eyes).
  • Jawline: Measure from the tip of your chin to the point below your ear where your jaw angles upward. Then, double this measurement.
  • Face Length: Measure from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Record each measurement on a piece of paper.

Step 2: Analyze Your Measurements

Now that you have your measurements, it's time to analyze them. Here are the different face shapes and their corresponding measurements:

  • Oval: Your face length is larger than your cheekbones, and your forehead is larger than your jawline. The angle of your jaw is rounded.
  • Round: Your face length and cheekbone measurements are similar. Your forehead and jawline measurements are also similar. The angle of your jaw is soft and rounded.
  • Square: Your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline measurements are similar. The angle of your jaw is sharp.
  • Heart: Your forehead measurement is larger than your cheekbones and jawline measurements. Your chin is pointed.
  • Diamond: Your cheekbones are the widest part of your face. Your forehead and jawline measurements are smaller. Your chin is pointed.
  • Triangle: Your jawline measurement is larger than your forehead and cheekbone measurements. Your chin is pointed.

Look at your measurements and compare them to the descriptions above. Which face shape do you think you have?

Step 3: Take the Face Shape Quiz

If you're still unsure about your face shape, you can take a face shape quiz. There are several quizzes available online that can help you determine your face shape based on a series of questions and photos. Here are some popular options:

  • Oprah's Face Shape Quiz
  • Good Housekeeping's Face Shape Quiz
  • Elle's Face Shape Quiz

Answer the questions honestly and follow the instructions carefully. Once you've completed the quiz, you should have a better idea of your face shape.

Tips on Determining Your Face Shape

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when determining your face shape:

  • Make sure you're measuring your face accurately. Use a mirror and a measuring tape or ruler.
  • Don't worry if your measurements are slightly different from the descriptions above. Most people have a combination of two or more face shapes.
  • Consider your facial features. Do you have a prominent forehead, high cheekbones, or a pointed chin? These can also help you determine your face shape.

FAQs

What if my measurements don't match any of the face shapes?

It's possible that you have a unique face shape that doesn't fit into any of the traditional categories. In this case, you can focus on finding hairstyles and makeup looks that flatter your features instead of trying to fit into a specific face shape.

Can I determine my face shape without measuring?

While measuring your face is the most accurate way to determine your face shape, you can also look at photos of yourself and compare them to the descriptions above. Keep in mind that lighting and angles can affect how your face looks in photos.

Hair Care Tips for Your Face Shape

Once you've determined your face shape, you can use this information to choose hairstyles and haircuts that flatter your features. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Oval: Almost any hairstyle looks good on oval faces, but avoid hairstyles that cover your forehead.
  • Round: Choose hairstyles that add height to your crown and elongate your face. Avoid blunt bangs and chin-length bobs.
  • Square: Soften your angles with layered hairstyles and side-swept bangs. Avoid straight, blunt cuts.
  • Heart: Choose hairstyles that add volume to your chin area and narrow your forehead. Avoid short, choppy styles.
  • Diamond: Choose hairstyles that add volume to your forehead and chin areas. Avoid flat, straight styles.
  • Triangle: Choose hairstyles that add volume to your crown and balance your jawline. Avoid styles that emphasize your chin.

Remember, these are just guidelines. Ultimately, the best hairstyle is one that makes you feel confident and beautiful.

Conclusion

Determining your face shape can be a fun and useful exercise. By following the steps and tips in this guide, you should have a better idea of your face shape and how to choose hairstyles and makeup looks that flatter your features. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and have fun with your appearance!
