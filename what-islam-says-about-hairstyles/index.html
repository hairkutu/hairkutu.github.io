What Islam Says About Hairstyles A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 08, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you are a Muslim looking for guidance on hairstyles or just curious about what Islam says about hair, you have come to the right place. In this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about hairstyles in Islam, including how to maintain your hair, what hairstyles are permissible or prohibited, and much more. So, without further ado, let's dive in!

What Does Islam Say About Hair?

Islam emphasizes the importance of cleanliness and grooming, and hair is no exception. Muslims are encouraged to keep their hair clean and well-maintained. In fact, Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said, "Cleanliness is half of faith."

Islam also teaches modesty, and this applies to hairstyles as well. Muslims are encouraged to adopt modest hairstyles that do not draw unnecessary attention to themselves.

Permissible Hairstyles in Islam

Islam does not prescribe any specific hairstyle for men or women. However, there are certain guidelines that Muslims are encouraged to follow when it comes to hairstyles. Here are some permissible hairstyles in Islam:

For Men

  • Short hair is preferred, but not required.
  • Beards are encouraged.
  • Shaving the head completely is allowed but not recommended.

For Women

  • Hair should be covered in public.
  • Long hair is permissible, but it should be kept neat and tidy.
  • Braids, ponytails, and buns are all acceptable hairstyles.

Prohibited Hairstyles in Islam

Islam also prohibits certain hairstyles that are considered immodest or inappropriate. Here are some hairstyles that are not allowed in Islam:

  • Hairstyles that resemble those of non-Muslims. For example, mohawks, afros, or dreadlocks.
  • Hairstyles that are associated with a particular gender. For example, men should not style their hair in a way that resembles women's hairstyles.
  • Hairstyles that are overly elaborate or attention-seeking.

How to Maintain Your Hair in Islam

Now that you know what hairstyles are permissible or prohibited in Islam, it's important to know how to maintain your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with shampoo to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.
  • Avoid using excessive hair products that can damage your hair or make it look unnatural.
  • Use a comb or brush to keep your hair neat and tidy.
  • For women, use a hijab or headscarf to cover your hair in public.

FAQs About Hairstyles in Islam

1. Can women dye their hair in Islam?

Yes, women can dye their hair in Islam as long as the dye is made from natural ingredients and does not harm the hair or scalp.

2. Can men wear their hair long in Islam?

Yes, men can wear their hair long in Islam, but it should be kept neat and tidy. Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) had long hair, but he always kept it well-groomed.

3. Can women cut their hair in Islam?

Yes, women can cut their hair in Islam, but it should be done modestly and not in a way that draws attention.

Conclusion

As we have seen, Islam places great importance on cleanliness, modesty, and grooming when it comes to hairstyles. Muslims are encouraged to keep their hair clean, well-maintained, and modest. By following these guidelines, Muslims can maintain a hygienic and dignified appearance that reflects their faith and values.
