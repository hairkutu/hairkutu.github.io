What Islam Says About Facial Hair A Guide For Men

Written by Aprilia Aug 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Islam is one of the most widely practiced religions in the world, with over 1 billion followers. One of the many aspects of Islam is the importance of facial hair for men. In this guide, we will explore what Islam says about facial hair and how men can care for their beards and mustaches in accordance with Islamic teachings.

What Does Islam Say About Facial Hair?

Islam places a great emphasis on the natural growth of facial hair for men. The Prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him, is believed to have encouraged men to grow their beards and keep them looking neat and tidy. This is because facial hair is seen as a sign of maturity, wisdom, and masculinity in Islam.

According to Islamic teachings, men should not shave their beards completely, as this is considered to be imitating women. However, it is permissible to trim and shape the beard, as long as it does not alter its natural appearance.

How to Care for Your Beard and Mustache in Islam

If you are a Muslim man who wants to take care of your beard and mustache in accordance with Islamic teachings, there are a few things you can do:

Step 1: Keep Your Beard Clean

It is important to keep your beard clean by washing it regularly with a mild soap or shampoo. This will help to remove dirt, oil, and dead skin cells that can accumulate in your beard and cause it to look unkempt.

Step 2: Trim Your Beard Regularly

Trimming your beard regularly will help to keep it looking neat and tidy. However, it is important to only trim the ends of your beard and not to shave it off completely. This will help to maintain the natural appearance of your beard.

Step 3: Use Beard Oil

Using beard oil can help to keep your beard soft, shiny, and healthy. Beard oil is typically made from natural oils such as jojoba oil, argan oil, and coconut oil, which can help to moisturize your skin and prevent your beard from becoming dry and itchy.

Step 4: Comb Your Beard

Combing your beard regularly can help to prevent tangles and keep it looking neat and tidy. Use a wide-toothed comb or a beard brush to gently comb through your beard, starting from the roots and working your way to the ends.

Tips for Maintaining a Healthy Beard

Here are a few additional tips for maintaining a healthy beard:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or hot tools on your beard, as these can damage your hair and skin.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid smoking and excessive drinking, as these can cause your beard to become dry and brittle.

FAQs About Facial Hair in Islam

Here are some frequently asked questions about facial hair in Islam:

Q: Is it haram to shave your beard?

A: Shaving your beard completely is considered to be haram, as it goes against Islamic teachings. However, it is permissible to trim and shape your beard.

Q: Can I dye my beard?

A: Yes, it is permissible to dye your beard, as long as the dye does not contain any harmful chemicals or substances that are prohibited in Islam.

Q: Is it sunnah to grow a beard?

A: Yes, growing a beard is considered to be sunnah, or a recommended practice, in Islam.

The Bottom Line

In conclusion, Islam places a great emphasis on the natural growth of facial hair for men. If you are a Muslim man who wants to take care of your beard and mustache in accordance with Islamic teachings, be sure to keep your beard clean, trim it regularly, use beard oil, and comb it regularly. By following these tips, you can maintain a healthy and attractive beard while also staying true to your religious beliefs.
