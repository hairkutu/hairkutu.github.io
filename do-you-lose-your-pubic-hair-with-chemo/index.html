Do You Lose Your Pubic Hair With Chemo

Written by Robby Apr 09, 2023 · 9 min read

Chemotherapy is a common treatment for cancer, and it involves powerful drugs that kill cancer cells. However, these drugs also affect healthy cells, including hair cells. As a result, many people who undergo chemotherapy experience hair loss, including loss of pubic hair. In this article, we’ll explore the topic of pubic hair loss during chemotherapy, and provide tips and advice for coping with this side effect.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Pubic hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy, along with hair loss on the scalp, eyebrows, and eyelashes. The severity of hair loss can vary depending on the type of chemotherapy drugs used, the dosage, and the individual’s overall health. Some people may experience complete hair loss, while others may only experience thinning or patchy hair loss.

Pubic hair loss can be a particularly sensitive issue for some people, as it is a private and personal area of the body. However, it is important to remember that hair loss is a temporary side effect of chemotherapy, and that pubic hair will usually grow back after treatment is completed.

How to

If you are experiencing pubic hair loss during chemotherapy, there are several things you can do to cope with this side effect:

1. Consider hair removal

If you are uncomfortable with the appearance of patchy or thinning pubic hair, you may want to consider removing it completely. Shaving, waxing, or using depilatory creams are all options. However, it is important to be gentle with your skin during chemotherapy, as it may be more sensitive than usual.

2. Wear loose-fitting clothing

Tight clothing can rub against the skin and cause irritation, especially if pubic hair is thinning or absent. Wearing loose-fitting clothing, especially cotton or other breathable fabrics, can help reduce irritation and discomfort.

3. Use gentle skin care products

Chemotherapy can cause skin to become dry and sensitive. Using gentle skin care products, such as fragrance-free moisturizers and mild soaps, can help reduce irritation and discomfort in the pubic area.

Step by Step

If you decide to remove your pubic hair during chemotherapy, here are some step-by-step instructions for shaving:

1. Trim hair with scissors or clippers

Before shaving, use scissors or clippers to trim the hair as short as possible. This will make shaving easier and reduce the risk of irritation.

2. Soak in warm water

Soak in a warm bath or shower for a few minutes to soften the hair and skin.

3. Apply shaving cream or gel

Apply a generous amount of shaving cream or gel to the pubic area, making sure to cover all the hair.

4. Shave gently

Using a sharp razor, shave in the direction of hair growth, using gentle strokes. Rinse the razor frequently to prevent clogging.

5. Rinse and moisturize

Rinse the area thoroughly with warm water and pat dry with a soft towel. Apply a fragrance-free moisturizer to soothe the skin.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for coping with pubic hair loss during chemotherapy:

1. Talk to your healthcare provider

If you are experiencing significant hair loss, including pubic hair loss, talk to your healthcare provider. They may be able to recommend treatments or products to help reduce irritation and discomfort.

2. Consider hair alternatives

If you are uncomfortable with the appearance of patchy or thinning pubic hair, consider using hair alternatives, such as wigs, hairpieces, or scarves. These can help boost your confidence and make you feel more comfortable.

3. Be patient

Remember that hair loss is a temporary side effect of chemotherapy, and that pubic hair will usually grow back after treatment is completed. Be patient and kind to yourself, and focus on taking care of your overall health and well-being.

FAQ

Does everyone lose their pubic hair during chemotherapy?

No, not everyone will experience complete pubic hair loss during chemotherapy. The severity of hair loss can vary depending on the type of chemotherapy drugs used, the dosage, and the individual’s overall health.

Will my pubic hair grow back after chemotherapy?

Yes, pubic hair will usually grow back after chemotherapy is completed. However, it may take several months for hair to grow back fully.

Is pubic hair loss permanent?

No, pubic hair loss due to chemotherapy is usually temporary, and hair will usually grow back after treatment is completed.

Hair Care

After chemotherapy, it is important to take care of your hair and scalp as they recover. Here are some tips for hair care after chemotherapy:

1. Be gentle

Be gentle with your hair and scalp, especially during the first few weeks after chemotherapy. Avoid harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes and perms, and use gentle shampoos and conditioners.

2. Protect your scalp

If your scalp is sensitive or tender, protect it from the sun and cold weather by wearing a hat or scarf.

3. Be patient

It may take several months for your hair to grow back fully, so be patient and kind to yourself. In the meantime, experiment with different hairstyles, wigs, or hairpieces to help boost your confidence.

Pubic hair loss can be an uncomfortable and sensitive issue for some people, but it is important to remember that it is a temporary side effect of chemotherapy. By following these tips and taking care of yourself, you can cope with pubic hair loss and focus on your overall health and well-being.
