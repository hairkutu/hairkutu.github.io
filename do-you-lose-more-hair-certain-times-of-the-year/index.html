Do You Lose More Hair Certain Times Of The Year

Written by Luffy Apr 26, 2023 · 6 min read

If you’ve ever experienced excessive hair loss, you know how frustrating it can be. You may wonder if there are certain times of the year when you lose more hair than others. The answer is yes, and it’s completely normal. In this article, we’ll explore the reasons behind seasonal hair loss and what you can do to minimize it.

Why Do You Lose More Hair Certain Times of the Year?

Hair growth is a complex process that is influenced by genetics, hormones, diet, and lifestyle. It’s normal to lose hair every day as part of the natural hair growth cycle. However, there are certain times of the year when you may notice more hair loss than usual. Here are some of the reasons:

Seasonal Shedding

Just like animals, humans shed their hair as a natural response to the changing seasons. In the fall and winter, you may notice more hair loss as your body prepares for the colder weather. This shedding is known as seasonal shedding and is completely normal.

Stress

The holiday season can be a stressful time for many people, and stress can trigger hair loss. When you’re stressed, your body produces more cortisol, a hormone that can lead to hair loss. This can result in more shedding during the fall and winter months.

Dietary Changes

During the summer months, you may consume more fruits and vegetables that contain vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth. When winter comes, you may not be getting as many of these nutrients, which can lead to more hair loss.

Less Sunlight

During the fall and winter months, there is less sunlight, which can lead to a decrease in vitamin D levels. Vitamin D is important for healthy hair growth, so a deficiency can lead to more hair loss.

How to Minimize Seasonal Hair Loss

While seasonal hair loss is normal, there are things you can do to minimize it:

Eat a Balanced Diet

Make sure you’re getting enough vitamins and minerals in your diet by eating a variety of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins.

Take Supplements

If you’re not getting enough nutrients from your diet, consider taking a multivitamin or hair growth supplement.

Reduce Stress

To reduce stress, try practicing yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises. You could also consider talking to a therapist or counselor.

Get Enough Vitamin D

If you live in an area with limited sunlight, consider taking a vitamin D supplement.

FAQ

Q: Is seasonal hair loss permanent?

A: No, seasonal shedding is temporary and your hair will grow back.

Q: How much hair loss is normal?

A: It’s normal to lose between 50 and 100 hairs per day.

Q: Can hair loss be a sign of a more serious medical condition?

A: Yes, hair loss can be a sign of a thyroid disorder, autoimmune disease, or other medical condition. If you’re concerned, talk to your doctor.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to the tips mentioned above, here are some other hair care tips to keep in mind:

Use a Gentle Shampoo

Choose a shampoo that is gentle on your hair and scalp. Avoid shampoos that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and lead to breakage. Try to avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners.

Protect Your Hair from the Elements

Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the cold, wind, and snow.

In conclusion, seasonal hair loss is normal and nothing to be overly concerned about. By taking care of your hair and making some lifestyle changes, you can minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
