How To Keep Your Hair Through Chemo The Ultimate Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 01, 2023 · 7 min read

Dealing with cancer is already a tough battle, and losing your hair due to chemotherapy can make it even harder. It can take a toll on your self-esteem and confidence, but there are ways to maintain your hair during chemo. In this article, we’ll share tips and tricks on how to keep your hair through chemo, so you can feel more in control during this difficult time.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Keep Your Hair Through Chemo

While there is no surefire way to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy, there are some things you can do to minimize it.

  • Use a scalp-cooling cap during treatment. This can reduce the amount of chemotherapy drugs that reach the hair follicles.
  • Switch to a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are free of harsh chemicals and fragrances.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, like blow dryers and straighteners. These can cause damage to your hair and make it more prone to falling out.
  • Consider getting a shorter haircut. Shorter hair is less likely to tangle and break.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you’re looking for a more detailed approach, here’s a step-by-step guide on how to keep your hair through chemo:

  1. Start by using a scalp-cooling cap during treatment. Ask your doctor or nurse about getting one.
  2. Switch to a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are free of harsh chemicals and fragrances.
  3. Avoid heat styling tools, like blow dryers and straighteners.
  4. Consider getting a shorter haircut to minimize tangling and breakage.
  5. Protect your hair from the sun and cold weather by wearing a hat or scarf.
  6. Avoid tight hairstyles, like braids and ponytails, which can pull on your hair and cause it to fall out.
  7. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or a soft-bristled brush.
  8. Avoid hair dyes and chemical treatments that can damage your hair.
  9. Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  10. Consider talking to a therapist or joining a support group to help you cope with the emotional effects of hair loss.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you keep your hair through chemo:

  • Use a silk pillowcase to minimize friction and prevent hair breakage.
  • Try using a leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized.
  • Consider using a hair growth serum, like minoxidil, to help stimulate hair growth.
  • Avoid using hair accessories, like hair ties and clips, that can pull on your hair.
  • Wash your hair less frequently to avoid over-washing and stripping your hair of its natural oils.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about keeping your hair through chemo:

Can I dye my hair during chemo?

No, it’s best to avoid hair dyes and chemical treatments during chemo, as they can damage your hair and scalp.

Will my hair grow back after chemo?

Yes, in most cases, your hair will grow back after chemo. However, it may be thinner or a different texture than before.

Can I use a hair dryer during chemo?

Avoid using heat styling tools, like hair dryers and straighteners, as they can cause damage to your hair.

Hair Care During Chemo

It’s important to take care of your hair during chemo to minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are free of harsh chemicals and fragrances.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, like blow dryers and straighteners.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and cold weather.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Talk to your doctor about using minoxidil or other hair growth serums.

By following these tips and tricks, you can minimize hair loss during chemo and promote healthy hair growth. Remember, hair loss is a temporary side effect of chemo, and your hair will likely grow back after treatment. In the meantime, take care of yourself and focus on your health.
