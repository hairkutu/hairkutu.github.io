How To Cut My Own Hair Into A Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 28, 2023 · 7 min read

Cutting your own hair can be daunting, especially if you are going for a specific style like a bob. But with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of how to cut your own hair into a bob.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut My Own Hair into a Bob
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

How to Cut My Own Hair into a Bob

Before you begin cutting your hair, it’s important to have a clear idea of what kind of bob you want. Do you want a classic bob, an angled bob, or a layered bob? Once you have decided on the style, gather the necessary tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror

It’s also helpful to have a reference photo of the bob style you want to achieve to use as a guide.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will give you a better idea of how it falls naturally and will make it easier to cut. Once your hair is dry, brush it out and part it in the middle.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section off your hair into four quadrants: two in the front and two in the back. Secure each section with a hair clip.

Step 3: Cut the Back of Your Hair

Starting with the back sections, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Continue doing this with small sections of hair, working your way up to the crown of your head.

Step 4: Cut the Front of Your Hair

Once you have finished cutting the back of your hair, move on to the front sections. Take a small section of hair from one side of your head and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to the desired length. Repeat on the other side.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you have finished cutting your hair, check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your hair is even and the length is what you want.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and you don’t want to make any mistakes because you’re tired.
  • Don’t be too hard on yourself. It takes practice to get good at cutting your own hair.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair into a bob if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair into a bob if you have curly hair. Just be sure to cut it when it’s dry so you can see how it falls naturally.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don’t panic. You can always take a break and come back to it later. If it’s a small mistake, you can try to fix it with a little more cutting. If it’s a bigger mistake, you may need to go to a salon to have it fixed.

How often should I cut my own hair?

It’s up to you how often you cut your own hair. Some people prefer to do it every few months, while others do it more frequently. Just be sure to keep your hair healthy and avoid cutting too much off at once.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Once you have finished cutting your own hair, it’s important to take care of it. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep it healthy and moisturized. You can also use styling products to add volume or texture to your new bob. And remember, practice makes perfect. The more you cut your own hair, the better you will get.
