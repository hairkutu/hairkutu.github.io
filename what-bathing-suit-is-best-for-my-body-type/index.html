What Bathing Suit Is Best For My Body Type

Written by Robby Sep 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Summer is here, and it's time to hit the beach or lounge by the pool. But before you do, you need to find the perfect bathing suit for your body type. With so many styles and cuts out there, it can be overwhelming to know which one will flatter your figure the most. Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll break down the best bathing suit options for every body type.

Table of Contents

  • Hourglass Body Type
  • Apple Body Type
  • Pear Body Type
  • Athletic Body Type

Hourglass Body Type

If you have an hourglass body type, your bust and hips are roughly the same size, with a smaller waist. To flatter your curves, look for bathing suits that accentuate your waist, such as high-waisted bottoms or belted one-pieces. Halter tops and underwire cups will also provide support and enhance your bust.

Tips:

  • Choose bold prints and bright colors to show off your curves.
  • Avoid overly skimpy bikinis that can make you look disproportionate.
  • Opt for thicker straps for more support.

Apple Body Type

If you have an apple body type, you carry most of your weight in your midsection, with a larger bust and narrower hips. To create a balanced look, choose bathing suits that draw attention away from your midsection and towards your bust and legs. Look for one-piece suits with ruching or shirring around the waistline, or bikinis with high-waisted bottoms.

Tips:

  • Choose dark-colored tops and light-colored bottoms to create the illusion of a smaller waist.
  • Avoid horizontal stripes or patterns around the midsection.
  • Opt for bra-sized swimwear for more support.

Pear Body Type

If you have a pear body type, your hips are wider than your bust, with a smaller waist. To balance out your proportions, look for bathing suits that enhance your bust and draw attention upwards. Halter tops, bandeau tops, and push-up cups are all great options. For bottoms, choose high-cut styles that elongate your legs.

Tips:

  • Choose bright-colored or patterned tops to draw attention upwards.
  • Avoid boyshorts or high-waisted bottoms that can emphasize your hips.
  • Opt for bottoms with a moderate coverage to balance out your figure.

Athletic Body Type

If you have an athletic body type, you have a straighter figure with less curves. To add more shape and definition, look for bathing suits that create the illusion of curves. Bikinis with ruffles, frills, or bows can add volume to your bust and hips. One-piece suits with cutouts or strategic color blocking can also create the appearance of more curves.

Tips:

  • Choose bright colors and bold prints to add dimension to your figure.
  • Avoid bandeau tops or straight-across necklines that can flatten your chest.
  • Opt for bikini bottoms with side ties or embellishments to create the illusion of wider hips.

FAQ

Q. What bathing suit is best for a plus-size body type?

A. Plus-size bodies come in all shapes and sizes, so it's important to look for bathing suits that flatter your individual figure. However, some general tips include choosing suits with built-in tummy control, opting for darker colors, and choosing one-piece suits with ruching or shirring around the waist.

Q. Can I wear a bikini if I have a larger midsection?

A. Yes, of course! Just look for high-waisted bottoms or bikinis with a higher rise to provide more coverage and support for your midsection. You can also choose bikini tops with underwire cups or halter necklines to enhance your bust.

Hair Care

Don't forget to take care of your hair while you're out in the sun! Saltwater, chlorine, and UV rays can all damage your locks. To protect your hair, wear a hat or scarf, use a leave-in conditioner or UV protectant spray, and rinse your hair with fresh water after swimming.

Now that you know what bathing suit is best for your body type, it's time to hit the beach with confidence! Remember, the most important thing is to feel comfortable and confident in your own skin.
