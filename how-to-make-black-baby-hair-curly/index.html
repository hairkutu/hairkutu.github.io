How To Make Black Baby Hair Curly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank May 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a parent of a black baby, you may be wondering how to make their hair curly. It's a common question, and luckily, there are several ways to achieve this look. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you make your black baby's hair curly.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Make Black Baby Hair Curly
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Making Black Baby Hair Curly
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Black Baby Hair

How to Make Black Baby Hair Curly

There are several ways to make black baby hair curly. Here are some of the most popular methods:

  • Twist outs: This involves twisting small sections of hair and leaving them in overnight. The next day, you can unravel the twists to reveal curly hair.
  • Bantu knots: This involves sectioning the hair and twisting each section into a small knot. Leave the knots in overnight and unravel them in the morning.
  • Finger coils: This involves twirling small sections of hair around your finger to create coils. You can do this all over the head or just in certain areas.
  • Rod sets: This involves wrapping small sections of hair around perm rods and leaving them in overnight. The next day, you can remove the rods to reveal curly hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make black baby hair curly:

  1. Start with clean, detangled hair. You can use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to clean the hair, and a wide-tooth comb to detangle it.
  2. Choose a method for making the hair curly (twist outs, Bantu knots, finger coils, or rod sets).
  3. Section the hair into small sections.
  4. Apply a styling product to each section of hair. You can use a curl cream or gel to help define the curls.
  5. Take each section of hair and twist it, knot it, coil it, or wrap it around a perm rod, depending on the method you chose.
  6. Leave the twists, knots, coils, or rods in overnight.
  7. In the morning, unravel the twists, knots, coils, or rods to reveal curly hair.
  8. Use your fingers to separate the curls and fluff them up if needed.
  9. Style the hair as desired.

Tips for Making Black Baby Hair Curly

Here are some tips to help you make your black baby's hair curly:

  • Use a styling product that works well for your baby's hair type. Some products may work better for coarse hair, while others may work better for fine hair.
  • Experiment with different methods to find what works best for your baby's hair.
  • Be gentle when detangling the hair to avoid breakage.
  • Protect the hair at night by wrapping it in a satin bonnet or using a satin pillowcase.
  • Avoid using heat on the hair, as this can cause damage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about making black baby hair curly:

How often should I make my baby's hair curly?

You can make your baby's hair curly as often as you'd like, but it's important not to over-manipulate the hair, as this can cause damage.

What products should I use to make my baby's hair curly?

You can use curl creams, gels, or mousses to help define the curls. Look for products that are specifically formulated for curly hair.

Can I use heat to make my baby's hair curly?

We do not recommend using heat on a baby's hair, as it can cause damage.

Hair Care for Curly Black Baby Hair

Here are some tips for caring for curly black baby hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to clean and condition the hair.
  • Detangle the hair gently with a wide-tooth comb.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help keep the hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat on the hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Protect the hair at night by wrapping it in a satin bonnet or using a satin pillowcase.

Now you know how to make black baby hair curly! With these tips and tricks, you can help your little one rock some beautiful curls.
