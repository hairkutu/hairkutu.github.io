What To Eat When You Lose Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Apr 13, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss and wondering what you can do to prevent it? The answer may be in your diet. Eating a balanced and nutritious diet is important for overall health, but it can also have a significant impact on the health of your hair.

Table of Contents

  • Why Diet Matters for Hair Loss
  • Foods to Eat for Healthy Hair
  • Foods to Avoid for Healthy Hair
  • Supplements for Healthy Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair
  • FAQs About Diet and Hair Loss

Why Diet Matters for Hair Loss

Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormones, stress, and illness. However, diet can also play a significant role in the health of your hair. Your hair is made up of protein, so it’s important to consume enough protein in your diet to support healthy hair growth. Additionally, certain vitamins and minerals, such as biotin, vitamin D, and iron, are essential for healthy hair.

Protein

Protein is essential for healthy hair growth, as hair is made up of a protein called keratin. Good sources of protein include:

  • Meat
  • Fish
  • Eggs
  • Beans
  • Nuts and seeds

Biotin

Biotin, also known as vitamin H, is a B vitamin that is essential for healthy hair growth. Good sources of biotin include:

  • Eggs
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Whole grains
  • Leafy greens

Vitamin D

Vitamin D is important for healthy hair growth, as it helps to regulate the growth and cycling of hair follicles. Good sources of vitamin D include:

  • Fatty fish
  • Egg yolks
  • Mushrooms

Iron

Iron is important for healthy hair growth, as it helps to deliver oxygen to hair follicles. Good sources of iron include:

  • Meat
  • Beans
  • Leafy greens
  • Tofu

Foods to Eat for Healthy Hair

Now that you know why diet matters for hair loss, let’s look at some specific foods that you can eat to promote healthy hair growth.

Eggs

Eggs are a good source of protein, biotin, and vitamin D, all of which are important for healthy hair growth. Additionally, the yolks contain selenium, which is important for scalp health.

Fatty Fish

Fatty fish, such as salmon and mackerel, are a good source of protein and vitamin D. They also contain omega-3 fatty acids, which are important for scalp health and can help to reduce inflammation.

Nuts and Seeds

Nuts and seeds, such as almonds and sunflower seeds, are a good source of protein, biotin, and vitamin E, which is important for scalp health.

Leafy Greens

Leafy greens, such as spinach and kale, are a good source of iron and vitamin C, which can help to promote healthy hair growth.

Foods to Avoid for Healthy Hair

Just as there are foods that can promote healthy hair growth, there are also foods that can contribute to hair loss. Here are some foods to avoid if you want to maintain healthy hair:

Sugar

Sugar can contribute to inflammation, which can damage hair follicles and contribute to hair loss.

Processed Foods

Processed foods are often high in sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats, all of which can contribute to inflammation and hair loss.

Alcohol

Alcohol can contribute to dehydration, which can lead to dry and brittle hair.

Supplements for Healthy Hair

In addition to getting the right nutrients from your diet, you may also want to consider taking supplements to promote healthy hair growth. Here are some supplements that may be beneficial:

Biotin

Biotin supplements can help to promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss.

Vitamin D

Vitamin D supplements can help to regulate the growth and cycling of hair follicles.

Iron

Iron supplements can help to prevent anemia, which can contribute to hair loss.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

In addition to eating a healthy and balanced diet, there are other things you can do to promote healthy hair growth:

  • Avoid harsh chemical treatments, such as perms and relaxers
  • Avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids and weaves, which can cause hair breakage
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, too frequently
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind
  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner

FAQs About Diet and Hair Loss

Q: Can eating a healthy diet cure hair loss?

A: While a healthy diet can certainly help to promote healthy hair growth, it may not be enough to cure hair loss completely. If you are experiencing hair loss, it’s important to see a healthcare professional to determine the underlying cause and appropriate treatment.

Q: Can certain foods cause hair loss?

A: While there is no one food that is directly responsible for hair loss, a diet that is high in sugar, processed foods, and unhealthy fats can contribute to inflammation and hair loss.

Q: What are some signs of nutrient deficiencies that can contribute to hair loss?

A: Signs of nutrient deficiencies that can contribute to hair loss may include fatigue, weakness, brittle nails, and dry skin.

In conclusion, eating a healthy and balanced diet is important for overall health, including the health of your hair. Incorporate plenty of protein, biotin, vitamin D, and iron into your diet, and avoid foods that can contribute to inflammation and hair loss. Additionally, take good care of your hair by avoiding harsh chemical treatments, tight hairstyles, and frequent use of hot tools.
