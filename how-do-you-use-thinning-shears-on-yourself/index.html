How To Use Thinning Shears On Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Thinning shears are a handy tool for those who want to add texture and volume to their hair without losing too much length. However, using thinning shears on yourself can be a bit tricky, especially if you're new to the process. In this article, we'll show you how to use thinning shears on yourself in a few easy steps, along with some tips and tricks to help you get the best results possible.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Yourself

Thinning shears are scissors with blades that have teeth or notches on them, which allow them to remove some hair while leaving some behind. They're a great tool for creating layers, reducing bulk, and adding texture to your hair. However, it's important to use them properly to avoid damaging your hair or creating an uneven look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to use thinning shears on yourself:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. It's important to work with clean, dry hair to get the best results.
  2. Section your hair into small sections, using clips to hold each section in place. This will make it easier to work with your hair and ensure that you don't miss any spots.
  3. Hold the thinning shears vertically and make a small cut in the middle of the section, about 1-2 inches away from your scalp. This will create a "guide" for you to follow.
  4. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up from your head. Hold the thinning shears horizontally and make small, quick snips along the section of hair. Be sure to keep the shears at an angle to avoid creating a blunt cut.
  5. Continue working through each section of hair, making sure to take small sections and work through them slowly and carefully. It's better to take your time and work through your hair in small sections than to rush and end up with an uneven look.
  6. When you're finished, use a comb to check for any uneven spots or areas that may need more attention. You can always go back and make additional cuts if needed.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Yourself

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when using thinning shears on yourself:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work slowly. It's better to take your time and work through your hair in small sections than to rush and end up with an uneven look.
  • Be sure to keep the shears at an angle to avoid creating a blunt cut. This will help you to create a more natural-looking texture.
  • Use a mirror to help you see what you're doing. It can be hard to see the back of your head, so use a mirror to make sure you're getting the results you want.
  • Don't use thinning shears on wet hair. Wet hair is more vulnerable to damage, so it's best to work with dry hair to avoid causing any harm.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some commonly asked questions about using thinning shears:

  • Can you use thinning shears on any hair type? Yes, thinning shears can be used on any hair type, but they work best on thick, coarse hair.
  • How often should you use thinning shears? It's best to use thinning shears every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking its best.
  • Can you use thinning shears to create bangs? Yes, thinning shears can be used to create bangs, but it's important to work slowly and carefully to avoid cutting too much hair.

Hair Care After Using Thinning Shears

After using thinning shears on your hair, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking its best.

With these tips and tricks, you can use thinning shears on yourself with confidence and create a beautiful, textured look that will make you feel amazing.
