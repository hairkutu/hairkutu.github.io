How To Sleep With Medium Hair Guys A Guide For A Good Night s Rest

Written by Robby Mar 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're sleeping beside a guy with medium-length hair, you might find yourself struggling to get a good night's rest. His hair can get in the way, causing discomfort and even pain. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll teach you how to sleep with medium hair guys and get the restful sleep you deserve.

Description

Medium hair guys are becoming more and more popular these days. They have hair that falls between short and long, usually hitting the shoulders or the nape of the neck. While they might look stylish during the day, their hair can be a nuisance when it's time to sleep. Their locks can tangle, get in the way, and even pull your hair, leading to a restless night. However, with the right techniques and tools, you can sleep peacefully beside your medium-haired partner.

How to Sleep with Medium Hair Guys

Step 1: Use a Silk Pillowcase

A silk pillowcase can do wonders for your hair, whether it's long or short. It reduces friction, preventing hair breakage and tangles, and it also helps retain moisture. For medium hair guys, a silk pillowcase can keep their locks smooth and shiny, reducing the chances of hair getting tangled with yours.

Step 2: Tie His Hair Up

If your partner's hair is long enough to tie up, ask him to do so before sleeping. He can use an elastic band or a scrunchie to tie his hair in a loose bun or ponytail. This will keep his hair away from your face and neck, reducing the chances of discomfort and pain.

Step 3: Create a Pillow Barrier

You can also create a barrier between you and your partner's hair by using an extra pillow. Simply place the pillow between your heads, and let your partner's hair fall on their side of the pillow. This will prevent his hair from getting in your face or neck, and you can sleep comfortably.

Tips for Sleeping with Medium Hair Guys

Here are some more tips that can help you sleep peacefully beside your medium-haired partner:

  • Ask him to brush his hair before sleeping. This will remove any tangles and knots that can cause discomfort.
  • Consider sleeping in different positions. If you usually sleep facing each other, try sleeping back-to-back or in opposite directions.
  • Invest in noise-cancelling headphones or earplugs. This can block out any rustling or movement from your partner's hair.
  • Communicate with your partner. Let him know if his hair is causing you discomfort, and work together to find a solution.

FAQs

Q: Can't I just ask him to cut his hair?

A: While this might seem like the easiest solution, it's not always the best. Hair is a personal choice, and your partner might not want to cut it. Plus, medium hair is a versatile length that can be styled in different ways. Instead of asking him to cut his hair, try finding ways to sleep comfortably beside him.

Q: What if my partner moves around a lot in his sleep?

A: If your partner moves around a lot, it can be challenging to keep his hair away from you. In this case, you can try using a body pillow or a bolster. This will create a barrier between you and your partner, reducing the chances of his hair getting in your face or neck.

Hair Care for Medium Hair Guys

Finally, here are some hair care tips that your medium-haired partner can follow:

  • Wash his hair regularly, but not too often. Over-washing can strip the hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and damage.
  • Use a conditioner to keep his hair soft and moisturized. Leave the conditioner on for a few minutes before rinsing it off.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often. These can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Trim his hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

By following these tips, your partner can maintain healthy and beautiful medium-length hair, while you can sleep peacefully beside him.

Conclusion

Sleeping beside a guy with medium hair can be a challenge, but it's not impossible. By using the right techniques and tools, you can sleep comfortably beside your partner and wake up feeling refreshed. Remember to communicate with your partner, invest in a silk pillowcase, and create a pillow barrier. With these tips, you can get a good night's rest, no matter your partner's hair length.
