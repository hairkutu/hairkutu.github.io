Why Do Dogs Have Patches Of Hair Missing

Written by Aprilia Jun 14, 2023 Â· 11 min read

If you're a dog owner, you might have noticed that your furry friend has some patches of hair missing. This can be a cause for concern, and you might wonder why this is happening. In this article, we'll explore some of the reasons why dogs have patches of hair missing.

Table of Content

  • Medical Reasons
  • Behavioral Reasons
  • Parasites
  • Allergies
  • Injury or Trauma

Medical Reasons

One of the main reasons why dogs have patches of hair missing is due to a medical condition. Certain illnesses or infections can cause hair loss in dogs. Some of the medical reasons why dogs have patches of hair missing include:

Hypothyroidism

Hypothyroidism is a condition in which the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough hormones. This can cause hair loss in dogs, among other symptoms. If you suspect that your dog has hypothyroidism, you should take them to the vet for a diagnosis.

Cushing's Disease

Cushing's Disease is a condition in which the body produces too much cortisol. This can cause hair loss in dogs, along with other symptoms such as increased thirst and appetite. If you suspect that your dog has Cushing's Disease, you should take them to the vet for a diagnosis.

Ringworm

Ringworm is a fungal infection that can cause hair loss in dogs. It's highly contagious, so if you suspect that your dog has ringworm, you should take them to the vet right away to prevent it from spreading to other pets or humans in your household.

Behavioral Reasons

Another reason why dogs have patches of hair missing is due to behavioral issues. Dogs can develop compulsive behaviors such as excessive licking or chewing of their fur, which can lead to hair loss. Some of the behavioral reasons why dogs have patches of hair missing include:

Anxiety or Stress

Dogs can develop anxiety or stress due to a variety of reasons such as separation anxiety, fear of loud noises, or changes in their environment. This can cause them to engage in compulsive behaviors such as excessive licking or chewing of their fur, which can lead to hair loss.

Boredom or Lack of Exercise

Dogs that are bored or don't get enough exercise can also develop compulsive behaviors such as excessive licking or chewing of their fur. This can lead to hair loss in certain areas of their body.

Parasites

Another reason why dogs have patches of hair missing is due to parasites. Parasites such as fleas, ticks, and mites can cause hair loss in dogs. Some of the parasites that can cause hair loss in dogs include:

Fleas

Fleas are a common parasite that can cause hair loss in dogs. They bite and feed on the dog's blood, which can cause itching and irritation. Dogs that are allergic to flea bites can develop a condition called flea allergy dermatitis, which can cause hair loss.

Ticks

Ticks are another common parasite that can cause hair loss in dogs. They can transmit diseases such as Lyme Disease, which can cause hair loss in certain areas of the dog's body.

Allergies

Dogs can also develop allergies that can cause hair loss. Some of the allergies that can cause hair loss in dogs include:

Food Allergies

Dogs can develop allergies to certain foods, which can cause hair loss and other symptoms such as itching and ear infections. If you suspect that your dog has a food allergy, you should take them to the vet for a diagnosis.

Environmental Allergies

Dogs can also develop allergies to environmental factors such as pollen, dust, and mold. This can cause hair loss, along with other symptoms such as itching and sneezing.

Injury or Trauma

Dogs can also develop patches of hair missing due to injury or trauma. This can be caused by a variety of factors such as bites, scratches, or burns. Some of the injuries or traumas that can cause hair loss in dogs include:

Bites or Scratches

If your dog gets into a fight with another animal, they can develop bites or scratches that can cause hair loss in certain areas of their body.

Burns

If your dog comes into contact with a hot surface or substance, they can develop burns that can cause hair loss in the affected area.

Hair Care for Dogs

If your dog has patches of hair missing, it's important to take them to the vet for a diagnosis. Depending on the cause of the hair loss, your vet may recommend a treatment plan that can help your dog grow their hair back. In addition to medical treatment, there are some things you can do to help your dog's hair grow back:

Regular Brushing

Regular brushing can help improve your dog's coat health and stimulate hair growth. You should brush your dog's coat at least once a week, or more frequently if they have a long or thick coat.

Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can also help improve your dog's coat health and promote hair growth. Make sure your dog is getting a balanced and nutritious diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals.

Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your dog's skin or coat, as this can cause further irritation and hair loss. Stick to gentle and natural products that are specifically designed for dogs.

FAQs

Can stress cause hair loss in dogs?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss in dogs. Dogs that are stressed or anxious can develop compulsive behaviors such as excessive licking or chewing of their fur, which can lead to hair loss.

Can fleas cause hair loss in dogs?

Yes, fleas can cause hair loss in dogs. They bite and feed on the dog's blood, which can cause itching and irritation. Dogs that are allergic to flea bites can develop a condition called flea allergy dermatitis, which can cause hair loss.

Can a dog's diet affect their hair growth?

Yes, a dog's diet can affect their hair growth. A healthy and balanced diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals can help promote hair growth and improve coat health.

Conclusion

There are many reasons why dogs have patches of hair missing, including medical conditions, behavioral issues, parasites, allergies, and injury or trauma. If your dog has patches of hair missing, it's important to take them to the vet for a diagnosis. Depending on the cause of the hair loss, your vet may recommend a treatment plan that can help your dog grow their hair back. In addition to medical treatment, there are some things you can do to help improve your dog's coat health and promote hair growth, such as regular brushing and a healthy diet.
