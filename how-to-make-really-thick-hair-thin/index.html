How To Make Really Thick Hair Thin A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Mar 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Do you have thick hair that feels heavy and unmanageable? Do you want to know how to make your hair thinner without resorting to drastic measures like cutting it all off? In this article, we'll discuss some tips and tricks for making really thick hair thin, so you can have the hair you've always wanted.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Make Really Thick Hair Thin
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Managing Thick Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thick Hair

Description

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can be beautiful and luxurious, it can also be heavy and difficult to manage. Thick hair can feel like a burden, especially during the hot summer months. If you want to make your hair thinner, there are several techniques you can try.

How to Make Really Thick Hair Thin

There are several ways to make really thick hair thin, including:

  • Thinning Shears
  • Layering
  • Straightening
  • Chemical Treatments

Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth on one blade. When you cut with thinning shears, they remove some of the hair, leaving behind a thinner layer. This is a great option for those who want to keep their length but reduce the thickness of their hair. It's important to have a professional use thinning shears on your hair to avoid any mishaps.

Layering

Layering involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create texture and reduce volume. This technique can be done on any hair length, and it's a great way to make thick hair appear thinner. The layers will remove some of the weight of the hair, making it easier to manage.

Straightening

Straightening hair can also make it appear thinner. When hair is straightened, it lays flat against the head, reducing volume. You can use a flat iron to straighten your hair, or you can get a chemical straightening treatment.

Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments like relaxers and keratin treatments can also make thick hair appear thinner. These treatments break down the protein bonds in the hair, making it easier to manage. However, it's important to note that these treatments can be damaging to the hair if not done properly, so it's important to have them done by a professional.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make really thick hair thin:

  1. Consult with a professional stylist to determine the best technique for your hair type and desired look.
  2. If using thinning shears, make sure a professional stylist uses them on your hair to avoid any mishaps.
  3. If layering, allow the stylist to cut your hair at different lengths to create texture and reduce volume.
  4. If straightening, use a flat iron or get a chemical straightening treatment.
  5. If getting a chemical treatment, make sure it's done by a professional to avoid damaging your hair.

Tips for Managing Thick Hair

Here are some tips for managing thick hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a lightweight conditioner to avoid adding more weight to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, as this can cause breakage.

FAQs

Q: Will cutting my hair make it thinner?

A: Cutting your hair can remove some of the weight, making it appear thinner. However, it's important to consult with a professional stylist to determine the best technique for your hair type and desired look.

Q: Can I make my hair thinner at home?

A: It's not recommended to try to make your hair thinner at home, as this can lead to uneven results and damage to your hair. It's best to consult with a professional stylist.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Here are some tips for caring for thick hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and flat irons too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hair elastics that can cause breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can make your really thick hair thin and manageable. Consult with a professional stylist to determine the best technique for your hair type and desired look, and enjoy your new, thinner hair.
