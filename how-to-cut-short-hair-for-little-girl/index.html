How To Cut Short Hair For Little Girl Tips Steps And Faq

Written by Luffy Jul 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have a little girl with short hair, you may be wondering how to give her a fresh haircut without spending a lot of money at the salon. Cutting your child's hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it can also be intimidating if you don't know where to start. In this article, we'll provide you with tips, step-by-step instructions, and answers to frequently asked questions about how to cut short hair for little girls.

  • How to Cut Short Hair for Little Girl
  • Step by Step
  • FAQ

Tips

Before you start cutting your little girl's hair, there are a few things you should keep in mind:

  • Choose the right tools: You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off the hair.
  • Start with dry hair: It's easier to see the shape of the haircut when the hair is dry.
  • Don't cut too much at once: Cut a little bit at a time, and step back frequently to check the overall shape.
  • Be patient: Cutting hair takes time and practice, so don't get discouraged if it doesn't turn out perfectly the first time.

How to Cut Short Hair for Little Girl

The first step in cutting short hair for a little girl is to decide on the overall shape you want to achieve. Some popular styles for little girls include a bob, a pixie cut, or a layered haircut. Once you've chosen your desired style, you can start cutting the hair.

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Start by washing your child's hair and drying it thoroughly. Comb out any tangles or knots, and section off the hair into manageable sections using hair clips.

Step 2: Cut the Sides

Begin by cutting the sides of the hair. Hold the hair at a 45-degree angle away from the head, and cut in a straight line to the desired length. Repeat on the other side, and check that both sides are even.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Next, move on to the back of the head. Hold the hair straight up, and cut in a straight line to the desired length. Use the previous sections as a guide to ensure that the back is even.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Finally, cut the top of the hair. Hold the hair at a 90-degree angle away from the head, and cut in a straight line to the desired length. Blend the top with the sides and the back by cutting at a slight angle.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting short hair for a little girl:

  1. Wash and dry the hair
  2. Section off the hair
  3. Cut the sides
  4. Cut the back
  5. Cut the top
  6. Blend the hair
  7. Check for evenness
  8. Trim any uneven bits
  9. Style the hair
  10. Clean up any loose hairs

FAQ

Here are some common questions about cutting short hair for little girls:

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on the style you choose and how quickly your child's hair grows. As a general rule, you should aim to trim the hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! If you make a mistake, you can always trim the hair a little shorter to even it out. If you're really unhappy with the result, you can always take your child to a professional stylist to fix it.

What if my child won't sit still?

Try distracting your child with a favorite toy or book, or consider cutting the hair in small sections over a few days. You can also try cutting the hair while your child is sleeping.

Hair Care

After cutting your child's hair, you'll want to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle the hair
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to help prevent tangles
  • Trim the hair regularly to maintain the shape

Cutting short hair for a little girl can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools, techniques, and a little bit of patience, you can achieve a great-looking haircut for your child. If you're unsure about cutting the hair yourself, consider taking your child to a professional stylist for a consultation.
