What To Do If You Cut Your Bangs Too Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you've ever cut your own bangs, you know how easy it is to make a mistake. Whether you were trying to save some money or just feeling adventurous, cutting your own bangs can be a risky move. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll go over what to do if you cut your bangs too short.

Description

Cutting your own bangs can be a fun and creative way to change up your hairstyle. But if you're not careful, you can end up with bangs that are too short or uneven. This can be a frustrating and embarrassing situation, but there are steps you can take to fix it.

How To Fix Your Bangs If You Cut Them Too Short

Step 1: Don't Panic

The first thing to do if you cut your bangs too short is to take a deep breath and try not to panic. It's easy to feel like your hair is ruined, but remember that hair grows back and there are ways to fix it.

Step 2: Assess the Damage

Take a close look at your bangs in the mirror and assess the damage. Are they too short all over or just in certain areas? Are they uneven or choppy? This will help you determine the best course of action.

Step 3: Style Your Bangs

If your bangs are just slightly too short, you may be able to style them in a way that hides the mistake. Try using a round brush and blow dryer to smooth them out and add some volume. You can also try using bobby pins or hair clips to pin them back or to the side.

Step 4: Trim Your Bangs

If your bangs are still too short or uneven, you may need to trim them. But be careful not to make the problem worse. Use sharp scissors and only cut a little bit at a time. Start by trimming the longest pieces and working your way up. It's better to take your time and be cautious than to rush and make things worse.

Step 5: Get Professional Help

If you're still not happy with your bangs or you're afraid to trim them yourself, it's time to seek professional help. A hairstylist can help fix your bangs and give you the look you want. They may also be able to suggest other hairstyles that work well with shorter bangs.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs

If you're still determined to cut your own bangs, here are some tips to help you avoid making the same mistake:

  • Use sharp scissors
  • Start with small snips
  • Don't cut straight across, angle the scissors
  • Take breaks and step back to assess your progress
  • Watch tutorials or ask for advice from a professional

FAQ

Can I Fix My Bangs If I Cut Them Too Short?

Yes, you can fix your bangs if you cut them too short. You can try styling them in a way that hides the mistake, trimming them yourself, or seeking professional help.

How Long Will It Take for My Bangs to Grow Back?

It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for your bangs to grow back, depending on how short they are and how fast your hair grows. In the meantime, try using hair clips or headbands to help hide the mistake.

Hair Care

After you've fixed your bangs, it's important to take care of your hair to promote healthy growth. This includes regular shampooing and conditioning, using a heat protectant when styling, and avoiding harsh chemicals and treatments that can damage your hair.

Cutting your own bangs can be a fun and daring move, but it can also be risky. If you do make a mistake, don't panic. There are steps you can take to fix it and get the look you want. Just remember to be patient and take your time, and if all else fails, seek professional help.
