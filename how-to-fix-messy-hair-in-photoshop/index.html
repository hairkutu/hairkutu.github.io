How To Fix Messy Hair In Photoshop A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 30, 2023 · 8 min read

If you've ever taken a photo and noticed that your hair looks messy, don't worry! With Photoshop, you can easily fix this problem in just a few simple steps. In this article, we'll show you how to fix messy hair in Photoshop and give you some tips on how to take care of your hair to avoid this issue in the future.

How to Fix Messy Hair in Photoshop

If you're ready to fix your messy hair in Photoshop, follow these steps:

Step 1: Open your photo in Photoshop

The first thing you need to do is open your photo in Photoshop. You can do this by going to File > Open and selecting the photo you want to edit.

Step 2: Duplicate the layer

Next, you need to duplicate the layer. You can do this by right-clicking on the layer and selecting Duplicate Layer. This will create a new layer that you can work on without affecting the original photo.

Step 3: Select the Clone Stamp tool

Now it's time to select the Clone Stamp tool. This tool allows you to copy pixels from one part of the photo and apply them to another part. You can find the Clone Stamp tool in the toolbar on the left-hand side of the screen.

Step 4: Adjust the brush size

Before you start using the Clone Stamp tool, you need to adjust the brush size. You can do this by using the bracket keys ([ and ]) on your keyboard or by clicking on the Brush Size option in the toolbar at the top of the screen.

Step 5: Alt-click to select a source area

Now it's time to select a source area. This is the part of the photo that you want to copy pixels from. To do this, hold down the Alt key and click on the area you want to use as the source.

Step 6: Paint over the messy hair

Once you've selected a source area, you can start painting over the messy hair. Make sure you're using a small brush size and that you're painting in the direction of the hair. You may need to use the Clone Stamp tool multiple times to get the desired result.

Step 7: Use the Healing Brush tool

If the Clone Stamp tool isn't working well for you, try using the Healing Brush tool instead. This tool allows you to blend pixels from one area of the photo with pixels from another area. You can find the Healing Brush tool in the toolbar on the left-hand side of the screen.

Step 8: Zoom in and check your work

Once you've finished fixing the messy hair, zoom in and check your work. Make sure there aren't any obvious areas where you've copied pixels or blended pixels. If there are, use the Clone Stamp tool or Healing Brush tool to fix them.

Step 9: Save your edited photo

Finally, save your edited photo. You can do this by going to File > Save or File > Save As.

Tips for Avoiding Messy Hair in Photos

While it's easy to fix messy hair in Photoshop, it's even better to avoid the problem altogether. Here are some tips for avoiding messy hair in photos:

  • Use hair products to keep your hair in place
  • Brush and style your hair before taking photos
  • Avoid windy or rainy conditions that can mess up your hair
  • Wear a hat or headband to keep your hair in place

FAQ about Fixing Messy Hair in Photoshop

Can I fix messy hair in Photoshop Elements?

Yes, you can fix messy hair in Photoshop Elements using the Clone Stamp tool or Healing Brush tool.

Is it better to use the Clone Stamp tool or Healing Brush tool?

It depends on the photo and your personal preference. Try both tools and use the one that works best for you.

Can I fix messy hair in Lightroom?

No, Lightroom doesn't have the same editing capabilities as Photoshop. You'll need to use Photoshop to fix messy hair in your photos.

Hair Care to Avoid Messy Hair

Proper hair care is essential to avoid messy hair. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair looking neat
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth and strength

By following these tips, you can avoid messy hair in your photos and keep your hair looking great in real life.

Now that you know how to fix messy hair in Photoshop, you can edit your photos with confidence. Whether you're taking selfies or professional photos, you can always look your best with a little bit of editing magic. Happy editing!
