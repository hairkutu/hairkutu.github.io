Why Do Cats Lose Hair

Written by Daniel Jul 07, 2023 · 6 min read

As a cat owner, it can be alarming to see your furry friend shedding excessively. While some amount of shedding is normal for cats, excessive hair loss can be a sign of an underlying health issue. In this article, we will discuss the reasons why cats lose hair and offer tips for managing the issue.

Causes of Hair Loss in Cats

There are several reasons why cats may lose hair. Some of the most common causes include:

Parasites

External parasites, such as fleas and ticks, can cause cats to lose hair. These pests irritate the skin, causing the cat to scratch and bite at the affected area. This can lead to hair loss, as well as infection.

Allergies

Cats can be allergic to a variety of things, including food, pollen, and dust. When a cat is exposed to an allergen, their immune system overreacts, causing inflammation and itching. This can lead to excessive grooming, which can in turn cause hair loss.

Hormonal Imbalances

Hormonal imbalances can also cause hair loss in cats. This is particularly common in older cats, as their hormone levels naturally decline with age. Hormonal imbalances can be caused by a variety of factors, including thyroid disease and Cushing's disease.

Infections

Infections, such as ringworm, can cause cats to lose hair. Ringworm is a fungal infection that can cause circular patches of hair loss. Other infections, such as bacterial infections, can also lead to hair loss.

How to Manage Hair Loss in Cats

If you suspect that your cat is losing hair due to an underlying health issue, it is important to take them to a veterinarian for a checkup. Your vet can help diagnose the issue and recommend a treatment plan.

In addition to seeking veterinary care, there are several things you can do to manage hair loss in cats:

Groom Your Cat Regularly

Grooming your cat regularly can help remove loose hair and prevent matting. This can also help reduce the amount of hair your cat sheds on a daily basis.

Use Flea Prevention Products

If your cat is losing hair due to external parasites, it is important to use flea prevention products to eliminate the pests. Your vet can recommend a safe and effective product for your cat.

Address Allergies

If your cat is allergic to something in their environment, it is important to address the issue. This may involve switching to a hypoallergenic cat food, using air filters to reduce allergens in the home, or taking other steps to address the issue.

FAQs About Hair Loss in Cats

Is it normal for cats to shed?

Yes, all cats shed to some degree. However, excessive shedding can be a sign of an underlying health issue.

What should I do if my cat is losing hair?

If your cat is losing hair, it is important to take them to a veterinarian for a checkup. Your vet can help diagnose the issue and recommend a treatment plan.

Can hair loss in cats be prevented?

While some causes of hair loss in cats are unavoidable, such as hormonal imbalances, there are things you can do to manage the issue. Regular grooming, flea prevention, and addressing allergies can all help reduce the amount of hair your cat loses on a daily basis.

Final Thoughts

Cats can lose hair for a variety of reasons, including parasites, allergies, hormonal imbalances, and infections. If you suspect that your cat is losing hair due to an underlying health issue, it is important to take them to a veterinarian for a checkup. By working with your vet and taking steps to manage the issue, you can help keep your cat healthy and happy.
