How To Style Hair To Keep Out Of Face A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of constantly pushing your hair out of your face? Whether you're trying to focus on work, hit the gym, or simply enjoy a day out without hair constantly getting in your eyes, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll show you how to style your hair to keep it out of your face, with tips for different hair types and lengths. Let's get started!

How to Style Hair to Keep Out of Face: Step by Step

Step 1: Start by washing your hair and conditioning it thoroughly. If you have curly or wavy hair, use a leave-in conditioner to make it easier to manage.

Step 2: Brush or comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots. You don't want any snags or bumps when you're trying to style it.

Step 3: Determine where you want your hair to be pulled back from your face. There are a few options here, depending on your personal preference:

  • Pull your hair straight back from your forehead.
  • Part your hair in the middle and pull back the sides.
  • Part your hair on one side and pull back the opposite side.

Step 4: Use a hair tie, clip, or headband to secure your hair in place. If you're using a hair tie, make sure it's not too tight, as this can cause headaches and even hair breakage over time.

Step 5: For added security, use bobby pins to hold any loose strands in place. You can also use hair spray to keep everything in place.

Tips for Different Hair Types and Lengths

Straight Hair

If you have straight hair, a high ponytail or bun is a great option for keeping hair out of your face. You can also try a half-up, half-down style, or a sleek low ponytail.

Curly Hair

Curly hair can be more challenging to style, as it tends to have a mind of its own. Try a messy bun, a loose braid, or a pineapple ponytail (where you gather your hair on top of your head, like a pineapple).

Short Hair

Short hair can be styled with bobby pins or hair clips to keep it back from your face. You can also try a headband or scarf for a more vintage look.

FAQ: How to Style Hair to Keep Out of Face

Q: Can I use gel or mousse to keep my hair back?

A: Yes, you can use styling products to help keep your hair in place. However, be careful not to use too much, as this can make your hair look greasy or weighed down.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people wash their hair every day, while others can go a few days without washing. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Q: How can I prevent hair breakage?

A: Avoid pulling your hair back too tightly or using hair ties with metal clasps. You can also use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction while you sleep.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to styling your hair to keep it out of your face, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to style your hair to keep it out of your face and look great doing it. Whether you're rocking a high ponytail, a messy bun, or a sleek low ponytail, you'll be able to focus on what matters without hair getting in your way. Happy styling!
