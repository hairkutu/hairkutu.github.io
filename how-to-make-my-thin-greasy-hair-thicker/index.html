How To Make My Thin Greasy Hair Thicker Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Jul 15, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have thin greasy hair, you may feel like there's not much you can do to make it look fuller and healthier. But don't worry – there are plenty of things you can do to boost the volume and thickness of your hair! In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for making your thin greasy hair look thicker and more voluminous.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make My Thin Greasy Hair Thicker
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Thicker, Fuller Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thin Greasy Hair

How to Make My Thin Greasy Hair Thicker

One of the most effective ways to make thin greasy hair look thicker is to use volumizing products. Look for shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that are specifically designed to add volume to fine hair. These products often contain ingredients like keratin and biotin, which can help strengthen hair and make it look thicker.

Another way to add volume to your hair is to blow-dry it upside down. This technique allows you to lift the roots of your hair and create volume at the crown. Use a round brush to create even more volume, and finish with a volumizing hairspray or mousse.

If you're looking for a more permanent solution, consider getting a haircut that adds layers to your hair. Layers can add texture and movement to your hair, making it look thicker and fuller. Talk to your stylist about the best haircut for your hair type and face shape.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to making your thin greasy hair look thicker:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel-dry your hair and apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots.
  3. Blow-dry your hair upside down using a round brush.
  4. When your hair is dry, use a straightening iron to add some texture and movement to your hair.
  5. Finish with a light hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Thicker, Fuller Hair

Here are some additional tips for making your thin greasy hair look thicker and more voluminous:

  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh your hair down.
  • Use a dry shampoo between washes to soak up excess oil and add volume.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it look flat and lifeless.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth and health.
  • Consider taking a biotin supplement to promote hair growth and thickness.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about making thin greasy hair look thicker:

Can I use a thickening spray on my greasy hair?

Yes, you can use a thickening spray on greasy hair. Look for a lightweight spray that won't weigh your hair down or make it look greasy.

Will using a volumizing shampoo make my hair greasier?

Not necessarily. Look for a volumizing shampoo that is specifically designed for oily hair, as these products are formulated to cleanse your hair without stripping it of its natural oils.

Can I use a hair mask to make my hair thicker?

Yes, using a hair mask can help strengthen and nourish your hair, which can make it look thicker and healthier over time. Look for a hair mask that is formulated for your hair type and apply it once a week for best results.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Greasy Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for managing thin greasy hair:

  • Wash your hair every other day to avoid over-washing it.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup from styling products and excess oil.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like curling irons and straighteners, as these can damage your hair and make it look thinner.
  • Consider using a scalp scrub to exfoliate your scalp and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners that can weigh your hair down.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your thin greasy hair look thicker and more voluminous. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, as it may take some time to see results.
