How To Curl Hair 50S Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking for a timeless and glamorous look, you can't go wrong with 50s-style curls. This iconic hairstyle is characterized by voluminous, bouncy curls that frame the face and add a touch of vintage sophistication to any outfit. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve this classic look, including tips for hair care and styling.

How to Curl Hair 50s Style

To achieve 50s-style curls, you'll need a few key tools and products. Here's what you'll need:

  • A curling iron with a barrel size of 1 inch or larger
  • Heat protection spray
  • Hairspray
  • Sectioning clips
  • A comb or brush

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you begin curling, it's important to prep your hair with a heat protection spray. This will help to prevent damage from the hot curling iron. Divide your hair into sections using sectioning clips.

Step 2: Curl Your Hair

Take a small section of hair and place it around the curling iron. Hold for a few seconds, then release. Repeat until all sections of your hair are curled.

Step 3: Brush Out Your Curls

After your curls have cooled, use a brush or comb to gently brush them out. This will create the signature bouncy, voluminous look of 50s-style curls.

Step 4: Finish with Hairspray

To help your curls hold their shape, finish by spraying your hair with hairspray. Use a light touch to avoid weighing down your hair.

Tips for Curling Hair 50s Style

  • For a more natural look, curl your hair away from your face.
  • Use a curling iron with a larger barrel size for looser, more relaxed curls.
  • If your hair is prone to frizz, use a smoothing serum before curling.
  • For longer-lasting curls, apply hairspray to each section of hair before curling.
  • Don't be afraid to play around with different curling techniques to find the look that works best for you.

FAQs

How long do 50s-style curls last?

The longevity of your curls will depend on a variety of factors, including your hair type and the products you use. In general, 50s-style curls can last anywhere from a few hours to a few days.

What hair types are best for 50s-style curls?

50s-style curls can work for a variety of hair types, including straight, wavy, and curly hair. However, if your hair is very fine or thin, you may find it difficult to achieve the volume and bounce of 50s-style curls.

What products should I use to maintain my 50s-style curls?

To maintain your curls, it's important to use products that will help them hold their shape. Look for a hairspray that provides a strong hold without weighing down your hair. You may also want to use a volumizing mousse or spray to add extra volume and body.

Hair Care for 50s-Style Curls

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, vibrant hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protection spray whenever you do use heat styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain healthy ends.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve stunning 50s-style curls that are sure to turn heads. Whether you're dressing up for a special occasion or just want to add a touch of vintage glamour to your everyday look, this classic hairstyle is a timeless choice that will never go out of style.
