How To Cut My Teenage Son s Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 24, 2023 · 6 min read

As a parent, you want to save money on haircuts for your teenage son. Cutting hair at home is a great way to do that. However, cutting hair is not as easy as it seems. It requires some skills and techniques to get the desired result. In this article, we will show you how to cut your teenage son's hair at home in a relaxed English language. So, let's start.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut My Teenage Son's Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Hair at Home
  • FAQs

How to Cut My Teenage Son's Hair at Home

Before we start cutting hair, we need to gather some tools. Here's what you need:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Cape or towel
  • Water spray bottle
  • Hair clips or rubber bands

Once you have gathered all the necessary tools, it's time to start cutting hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow the steps below to cut your teenage son's hair at home:

  1. Wet your son's hair thoroughly with the water spray bottle. This will make it easier to cut.
  2. Section the hair using hair clips or rubber bands. Start with the back of the head and work your way forward.
  3. Use the clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back of the head. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard for a more even cut.
  4. Use the scissors to cut the hair on the top of the head. Start by trimming the longer hair and work your way to the shorter hair.
  5. Blend the hair using the clippers or scissors. This will create a more natural look.
  6. Remove the hair clips or rubber bands and comb the hair to make sure it is even.
  7. Trim the hair around the ears and the neckline using the clippers or scissors.
  8. Clean up any loose hairs with the comb and scissors.
  9. Style the hair as desired using a hair gel or styling product.
  10. Clean up the area and wash the tools.

Tips for Cutting Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you cut your teenage son's hair at home:

  • Start with a longer guard on the clippers and work your way down to a shorter guard for a more even cut.
  • Use the comb to hold the hair in place while trimming with the scissors.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting hair is not a race.
  • Watch online tutorials to learn different techniques for cutting hair.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about cutting hair at home:

Q: Can I cut my teenage son's hair with regular scissors?

A: It's best to use hair cutting scissors, as they are sharper and more precise.

Q: How often should I cut my teenage son's hair?

A: It depends on how fast his hair grows. On average, every 4-6 weeks is a good time frame.

Q: Can I cut my teenage son's hair if he has curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut curly hair, but it requires some different techniques. Watch online tutorials to learn more.

Hair Care Tips

After cutting your teenage son's hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on the hair, such as blow dryers or straighteners.
  • Trim the hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth and health.

That's it! You now know how to cut your teenage son's hair at home. With practice, you'll become a pro at cutting hair and save money on haircuts in the long run.
