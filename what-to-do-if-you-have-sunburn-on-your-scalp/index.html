What To Do If You Have Sunburn On Your Scalp

Written by Petter Apr 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Summer is the time to enjoy the sunshine and have fun outdoors. However, spending too much time under the sun can lead to sunburn, which can be painful and irritating. Sunburn on your scalp can be particularly uncomfortable, especially if you have a lot of hair or a sensitive scalp. In this article, we will discuss what to do if you have sunburn on your scalp and how to take care of your hair while recovering.

How to Treat Sunburn on Your Scalp

If you have sunburn on your scalp, there are several things you can do to relieve the pain and promote healing.

1. Cool Compress

One of the easiest ways to relieve the pain of sunburn on your scalp is to apply a cool compress. This can be a damp cloth or a towel soaked in cool water. Apply the compress to the affected area for 15-20 minutes at a time, several times a day.

2. Aloe Vera Gel

Aloe vera gel is a natural remedy that can soothe sunburned skin. Apply a generous amount of aloe vera gel to your scalp and massage it in gently. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it off with cool water.

3. Pain Relief Medication

If your sunburn is particularly painful, you can take over-the-counter pain relief medication like ibuprofen or acetaminophen. Follow the instructions on the label and do not exceed the recommended dosage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to treat sunburn on your scalp:

Step 1: Cleanse Your Scalp

Before applying any remedies, it is important to cleanse your scalp to remove any dirt or debris. Use a gentle shampoo and rinse your scalp thoroughly with cool water.

Step 2: Apply Cool Compress

Take a damp cloth or towel soaked in cool water and apply it to the affected area. Leave it on for 15-20 minutes and repeat several times a day.

Step 3: Apply Aloe Vera Gel

Apply a generous amount of aloe vera gel to your scalp and massage it in gently. Leave it on for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it off with cool water.

Step 4: Take Pain Relief Medication

If your sunburn is particularly painful, you can take over-the-counter pain relief medication like ibuprofen or acetaminophen. Follow the instructions on the label and do not exceed the recommended dosage.

Tips for Preventing Sunburn on Your Scalp

Prevention is key when it comes to sunburn on your scalp. Here are some tips to help you prevent sunburn:

1. Wear a Hat

Wearing a hat can provide shade and protect your scalp from the sun's harmful UV rays. Choose a hat with a wide brim to provide maximum coverage.

2. Use Sunscreen

Apply sunscreen to your scalp before going outside. Choose a sunscreen with a high SPF and reapply every two hours.

3. Seek Shade

Avoid spending too much time in direct sunlight. Seek shade under trees, umbrellas, or canopies.

FAQs

Q: Can sunburn on my scalp cause hair loss?

A: Sunburn on your scalp is unlikely to cause hair loss. However, if you scratch or pick at the sunburned area, it can damage the hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

Q: How long does it take for sunburn on my scalp to heal?

A: The healing time for sunburn on your scalp can vary depending on the severity of the burn. Mild sunburn can heal within a few days, while severe sunburn can take up to two weeks to heal.

Hair Care Tips While Recovering from Sunburn on Your Scalp

While recovering from sunburn on your scalp, it is important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, curling irons, or straighteners. Heat can further damage your sunburned scalp and hair.

2. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo to cleanse your hair and scalp. Avoid shampoos with harsh chemicals or sulfates that can irritate your skin.

3. Condition Your Hair

Use a deep conditioning treatment to nourish your hair and scalp. Look for a conditioner with natural ingredients like coconut oil or shea butter.

4. Protect Your Scalp

Wear a hat or use an umbrella to protect your scalp from the sun while your skin is still healing.

By following these tips and remedies, you can relieve the pain of sunburn on your scalp and promote healing. Remember to take care of your hair while recovering to prevent further damage and enjoy the rest of the summer sunburn-free!
