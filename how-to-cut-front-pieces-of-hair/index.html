How To Cut Front Pieces Of Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby May 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to change up your hairstyle, cutting your front pieces of hair is a great way to do it. This technique can frame your face, add volume, and even make you look younger. However, cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you've never done it before. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut front pieces of hair like a pro, with simple steps and helpful tips.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Front Pieces of Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Front Pieces of Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Front Pieces

How to Cut Front Pieces of Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's start with the basics. When cutting front pieces of hair, you want to:

  • Create a natural-looking frame around your face
  • Ensure the length is even on both sides
  • Use sharp scissors and a comb to achieve a clean cut

Now that you know what to aim for, let's get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with dry hair that's styled the way you normally wear it. This will help you see how the front pieces of hair will look after you cut them.

Step 2: Part your hair in the middle or on the side, depending on your preference.

Step 3: Take a section of hair from the front, about an inch wide. This will be the first piece you cut.

Step 4: Comb the section of hair straight down, ensuring it's tangle-free.

Step 5: Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, about an inch away from the desired length. This will give you some room to adjust if needed.

Step 6: Using sharp scissors, cut the hair straight across. Be careful not to cut too much at once.

Step 7: Repeat steps 3-6 on the other side, ensuring the length is even.

Step 8: Take a step back and look in the mirror to see if the length and framing look natural. If not, make small adjustments until you're happy with the result.

Tips for Cutting Front Pieces of Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with small sections of hair, and cut less than you think you need to. You can always cut more if needed, but you can't add hair back.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut. Dull scissors can create split ends and make the hair look frayed.
  • When cutting, keep your elbows close to your body and your wrists straight. This will give you more control and precision.
  • If you're not confident in your cutting skills, it's always better to seek the help of a professional stylist.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my front pieces of hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to maintain the length and framing. As a general rule, every 6-8 weeks is a good timeline.

Q: Can I cut my front pieces of hair if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can! However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink up when dry, so you may want to cut it longer than you think you need to.

Hair Care After Cutting Front Pieces

After you've cut your front pieces of hair, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking their best:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage and split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause breakage and dryness.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the length and framing.
  • Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

With these tips and steps, you're now ready to cut your front pieces of hair like a pro! Remember to take your time, start small, and adjust as needed. Happy cutting!
