What To Do With Naturally Curly Hair After Shower Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi May 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, you have beautiful, bouncy curls that people envy, but on the other hand, you have to deal with frizz, tangles, and sogginess after a shower. In this article, we will discuss what to do with naturally curly hair after a shower, including tips and tricks to make your curls look their best.

Table of Contents

  2. Step-by-Step Guide to Managing Curly Hair After a Shower
  3. Tips and Tricks for Styling Naturally Curly Hair

How to Care for Naturally Curly Hair

Before we dive into what to do after a shower, it's important to discuss how to care for naturally curly hair. Curly hair is more prone to dryness, tangles, and breakage, so it's essential to use products that cater to its specific needs.

Here are some tips for caring for naturally curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized and tangle-free.
  • Avoid using a brush on your curls and opt for a wide-tooth comb instead.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help define your curls and reduce frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide to Managing Curly Hair After a Shower

After a shower, curly hair can be a mess of tangles and frizz. Follow these steps to manage your curls and keep them looking their best:

  1. Start by gently blotting your hair with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel, as this can cause frizz and breakage.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your damp hair. This will help nourish your curls and reduce frizz.
  3. Using a wide-tooth comb, gently detangle your hair from the ends up. Be patient and take your time to avoid breaking your hair.
  4. Scrunch your hair upwards to encourage curl formation and remove excess water.
  5. Diffuse your hair with a blow dryer on a low heat setting to dry your curls. Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying to avoid causing frizz.
  6. Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron or wand to touch up any areas that need extra definition.
  7. Finish with a hairspray or hair oil to hold your curls in place and add shine.

Tips and Tricks for Styling Naturally Curly Hair

Styling naturally curly hair can be a challenge, but with these tips and tricks, you can rock your curls with confidence:

  • Experiment with different hair products to find what works best for your curls.
  • Consider getting a haircut specifically designed for curly hair to help your curls look their best.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and keep your curls looking fresh.
  • Try sleeping with your hair in a pineapple or loose bun to preserve your curls overnight.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help enhance your curls.

FAQs About Naturally Curly Hair Care

Here are some frequently asked questions about caring for naturally curly hair:

How often should I wash my curly hair?

It's best to wash curly hair every 2-3 days to avoid drying it out. However, this can vary depending on your hair type and lifestyle.

Should I brush my curly hair?

No, you should avoid brushing curly hair as it can cause breakage and frizz. Use a wide-tooth comb instead.

How can I reduce frizz in my curly hair?

Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized, and avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying to reduce frizz.

Conclusion

Naturally curly hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right care and styling techniques, you can embrace your curls and look amazing. Remember to use products that cater to your hair's specific needs, be gentle when detangling your hair, and experiment with different styling techniques until you find what works best for you.
