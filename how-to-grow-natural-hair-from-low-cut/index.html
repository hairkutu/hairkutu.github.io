How To Grow Natural Hair From Low Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of constantly relaxing your hair and want to embrace your natural curls? Growing out your hair from a low cut can be daunting, but with the right techniques and hair care, it's possible to achieve healthy and luscious natural hair. In this article, we'll take you through a step-by-step guide on how to grow natural hair from a low cut.

Table of Contents

  2. How to Grow Natural Hair from Low Cut
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Growing Natural Hair from Low Cut

Description

Low cuts are a trendy and stylish hairstyle, but they can be a challenge to grow out. If you're planning on growing out your hair from a low cut, it's important to have patience and take good care of your hair. The process can be slow, but with the right techniques, you can achieve healthy and luscious natural hair.

How to Grow Natural Hair from Low Cut

The first step to growing natural hair from a low cut is to stop relaxing your hair. Relaxers contain strong chemicals that can damage your hair and scalp, causing breakage and hair loss. It's important to allow your hair to grow out naturally without any chemical treatments.

Next, focus on keeping your hair healthy and moisturized. Natural hair requires extra moisture to thrive, so use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. You can also use natural oils like coconut oil, olive oil, or jojoba oil to add moisture to your hair.

Another important step in growing natural hair from a low cut is to avoid heat styling. Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and cause breakage. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using heatless styling techniques like bantu knots or twist-outs.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to grow natural hair from a low cut:

  1. Stop relaxing your hair
  2. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  3. Add moisture to your hair with natural oils
  4. Avoid heat styling
  5. Treat your hair gently and avoid tight hairstyles
  6. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  7. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  8. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water
  9. Get enough sleep to promote hair growth
  10. Be patient and don't give up!

Tips for Growing Natural Hair from Low Cut

Here are some additional tips for growing natural hair from a low cut:

  • Protect your hair at night with a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase
  • Avoid using tight hair bands or hair accessories that can cause breakage
  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and hair growth
  • Avoid using heavy products or too much product on your hair
  • Consider taking hair growth supplements or using hair growth treatments

FAQs

How long does it take to grow natural hair from a low cut?

It can take anywhere from 6 months to 2 years or more to grow natural hair from a low cut, depending on your hair type and how quickly your hair grows.

Can I still dye my hair while growing natural hair from a low cut?

Yes, you can still dye your hair while growing natural hair from a low cut, but be sure to use a gentle dye that won't damage your hair. Avoid using bleach or harsh chemicals that can cause breakage.

Do I need to cut my hair regularly while growing natural hair from a low cut?

Yes, it's important to trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage. Aim to get a trim every 8-12 weeks.

Hair Care for Natural Hair

Once you've grown out your natural hair from a low cut, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips for caring for natural hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Deep condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or treatments on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water

By following these tips and techniques, you can successfully grow natural hair from a low cut and achieve healthy and beautiful hair. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair, and you'll soon see the results you're looking for.
