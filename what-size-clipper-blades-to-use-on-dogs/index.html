What Size Clipper Blades To Use On Dogs A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 04, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you are a dog owner, you know how important it is to keep your furry friend's coat healthy and shiny. One of the most common ways of doing so is by using clippers to trim your dog's hair. But with so many clipper blade sizes available, it can be challenging to know which one to use. In this article, we will provide you with a complete guide on what size clipper blades to use on dogs to make sure your furry friend looks their best.

Description

Clipper blades come in different sizes, each with a specific function. The size of the blade determines how close the clip will cut to the skin. The higher the number, the shorter the hair will be. For example, a #10 blade will cut closer to the skin than a #30 blade. It is essential to choose the right size clipper blade to ensure your dog's comfort during grooming.

How to Choose the Right Size Clipper Blade

Choosing the right size clipper blade depends on the type of coat your dog has and the length you want to achieve. Here's how to choose the right size clipper blade:

Step 1: Determine Your Dog's Coat Type

The first step in choosing the right size clipper blade is to determine your dog's coat type. Dogs can have long, medium, or short hair. The length and thickness of your dog's coat will determine the size of the blade you need.

Step 2: Decide on the Length You Want to Achieve

The second step is to decide on the length you want to achieve. If you want your dog's coat to be shorter, you will need a smaller blade. If you want your dog's coat to be longer, you will need a larger blade.

Step 3: Choose the Right Blade Size

Once you have determined your dog's coat type and the length you want to achieve, it's time to choose the right blade size. Here are some of the most common blade sizes and their uses:

  • #10 blade - perfect for trimming the face, paws, and sanitary areas
  • #30 blade - ideal for a smooth, short finish on the entire body
  • #40 blade - great for a super-short finish or shaving matted hair
  • #5F blade - perfect for medium-length coats
  • #7F blade - ideal for a longer finish on medium-length coats
  • #3F blade - great for longer haircuts on thick-coated breeds

Step by Step Guide on How to Use the Right Size Clipper Blade

Now that you know how to choose the right size clipper blade let's look at how to use it effectively. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to use the right size clipper blade:

Step 1: Prepare Your Dog

Before you begin grooming your dog, make sure they are comfortable and relaxed. Brush their coat to remove any tangles or mats and give them a bath if necessary. Dry your dog's coat thoroughly before clipping to avoid any discomfort.

Step 2: Attach the Right Blade Size

Attach the right size clipper blade to your clippers, following the manufacturer's instructions.

Step 3: Start Clipping

Begin clipping your dog's coat, starting from the neck and working your way down. Use firm but gentle strokes, taking care not to press the clipper too hard against your dog's skin. Work in the direction of hair growth, moving the clipper in long, smooth strokes.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

Occasionally stop and check your progress to make sure you are achieving the desired length. If necessary, switch to a smaller or larger blade size to achieve the desired length.

Step 5: Finish Up

Once you have finished clipping your dog's coat, brush them again to remove any loose hair. Give them a treat and lots of praise for being a good boy or girl.

Tips for Using Clipper Blades on Dogs

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using clipper blades on your dog:

  • Always use a sharp blade to avoid pulling your dog's hair
  • Take breaks to avoid overheating the clipper
  • Use a clipper spray or lubricant to keep the blade cool and lubricated
  • Avoid sensitive areas, such as the ears and paws
  • Always groom your dog in a well-lit area to avoid missing any spots

FAQ

What size clipper blade should I use for a short-haired dog?

If your dog has short hair, use a #30 blade to achieve a smooth, short finish.

What size clipper blade should I use for a long-haired dog?

If your dog has long hair, use a #3F or #7F blade to achieve a longer finish.

Can I use the same clipper blade on different dog breeds?

Yes, you can use the same clipper blade on different dog breeds. However, the length of the coat and the type of hair may affect the outcome, so it's essential to choose the right size blade for each dog.

Hair Care After Using Clipper Blades on Dogs

After using clipper blades on your dog, it's important to take care of their coat. Brush their hair daily to remove any tangles or mats and give them a bath every few weeks to keep their coat healthy and shiny.

By following the tips and steps outlined in this article, you can use the right size clipper blade on your dog to achieve the perfect cut every time. Remember to always take care of your dog's coat to keep them looking and feeling their best.
