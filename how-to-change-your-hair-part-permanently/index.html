How To Change Your Hair Part Permanently A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 03, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of the same old hair part? Maybe you're looking to switch things up and try something new. Changing your hair part can be a great way to freshen up your look and add some variety to your hairstyle. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to changing your hair part permanently. From preparation to aftercare, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents:

  • Preparation
  • How to Change Your Hair Part Permanently
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Changing Your Hair Part

Preparation:

Before you begin changing your hair part, it's important to prepare properly. Start by deciding where you want your new part to be. Consider your face shape, hair texture, and personal style when making this decision. Once you've decided on a new part, gather the tools you'll need, including a comb, hair clips, and a mirror.

How to Change Your Hair Part Permanently:

Now that you're prepared, it's time to start changing your hair part. Follow these steps:

  1. Section off your hair: Use hair clips to section off your hair so that only the section you're working on is exposed.
  2. Wet your hair: Use a spray bottle to wet the section of hair you're working on.
  3. Comb your hair: Use a comb to create your new part. Start at the front of your head and comb straight back. Use the end of the comb to create a straight part.
  4. Dry your hair: Use a blow dryer to dry the section of hair you just combed. Repeat this process for each section of hair you want to change.

Tips for Success:

Changing your hair part can be tricky, so here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Take your time: Rushing through the process can lead to mistakes.
  • Practice: Try combing your hair in different parts before committing to a permanent change.
  • Use a mirror: Make sure you have a good view of the back of your head.
  • Be patient: It may take a few days for your hair to adjust to the new part.

FAQs:

Here are some common questions people have about changing their hair part:

  • Will changing my hair part damage my hair? No, changing your hair part shouldn't cause any damage to your hair.
  • Can I change my hair part if I have bangs? Yes, you can change your hair part even if you have bangs. Just be sure to incorporate them into your new part.
  • Is it possible to change my hair part if I have a cowlick? Yes, it's possible, but it may be more challenging. You may need to use some styling products to keep your hair in place.

Hair Care After Changing Your Hair Part:

Once you've changed your hair part, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Changing your hair part can be a fun and easy way to switch up your look. By following these steps and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy your new part for weeks to come.
