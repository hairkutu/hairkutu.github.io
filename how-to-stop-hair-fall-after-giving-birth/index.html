How To Stop Hair Fall After Giving Birth Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Mar 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you experiencing hair fall after giving birth? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many women experience postpartum hair loss, also known as telogen effluvium. This occurs when the hair growth cycle is disrupted due to hormonal changes and stress during pregnancy and childbirth. However, there are ways to stop hair fall and promote hair growth. In this blog post, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to stop hair fall after giving birth.

How to Stop Hair Fall After Giving Birth

If you're experiencing hair fall after giving birth, here are some tips that can help:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

Make sure you're getting enough nutrients in your diet, including protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, D, and E. These nutrients are essential for healthy hair growth.

2. Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water to keep your body and scalp hydrated. Dehydration can cause dryness and breakage of hair.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. These tools can damage your hair and cause breakage.

4. Use Gentle Hair Care Products

Use gentle hair care products that are free of sulfates, parabens, and alcohol. These chemicals can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage.

5. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can improve blood flow and stimulate hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp in circular motions for a few minutes each day.

6. Get Enough Sleep

Make sure you're getting enough sleep to reduce stress and promote healthy hair growth. Aim for at least 7-8 hours of sleep per night.

7. Reduce Stress

Stress can cause hair fall, so try to reduce stress levels by practicing relaxation techniques like deep breathing, yoga, or meditation.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to stop hair fall after giving birth:

Step 1: Consult Your Doctor

Consult your doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions that may be causing hair fall.

Step 2: Eat a Balanced Diet

Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, D, and E.

Step 3: Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water to keep your body and scalp hydrated.

Step 4: Use Gentle Hair Care Products

Use gentle hair care products that are free of sulfates, parabens, and alcohol.

Step 5: Massage Your Scalp

Massage your scalp in circular motions for a few minutes each day to improve blood flow and stimulate hair growth.

Tips to Prevent Hair Fall

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair fall:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like braids, buns, and ponytails that can cause tension and breakage of hair.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can cause breakage.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about postpartum hair fall:

1. Is postpartum hair fall normal?

Yes, postpartum hair fall is a common condition that affects many women after giving birth.

2. When does postpartum hair fall occur?

Postpartum hair fall can occur anytime within the first six months after giving birth.

3. Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair fall?

Yes, your hair will grow back after postpartum hair fall. It may take several months for your hair to return to its normal thickness.

Hair Care Routine

Here's a hair care routine that can help prevent hair fall:

1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that are gentle on your hair and scalp.

2. Apply a hair mask once a week.

Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.

3. Limit heat styling.

Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons.

4. Take a hair supplement.

Take a hair supplement that contains vitamins and minerals that promote healthy hair growth.

Follow the above tips and tricks to stop hair fall after giving birth. Remember, postpartum hair fall is a temporary condition that can be treated with proper hair care and a healthy lifestyle.
