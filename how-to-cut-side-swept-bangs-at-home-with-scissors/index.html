How To Cut Side Swept Bangs At Home With Scissors

Written by Frank Apr 27, 2023 · 6 min read

If you want to change up your hairstyle without going to the salon, cutting side swept bangs at home can be a fun and easy way to do so. In this step-by-step guide, we’ll show you how to cut side swept bangs at home with scissors.

Description

Side swept bangs are a popular hairstyle that adds a touch of elegance and youthfulness to your look. They are versatile, and can be worn with long, medium, or short hair. Cutting your own bangs can be intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it’s easy to achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home.

How to Cut Side Swept Bangs at Home with Scissors

Step 1: Gather your tools

Before you start cutting your bangs, you’ll need the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle with water

Step 2: Section your hair

Section off the hair you want to cut into your bangs. Use a comb to create a triangular section that starts from the crown of your head and ends at the point where you want your bangs to fall. Clip the rest of your hair away from your face.

Step 3: Wet your hair

Spray the section of hair you’ll be cutting with water. This will make it easier to cut, and will also help you see the natural fall of your hair.

Step 4: Start cutting

Using your sharp scissors, start cutting your bangs at an angle. Begin by cutting a small amount of hair, and then gradually cut more as you go. Keep in mind that you can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut. Cut your bangs longer than you think you want them, as they will shrink up when they dry.

Step 5: Check your work

After you’ve cut your bangs, release the clipped hair and check your work in the mirror. If you need to make any adjustments, do so carefully.

Step 6: Style your bangs

Style your bangs by blow drying them and using a round brush to shape them. You can also use a flat iron or curling iron to add some waves or curls.

Tips

  • Start by cutting less hair than you think you need. You can always go back and cut more if you want shorter bangs.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don’t pull your hair too tightly while cutting, as this can cause unevenness.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting your own bangs can be nerve-wracking, but it’s worth it when you can achieve the look you want.

FAQ

Can I cut bangs with regular scissors?

It’s best to use sharp, professional scissors when cutting your own hair. Regular scissors may not be sharp enough, and can damage your hair.

Can I cut my bangs when they’re dry?

It’s best to cut your bangs when they’re wet, as this will make them easier to cut and will also help you see the natural fall of your hair.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It’s recommended to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

Hair Care

After cutting your bangs, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant when you do. Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay, and consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
