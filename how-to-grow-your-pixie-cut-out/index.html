How To Grow Your Pixie Cut Out A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 12, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you've ever had a pixie cut, you know that growing it out can be a real pain. It seems like it takes forever for your hair to grow, and in the meantime, you're stuck with an awkward in-between length that's hard to style. But fear not, because we've put together a step-by-step guide to help you grow out your pixie cut with ease.

Table of Contents

  • How to Grow Your Pixie Cut Out
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Growing Out Your Pixie Cut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Growing Out Your Pixie Cut

How to Grow Your Pixie Cut Out

The first thing you need to do when growing out your pixie cut is to be patient. Hair grows at an average rate of 0.5 inches per month, so it can take several months to grow out a short haircut. However, there are a few things you can do to speed up the process.

1. Get Regular Trims

It may seem counterintuitive to cut your hair when you're trying to grow it out, but getting regular trims can actually help your hair grow faster. Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks will help prevent split ends and breakage, which can slow down the growth process.

2. Try Hair Growth Products

There are a variety of hair growth products on the market that claim to help speed up hair growth. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and amino acids, which are known to promote healthy hair growth.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Your diet can also play a role in the health and growth of your hair. Make sure you're eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals. Foods like salmon, eggs, spinach, and sweet potatoes are all great for promoting healthy hair growth.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Find a Stylist You Trust

When growing out your pixie cut, it's important to find a stylist who understands your hair goals and can help you achieve them. Look for a stylist who specializes in short haircuts and has experience with growing them out.

2. Start with a Plan

Before you start growing out your pixie cut, sit down with your stylist and come up with a plan. Discuss your desired length and any specific styles you want to try as your hair grows out.

3. Be Patient

As we mentioned earlier, growing out your hair takes time. Be patient and don't get discouraged if your hair doesn't seem to be growing as quickly as you'd like.

4. Experiment with Styles

As your hair grows, you'll likely go through an awkward in-between stage. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you. Braids, buns, and headbands can all be great options for hiding awkward lengths.

5. Maintain Your Hair Health

As you're growing out your hair, it's important to maintain its health. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and make sure to deep condition your hair regularly. Avoid using heat tools as much as possible, and always use a heat protectant when you do.

Tips for Growing Out Your Pixie Cut

1. Be Prepared for Some Awkward Stages

As we mentioned earlier, growing out your pixie cut can be awkward. Be prepared for some in-between stages, and try to have fun with your hair during this time.

2. Accessorize

Headbands, hats, and scarves can all be great accessories to help hide awkward lengths and add some style to your hair.

3. Embrace Your Natural Texture

As your hair grows out, you may notice that your natural texture is different than it was when you had a pixie cut. Embrace your natural texture and try out some new styles that work with it.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to grow out a pixie cut?

A: On average, hair grows at a rate of 0.5 inches per month. Depending on how short your pixie cut is, it could take several months to a year to grow it out.

Q: Should I color my hair while growing out a pixie cut?

A: It's best to avoid coloring your hair while growing out a pixie cut, as coloring can cause damage and slow down the growth process. If you do decide to color your hair, be sure to use a gentle, sulfate-free color and deep condition your hair regularly.

Hair Care for Growing Out Your Pixie Cut

1. Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo and Conditioner

Sulfates can be harsh on your hair and strip it of its natural oils. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

2. Deep Condition Your Hair Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help keep your hair healthy and strong as it grows out. Try using a deep conditioning mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

3. Avoid Heat Tools

Heat tools like flat irons and curling irons can be damaging to your hair. Avoid using them as much as possible, and always use a heat protectant when you do.

By following these tips and tricks, you'll be well on your way to growing out your pixie cut with ease. Remember to be patient and have fun with your hair during this process!
