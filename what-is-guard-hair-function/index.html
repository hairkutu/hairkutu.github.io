What Is Guard Hair Function Understanding The Role Of Guard Hair In Hair Care

Written by William Jul 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Guard hairs, also known as primary hairs, are the long, coarse hairs that cover the outer layer of an animal's coat. While many people may not be familiar with the term "guard hair," they are likely familiar with the function that these hairs serve. In simple terms, guard hairs are responsible for protecting the animal's skin and insulating it from the elements. But what does this mean for our own hair care? Let's explore the role of guard hair in more detail.

What is Guard Hair?

Guard hairs are the longest and most visible hairs on an animal's body. In some animals, such as dogs and cats, the guard hairs can be easily seen and felt. In others, such as cows and horses, the guard hairs are less visible but still present. These hairs are made up of a tough protein called keratin, the same protein that makes up our own hair and nails.

What is the Function of Guard Hair?

The primary function of guard hair is to protect the animal's skin from the elements. This includes protecting the skin from the sun's harmful UV rays, as well as from wind and rain. Guard hair also helps to insulate the animal, keeping it warm in cold temperatures and cool in hot temperatures. In addition to protection and insulation, guard hair can also play a role in communication and camouflage.

How Does Guard Hair Affect Our Hair Care?

While guard hair is typically associated with animals, it can also play a role in our own hair care. In fact, many hair care products are designed to mimic the protective and insulating properties of guard hair. For example, shampoos and conditioners can help to protect the hair from the sun's harmful rays and from damage caused by styling tools. Hair oils and serums can help to insulate the hair, keeping it healthy and shiny.

Steps to Protect Your Hair Like Guard Hair

If you want to protect your hair like guard hair, there are a few steps you can take:

  1. Choose hair care products that contain UV protection.
  2. Avoid using high-heat styling tools, or use a heat protectant spray before styling.
  3. Use a hair oil or serum to help insulate the hair.
  4. Wear a hat or scarf when spending time in the sun.

Tips to Keep Your Hair Healthy

In addition to protecting your hair like guard hair, there are a few tips you can follow to keep your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

FAQs About Guard Hair Function

What is the difference between guard hair and fur?

Guard hair is the long, coarse hair that covers the outer layer of an animal's coat, while fur is the soft, dense hair that lies beneath. While both guard hair and fur serve to protect and insulate the animal, guard hair is typically longer and more visible.

Can humans have guard hair?

While humans do not have guard hair in the traditional sense, our hair can still serve a similar protective and insulating function. By using hair care products that contain UV protection and by avoiding high-heat styling tools, we can help to protect and insulate our hair in the same way that guard hair does for animals.

What is the best way to care for guard hair?

The best way to care for guard hair is to keep it clean and well-maintained. This may include regular washing and conditioning, as well as using protective products to help insulate and protect the hair. It is also important to avoid using high-heat styling tools, as these can damage and dry out the hair.

The Bottom Line

Guard hair may be a term that is more commonly associated with animals, but it can also play an important role in our own hair care. By understanding the function of guard hair and taking steps to protect and maintain our own hair, we can help to keep it healthy, shiny, and strong.
