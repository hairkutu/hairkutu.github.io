How To Thin Thick Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline May 28, 2023 · 6 min read

Having thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it looks stunning when styled properly, it can also be a hassle to manage and style on a daily basis. One way to make thick hair more manageable is to thin it out. While you can go to a salon to get your hair thinned, it can be quite expensive. However, with the right tools and technique, you can easily thin your hair at home. In this article, we will walk you through the steps on how to thin thick hair at home.

How to Thin Thick Hair at Home

Before we dive into the steps, let’s talk about the tools you will need. You will need a pair of good quality thinning shears, a comb, and hair clips.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start thinning it.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. This will make it easier for you to work on one section at a time.

Step 3: Start Thinning Your Hair

Take one section of your hair and comb it out. Hold the thinning shears vertically and start cutting into the section of hair. Cut small sections of hair at a time, and be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Repeat this process on all sections of your hair, making sure to cut evenly on both sides.

Step 4: Blend Your Hair

Once you have finished thinning your hair, comb through it to blend the layers. This will make your hair look more natural and less choppy.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. You will notice that your hair is now more manageable and easier to style.

Tips for Thinning Thick Hair

Thinning your hair can be tricky, so here are some tips to help you along the way:

  • Use good quality thinning shears
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time
  • Be patient and take your time
  • Don’t cut too much hair at once
  • Blend your hair after thinning it

FAQ

Q: Will thinning my hair damage it?

A: No, as long as you use good quality thinning shears and don’t cut too much hair at once, your hair should not be damaged.

Q: Can I thin my hair if it’s already damaged?

A: It’s best to avoid thinning your hair if it’s already damaged. Instead, focus on repairing your hair before attempting to thin it out.

Q: How often should I thin my hair?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. On average, it’s recommended to thin your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care

It’s important to take care of your hair after thinning it. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week

With these tips and techniques, you can easily thin your thick hair at home. Remember to take your time and be patient, and soon you’ll have the perfect hairstyle that’s easy to manage and style.
