What Is The Best Mousse For Short Hair

Written by William Sep 01, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of dealing with flat, lifeless hair? Do you want to add volume and texture to your short hair? If so, you may want to consider using a mousse. Mousse is a lightweight styling product that can add volume, hold, and texture to your hair without weighing it down. However, not all mousses are created equal. In this article, we will discuss what is the best mousse for short hair and provide tips on how to use it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Mousse on Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Using Mousse on Short Hair

Description

The best mousse for short hair is one that provides volume, hold, and texture without leaving your hair feeling sticky or crunchy. Look for mousses that are lightweight, non-greasy, and easy to apply. Some of the best mousses for short hair include:

  • TRESemmé Thermal Creations Volumizing Mousse
  • OGX Locking + Coconut Curls Decadent Creamy Mousse
  • Paul Mitchell Extra-Body Sculpting Foam
  • Redken Full Frame 07 All-Over Volumizing Mousse
  • Matrix Biolage Volumizing Whipped Mousse

How to Use Mousse on Short Hair

Before applying mousse, make sure your hair is clean and towel-dried. Mousse works best on damp hair, so avoid using it on completely dry hair. Follow these steps to use mousse on short hair:

  1. Shake the can of mousse well before use.
  2. Dispense a golf ball-sized amount of mousse onto your palm.
  3. Work the mousse into your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to distribute the mousse evenly throughout your hair.
  5. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to create volume and texture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to use mousse on short hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel-dry your hair to remove excess moisture.
  3. Shake the can of mousse well before use.
  4. Dispense a golf ball-sized amount of mousse onto your palm.
  5. Flip your head upside down and work the mousse into your roots, massaging it with your fingertips.
  6. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the mousse evenly throughout your hair.
  7. Flip your head back up and blow-dry your hair using a round brush.
  8. Style your hair as desired.
  9. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to set your style in place.

Tips for Using Mousse on Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your mousse:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to prep your hair before using mousse.
  • Apply mousse to damp hair for best results.
  • Work the mousse into your roots to add volume and texture.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the mousse evenly through your hair.
  • Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to create volume and texture.
  • Avoid using too much mousse, as this can leave your hair feeling sticky and crunchy.
  • Experiment with different brands and types of mousse to find the one that works best for your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using mousse on short hair:

Q: Can I use mousse on dry hair?

A: While you can use mousse on dry hair, it works best on damp hair. Mousse is designed to add volume and texture to your hair, and it needs moisture to do so.

Q: How much mousse should I use on my short hair?

A: Use a golf ball-sized amount of mousse on short hair. Using too much mousse can leave your hair feeling sticky and crunchy.

Q: Can I use mousse every day?

A: Yes, you can use mousse every day. However, be sure to use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any product buildup from your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Here are some general hair care tips to keep your short hair looking its best:

  • Wash your hair every other day to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, especially when it is wet.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair every day.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it healthy and hydrated.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends.

Now that you know what is the best mousse for short hair, go ahead and give it a try. With the right mousse and technique, you can achieve voluminous, textured hair that will turn heads.
