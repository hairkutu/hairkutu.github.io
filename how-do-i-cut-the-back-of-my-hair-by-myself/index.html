How Do I Cut The Back Of My Hair By Myself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Mar 19, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of paying for expensive haircuts? Or maybe you just want to try cutting your own hair at home? Whatever your reason, learning how to cut the back of your hair by yourself can be a daunting task. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step-by-step, and provide you with some tips and tricks along the way.

How to Cut the Back of Your Hair By Yourself

Cutting the back of your hair by yourself can be tricky, especially if you're not used to doing it. But with a little bit of practice, you can achieve great results. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror. You may also want to use hair clips to help keep your hair in place.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can be harder to cut, and may not give you the best results.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair Into Sections

Using your comb, divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle, and then divide each side into two sections. Clip each section out of the way, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with the bottom section, comb your hair straight down and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut off the desired length. Be sure to cut in a straight line, and avoid cutting too much off at once.

Step 5: Move Upwards

Once you've finished cutting the bottom section, release the next section and repeat the process. Continue moving upwards until you've cut all the sections on both sides.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, use your handheld mirror to check the back of your hair. Make any necessary adjustments, and trim any uneven sections.

Tips for Cutting the Back of Your Hair By Yourself

Cutting the back of your hair can be challenging, but there are some tips and tricks that can help make the process easier:

  • Start with small sections, and don't cut too much off at once.
  • Use a mirror to check your work as you go.
  • Be patient, and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • Consider investing in a pair of professional hairdressing scissors. They'll make the job easier and give you better results.

FAQs

How often should I cut the back of my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and the style you're going for. As a general rule, most people need a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain their style.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

You can, but it's not recommended. Regular scissors can be dull and uneven, making it harder to achieve a clean and precise cut.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. It's just hair, and it will grow back. You can always trim any uneven sections or visit a professional stylist to fix any major mistakes.

Hair Care

Once you've finished cutting the back of your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner that's suited to your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals, as this can help promote healthy hair growth.

Cutting the back of your hair by yourself can be a little intimidating at first, but with some practice and patience, you can achieve great results. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
