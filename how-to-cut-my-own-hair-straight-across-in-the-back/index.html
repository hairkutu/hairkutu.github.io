How To Cut My Own Hair Straight Across In The Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to save money on salon visits or simply want to learn a new skill, cutting your own hair straight across in the back is a great place to start. With a few tools and some patience, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll walk you through the process step-by-step and provide some helpful tips along the way.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Success
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Your Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few tools:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • A comb or brush
  • A handheld mirror
  • A larger mirror or wall mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp and specifically designed for haircutting. Dull scissors can lead to uneven cuts and split ends.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. It's important to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and lead to an uneven cut.

Once your hair is dry, brush it out to remove any tangles or knots. If you have curly hair, consider straightening it before cutting to get a more accurate cut.

Sectioning Your Hair

To ensure an even cut, you'll need to section your hair. Start by parting your hair down the middle and bringing it forward over your shoulders.

Next, use your comb to create a horizontal part at the back of your head, about 1-2 inches above your hairline. Clip the top section of hair up and out of the way.

Repeat this process, working your way up the back of your head and clipping each section of hair as you go.

Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting. Begin with the bottom section of hair, and take a small section of hair between your fingers. Hold it tightly and cut straight across.

Work your way up the back of your head, taking small sections of hair and cutting straight across.

When you reach the top section of hair, be sure to take extra care to ensure an even cut.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting, remove the clips and shake out your hair. Check your cut in both mirrors to make sure it's even.

If you notice any uneven areas, go back and make small adjustments until you're satisfied with the result.

Tips for Success

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always take more off, but you can't put it back on.
  • Use your comb as a guide to ensure a straight cut.
  • If you have longer hair, consider using hair ties or hair clips to create more manageable sections.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can! However, keep in mind that curly hair can shrink when it dries, so you may want to cut your hair a bit longer than you normally would to account for this.

Should I wet my hair before cutting it?

No, it's best to cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can stretch and lead to an uneven cut.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, you should always use haircutting scissors. Regular scissors can lead to split ends and an uneven cut.

Hair Care After Your Cut

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Keep your hair moisturized with a good conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent further damage.

In conclusion, cutting your own hair straight across in the back is a great way to save money and learn a new skill. With the right tools and some patience, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. Be sure to follow these steps and take care of your hair properly after your cut for the best results.
