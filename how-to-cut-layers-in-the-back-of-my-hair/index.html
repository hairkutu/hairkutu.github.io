How To Cut Layers In The Back Of My Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Apr 16, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your hair looking flat and lifeless? Do you want to add some dimension to your locks? Cutting layers in the back of your hair can give you the volume and movement you crave. In this step-by-step guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting layers in the back of your hair. We'll also provide some tips and tricks to ensure that you achieve the look you want. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Layers in the Back of My Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting Layers

How to Cut Layers in the Back of My Hair

Before we get started, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also helpful to have a mirror so you can see the back of your head.

When cutting layers in the back of your hair, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. This will ensure that you get an accurate cut.

Next, you'll want to section off the hair that you want to cut. If you're going for a subtle look, you can section off a small portion of hair. If you want more dramatic layers, you can section off a larger portion of hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your tools and hair sectioned off, it's time to start cutting layers in the back of your hair. Follow these steps:

  1. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down.
  2. Hold the hair between your fingers, about an inch or two from the ends.
  3. Using your scissors, cut into the hair at a slight angle. Start with small snips and work your way up to larger ones.
  4. Release the hair and check to see if you achieved the desired layer length.
  5. Repeat steps 1-4 with the rest of the hair in the section you've chosen.
  6. Move on to the next section of hair and repeat steps 1-5 until you've cut layers in the entire back of your hair.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting layers in the back of your hair:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to larger ones. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's been cut.
  • Don't cut straight across. Instead, cut at a slight angle to create more movement.
  • Use hair clips to keep the rest of your hair out of the way while you're cutting.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting hair can be tiring!

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting layers in the back of your hair:

How do I know how much hair to cut?

It's best to start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. Remember, you can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's been cut.

Can I cut layers in my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair! However, keep in mind that the layers may look different when your hair is dry versus when it's wet. It's a good idea to cut curly hair when it's dry.

Should I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry. This will give you a more accurate idea of how the layers will look.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

Once you've cut layers in the back of your hair, it's important to take care of your locks. Here are some tips:

  • Use a heat protectant when styling your hair with heat tools.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your layers.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.

Cutting layers in the back of your hair is a great way to add volume and movement to your locks. Follow this step-by-step guide and you'll have a gorgeous new look in no time!
