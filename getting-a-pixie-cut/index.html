Getting a Pixie Cut: A Relaxed Guide

Are you ready to take the plunge and get a pixie cut? This short and sassy hairstyle has been a trend for years and it's not going anywhere. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about getting a pixie cut, from the initial decision to the aftercare. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Making the Decision
  • Preparation
  • The Cutting Process
  • Styling Your Pixie Cut
  • Maintenance and Hair Care
  • FAQs

Making the Decision

Before you rush to the salon, take some time to think about whether a pixie cut is the right choice for you. This hairstyle is not for everyone and it requires a lot of confidence to pull off. If you're still unsure, try out a virtual makeover app to see how you would look with a pixie cut.

Another important factor to consider is your face shape. Pixie cuts look great on oval, heart, and square faces, but may not be as flattering on round or long faces. Talk to your stylist about what would work best for you.

Preparation

Once you've made the decision to get a pixie cut, it's time to prepare for your appointment. Start by doing some research on different pixie cut styles and find some inspiration photos to bring to your stylist. This will help them understand exactly what you want.

You should also consider your hair texture and how it will affect your new hairstyle. If you have thick, curly hair, you may need to have it thinned out before the cut. If you have fine hair, your stylist may recommend adding some layers to give your hair more volume.

The Cutting Process

The cutting process for a pixie cut can be quite different from a traditional haircut. Your stylist will likely use clippers to create the short back and sides, leaving the top longer. They will then use scissors to shape the top into the desired style.

It's important to communicate with your stylist throughout the process to ensure you're happy with the length and shape. Don't be afraid to speak up if you want something changed.

Styling Your Pixie Cut

Styling a pixie cut is relatively easy and requires minimal products. You can use a molding paste or wax to create texture and hold. If you want a sleek look, try using a flat iron to smooth the top of your hair.

If you're feeling adventurous, you can also experiment with different hairstyles. Pixie cuts can be styled into messy, textured looks or slicked back for a more sophisticated style.

Maintenance and Hair Care

Maintaining a pixie cut requires regular trims to keep the shape and length. Your stylist will likely recommend coming in every 4-6 weeks for a touch-up.

When it comes to hair care, it's important to use products that are gentle and nourishing. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are sulfate-free and contain ingredients like argan oil or coconut oil to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

FAQs

How long does a pixie cut take to grow out?

It varies from person to person, but on average it takes about 6 months to a year for a pixie cut to grow out to a bob length.

Can I still wear hair accessories with a pixie cut?

Absolutely! Pixie cuts can be accessorized with headbands, clips, and even hats.

Will a pixie cut make my hair look thinner?

No, a pixie cut can actually give the illusion of thicker hair by adding volume and texture.

Getting a pixie cut can be a bold and empowering decision. With these tips and tricks, you'll be ready to rock your new hairstyle with confidence. Happy chopping!
