Is Coarse Hair The Same As Thick Hair

Written by Petter Apr 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

When it comes to hair, many people use the terms "coarse" and "thick" interchangeably. However, they actually refer to two different characteristics of hair. In this article, we'll explore the difference between coarse and thick hair, and answer the question: is coarse hair the same as thick hair?

Table of Contents

  • Difference Between Coarse and Thick Hair
  • Is Coarse Hair the Same as Thick Hair?
  • How to Determine Your Hair Type
  • Tips for Caring for Coarse and Thick Hair
  • FAQ

Difference Between Coarse and Thick Hair

Coarse hair refers to the texture of individual hair strands. Coarse hair strands are thicker in diameter, and can feel rough or wiry to the touch. This texture is often genetic, and can also be influenced by environmental factors like exposure to sun, wind, and chemicals.

Thick hair, on the other hand, refers to the density of hair on the scalp. People with thick hair have a high number of hair strands per square inch of scalp, which can make their hair appear fuller and more voluminous. Thick hair can be genetic, but can also be influenced by factors like hormonal changes, diet, and stress.

Is Coarse Hair the Same as Thick Hair?

No, coarse hair and thick hair are not the same. While they can often occur together, they refer to different characteristics of hair. It's possible to have coarse, thin hair or fine, thick hair.

It's important to determine your hair type so that you can choose the right products and styling techniques to care for your hair. Coarse hair may benefit from heavier, more moisturizing products to help tame frizz and soften the texture. Thick hair may benefit from lighter, volumizing products to help prevent flatness and promote movement.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

The easiest way to determine your hair type is to look at the thickness of individual hair strands and the density of hair on your scalp. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Coarse hair: thick individual strands that feel rough or wiry to the touch
  • Fine hair: thin individual strands that can feel soft and delicate
  • Thin hair: low density of hair on the scalp, can appear flat and lack volume
  • Thick hair: high density of hair on the scalp, can appear full and voluminous

Of course, there are many variations within each hair type, and you may have a combination of different characteristics. It's best to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair.

Tips for Caring for Coarse and Thick Hair

Here are some general tips for caring for coarse and thick hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help soften coarse hair
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help tame frizz and add shine
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can dry out and damage coarse hair
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to help prevent flatness in thick hair
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down thick hair
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for your hair type and texture

FAQ

Can you have both coarse and thick hair?

Yes, it's possible to have both coarse and thick hair. This combination of characteristics can make hair feel dense and heavy.

Can you change the texture of your hair?

While you can't change the genetic texture of your hair, you can alter the appearance and feel of your hair with different styling techniques and products.

What's the best way to care for coarse, thick hair?

Coarse, thick hair benefits from heavy, moisturizing products to help soften the texture and tame frizz. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for your hair.
