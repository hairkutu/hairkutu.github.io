How To Cut Wavy Hair For More Volume A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Apr 03, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have naturally wavy hair, you might find that it lacks volume and body. This can be frustrating, but the good news is that there are ways to cut your hair to add more volume and movement. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut wavy hair for more volume and give you some tips on how to take care of your new cut.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Wavy Hair for More Volume
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Styling Wavy Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

How to Cut Wavy Hair for More Volume

The key to cutting wavy hair for more volume is to create layers that add movement and texture to your hair. Layers help to break up the weight of your hair and create more volume at the roots. Here are the basic steps to follow:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see where the natural waves and curls fall and how they will shape your new cut. If your hair is wet or damp, it will be harder to see the natural shape of your hair.

2. Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place. This will make it easier to cut your hair in even sections and ensure that you don’t miss any areas.

3. Cut Your Hair in Layers

Start by cutting the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair and cut it at a slight angle, starting at the back of your head and working your way forward. This will create a gradual layer that will add volume to your hair.

Repeat this step for each section of your hair, moving up to the next layer each time. Be sure to cut each section at a slight angle to create a smooth, layered look.

4. Blend the Layers

Once you have cut all of the layers, it’s time to blend them together. Take a comb and run it through your hair, gently pulling the ends of your hair to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless look and prevent any choppy or uneven sections.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to cutting wavy hair for more volume:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Section your hair into four parts
  3. Cut your hair in layers, starting with the bottom layer
  4. Repeat for each section, cutting at a slight angle
  5. Blend the layers together with a comb
  6. Trim any uneven ends or flyaways
  7. Style your hair as desired

Tips for Styling Wavy Hair

Once you have cut your wavy hair for more volume, there are a few tips you can follow to style it and keep it looking great:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add extra body to your hair
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add more volume at the roots
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural waves
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, as this can cause frizz and break up the natural waves
  • Use a light hold hairspray to keep your hair in place without weighing it down

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my wavy hair?

A: It depends on the length and style of your hair, but generally you should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my wavy hair myself?

A: While it is possible to cut your own hair, we recommend going to a professional stylist to ensure that your hair is cut evenly and to your desired length.

Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for wavy hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as a brush can cause frizz and breakage

By following these tips and using the right cutting techniques, you can achieve a voluminous, bouncy look for your wavy hair.
