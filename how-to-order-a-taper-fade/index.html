How To Order A Taper Fade A Guide For Men

Written by Aprilia Jun 02, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're looking for a trendy and stylish haircut, a taper fade might be just what you need. This popular hairstyle features short hair on the sides that gradually fades into longer hair on the top. But how do you ask your barber for a taper fade? Here's everything you need to know.

Description

A taper fade is a type of haircut where the hair is gradually cut shorter on the sides and back, creating a tapered effect. The hair on the top is left longer, and can be styled in a variety of ways. This hairstyle is popular among men of all ages and can be customized to suit your personal preferences and face shape.

How to Order

When you're ready to get a taper fade, it's important to communicate your desired style to your barber. Here's how to order a taper fade:

1. Research

Before you go to the barber, do some research to find inspiration for your new haircut. Look up pictures of taper fades and find a few that you like. This will help you communicate your desired style to your barber.

2. Communicate

When you arrive at the barber, tell them that you want a taper fade. Be specific about how short you want the sides to be and how long you want the top to be. Show them the pictures you found during your research to give them a better idea of what you're looking for.

3. Trust Your Barber

Your barber is a professional and knows what will look good on you. Trust their advice and let them make suggestions based on your face shape and hair type. Remember, a taper fade can be customized to suit your individual features.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a taper fade:

1. Choose Your Style

Before you head to the barber, decide on the style of taper fade you want. Do you want a high fade, mid fade, or low fade? Do you want a skin fade or a bald fade? Think about how short you want the sides to be and how long you want the top to be.

2. Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before your haircut, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier for your barber to cut and style your hair.

3. Talk to Your Barber

When you arrive at the barber, tell them that you want a taper fade. Show them pictures of the style you want and be specific about how short you want the sides to be and how long you want the top to be.

4. Sit Still

During the haircut, sit still and follow your barber's instructions. This will ensure that your taper fade turns out just the way you want it.

5. Style Your Hair

Once your haircut is complete, your barber will style your hair. You can ask for advice on how to style your hair at home, or you can experiment with different products and techniques to find the look that works for you.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a taper fade:

  • Bring pictures of the style you want to show your barber
  • Listen to your barber's advice and trust their expertise
  • Be patient during the haircut and sit still
  • Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find the look that works for you

FAQ

What is a taper fade?

A taper fade is a type of haircut where the hair is gradually cut shorter on the sides and back, creating a tapered effect. The hair on the top is left longer, and can be styled in a variety of ways.

How long does a taper fade last?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your haircut. Generally, a taper fade will last 2-4 weeks before it needs to be touched up.

How do I maintain my taper fade?

To maintain your taper fade, you'll need to get it touched up every few weeks. You can also experiment with different styling products and techniques to keep your hair looking fresh.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, it's important to take care of it on a daily basis. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers and straighteners too often
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Get regular haircuts to prevent split ends and breakage

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock your new taper fade with confidence. Remember to communicate with your barber, trust their advice, and experiment with different styling techniques to find the look that works for you.
