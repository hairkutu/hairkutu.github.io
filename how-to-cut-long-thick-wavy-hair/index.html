How To Cut Long Thick Wavy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 10, 2023 · 7 min read

Do you have long, thick, wavy hair that’s in need of a haircut? If you’re looking to trim a few inches or freshen up your style, you’ve come to the right place. In this article, we’ll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut long thick wavy hair, as well as some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect look.

How to Cut Long Thick Wavy Hair

Before we get started, it’s important to note that cutting your own hair can be challenging, especially if you’re doing it for the first time. If you’re not confident in your abilities, it may be best to visit a professional stylist. However, if you’re up for the challenge, here’s how to cut long thick wavy hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

The first step is to wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner. This will help remove any product buildup and make your hair easier to work with. Once you’ve washed your hair, dry it thoroughly with a towel or blow dryer.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then create two more sections by parting your hair from the top of your ear to the back of your head on each side. Clip each section out of the way.

Step 3: Trim the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair, about an inch wide, and hold it between your fingers. Use hair cutting scissors to trim the ends of the hair, cutting at an angle to create a soft, layered look.

Step 4: Work Your Way Up

Continue trimming small sections of hair, working your way up from the bottom layer to the top. Take care to cut each section evenly, and remember to cut at an angle to create layers.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve finished cutting all the sections, it’s time to blend the layers. Comb your hair straight down and use your scissors to make small, vertical cuts to blend the layers together.

Step 6: Check Your Work

When you’ve finished cutting your hair, check your work in the mirror. Take small sections of hair and check that they’re even and blended. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim them again.

Tips for Cutting Long Thick Wavy Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect cut:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. It’s always better to take off less hair than you think you need, as you can always trim more later.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a clean cut.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet. Wet hair is heavier and longer than dry hair, so you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  • Take breaks if you need them. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don’t be afraid to take a break if you need to rest your arms or hands.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long thick wavy hair:

Can I cut my own hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair, but it’s important to be careful and take your time. If you’re not confident in your abilities, it may be best to visit a professional stylist.

How often should I cut my hair?

It’s recommended to cut your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

How can I care for my hair after cutting it?

After cutting your hair, it’s important to keep it healthy by using a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible.

Conclusion

Cutting long thick wavy hair can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, it’s possible to achieve a beautiful, layered look. Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and check your work frequently. If you’re not confident in your abilities, it’s always best to visit a professional stylist.
