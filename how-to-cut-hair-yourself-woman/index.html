How To Cut Hair Yourself Woman A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 02, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of spending hundreds of dollars every year on haircuts? Or maybe you just don't have the time to go to a salon. Whatever the reason, cutting your own hair can be a daunting task. But fear not, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut hair yourself woman, as well as some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Tools You'll Need

Before we get started, let's talk about the tools you'll need to cut your own hair. You'll need:

  • Sharp scissors (haircutting scissors work best, but regular scissors will do)
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • A handheld mirror
  • Hair clips or elastics to section your hair

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends. You should also clean your scissors with rubbing alcohol before and after each use to prevent the spread of bacteria.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry until it's about 80% dry. Wet hair is harder to cut and can cause your hair to shrink, resulting in a shorter haircut than you intended. Comb your hair to remove any tangles and knots.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is important to ensure that you cut all of your hair evenly. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then from ear to ear. Use hair clips or elastics to secure each section of hair, leaving only the section you're going to cut loose.

Cutting Your Hair

Begin by cutting the ends of your hair. Hold a small section of your hair between your fingers and slide your fingers down to the desired length. Use your scissors to cut straight across. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working from the back of your head to the front.

Blending Your Hair

After you've cut the ends of your hair, it's time to blend your hair to create a seamless, layered look. Take a small section of hair from the top of one section and hold it up at a 90-degree angle. Use your scissors to cut into the hair at a slight angle, working your way down the section. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working from the back of your head to the front.

Trimming Your Hair

If you only want to trim your hair, you can skip the previous steps and go straight to trimming. Use your comb to comb your hair straight down and hold it with your fingers. Use your scissors to trim the ends of your hair, making sure to cut straight across.

Cutting Your Bangs

Cutting your own bangs can be tricky, so proceed with caution. Start by sectioning your bangs and combing them straight down. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and slide your fingers down to the desired length. Use your scissors to cut straight across. If you want a choppy look, you can cut into your bangs at an angle with your scissors.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head while you're cutting.
  • Take breaks if you get tired or frustrated.
  • Watch tutorials online to learn different cutting techniques.
  • Remember, it's always better to cut less than to cut too much.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut curly hair yourself, but be aware that curly hair shrinks when it dries, so you may want to cut your hair a little longer than you want it to be.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim your hair a little shorter or style it differently to hide the mistake.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks, but you can cut your hair more or less often depending on your hair type and length.

Hair Care After Cutting

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain your new look. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair looking and feeling its best.

Cutting your own hair can be a rewarding experience, but it takes practice and patience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut from the comfort of your own home.
