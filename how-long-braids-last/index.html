How Long Do Braids Last Tips And Care For Long Lasting Braids

Written by William Jun 12, 2023 · 7 min read

Braids are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They have been worn by people of all ages and genders, and are loved for their versatility and low maintenance. If you're considering getting braids, one of the questions you might have is, "how long do braids last?" In this article, we'll answer that question and give you tips on how to care for your braids to make them last as long as possible.

Table of Contents

  • How Long Do Braids Last?
  • How to Make Braids Last Longer
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Braiding Your Hair
  • Tips for Taking Care of Your Braids
  • FAQ About Braids
  • Final Thoughts

How Long Do Braids Last?

The lifespan of braids depends on various factors, such as the type of hair, the braiding technique, and how well they are cared for. On average, braids can last anywhere from two to eight weeks. However, some people have reported keeping their braids for up to twelve weeks.

The type of hair you have can also affect the longevity of your braids. If you have fine hair, your braids will not last as long as someone with thick hair. Fine hair is more prone to slipping out of braids, which can cause them to unravel faster.

How to Make Braids Last Longer

If you want your braids to last as long as possible, there are a few things you can do to make that happen:

  • Choose the right type of hair for your braids. If you have fine hair, consider using extensions to add volume and length to your braids.
  • Get your braids done by a professional. A skilled braider will use the right technique and tension to ensure your braids stay in place for a long time.
  • Avoid getting your braids wet. Water can cause your braids to unravel faster. If you need to wash your hair, use a dry shampoo or a gentle cleansing spray.
  • Sleep with a silk or satin scarf or pillowcase. These materials are gentler on your hair than cotton, which can cause friction and damage to your braids.

Step-by-Step Guide to Braiding Your Hair

If you're considering braiding your hair yourself, here's a step-by-step guide to help you:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into the desired size and shape.
  3. Take a small section of hair from one of the sections and divide it into three equal parts.
  4. Start braiding by crossing the right section over the middle section, then the left section over the middle section.
  5. Continue braiding until you reach the end of the section.
  6. Tie off the braid with a small elastic or a piece of thread.
  7. Repeat steps 3-6 until you have braided all the sections of your hair.

Tips for Taking Care of Your Braids

Here are some tips to help you take care of your braids:

  • Keep your scalp moisturized. Use a light oil or moisturizing spray to keep your scalp from getting dry and itchy.
  • Avoid using heavy products on your braids. Heavy products can weigh down your braids and cause them to unravel faster.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your braids. This can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Don't keep your braids in for too long. Leaving your braids in for too long can cause hair breakage and damage.

FAQ About Braids

Q: Can I wash my hair with braids?

A: Yes, you can wash your hair with braids. However, be gentle and avoid rubbing your braids too hard. Use a gentle cleansing spray or dry shampoo to avoid getting your braids too wet.

Q: How do I prevent my braids from unraveling?

A: To prevent your braids from unraveling, make sure they are secure at the base. Use a small elastic or a piece of thread to tie off each braid at the base.

Q: How long should I keep my braids in?

A: It's recommended that you keep your braids in for two to eight weeks. Leaving your braids in for too long can cause hair breakage and damage.

Final Thoughts

Braids are a great hairstyle that can last for weeks if cared for properly. Whether you're getting braids done by a professional or doing them yourself, make sure to follow the tips in this article to keep your braids looking great for as long as possible.
