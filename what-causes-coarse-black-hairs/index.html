What Causes Coarse Black Hairs

Written by Bardi Apr 16, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As someone with black hair, you might have noticed that your hair sometimes feels coarse or rough to the touch. This can be frustrating, especially if you've recently relaxed your hair and were hoping for a smooth, silky finish. In this article, we'll explore what causes coarse black hairs and what you can do to keep your hair healthy and manageable.

Causes of Coarse Black Hairs

There are several factors that can contribute to coarse black hairs, including genetics, hair care practices, and damage from chemical treatments. Let's take a closer look.

Genetics

Some people are simply born with naturally coarse hair. This is often due to genetics, which can influence the texture, thickness, and overall health of your hair. If your parents or other family members have coarse hair, you're more likely to have it as well.

Hair Care Practices

If you're not taking proper care of your hair, it can become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. This can make it feel rough and coarse to the touch. Some common hair care practices that can contribute to coarse black hairs include:

  • Using harsh shampoos that strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Skipping conditioner or using the wrong type of conditioner for your hair type
  • Excessive heat styling with flat irons, curling irons, or blow dryers
  • Over-brushing or using the wrong type of brush for your hair type

Chemical Treatments

If you've recently relaxed or chemically straightened your hair, this can also contribute to coarse black hairs. Chemical treatments can damage the hair cuticle, which is the protective outer layer of your hair. This can make your hair feel rough and brittle, and can also lead to split ends and breakage.

How to Prevent Coarse Black Hairs

If you're struggling with coarse black hairs, there are several things you can do to prevent further damage and keep your hair healthy and manageable. Here are some tips:

  • Choose a mild, sulfate-free shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Use a moisturizing conditioner every time you wash your hair
  • Avoid excessive heat styling and use a heat protectant spray if you must use heat
  • Brush your hair gently with a wide-tooth comb or boar bristle brush
  • Avoid chemical treatments or limit them to no more than once every 8-12 weeks

Step-by-Step Guide to Managing Coarse Black Hairs

If your hair is already coarse and rough, you'll need to take some additional steps to manage it and restore its natural texture. Here's a step-by-step guide to getting smooth, silky hair:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a mild, sulfate-free shampoo. Be sure to massage your scalp gently and rinse thoroughly.
  2. Apply a moisturizing conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the conditioner on for at least 5 minutes before rinsing thoroughly.
  3. Gently squeeze excess water from your hair and wrap it in a microfiber towel or t-shirt to absorb the moisture. Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  4. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to your damp hair. This will help to moisturize and protect your hair from heat styling and other damage.
  5. Blow dry your hair on low heat, using a round brush to smooth and straighten your hair. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the blow dryer.
  6. Finish with a light mist of hair oil or shine spray to add extra moisture and shine to your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy, Manageable Hair

In addition to the steps outlined above, there are several other things you can do to keep your hair healthy and manageable:

  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet rich in vitamins and nutrients
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause damage
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and other environmental factors
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water

Frequently Asked Questions about Coarse Black Hairs

Q: Can I use a deep conditioning treatment on my coarse black hair?

A: Yes, deep conditioning treatments can be very beneficial for coarse black hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for dry, damaged hair and follow the instructions carefully.

Q: How often should I wash my coarse black hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much oil your scalp produces. Some people can go several days between washes, while others need to wash their hair every day. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Q: Can I use a flat iron or curling iron on my coarse black hair?

A: Yes, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using excessive heat. You should also limit your use of heat styling tools to no more than once or twice a week.

The Importance of Hair Care

Your hair is an important part of your overall appearance, so it's important to take good care of it. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can prevent coarse black hairs and keep your hair healthy, shiny, and manageable. Remember, healthy hair starts with a healthy scalp and a good hair care routine!
