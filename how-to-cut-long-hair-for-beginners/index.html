How To Cut Long Hair For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 10, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to change up your look or save some money on haircuts, cutting your own hair can be a great option. However, if you have long hair, the task can seem daunting. But fear not! With a little guidance, cutting long hair can be a simple and satisfying experience. In this article, we'll take you through the steps of how to cut long hair for beginners, as well as some tips and hair care advice.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut Long Hair for Beginners
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Long Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting

How to Cut Long Hair for Beginners

The first step in cutting long hair is to make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp haircutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. It's also a good idea to have a handheld mirror to check the back of your head as you cut.

Before you start cutting, it's important to decide on the length you want to achieve. You can do this by measuring where you want your hair to fall or by using a reference picture. Once you've decided on the length, it's time to get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural shape of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section off your hair into manageable sections using hair clips. Start at the back of your head, and work your way forward.

Step 3: Take a small section of hair from one of the sections you've clipped up. Comb it out, and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Use your other hand to hold the scissors, and cut the hair to your desired length.

Step 4: Move onto the next section of hair, and repeat the process. Make sure to cut the hair in small sections to ensure an even cut.

Step 5: Once you've cut all the sections, take a look in the mirror to check for any uneven spots. If you notice any areas that need fixing, snip them with your scissors.

Tips for Cutting Long Hair

Tip 1: Always cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can be deceiving, and you may end up cutting too much off.

Tip 2: Start with small cuts, and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back on!

Tip 3: Use your fingers to measure the length of your hair. This will give you a more natural and even cut than using a ruler.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your ends healthy.

Q: Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

A: While it's possible to use regular scissors, it's not recommended. Haircutting scissors are specifically designed to cut hair and will give you a cleaner and more precise cut.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Make sure to use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and avoid using heat tools too often. If you're unsure about how to care for your new 'do, ask your hairstylist for advice.

Now that you know how to cut long hair for beginners, it's time to give it a try! Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With practice, you'll be a pro in no time.
