How To Change Your Hair Texture Black Male A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a black male looking to change your hair texture, you're not alone. Many men want to switch up their hair game and try something new. Changing your hair texture can be a great way to achieve a new look, but it can also be a tricky process. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about how to change your hair texture as a black male.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Decide on Your Desired Hair Texture
  • Step 2: Choose the Right Relaxer
  • Step 3: Prepare Your Hair
  • Step 4: Apply the Relaxer
  • Step 5: Rinse and Neutralize
  • Tips for Changing Your Hair Texture
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Changing Your Hair Texture

Step 1: Decide on Your Desired Hair Texture

The first step in changing your hair texture is deciding on your desired outcome. Do you want straight hair? Curly hair? Wavy hair? Once you know what you want, you can move on to choosing the right relaxing product.

Step 2: Choose the Right Relaxer

There are many different types of relaxers on the market, so it's important to choose the right one for your hair type. Look for a relaxer that is specifically designed for black hair, and make sure to read the instructions carefully before use.

Step 3: Prepare Your Hair

Before applying the relaxer, you'll need to prepare your hair. Start by washing it thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up. Next, apply a protective cream or petroleum jelly to your scalp and hairline to prevent burning.

Step 4: Apply the Relaxer

Now it's time to apply the relaxer. Follow the instructions carefully, and be sure to work quickly to avoid over-processing your hair. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward, making sure to apply the relaxer evenly throughout your hair.

Step 5: Rinse and Neutralize

Once the relaxer has been applied, it's time to rinse and neutralize your hair. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water until all of the relaxer has been removed. Next, apply a neutralizing shampoo to your hair and work it in for several minutes. Rinse your hair again, and then apply a leave-in conditioner.

Tips for Changing Your Hair Texture

  • Be sure to do a patch test before applying the relaxer to your entire head.
  • Avoid over-processing your hair by working quickly and following the instructions carefully.
  • Consider getting your hair professionally relaxed if you're not comfortable doing it yourself.
  • Be prepared for some hair breakage and shedding after the relaxing process.

FAQs

Can I change my hair texture without using a relaxer?

Yes, there are other ways to change your hair texture, such as using heat styling tools, getting a perm, or using a texturizer. However, these methods may not be suitable for all hair types and may cause damage if not done properly.

How often should I relax my hair?

You should only relax your hair every 6-8 weeks, as relaxing too frequently can cause damage and breakage.

Can I still wear my hair in its natural texture after relaxing it?

Yes, you can still wear your hair in its natural texture after relaxing it, but it may require more styling and maintenance.

Hair Care After Changing Your Hair Texture

After changing your hair texture, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too frequently. Consider getting regular trims to prevent split ends, and use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.

Changing your hair texture can be a fun and exciting way to switch up your look, but it's important to take the process seriously and follow the instructions carefully. With the right products and techniques, you can achieve the hair texture you've always wanted.
