What To Say For New Haircut A Guide For Relaxed English Language

Written by Petter Aug 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you heading to the salon or barbershop for a fresh new haircut? Knowing what to say to your stylist or barber can be intimidating, especially if English isn't your first language. But don't worry, we've got you covered with this guide on what to say for new haircut in relaxed English language. Let's dive in!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

When getting a new haircut, it's important to communicate clearly with your stylist or barber. They need to understand what you want and how you want it to look. This guide will provide you with the key phrases and vocabulary you need to express yourself in relaxed English language.

How To

The first step is to determine what kind of haircut you want. Do you want a specific style or just a trim? Look for pictures online or in magazines to help you visualize what you want. Then, practice saying the phrases out loud so you feel more confident when you're at the salon or barbershop.

Step By Step

Here are some phrases you can use when talking to your stylist or barber:

1. Describing What You Want

- "I want a trim, please."
- "I want a short/medium/long haircut."
- "I want a fade/taper/undercut."
- "I want a layered/bob/pixie cut."
- "I want my hair to be styled/curled/blow-dried."

2. Describing What You Don't Want

- "I don't want it too short."
- "I don't want it too layered."
- "I don't want bangs/fringe."
- "I don't want it to be too thin."

3. Asking for Advice

- "What do you think would look good on me?"
- "Do you think this style would suit me?"
- "What would you recommend for my hair type?"

Tips

- Bring a picture of the style you want to show your stylist or barber.
- Be specific about the length and style you want.
- Don't be afraid to ask questions or for clarification.
- Speak slowly and clearly, and don't be afraid to repeat yourself if necessary.
- If you're not happy with the result, politely ask for changes or adjustments.

FAQ

Q: What if I don't know the English words for the style I want?

A: You can bring a picture or use hand gestures to help communicate what you want. You can also try using a translation app on your phone.

Q: What if I'm not happy with the result?

A: Politely ask for changes or adjustments. Your stylist or barber wants you to be happy with your haircut, so don't be afraid to speak up.

Hair Care

Once you've gotten your new haircut, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

- Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner that's suited for your hair type.
- Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
- Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow-drying or flat ironing.
- Get regular trims to maintain your haircut.
- Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors.

Now that you know what to say for new haircut in relaxed English language, go ahead and book that appointment with confidence. Happy styling!
