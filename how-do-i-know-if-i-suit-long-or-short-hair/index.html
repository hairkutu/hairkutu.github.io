How Do I Know If I Suit Long Or Short Hair

Written by Eveline Sep 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you contemplating whether to chop off your long locks or let them grow? Choosing between long or short hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're unsure which one suits you best.

But don't worry! In this article, we'll guide you on how to determine which length flatters your face shape, hair type, and personal style. Read on to find out how you can make the right decision.

Table of Contents

  1. Identify Your Face Shape
  2. Consider Your Hair Type
  3. Think About Your Lifestyle
  4. Try Virtual Hairstyling Tools
  5. Tips for Choosing the Right Length
  6. FAQs
  7. Hair Care Tips

Step 1: Identify Your Face Shape

The first step in determining which length suits you best is to identify your face shape. There are six common face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Each shape has its features and characteristics that affect how different hairstyles look on you.

For example, if you have a round face, you may want to avoid hairstyles that add fullness to the sides, as they can make your face appear wider. Instead, opt for styles that create height and length, such as long layers or a side-swept bang.

On the other hand, if you have a square face, you may want to soften your features with hairstyles that have soft, rounded edges, such as a layered bob or a side-parted pixie cut.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Type

Your hair type also plays a significant role in determining which length suits you best. If you have thin, fine hair, a short haircut may add volume and texture to your locks. In contrast, if you have thick, coarse hair, a long hairstyle can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.

Consider your hair's texture, density, and natural wave or curl when choosing a length. You may want to consult with your stylist to determine which style will work best for your hair type.

Step 3: Think About Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle also influences which length suits you best. If you're always on-the-go and don't have much time to style your hair, a short haircut may be more manageable than long locks that require more maintenance.

On the other hand, if you enjoy experimenting with different hairstyles and have the time to style your hair, long locks may be more versatile and offer more styling options.

Step 4: Try Virtual Hairstyling Tools

If you're still unsure which length to choose, try virtual hairstyling tools. Many websites and apps allow you to upload a photo of yourself and try on different hairstyles virtually.

This can give you a better idea of how different lengths and styles look on you and help you make a more informed decision.

Tips for Choosing the Right Length

Here are some additional tips to consider when choosing between long or short hair:

  • Think about your personal style and what length makes you feel the most confident.
  • Consider your age and lifestyle when choosing a length.
  • Consult with your stylist to determine which length will work best for your hair type and face shape.
  • Don't be afraid to try something new! Hair grows back, and you can always switch up your style if you're not happy with the length you choose.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about choosing between long or short hair:

Q: Does age matter when choosing a hair length?

A: Age is not the determining factor when choosing a hair length. However, it's essential to consider your personal style, lifestyle, and face shape when making a decision.

Q: Can I still wear my hair up if I choose a short haircut?

A: Yes! Short hair can be styled in various ways, including updos, braids, and half-up styles.

Hair Care Tips

Regardless of which length you choose, it's essential to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage and dry out your locks.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.

By following these tips and choosing the right length for your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle, you can find a hairstyle that flatters your features and makes you feel confident and beautiful.
