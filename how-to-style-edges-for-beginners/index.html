How To Style Edges For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Mar 28, 2023 · 8 min read

If you’re new to styling your edges, you might be feeling a little overwhelmed. But don’t worry – with a little practice and the right techniques, you can achieve a sleek and polished look in no time. In this guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to style your edges like a pro. From the tools you’ll need to the tips and tricks that will help you get the perfect finish, we’ve got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You’ll Need
  • Prepping Your Edges
  • Choosing the Right Products
  • The Right Technique
  • Tips for Perfect Edges
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Healthy Edges

Tools You’ll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following tools on hand:

  • A fine-tooth comb
  • A brush with firm bristles
  • A hair tie or scarf
  • Edge control or gel
  • A toothbrush or edge brush

With these tools, you’ll be able to create a variety of styles and finishes.

Prepping Your Edges

Before you start styling, you’ll need to prep your edges. This means making sure they’re clean and free of product buildup. Use a gentle shampoo to cleanse your hair, and then apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your edges hydrated and healthy.

Once your hair is clean and conditioned, apply a small amount of oil or serum to your edges. This will help to smooth the hair and make it easier to style. Use your fingers or a brush to distribute the product evenly.

Choosing the Right Products

When it comes to styling your edges, the right products can make all the difference. Look for an edge control or gel that’s designed to provide a strong hold without flaking or causing buildup. Choose a product that’s formulated for your hair type and texture, and make sure it’s compatible with any other products you’re using.

Some popular edge control products include:

  • Eco Styler Gel
  • Hicks Edges
  • Creme of Nature Perfect Edges
  • Gorilla Snot Gel

Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair type and styling needs.

The Right Technique

When it comes to styling your edges, there are a few different techniques you can use. Here’s a step-by-step guide to one of the most popular methods:

  1. Start by brushing your edges forward, away from your scalp.
  2. Apply a small amount of edge control or gel to your edges, using a toothbrush or edge brush to distribute the product evenly.
  3. Use your brush to smooth your edges back toward your scalp, creating a sleek finish.
  4. Repeat as needed to create the desired level of hold and smoothness.
  5. Use a scarf or hair tie to hold your edges in place for a few minutes, allowing the product to set.
  6. Remove the scarf or hair tie and use your fingers or a brush to fluff and shape your edges.

Remember, practice makes perfect – so keep trying different techniques until you find the one that works best for you.

Tips for Perfect Edges

Here are a few tips and tricks that will help you get the perfect edge style:

  • Use a fine-tooth comb to create precise partings and shapes.
  • Apply product sparingly to avoid flaking and buildup.
  • Wrap a scarf or hair tie around your edges while they set to create a polished finish.
  • Don’t over-brush your edges – this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your edges healthy and strong.

FAQs

Q: Can I style my edges every day?

A: While it’s possible to style your edges every day, it’s not recommended. Over-styling can cause breakage and damage, so it’s important to give your edges a break to rest and recover.

Q: How long will my edge style last?

A: The longevity of your edge style will depend on a variety of factors, including the products you use, your hair type, and your daily activities. In general, most edge styles will last for a day or two – but you may need to touch up your edges throughout the day to maintain the look.

Q: Can I use regular gel to style my edges?

A: While regular gel can be used to style your edges, it’s not recommended. Regular gel can cause flaking and buildup, and it may not provide the same level of hold as a product that’s specifically designed for edges.

Hair Care for Healthy Edges

Styling your edges is just one part of maintaining healthy hair. To keep your edges in top condition, make sure you’re following a healthy hair care routine. This includes:

  • Washing your hair regularly
  • Deep conditioning your hair once a week
  • Using a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoiding excessive heat styling
  • Protecting your hair with a satin scarf or bonnet at night

By following these tips, you can keep your hair – and your edges – looking and feeling their best.
