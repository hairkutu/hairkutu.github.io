How To Cut Pixie Bangs Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Mar 27, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for a way to change up your hairstyle without breaking the bank, cutting your own pixie bangs might be the answer. Pixie bangs are short, choppy bangs that can add a touch of edginess to any haircut. However, cutting your own hair can be daunting, especially if you've never done it before. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting pixie bangs yourself, as well as tips and tricks to ensure a successful haircut.

How to Cut Pixie Bangs Yourself

Before we get started, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp while cutting.

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by separating your bangs from the rest of your hair. Use a comb to create a triangle-shaped section at the front of your head. The point of the triangle should be in the middle of your forehead, and the base should be at the top of your temples.

Step 2: Determine the Length

Decide how short you want your bangs to be. It's always better to start longer and trim more later, so be conservative with your first cut. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Don't worry if it's not perfect â you can always go back and trim more later.

Step 3: Create Texture

Using your scissors, cut into the ends of your bangs to create texture. This will give your bangs a choppy, edgy look. Start at the ends and work your way up, making small snips to create texture.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

Use your comb to check for evenness. Comb your bangs to the side and check for any longer pieces. Trim as necessary to make sure both sides are even.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Once you're happy with the length and texture of your bangs, style them as desired. You can blow dry them straight, curl them, or leave them natural. Play around with different styles to find what works best for you.

Tips for Cutting Pixie Bangs Yourself

1. Start with clean, dry hair. It's easier to cut hair when it's not wet or greasy.

2. Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can create split ends and make it harder to get a clean cut.

3. Take your time. Cutting hair can be nerve-wracking, but rushing can lead to mistakes.

4. Cut less than you think you need to. It's always easier to trim more later than to try and fix a haircut that's too short.

5. Don't be afraid to seek professional help. If you're not confident in your own abilities, it's always better to go to a salon and have a professional cut your hair.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if it's curly. However, keep in mind that curly hair can shrink up when it dries, so be conservative with your first cut.

How often should I trim my pixie bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. On average, you should plan on trimming your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go to a salon and have a professional fix it. Alternatively, you can try styling your hair in a way that hides the mistake, such as sweeping your bangs to the side.

Hair Care for Pixie Bangs

Now that you've cut your own pixie bangs, it's important to take care of them. Here are some tips:

1. Wash your hair regularly. Clean hair is healthier hair.

2. Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

3. Avoid using heat tools too often. Heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

4. Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting your own pixie bangs can be a fun and empowering experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut without ever leaving your house. Just remember to take your time, be conservative with your first cut, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
