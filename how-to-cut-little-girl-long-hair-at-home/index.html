How To Cut Little Girl Long Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Apr 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a simple and cost-effective way to give your little girl a fresh haircut, then cutting her hair at home might be the way to go. While it may seem daunting at first, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve professional-looking results without leaving your house. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps on how to cut little girl long hair at home, providing tips and tricks along the way.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Little Girl Long Hair at Home
  • Tools You'll Need
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools on hand:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Cape or towel to cover your child's clothes
  • Chair or stool for your child to sit on

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your little girl's long hair at home:

Step 1: Prep

Start by washing and drying your child's hair. Comb out any tangles and part the hair as you normally would. Put a cape or towel around your child's neck to protect their clothes.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Section the hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair ties to secure each section.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Start with the back sections. Spray the hair with water to dampen it. Comb it straight down and cut across the hair in a straight line. Use your fingers or a comb to guide the scissors and ensure evenness. Cut off any split ends or damaged hair.

Step 4: Cut the Front

Move on to the front sections. Comb the hair straight down and cut it in a straight line, angling the scissors slightly towards the center of the face to create layers. Be sure to cut the hair longer than you want it, as you can always go back and trim more if needed.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Blend the layers by combing the hair straight down and cutting in a zigzag pattern. This will create a natural-looking blend between the layers.

Step 6: Trim the Bangs

If your child has bangs, comb them straight down and trim them to the desired length. Be sure to cut them slightly longer than you want them, as they will appear shorter once they spring back into place.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

After you've finished cutting, check the hair for evenness. Comb it straight down and look for any uneven spots. If you find any, trim them with the scissors.

Step 8: Style the Hair

Style your child's hair as desired. You can blow-dry it or let it air-dry. Use a comb or brush to style it into place.

Tips and Tricks

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take breaks if your child gets restless.
  • Practice on a small section of hair first to get the hang of it.
  • Don't pull the hair too tightly while cutting, as this can result in an uneven cut.
  • Be patient and take your time to ensure a precise cut.

FAQ

Can I cut my child's hair with clippers?

Yes, you can use clippers to cut your child's hair. However, be sure to use the appropriate guard size to achieve the desired length.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim more hair or take your child to a professional stylist to fix it.

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on how fast their hair grows and how quickly it becomes damaged. Generally, it's recommended to trim hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your child's hair, be sure to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heating tools like curling irons or straighteners too often. Brush the hair regularly to prevent tangles and breakage.

Now that you know how to cut little girl long hair at home, you can save time and money while still achieving a professional-looking haircut. Remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and follow these steps for the best results.
