How To Figure Out What Haircut Suits You

Written by Luffy Jul 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of getting a haircut that doesn't suit your style, face shape, or personality? It's time to figure out what haircut suits you best. There are a few things you should consider when choosing a haircut, like your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle. In this article, we will give you step-by-step guidance on how to figure out what haircut suits you.

Table of Contents

  • Take Your Hair Texture Into Account

Consider Your Face Shape

The first thing you should consider when choosing a haircut is your face shape. Different haircuts flatter different face shapes. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Round face: try a layered haircut or a side-swept bang to elongate your face.
  • Square face: go for a soft, layered haircut or a side part to soften your jawline.
  • Heart-shaped face: try a chin-length bob or long layers to balance out your forehead.
  • Oval face: lucky you, you can pull off almost any haircut!

Remember that these are just general guidelines, and you should always go with what makes you feel confident and beautiful.

Take Your Hair Texture Into Account

Your hair texture can also play a role in determining what haircut suits you best. Do you have straight, wavy, or curly hair? Is your hair fine or thick? Here are some tips:

  • Straight hair: try a blunt bob or a long, straight haircut.
  • Wavy hair: go for a layered haircut or a shoulder-length cut with layers.
  • Curly hair: try a layered haircut or a short, curly bob.
  • Fine hair: opt for a blunt bob or a pixie cut.
  • Thick hair: go for a layered, textured haircut or a long, straight haircut.

Remember that your hair texture can change with the weather, so you may need to adjust your haircut accordingly.

Think About Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle can also play a role in determining what haircut suits you best. Are you a busy mom who needs a low-maintenance haircut? Or are you a fashionista who loves experimenting with different hairstyles? Here are some tips:

  • Low-maintenance: opt for a short, easy-to-style haircut.
  • Active lifestyle: go for a ponytail-friendly haircut, like a shoulder-length cut or a bob.
  • Career-focused: try a classic, polished haircut that will look professional in the office.
  • Fashion-forward: experiment with different haircuts and styles to express your personality.

Remember that your haircut should work with your lifestyle, not against it.

Match Your Haircut to Your Personality

Your personality can also play a role in determining what haircut suits you best. Do you have a bold, edgy personality? Or are you more laid-back and relaxed? Here are some tips:

  • Edgy: go for a choppy, textured haircut or a bold, asymmetrical cut.
  • Classic: try a polished, timeless haircut like a bob or a layered cut.
  • Bohemian: go for a long, wavy haircut or a messy, undone bob.
  • Minimalist: opt for a simple, low-maintenance haircut like a pixie cut or a blunt bob.

Remember that your haircut should reflect your personality and make you feel confident and beautiful.

Tips for Choosing the Right Haircut

Here are some additional tips for choosing the right haircut:

  • Bring pictures to your hair appointment to show your stylist what you have in mind.
  • Ask your stylist for their professional opinion on what haircut would look best on you.
  • Consider your hair's natural texture and how much time you're willing to spend styling it.
  • Try out different haircuts with virtual makeover tools or by experimenting with wigs.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about choosing the right haircut:

How do I know if a haircut will look good on me?

Consider your face shape, hair texture, lifestyle, and personality when choosing a haircut. You can also bring pictures to your stylist and ask for their professional opinion.

What haircut is best for fine hair?

A blunt bob or a pixie cut can work well on fine hair.

What haircut is best for thick hair?

A layered or textured haircut can help thin out thick hair and make it more manageable.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've chosen the right haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your haircut looking fresh.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather with a hat or scarf.

By following these hair care tips and choosing the right haircut for you, you can achieve a beautiful, healthy, and stylish look that reflects your personality and lifestyle.
