How To Find The Right Short Haircut For You

Written by Eveline Apr 10, 2023 Â· 12 min read
How to Find the Right Short Haircut for You | Tips and FAQ | Hair Care

Are you thinking of getting a short haircut but don't know where to start? Finding the right short haircut for you can be overwhelming, but don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll provide you with tips and answers to frequently asked questions to help you find the perfect short haircut for your face shape and hair type.

Table of Contents

  • Determining Your Face Shape
  • Understanding Your Hair Type
  • Popular Short Haircuts
  • Finding the Right Short Haircut for You
  • Styling Tips for Short Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Short Hair

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step to finding the right short haircut for you is determining your face shape. This will help you choose a haircut that flatters your features and enhances your natural beauty. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and long.

To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and examine your face in the mirror. Take note of the widest part of your face, the length of your face, and the shape of your jawline. You can also use a measuring tape to measure the width and length of your face.

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, you're in luck! This is considered the most versatile face shape, and you can pull off almost any short haircut. Pixie cuts, bobs, and lobs all look great on oval faces.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, you want to create the illusion of length and height. Avoid cuts that add width to your face, such as blunt bobs or straight-across bangs. Instead, opt for longer layers or asymmetrical cuts that add angles to your face.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, you want to soften your angles and create a more rounded look. Consider a layered bob or a shaggy pixie cut that adds texture and movement to your hair.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, you want to balance out your wider forehead with your narrower jawline. A textured pixie cut or a bob with side-swept bangs can achieve this balance.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, you want to create the illusion of width at your forehead and chin. A chin-length bob or a layered pixie cut with volume at the crown can flatter your features.

Long Face Shape

If you have a long face shape, you want to add width and volume to your hair. A layered bob or a curly pixie cut can achieve this balance and enhance your natural beauty.

Understanding Your Hair Type

The second step to finding the right short haircut for you is understanding your hair type. Your hair type affects how your hair looks and behaves, and can impact the type of short haircut that will work best for you.

There are four basic hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and kinky. Within each hair type, there are different textures and thicknesses that can impact how your hair looks and behaves.

Straight Hair

Straight hair is typically shiny, smooth, and silky. It can be fine or thick and is easy to manage. If you have straight hair, you can pull off a variety of short haircuts, including blunt bobs and sleek pixie cuts.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair has a natural texture that creates movement and volume. It can be fine or thick and is often easy to style. If you have wavy hair, you may want to consider a layered bob or a long pixie cut with textured layers.

Curly Hair

Curly hair has a defined curl pattern that can range from loose waves to tight coils. It can be fine or thick and is often prone to dryness and frizz. If you have curly hair, you may want to consider a curly pixie cut or a layered bob with textured layers.

Kinky Hair

Kinky hair has a tight curl pattern that can range from coils to zigzags. It is often coarse and prone to dryness and breakage. If you have kinky hair, you may want to consider a tapered pixie cut or a curly bob with layers and volume.

Popular Short Haircuts

Now that you understand your face shape and hair type, it's time to explore some popular short haircuts. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Pixie cut: A short, layered cut that is typically longer on top and shorter on the sides and back.
  • Bob: A chin-length or shoulder-length cut that can be blunt or layered.
  • Lob: A longer bob that falls just above the shoulders.
  • Shag: A layered cut with choppy, textured layers and lots of volume.
  • Crop: A very short cut that is typically buzzed or shaved.

Finding the Right Short Haircut for You

Now that you have a better understanding of your face shape, hair type, and popular short haircuts, it's time to find the right haircut for you. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Consider your lifestyle and how much time you want to spend styling your hair.
  • Bring pictures of short haircuts you like to your stylist for inspiration.
  • Ask your stylist for their professional opinion on what haircut will work best for you.
  • Experiment with different haircuts and styles until you find the perfect one.

Styling Tips for Short Hair

Once you've found the right short haircut for you, it's important to know how to style it. Here are a few tips to help you get the most out of your short hair:

  • Use the right products for your hair type to enhance your natural texture and add volume.
  • Invest in a good quality blow dryer and round brush to create volume and movement.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques, such as curling or flat ironing, to create different looks.
  • Visit your stylist regularly for trims and touch-ups to keep your haircut looking fresh.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will a short haircut make my face look rounder?

A: It depends on the cut. Some short haircuts, such as blunt bobs, can make your face look rounder. However, there are many short haircuts, such as layered bobs or pixie cuts, that can create angles and lengthen your face.

Q: Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail with a short haircut?

A: It depends on the length of your hair. If your hair is long enough, you can still wear it in a ponytail or updo. However, if your hair is very short, you may not be able to pull it back.

Q: Will a short haircut work with my thick hair?

A: Absolutely! In fact, many short haircuts look great on thick hair because they can add movement and texture. Talk to your stylist about the best short haircut for your hair type and texture.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Short hair requires different maintenance than long hair. Here are a few tips to keep your short hair looking healthy and vibrant:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent oil buildup.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Visit your stylist every 4-6 weeks for trims and touch-ups.

Now that you know how to find the right short haircut for you, it's time to book your appointment and rock your new look!
