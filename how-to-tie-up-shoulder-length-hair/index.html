How To Tie Up Shoulder Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 23, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have shoulder length hair, you may sometimes find it difficult to keep it out of your face or off your neck. Fortunately, there are many ways to tie up your hair that are both functional and stylish. In this post, we’ll be sharing some tips and tricks for tying up your shoulder length hair, as well as a step by step guide to three different hairstyles.

Table of Contents

  • Tips for Tying Up Shoulder Length Hair
  • Three Hairstyles for Shoulder Length Hair
  • FAQs About Tying Up Shoulder Length Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Shoulder Length Hair

Tips for Tying Up Shoulder Length Hair

Before we dive into the hairstyles, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is wet or oily, it may be more difficult to style and may not hold its shape as well.
  • Use hair ties and clips that are gentle on your hair. Avoid using rubber bands or clips with metal clasps, as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for your hair type and face shape.
  • Accessorize! Adding a scarf, headband, or hairpin can take a simple hairstyle to the next level.

Three Hairstyles for Shoulder Length Hair

1. Half-Up Half-Down

The half-up half-down hairstyle is a classic and versatile look that works well for shoulder length hair. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Start by sectioning off the top half of your hair. Use a comb to create a straight part from ear to ear.
  2. Gather the top section of hair and secure it with a hair tie or clip. You can leave some face-framing pieces loose if you like.
  3. Optional: add a hair accessory like a clip or headband for extra style.

2. Low Ponytail

A low ponytail is a simple but chic hairstyle that’s perfect for keeping your hair out of your face. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Gather all of your hair at the nape of your neck.
  2. Secure with a hair tie or clip. You can wrap a small section of hair around the hair tie to hide it.
  3. Optional: pull a few pieces of hair loose around your face for a more relaxed, effortless look.

3. Messy Bun

The messy bun is a go-to hairstyle for many people, and it works especially well for shoulder length hair. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Gather your hair into a high ponytail at the crown of your head.
  2. Twist the ponytail and wrap it around itself to create a bun shape.
  3. Secure the bun with a hair tie or bobby pins. Don’t worry about making it perfect – the messier, the better!
  4. Optional: pull a few pieces of hair loose around your face or at the back of your neck for a more relaxed look.

FAQs About Tying Up Shoulder Length Hair

What kind of hair ties are best for shoulder length hair?

Look for hair ties that are gentle on your hair, such as fabric scrunchies or spiral hair ties. Avoid using rubber bands or clips with metal clasps, as they can cause damage and breakage.

How can I make my hairstyles last longer?

Using a hairspray or texturizing spray can help your hairstyles last longer. You can also try sleeping with your hair in a loose bun or braid to help it maintain its shape overnight.

What should I do if my hair is too slippery to hold a hairstyle?

If your hair is too slippery, try using a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add some grip. You can also try backcombing or teasing your hair at the roots to create more texture and hold.

Hair Care Tips for Shoulder Length Hair

In addition to tying up your hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it healthy and strong. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling as much as possible. If you do use heat tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

With these tips and hairstyles, you’ll be able to keep your shoulder length hair looking great no matter what the occasion!
