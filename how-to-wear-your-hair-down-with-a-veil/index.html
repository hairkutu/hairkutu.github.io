How To Wear Your Hair Down With A Veil

Written by Robby Apr 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you a bride-to-be who wants to wear her hair down for her big day? Do you want to pair it with a beautiful veil, but don't know how to make it work? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll show you how to wear your hair down with a veil, step by step.

Table of Contents:

  • Hair Prep
  • Veil Placement
  • Types of Veils
  • Hair Accessories
  • Hair Care
  • FAQ

Hair Prep

The first step to wearing your hair down with a veil is to make sure your hair is prepped correctly. Your hair should be clean and dry but not too clean. Washing your hair the day before the wedding is ideal as it will give your hair some texture to work with.

Use a volumizing product to give your hair some lift and body. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to create a loose wave. If you have naturally curly hair, you can use a diffuser to enhance your curls.

Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron to create loose curls. Brush through your curls to create a soft, natural look.

Veil Placement

Once your hair is prepped, it's time to place your veil. The placement of your veil will depend on the type of veil you have.

Cathedral Veil

A cathedral veil is a long, dramatic veil that extends beyond the train of your gown. To place a cathedral veil, position the comb at the crown of your head and allow the veil to flow behind you. This will create a stunning, dramatic effect.

Mantilla Veil

A mantilla veil is a circular veil that is draped over the head and shoulders. To place a mantilla veil, position the comb at the crown of your head and allow the veil to drape over your shoulders. This will create a romantic, elegant look.

Blusher Veil

A blusher veil is a short veil that covers your face. To place a blusher veil, position the comb at the crown of your head and allow the veil to cover your face. This is a traditional look that is perfect for a classic wedding.

Types of Veils

There are many types of veils to choose from, and each one will give you a different look. Here are some of the most popular types of veils:

Cathedral Veil

A cathedral veil is a long, dramatic veil that extends beyond the train of your gown. This type of veil is perfect for a formal wedding and will create a stunning, dramatic effect.

Mantilla Veil

A mantilla veil is a circular veil that is draped over the head and shoulders. This type of veil is perfect for a romantic, elegant wedding.

Blusher Veil

A blusher veil is a short veil that covers your face. This type of veil is perfect for a classic wedding and will create a traditional, timeless look.

Hair Accessories

If you want to add some extra sparkle to your look, you can add some hair accessories. A hairpin or hair comb can add some extra glamour to your hairstyle. Just make sure to choose something that complements your veil and gown.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair leading up to your wedding day. Here are some tips to keep your locks healthy:

  • Get regular trims to keep your ends healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.

FAQ

Can I wear my hair down with any type of veil?

Yes, you can wear your hair down with any type of veil. The placement of the veil will depend on the type of veil you have.

Do I need to use hairspray to keep my hair in place?

If you have fine or thin hair, you may want to use some hairspray to keep your hair in place. However, if you have thick or curly hair, your hair should hold the style without hairspray.

Can I wear a veil with short hair?

Yes, you can wear a veil with short hair. Just make sure to choose a veil that complements your hairstyle.

Now that you know how to wear your hair down with a veil, you're ready to rock your wedding day look. Remember to take good care of your hair leading up to your big day and choose a veil that complements your gown and hairstyle. With these tips, you'll look and feel your best on your wedding day.
