How To Use Thinning Shears On Your Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy May 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning shears are a handy tool to have in your hair care kit, especially if you have thick hair or bangs that tend to get bulky. Thinning shears can help you achieve a softer, more textured look by removing some of the bulk from your hair without sacrificing length. In this guide, we'll show you how to use thinning shears on your bangs for a natural, effortless look.

Description

Thinning shears, also known as texturizing shears, are scissors with teeth on one or both blades. The teeth are designed to cut some of the hair while leaving some strands uncut, creating a softer, more textured look. Thinning shears are a great tool to have if you have thick hair, curly hair, or bangs that tend to get bulky. They can help you achieve a more natural, effortless look by removing some of the bulk from your hair without sacrificing length.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Your Bangs

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before using thinning shears on your bangs, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will ensure that you have a clear view of your bangs and that the hair is in its natural state.

Step 2: Section Your Bangs

Using a comb, section off your bangs from the rest of your hair. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you only thin out the necessary parts.

Step 3: Hold the Thinning Shears Properly

Hold the thinning shears with your dominant hand, and your comb with the other. The teeth of the thinning shears should be facing upwards, and the blades should be parallel to your bangs.

Step 4: Start Thinning Out Your Bangs

Begins by placing the thinning shears a few centimeters from the roots of your bangs. Hold the shears at a slight angle, and make small cuts down the length of your bangs. Be sure to cut in a zigzag pattern to avoid creating a harsh, blunt line.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After making a few cuts, check your progress in the mirror. You should see a softer, more textured look to your bangs. If you need to remove more bulk, repeat steps 4 and 5 until you achieve the desired look.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Your Bangs

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always remove more hair, but you can't put it back.
  • Make sure to cut in a zigzag pattern to avoid creating a harsh, blunt line.
  • Don't use thinning shears on wet hair. This can cause the hair to clump together and create an uneven cut.
  • Use thinning shears sparingly. Overusing them can lead to a choppy, uneven look.

FAQs

Can I use thinning shears on my own?

While it's possible to use thinning shears on your own, it's best to have a professional stylist do it. They can ensure that the cuts are even and that you achieve the desired look.

Can I use thinning shears on any type of hair?

Thinning shears work best on thick or curly hair. If you have thin or fine hair, it's best to avoid using thinning shears, as they can create an uneven look.

Hair Care Tips

Using thinning shears on your bangs can help you achieve a natural, effortless look. However, it's important to take care of your hair afterward to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using thinning shears correctly, you can achieve a natural, textured look for your bangs. Remember to start with small cuts, use a zigzag pattern, and check your progress often to achieve the desired look.
