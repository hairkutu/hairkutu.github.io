How To Cut Your Own Permed Hair Step By Step Guide

How to Cut Your Own Permed Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, it's important to note that cutting your own hair can be tricky, especially if you've never done it before. It's best to start with small snips and work your way up slowly. If you're not sure about anything, it's better to leave it to the professionals. With that said, here's how to cut your own permed hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. Comb it thoroughly to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to keep them separate.

Step 3: Take one section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, then use hair shears to cut off the desired length. Start with small snips and work your way up.

Step 4: Repeat step 3 with the other sections of hair.

Step 5: Check your hair in the mirror and make any final adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Permed Hair

Tip 1: Invest in a good pair of hair shears. Using regular scissors can damage your hair and cause split ends.

Tip 2: Use a comb to make sure your hair is straight before cutting.

Tip 3: Start with small snips and work your way up. It's better to take off too little than too much.

Tip 4: Cut your hair in a well-lit area to avoid any mistakes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I cut my permed hair while it's wet?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can stretch, making it difficult to get an accurate cut.

Q: How often should I cut my permed hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your style loses its shape. On average, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Q: What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

A: Don't panic! Mistakes happen, even to professional hairstylists. If you make a mistake, try to even it out on the other side. If it's a major mistake, seek the help of a professional.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Permed Hair

After cutting your own permed hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its shape and health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent further damage.
  • Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you should be able to cut your own permed hair successfully. Remember to take your time and start with small snips. And if you're not sure about anything, it's always better to seek the help of a professional. Happy cutting!
