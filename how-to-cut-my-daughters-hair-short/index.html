How To Cut My Daughter s Hair Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 03, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If your daughter has been begging you to cut her hair short, but you're not quite sure how to do it, you're in the right place. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your daughter's hair short. From choosing the right hairstyle to actually cutting the hair, we've got you covered. So, let's get started!

  • FAQs

Choose the Right Hairstyle

Before you start cutting your daughter's hair, you need to decide on the right hairstyle. There are many short hairstyles for girls, including the pixie cut, the bob, and the lob. Take some time to research different hairstyles to find the one that will suit your daughter's face shape and hair type.

Some popular short hairstyles for girls include:

  • Pixie cut
  • Bob
  • Lob
  • Shaggy bob
  • Asymmetrical bob

Gather Your Supplies

Once you've chosen the right hairstyle, it's time to gather your supplies. You'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Cape or towel to protect clothing

Prep Your Daughter's Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to prep your daughter's hair. Start by washing and drying her hair. Then, comb out any tangles and knots. If your daughter has curly hair, you may want to wet it down with a spray bottle to make it easier to work with.

Section Your Daughter's Hair

Next, it's time to section your daughter's hair. Use clips to separate the top and bottom sections of her hair. Then, create a center part from the front of her hairline to the back of her head. Use clips to separate the left and right sections of her hair.

Cut Your Daughter's Hair

Now it's time to cut your daughter's hair. Start by cutting the hair in the back of her head. Use your comb to section off a small piece of hair and hold it between your fingers. Then, cut the hair straight across. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've cut all the hair in the back.

Next, move on to the sides of her head. Start at the front and work your way back. Use your comb to section off small pieces of hair and cut them straight across. Make sure to blend the hair in the back with the hair on the sides.

Finally, it's time to cut the top of her hair. Use your comb to section off small pieces of hair and cut them straight across. Make sure to blend the top of her hair with the sides and back.

Style Your Daughter's Hair

Once you've cut your daughter's hair, it's time to style it. Use your comb to style her hair the way she likes it. If she wants a sleek look, use a flat iron. If she wants a more textured look, use a curling iron or texturizing product.

Clean Up

After you've finished cutting your daughter's hair, it's important to clean up. Sweep up any hair on the floor and put it in the trash. Wash your daughter's hair to remove any loose hair. Finally, clean your scissors and other tools with soap and water.

Tips for Cutting Your Daughter's Hair

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to a shorter length
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends
  • Don't cut too much hair at once
  • Make sure to blend the hair on the sides and back with the top
  • Take breaks if your daughter gets antsy or uncomfortable

FAQs

How often should I cut my daughter's hair?

It depends on how quickly her hair grows and how short she wants it. Generally, it's recommended to cut hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the length and shape.

What if I make a mistake while cutting her hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your daughter's hair, don't panic. You can always take her to a professional stylist to fix the mistake.

Hair Care after Cutting

After you've cut your daughter's hair, it's important to take care of it. Make sure to wash and condition her hair regularly. Use a detangling spray to prevent tangles and knots. Finally, trim any split ends as they appear to keep her hair healthy.

Now that you know how to cut your daughter's hair short, it's time to give it a try. Remember to take your time and be patient. With a little practice, you'll be a pro in no time!
