How To Cut Your Own Low Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of spending money and time at the barbershop? Do you want to learn how to cut your own low fade? Look no further! In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to achieve a low fade haircut at home. We'll also include tips and tricks to help you achieve the best results.

Why a Low Fade?

A low fade is a popular hairstyle that features short hair on the sides and back, gradually blending into longer hair on top. This haircut is versatile and can be customized to suit your personal style. A low fade is also easy to maintain and can be done at home with the right tools and techniques.

What You'll Need

Before you start cutting your own low fade, you'll need to gather the necessary tools:

  • Clipper with adjustable blade
  • Comb
  • Scissors
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Mirror
  • Water spray bottle
  • Hair products (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve a low fade haircut at home:

Step 1: Choose the Right Length

Decide how short you want your hair to be on the sides and back. Use the clipper with the adjustable blade to achieve the desired length. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one until you get the desired length.

Step 2: Create a Guideline

Use the clipper without a guard to create a guideline around the perimeter of your hairline. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up to the temples. This will help you achieve a clean and even fade.

Step 3: Blend the Sides

Attach a guard to the clipper and blend the hair on the sides and back. Start with the longest guard and work your way down to a shorter one. Use a comb to lift the hair and clipper to blend the hair.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Use scissors to cut the hair on top to the desired length. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly.

Step 5: Blend the Top and Sides

Use the clipper without a guard to blend the hair on the top and sides. Start at the crown of the head and work your way down to the temples. Blend the hair evenly to achieve a seamless transition between the top and sides.

Step 6: Clean up the Hairline

Use the clipper or scissors to clean up the hairline around the ears and neck. Use the comb to create a natural hairline that complements your face shape.

Step 7: Style as Desired

Apply hair products, such as pomade or wax, to style your hair as desired. Use your fingers or a comb to create your desired look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from different angles.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting your own hair can be challenging, but with practice, you'll get better.
  • Ask for help if you need it. It can be helpful to have someone else check your work and provide feedback.

FAQ

Can I achieve a low fade without clippers?

While clippers are the most efficient tool for achieving a low fade, you can also use scissors or a razor. However, this may require more time and skill.

How often should I cut my low fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade grows out. Typically, a low fade should be cut every 2-4 weeks to maintain its shape.

Can I achieve a low fade on curly hair?

Yes! A low fade can be achieved on any hair type, including curly hair. However, it may require different techniques and tools. Consult with a hairstylist or do research to find the best approach for your specific hair type.

Hair Care

After cutting your own low fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or flat irons, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use hair products, such as leave-in conditioners or oils, to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Visit a hairstylist every few months to trim your hair and keep it looking its best.

By following these tips and using the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a low fade haircut at home. Remember to be patient and take your time, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With practice, you'll be able to master the art of cutting your own hair and achieve the perfect low fade every time.
