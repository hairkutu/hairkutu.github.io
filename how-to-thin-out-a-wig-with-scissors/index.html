How To Thin Out A Wig With Scissors

Written by Eveline Sep 13, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Wigs have become an essential part of our lives, especially for those who love experimenting with their hairstyles. However, sometimes wigs can be too thick, which can make them look unnatural. In such cases, thinning out the wig can make it more manageable and natural-looking. In this article, we will guide you on how to thin out a wig with scissors.

Description

Thinning out a wig means reducing the volume of hair on the wig to make it look natural and manageable. This technique is used to make wigs look more realistic and blend better with natural hair. It involves cutting some of the hair from the wig's wefts or layers to create a more natural-looking hairstyle.

How to Thin Out a Wig with Scissors

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin out a wig with scissors:

Step 1: Prepare Your Tools

The first step is to gather all the tools you need. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a wig stand. Ensure that your scissors are sharp enough to cut through the wig's hair easily.

Step 2: Detangle the Wig

Before you start thinning out the wig, you need to detangle it. Use a wide-tooth comb to remove any knots or tangles in the wig's hair. This will make it easier to separate the hair into sections and cut it evenly.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Section the wig's hair into small sections using clips or hair ties. This will help you control the amount of hair you cut and ensure that you cut the hair evenly.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Hold the section of hair you want to thin out between your fingers and cut it with the scissors. Start by cutting small sections of hair and gradually increase the amount of hair you cut as you get more comfortable with the technique.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you have thinned out a section of hair, comb it to check your progress. If you feel that you have not thinned out enough hair, you can go back and cut some more.

Step 6: Repeat the Process

Repeat the process of sectioning the hair, cutting it, and checking your progress until you have thinned out the entire wig. Be patient and take your time to ensure that the wig looks natural and even.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out a wig:

  • Start by cutting small sections of hair and gradually increase the amount of hair you cut as you get more comfortable with the technique.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure that you cut the hair evenly.
  • Be patient and take your time to ensure that the wig looks natural and even.
  • Detangle the wig before you start cutting to make it easier to separate the hair into sections.
  • Check your progress after every section to ensure that you are thinning out the hair evenly.

FAQ

Can I thin out a wig with regular scissors?

While you can use regular scissors to thin out a wig, it is recommended that you use scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Hair cutting scissors are sharper and more precise, which will make it easier to cut the hair evenly.

How much hair should I thin out from the wig?

The amount of hair you should thin out from the wig depends on how thick the wig is and how natural you want it to look. Start by cutting small sections of hair and gradually increase the amount of hair you cut as you get more comfortable with the technique.

Can I thin out a synthetic wig?

Yes, you can thin out a synthetic wig, but you need to be careful not to damage the wig. Synthetic wigs are made from synthetic fibers that can melt or fray if they are cut with the wrong scissors or at the wrong temperature. It is recommended that you use scissors specifically designed for cutting synthetic hair.

Hair Care

Thinning out a wig can make it more manageable and natural-looking, but it is important to take care of the wig to ensure that it lasts longer. Here are some tips for taking care of your wig:

  • Store the wig in a cool, dry place when you are not wearing it.
  • Wash the wig regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Brush the wig gently to prevent tangling.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools on the wig, as this can damage the synthetic fibers.

Thinning out a wig with scissors can be a daunting task, but with patience and practice, you can achieve a natural-looking hairstyle. Remember to use sharp scissors, section the hair, and check your progress regularly to ensure that the wig looks even and natural. With proper care, your wig will last longer and continue to look great.
