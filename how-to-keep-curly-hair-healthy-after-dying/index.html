How To Keep Curly Hair Healthy After Dying

Written by Bardi Sep 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you someone who loves to experiment with hair colors but is worried about the damage it may cause to your naturally curly hair? If yes, then you have come to the right place. In this article, we will guide you on how to keep curly hair healthy after dying. We have also included some tips and FAQ's to help you maintain your curly hair's health and shine.

Step 1: Choose the Right Hair Dye

The first step towards keeping your curly hair healthy after dying is to choose the right hair dye. Always opt for a hair dye that is ammonia-free and contains natural ingredients like henna, indigo, or beetroot. These ingredients will not only give your hair a beautiful color but also provide nourishment to your hair.

Step 2: Pre-Dye Hair Care

Before you dye your hair, it is essential to prepare your hair for the process. Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. This will help remove any build-up and make your hair more receptive to the hair dye. Additionally, you can also apply a hair mask a day before dying your hair. This will help strengthen your hair and prevent any damage caused by the hair dye.

Step 3: Post-Dye Hair Care

After dying your hair, it is crucial to take care of your hair to prevent any damage. Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Additionally, use a leave-in conditioner to provide extra nourishment to your hair. It is also recommended to avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Step 4: Regular Trimming

Regular trimming is essential for maintaining healthy curly hair after dying. Trimming your hair every six to eight weeks will prevent any split ends and breakage, helping your hair grow longer and healthier. Additionally, trimming your hair will also help maintain the shape of your curls.

Step 5: Protect Your Hair from Heat

Excessive heat exposure can cause significant damage to your curly hair. Therefore, it is essential to protect your hair from heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling wands. If you must use these tools, use a heat protectant spray or serum to minimize the damage.

Step 6: Use Hair Masks and Oils

Using hair masks and oils is an excellent way to keep your curly hair healthy after dying. Hair masks help restore moisture to your hair and prevent any dryness caused by the hair dye. Additionally, using hair oils like coconut, jojoba, or argan oil can also provide nourishment to your hair and prevent any damage caused by styling tools.

Tips to Keep Curly Hair Healthy After Dying

  • Use cold water to rinse your hair after dyeing to prevent any damage caused by hot water.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent any friction and damage to your hair while you sleep.
  • Avoid using hair elastics that are too tight, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Include protein-rich foods like eggs, fish, and nuts in your diet to promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ's

Q. How long should I wait before dyeing my hair again?

A. It is recommended to wait at least six to eight weeks before dyeing your hair again. This will give your hair enough time to recover and prevent any damage caused by excessive dyeing.

Q. Can I dye my curly hair at home?

A. Yes, you can dye your curly hair at home. However, it is essential to follow the instructions carefully and choose the right hair dye to prevent any damage to your hair.

Q. Can I use hair oils on my hair after dyeing?

A. Yes, you can use hair oils on your hair after dyeing. In fact, using hair oils can help nourish your hair and prevent any damage caused by the hair dye.

Hair Care Routine for Curly Hair

Following a proper hair care routine is essential for maintaining healthy curly hair after dying. Here is a simple hair care routine you can follow:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to provide extra nourishment to your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently.
  • Use hair masks and oils regularly to prevent any damage caused by styling tools and hair dye.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently.
  • Include protein-rich foods in your diet to promote healthy hair growth.

Following these simple steps can help you maintain healthy, shiny, and beautiful curly hair after dyeing. Remember, healthy hair is happy hair!
