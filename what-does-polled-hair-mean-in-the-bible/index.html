Description

Polled hair is a term used in the Bible to describe hair that is shaved or cut very short. This term is used in both the Old and New Testaments of the Bible. In the Old Testament, the term is often used in reference to the Nazirite vow, which required a person to abstain from wine, let their hair grow long, and avoid contact with dead bodies. When the person completed the vow, they would shave their head, which was known as polling the hair.

In the New Testament, the term is used in reference to the soldiers who mocked and beat Jesus before his crucifixion. The soldiers are described as having "plaited a crown of thorns, and put it on his head, and they put on him a purple robe, And said, Hail, King of the Jews!" (John 19:2-3, KJV). The soldiers then proceeded to strike Jesus on the head with a reed, causing the thorns to pierce his skin and draw blood.

How to

If you are interested in polling your hair, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it is important to have the right tools. You will need clippers or scissors, and possibly a comb or brush to help guide the hair as you cut it. It is also important to have a clear idea of the length you want to cut your hair to, and to be careful not to cut too short or too unevenly.

Step by Step

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Decide on the length you want to cut your hair to.
  3. Use clippers or scissors to cut your hair to the desired length, being careful not to cut too short or too unevenly.
  4. Use a comb or brush to guide the hair as you cut, if necessary.
  5. Be sure to clean up any stray hairs when you are finished.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the best results when polling your hair:

  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter, as it is easier to correct mistakes when you have more hair to work with.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you cut.
  • Consider having a friend or family member help you, especially if you are cutting the back of your head.
  • Be sure to use sharp scissors or clippers to avoid pulling or damaging the hair.

FAQ

What is the significance of polled hair in the Bible?

Polled hair is often associated with the Nazirite vow, which was a way for people to demonstrate their devotion to God. By letting their hair grow long and then shaving it off, they were symbolically offering themselves to God as a sacrifice.

Is it a sin to cut your hair?

No, it is not a sin to cut your hair. While some religious traditions may place restrictions on hair length or style, there is no biblical mandate that requires hair to be a certain length or left uncut.

Hair Care

If you have recently polled your hair, it is important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish the hair and prevent breakage.
  • Trim the hair regularly to prevent split ends and keep the hair looking neat and tidy.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy the benefits of having a stylish and easy-to-maintain haircut.
