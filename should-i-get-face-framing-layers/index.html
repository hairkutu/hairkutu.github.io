Should I Get Face Framing Layers A Complete Guide

Written by William Sep 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are thinking about getting a new hairstyle, you may be considering face framing layers. These layers can be a great way to add dimension and movement to your hair, but they may not be right for everyone. In this guide, we will explore whether you should get face framing layers, how to get them, and tips for maintaining your new hairstyle.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get Face Framing Layers
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Face Framing Layers
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Face Framing Layers

Description

Face framing layers are a popular hairstyle that involves cutting layers of hair around the face. These layers can add softness, dimension, and movement to your hair. They can also help to highlight your facial features and create a more flattering look. However, face framing layers may not be suitable for everyone. If you have very fine hair or a round face, for example, face framing layers may not be the best choice.

How to Get Face Framing Layers

If you have decided that you want to get face framing layers, the first step is to find a stylist who is experienced in cutting this type of hairstyle. You can ask friends or family members for recommendations, or you can search for stylists in your area online.

When you go to your appointment, be sure to bring pictures of the type of layers you are interested in. This will help your stylist to understand your vision and create a hairstyle that suits your face shape and hair type.

Step by Step Guide

The process of getting face framing layers will vary depending on your hair type and the style you are going for. However, here is a general step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section off the hair around your face.
  3. Cut the hair in the section to the desired length and angle.
  4. Repeat on the other side of your face.
  5. Blend the layers with the rest of your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Face Framing Layers

Once you have your face framing layers, you will want to take steps to maintain them. Here are some tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray when styling with heat tools.
  • Trim your layers every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Consider using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques to find the look that works best for you.

FAQ

Q: Will face framing layers work for my hair type?

A: Face framing layers can work for many hair types, but they may not be the best choice for very fine or thin hair. Talk to your stylist to determine if this hairstyle is right for you.

Q: How do I style face framing layers?

A: There are many ways to style face framing layers, including blow-drying with a round brush, curling with a curling iron, or straightening with a flat iron. Experiment with different techniques to find the look that works best for you.

Q: How often should I wash my hair with face framing layers?

A: How often you wash your hair will depend on your hair type and personal preference. However, it is generally recommended to wash your hair every two to three days to prevent it from becoming too dry or damaged.

Hair Care for Face Framing Layers

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and beautiful face framing layers. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools too frequently, as this can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid tying your hair up too tightly, as this can cause breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors with a hat or scarf.

In conclusion, face framing layers can be a great way to add dimension and movement to your hair. If you are interested in this hairstyle, be sure to find a stylist who is experienced in cutting face framing layers, and take steps to maintain your new look.
