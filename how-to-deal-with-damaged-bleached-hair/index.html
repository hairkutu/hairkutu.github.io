How To Deal With Damaged Bleached Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine May 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Dealing with damaged bleached hair can be a nightmare for anyone who loves to experiment with their hair. While bleaching can give you a stunning and edgy look, it can also lead to hair damage, breakage, and split ends. If you have bleached your hair and now struggling with the aftermath, then this guide is for you. Here, we will discuss how to deal with damaged bleached hair and get your hair back to its healthy state.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips to Prevent Hair Damage

How to Deal with Damaged Bleached Hair

Damaged bleached hair can be a result of several factors such as over-processing, using harsh chemicals, and not following the proper hair care routine. Here are some ways to deal with damaged bleached hair:

  • Trim your hair: If you have split ends or hair breakage, it's best to trim your hair to get rid of the damaged ends.
  • Use a protein-rich hair mask: Bleached hair loses its protein, which can lead to hair damage. Using a protein-rich hair mask can help restore the protein and improve the hair's texture.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools can further damage bleached hair. Avoid using them as much as possible.
  • Wash your hair less frequently: Over-washing can strip the natural oils from your hair, leaving it dry and damaged. Wash your hair two to three times a week.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're dealing with damaged bleached hair, follow these steps to restore your hair's health:

  1. Trim your hair to get rid of split ends and hair breakage.
  2. Use a protein-rich hair mask once a week to restore the protein in your hair.
  3. Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  4. Wash your hair two to three times a week with a sulfate-free shampoo.
  5. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after every wash.
  6. Avoid using hair elastics or clips that can cause hair breakage.
  7. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  8. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as it can cause hair breakage.
  9. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes or perms.
  10. Consult a professional stylist for advice on hair care products that are suitable for your hair type.

Tips to Prevent Hair Damage

Prevention is always better than cure. Here are some tips to prevent hair damage:

  • Avoid over-processing your hair with harsh chemicals.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause hair breakage.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet to promote hair growth.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too often, as it can cause hair breakage.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent hair breakage while sleeping.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about dealing with damaged bleached hair:

Q. Can damaged bleached hair be repaired?
A. Yes, damaged bleached hair can be repaired with proper hair care routine and products. Q. How long does it take to repair damaged bleached hair?
A. It depends on the extent of the damage. It may take a few weeks to a few months to restore your hair's health. Q. Can I bleach my hair again after it's been damaged?
A. It's best to avoid bleaching your hair again until it's fully recovered.

Hair Care Routine for Bleached Hair

Here's a hair care routine for bleached hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to wash your hair two to three times a week.
  • Apply a protein-rich hair mask once a week.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after every wash.
  • Avoid using hair elastics or clips that can cause hair breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as it can cause hair breakage.
  • Consult a professional stylist for advice on hair care products that are suitable for your hair type.

In conclusion, dealing with damaged bleached hair can be challenging, but it's not impossible to restore your hair's health. With proper hair care routine and products, you can get rid of hair damage, breakage, and split ends. Follow the steps and tips mentioned in this guide, and your hair will be back to its healthy state in no time.
