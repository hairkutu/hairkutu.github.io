How To Find Colours That Suit Your Skin Tone A Guide For 2023

Are you tired of wearing clothes in colours that don't flatter your skin tone? Do you want to learn how to find colours that make you look your best? In this guide, we'll show you how to determine your skin tone and choose colours that complement it. From warm undertones to cool undertones, we'll cover it all. Let's get started!

Determining Your Skin Tone

The first step in finding colours that suit your skin tone is to determine what your skin tone actually is. There are three main categories: warm, cool, and neutral. Here's how to figure out which one you fall into:

Warm Undertones

If you have warm undertones, your skin will have a yellow, peachy, or golden hue to it. You may also have freckles or a greenish tint to your veins. To confirm your warm undertones, try holding up a piece of gold jewellery next to your skin. If it looks flattering, you likely have warm undertones.

Cool Undertones

If you have cool undertones, your skin will have a pink, red, or bluish hue to it. Your veins will appear blue or purple, and you may burn easily in the sun. To confirm your cool undertones, try holding up a piece of silver jewellery next to your skin. If it looks flattering, you likely have cool undertones.

Neutral Undertones

If you have neutral undertones, your skin will have a mix of warm and cool tones. You may have difficulty determining which category you fall into, but there are a few indicators to look for. Your veins may appear blue-green, and you might not burn or tan easily in the sun.

Choosing Colours That Suit Your Skin Tone

Now that you've determined your skin tone, it's time to choose colours that complement it. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Warm Undertones: Look for colours that have a yellow or gold base, such as earthy tones like olive, rust, and mustard. Pastels can also look great on warm skin tones.
  • Cool Undertones: Look for colours that have a blue or pink base, such as jewel tones like emerald, sapphire, and amethyst. Bright and bold colours can also look great on cool skin tones.
  • Neutral Undertones: You have the most flexibility when it comes to choosing colours. You can wear both warm and cool tones, as well as neutrals like black, white, and grey.

Remember, these are just guidelines. The most important thing is to wear colours that make you feel confident and comfortable.

Tips for Finding the Right Colours

Here are some additional tips for finding colours that suit your skin tone:

  • Experiment with different shades of the same colour. For example, if you have warm undertones, try wearing both peach and coral to see which one looks best on you.
  • Try on clothes in natural light. Artificial lighting can distort colours, so it's best to try on clothes near a window or outside.
  • Don't be afraid to mix and match colours. If you have warm undertones, you can pair a mustard top with olive pants for a stylish and complementary outfit.

FAQs About Skin Tone and Colour

Here are some frequently asked questions about skin tone and colour:

  • Can my skin tone change over time? Yes, it's possible for your skin tone to change due to factors like aging, sun exposure, and health conditions.
  • Can I wear white if I have a warm skin tone? Yes, you can wear white regardless of your skin tone. However, if you have warm undertones, you may want to opt for a cream or ivory shade instead.
  • Can I wear black if I have a cool skin tone? Yes, you can wear black regardless of your skin tone. However, if you have cool undertones, you may want to opt for a navy or charcoal shade instead.

Hair Care Tips for Your Skin Tone

Your hair colour can also have an impact on how your skin tone looks. Here are some tips for choosing a hair colour that complements your skin tone:

  • If you have warm undertones, look for hair colours that have a golden or reddish hue, such as honey blonde or auburn.
  • If you have cool undertones, look for hair colours that have a blue or violet base, such as platinum blonde or ash brown.
  • If you have neutral undertones, you have the most flexibility when it comes to hair colour. You can experiment with both warm and cool tones.

Remember, these are just guidelines. The most important thing is to choose a hair colour that makes you feel confident and beautiful.

By following these tips, you'll be able to find colours that suit your skin tone and enhance your natural beauty. Don't be afraid to experiment with different colours and shades until you find the perfect match!
