How To Tell What Type Of Hair You Have Guys

Knowing what type of hair you have is essential in taking care of your hair. It can help you determine what products to use, what hairstyles will work for you, and how to properly care for your hair. In this article, we will discuss how to tell what type of hair you have guys.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

There are three main types of hair: straight, wavy, and curly. Within these types, there are subcategories that can help you determine your specific hair type. Here are the main factors to consider:

Hair Texture

Hair texture refers to the thickness or diameter of your hair strands. There are three main categories:

  • Fine: Your hair strands are thin and delicate.
  • Medium: Your hair strands are thicker than fine hair but not as thick as coarse hair.
  • Coarse: Your hair strands are thick and strong.

Hair Density

Hair density refers to how much hair you have on your head. You can determine your hair density by looking at how much hair you can see when your hair is pulled back or styled. There are three main categories:

  • Low: You can see a lot of your scalp when your hair is pulled back or styled.
  • Medium: You can see some scalp when your hair is pulled back or styled.
  • High: You cannot see much scalp when your hair is pulled back or styled.

Hair Porosity

Hair porosity refers to how easily your hair absorbs and retains moisture. There are three main categories:

  • Low: Your hair has a hard time absorbing moisture and tends to repel it.
  • Medium: Your hair absorbs and retains moisture well.
  • High: Your hair absorbs moisture easily but has a hard time retaining it.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the main factors to consider, here is a step-by-step guide to help you determine your hair type:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Do not use any products or styling tools.
  3. Look at your hair in a natural, unstyled state.
  4. Determine your hair texture by feeling your hair strands. If your hair strands are thin and delicate, you have fine hair. If your hair strands are thick and strong, you have coarse hair. If your hair strands are somewhere in between, you have medium hair.
  5. Determine your hair density by looking at how much hair you can see when your hair is pulled back or styled. If you can see a lot of your scalp, you have low hair density. If you can see some scalp, you have medium hair density. If you cannot see much scalp, you have high hair density.
  6. Determine your hair porosity by taking a strand of hair and placing it in a glass of water. If your hair sinks to the bottom, you have high porosity hair. If your hair floats in the middle, you have medium porosity hair. If your hair floats on top, you have low porosity hair.

Tips for Hair Care Based on Your Hair Type

Once you have determined your hair type, you can use these tips to properly care for your hair:

  • If you have fine hair, use lightweight products that won't weigh down your hair. Avoid heavy oils and creams.
  • If you have coarse hair, use rich, moisturizing products to help tame frizz and add shine.
  • If you have low hair density, avoid heavy products that can make your hair look greasy. Use volumizing products to add body and lift to your hair.
  • If you have high hair density, use moisturizing products to help hydrate and nourish your hair.
  • If you have low porosity hair, use products that can penetrate your hair shaft, such as lightweight oils and leave-in conditioners.
  • If you have high porosity hair, use heavy, moisturizing products to help seal in moisture and prevent breakage.

FAQs

Q: Can my hair type change over time?

A: Yes, your hair type can change due to hormonal changes, aging, and other factors.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people may need to wash their hair daily, while others may only need to wash their hair once a week.

Q: Can I use the same products as my girlfriend/wife/sister?

A: It's best to use products that are specifically formulated for your hair type. Women's hair products may contain ingredients that are not suitable for men's hair.

Q: Is it necessary to use conditioner?

A: Yes, conditioner helps to hydrate and nourish your hair, making it softer and easier to manage.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential in keeping your hair healthy and looking its best. By determining your hair type and using the right products and techniques, you can achieve healthy, beautiful hair.
