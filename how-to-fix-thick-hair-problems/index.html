How To Fix Thick Hair Problems

Written by Eveline Mar 22, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you have thick hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, thick hair can be voluminous and full of body. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. If you're struggling with thick hair problems, don't worry - there are solutions. Below, we'll provide a step-by-step guide to fixing common thick hair problems.

Problem: Frizz

Thick hair is often prone to frizz, especially in humid weather. To combat frizz, try using a leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum. Apply the product to damp hair and let it air dry. If you must blow dry your hair, use a diffuser attachment to minimize frizz. Additionally, avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.


Problem: Tangles

Tangles are a common problem for those with thick hair. To prevent tangles, start by using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. When you're ready to detangle your hair, use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up, gently detangling each section. If you encounter a particularly tough knot, try applying a leave-in conditioner or detangling spray to help loosen it.


Problem: Flat Hair

While thick hair can be voluminous, it can also be prone to falling flat. To add volume to your hair, try blow drying your hair upside down. Use a round brush to lift your roots and create volume. You can also use volumizing products, such as mousse or root-lifting spray, to add body to your hair. Additionally, avoid weighing your hair down with heavy styling products or using too much conditioner.


Problem: Heavy Hair

Heavy hair can be difficult to manage and can cause headaches or neck pain. To lighten the load, try getting a haircut that reduces the weight of your hair. Layers, thinning, or texturizing can all help to reduce the heaviness of your hair. Additionally, avoid using heavy styling products or blow drying your hair upside down, as this can add weight to your hair.


Problem: Dry Hair

Thick hair can be prone to dryness, especially if it's color-treated or frequently styled with heat tools. To combat dryness, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Additionally, use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to hydrate your hair. Avoid using hot tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray when you do. You can also try using a hair oil or serum to add moisture to your hair.


Problem: Split Ends

Split ends are a common problem for those with thick hair. To prevent split ends, get regular trims every 6-8 weeks. Additionally, avoid using hot tools too often and always use a heat protectant spray when you do. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and be gentle when brushing. You can also try using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help prevent split ends.


Problem: Dull Hair

Thick hair can be prone to looking dull or lifeless. To add shine to your hair, use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove buildup. Additionally, rinse your hair with cool water after conditioning to help seal the cuticle and add shine. You can also try using a shine-enhancing product, such as a hair oil or serum.


Problem: Hair Breakage

Thick hair can be prone to breakage, especially if it's color-treated or frequently styled with heat tools. To prevent breakage, use a protein-based conditioner to strengthen your hair. Avoid using hot tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray when you do. Additionally, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and be gentle when brushing. You can also try using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help prevent breakage.


Problem: Scalp Irritation

Thick hair can be prone to scalp irritation, especially if you use products that contain harsh chemicals. To prevent scalp irritation, use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners. You can also try using a scalp treatment or hair mask once a week to soothe your scalp. Additionally, avoid using hot tools too often and always use a heat protectant spray when you do.


Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of thick hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray when you do.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove buildup.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products or too much conditioner.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a hair mask on thick hair?

A: Yes, hair masks are great for thick hair. They can help to hydrate and nourish your hair, making it more manageable and healthy.

Q: What type of brush is best for thick hair?

A: A wide-tooth comb or detangling brush is best for thick hair. These brushes are gentle and won't cause breakage or damage to your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my thick hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every day. If you have dry hair, you may only need to wash it once or twice a week. Experiment with different washing schedules to find what works best for your hair.


Thick hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right products and techniques, you can have healthy, beautiful hair. Try incorporating some of the tips and tricks above into your hair care routine, and enjoy the benefits of having thick, voluminous hair.
