What Does Cutting Your Hair In Layers Do A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you thinking of getting a haircut and wondering what does cutting your hair in layers do? Layered haircuts have been a trend for many years now, and for a good reason. They add volume, texture, and movement to your hair, making it look more dynamic and manageable. In this article, we'll explain what layering is, how to do it, and how to care for your hair after getting a layered haircut.

What Does Cutting Your Hair in Layers Do?

Layering is a haircutting technique that involves cutting different lengths of hair to create layers. The shortest layers are usually at the top of the head, and the longest ones are at the bottom. Layering can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair, making it look more dynamic and manageable. It can also help to frame your face and highlight your best features.

How to Cut Your Hair in Layers

If you want to cut your hair in layers, you can either do it yourself or go to a salon. However, we recommend going to a professional stylist who has experience cutting layered hair. Here's how a stylist would do it:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. This will help your stylist to see the natural texture and movement of your hair.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier for your stylist to cut each layer precisely.

Step 3: Cut the Layers

Your stylist will start by cutting the shortest layer at the top of your head. They will then work their way down, cutting each layer slightly longer than the previous one. Your stylist may also use thinning scissors to remove some bulk and add texture to your hair.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

After cutting the layers, your stylist will blend them together to create a seamless look. This will prevent any harsh lines or choppy layers.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Your stylist will then style your hair to showcase the layers. They may use a hairdryer, curling iron, or straightener to add volume or texture to your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Layered Hair

Now that you have a layered haircut, it's essential to take care of your hair to maintain its shape and texture. Here are some tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add volume to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your layers.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

FAQs

1. Is Layered Hair Good for Thin Hair?

Yes, layered haircuts can add volume and texture to thin hair, making it look fuller and more dynamic.

2. How Often Should You Get a Layered Haircut?

You should get a layered haircut every six to eight weeks to maintain its shape and texture.

3. Can You Layer Curly Hair?

Yes, you can layer curly hair. However, you need to be careful not to cut the curls too short, as this can cause them to lose their shape.

Hair Care for Layered Hair

Layered hair requires proper care to maintain its shape and texture. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to hydrate and nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as it can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools to avoid damaging your hair.

Cutting your hair in layers can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair, making it look more dynamic and manageable. However, it's essential to get it done by a professional stylist and take care of your hair properly to maintain its shape and texture. Follow our tips, and you'll have beautiful, layered hair that you'll love!
