Best Hair Products For Natural Thick Curly Hair A Complete Guide

If you have natural thick curly hair, you know how challenging it can be to find the right hair products that work for your hair type. Thick curly hair requires special care, and using the wrong products can lead to dryness, frizz, and breakage. In this guide, we'll take a look at the best hair products for natural thick curly hair and provide you with tips on how to care for your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.

How to Care for Natural Thick Curly Hair

Caring for natural thick curly hair requires a different approach than caring for other hair types. Here are some tips to help you keep your hair healthy and looking its best:

1. Use a gentle sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfate-free shampoos are less harsh and won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for a shampoo that is specifically formulated for curly hair.

2. Deep condition regularly

Deep conditioning treatments will help keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage. Use a deep conditioner once a week, or more often if your hair is particularly dry.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb

A wide-tooth comb will help prevent breakage and minimize frizz. Always comb your hair from the ends up to the roots.

4. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and cause breakage. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low.

Best Hair Products for Natural Thick Curly Hair

Here are some of the best hair products for natural thick curly hair:

1. SheaMoisture Coconut & Hibiscus Curl Enhancing Smoothie

This curl-enhancing smoothie is a favorite among natural curly girls. It's made with coconut oil and hibiscus flower extract, which help define curls and reduce frizz.

2. DevaCurl No-Poo Original Zero Lather Conditioning Cleanser

This conditioning cleanser is gentle on curly hair and won't strip it of its natural oils. It's sulfate-free and contains moisturizing ingredients like peppermint oil and grapeseed oil.

3. Ouidad Advanced Climate Control Heat & Humidity Gel

This heat and humidity gel is perfect for natural curly hair. It helps define curls and fights frizz, even in humid weather.

4. Carol's Daughter Black Vanilla Moisture & Shine Leave-In Conditioner

This leave-in conditioner is great for moisturizing and detangling natural curly hair. It's made with ingredients like aloe leaf juice and sweet almond oil.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Natural Thick Curly Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide to styling natural thick curly hair:

1. Start with clean, wet hair

Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and deep condition. Blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to moisturize and detangle. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the conditioner evenly.

3. Apply a curl-enhancing product

Apply a curl-enhancing product to define your curls and reduce frizz. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair to encourage curl formation.

4. Diffuse your hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair. Use a low heat setting to avoid heat damage.

5. Finish with a hair oil

Apply a hair oil to add shine and lock in moisture. Use sparingly to avoid weighing down your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Natural Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining healthy natural thick curly hair:

1. Sleep on a satin pillowcase

Satin pillowcases are gentler on your hair than cotton pillowcases, which can cause breakage and frizz.

2. Don't over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils. Try to wash your hair no more than once a week.

3. Wear protective styles

Protective styles like braids and twists can help keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage.

FAQs about Natural Thick Curly Hair

1. How often should I deep condition my natural thick curly hair?

You should deep condition your hair at least once a week, or more often if your hair is particularly dry.

2. Can I use regular shampoo on my natural thick curly hair?

You should use a sulfate-free shampoo that is specifically formulated for curly hair.

3. What is the best way to detangle natural thick curly hair?

Use a wide-tooth comb and start combing from the ends up to the roots.

In conclusion, taking care of natural thick curly hair requires using the right products and techniques. By following the tips in this guide and using the best hair products for natural thick curly hair, you can keep your hair healthy, moisturized, and looking its best.
