How To Keep Your Hair From Falling Out During Chemo A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine May 14, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you or a loved one have been diagnosed with cancer, you may be facing the prospect of undergoing chemotherapy. While chemotherapy can be life-saving, it can also have a number of side effects, one of which is hair loss. Losing your hair can be a distressing experience, but there are steps you can take to minimize hair loss during chemo. In this article, we'll explore how to keep your hair from falling out during chemo, with tips, step-by-step instructions, and answers to frequently asked questions.

How to Keep Your Hair from Falling Out During Chemo: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Talk to Your Doctor

The first step in preventing hair loss during chemo is to talk to your doctor. Your doctor can help you understand the type of chemo you'll be receiving and the likelihood of hair loss. They may also be able to recommend treatments or medications that can help prevent hair loss.

Step 2: Consider Cold Cap Therapy

Cold cap therapy is a technique that involves cooling the scalp before, during, and after chemotherapy. By reducing blood flow to the hair follicles, cold cap therapy can help protect hair from the damaging effects of chemotherapy. If you're interested in cold cap therapy, talk to your doctor about whether it's a good option for you.

Step 3: Use Gentle Hair Products

During chemotherapy, it's important to use gentle hair products that won't irritate the scalp. Look for products that are free of sulfates, parabens, and other harsh chemicals. You may also want to avoid styling products that can weigh down hair and make it more prone to breakage.

Step 4: Protect Your Hair from Heat

Heat styling tools can be damaging to hair even in the best of circumstances. During chemotherapy, it's especially important to avoid heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons that can weaken hair and make it more prone to falling out.

Step 5: Be Gentle When Brushing and Combing

When you do need to brush or comb your hair, be gentle. Use a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles, and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair Healthy During Chemo

In addition to the steps outlined above, there are a number of other things you can do to keep your hair healthy during chemotherapy.

Tip 1: Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet that's rich in vitamins and nutrients can help keep your hair healthy during chemotherapy. Focus on foods that are high in protein, like lean meats, fish, and beans, and consider taking a multivitamin or other supplement if recommended by your doctor.

Tip 2: Get Plenty of Rest

Getting enough rest is important for overall health, but it can also help keep your hair healthy during chemotherapy. Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep per night, and try to avoid staying up too late or getting up too early.

Tip 3: Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help keep your hair healthy during chemotherapy. Aim for at least 8 glasses of water per day, and consider drinking other fluids like herbal tea or coconut water to stay hydrated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will all types of chemotherapy cause hair loss?

A: No, not all types of chemotherapy cause hair loss. The likelihood of hair loss depends on the type of chemotherapy you're receiving and other factors like your age and health. Your doctor can help you understand the likelihood of hair loss with your specific treatment plan.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after chemo?

A: It can take several months for hair to grow back after chemotherapy. Hair may grow back thinner or a different texture than before, but it will eventually grow back.

Q: Is it safe to color or perm hair during chemotherapy?

A: It's generally not recommended to color or perm hair during chemotherapy, as these treatments can be damaging to hair that's already weakened by chemotherapy. However, your doctor can advise you on whether it's safe for you to color or perm your hair during treatment.

Hair Care During and After Chemotherapy

If you do experience hair loss during chemotherapy, it's important to take care of your scalp and any remaining hair. Use gentle, moisturizing products to soothe the scalp, and consider wearing a soft, comfortable hat or scarf to protect your scalp from sun and wind. After chemotherapy is over, you can begin to focus on regrowing your hair. Talk to your doctor about any treatments or medications that may be helpful, and continue to take good care of your hair by eating a balanced diet, getting plenty of rest, and using gentle hair products.
