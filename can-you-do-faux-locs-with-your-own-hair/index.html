Can You Do Faux Locs With Your Own Hair

Written by Frank Jul 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you interested in trying out the faux locs hairstyle but wondering if you can do it with your own hair? The answer is yes, you can! Whether you have natural or relaxed hair, you can achieve the faux locs look with some preparation and patience. In this article, we will guide you through the process of doing faux locs with your own hair, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice.

Description

Faux locs are a trendy hairstyle that simulates the look of dreadlocks without having to commit to the permanent style. They are a versatile protective hairstyle that can be styled in various ways and can last up to several weeks. Faux locs can be done with synthetic hair, but you can also use your own hair to achieve the look. Doing faux locs with your own hair requires some preparation and technique, but the results are worth it.

How to

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before starting the faux locs, make sure your hair is clean, moisturized, and detangled. You can wash and condition your hair a day or two before the installation to ensure that it is clean and soft. You can also apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized. Detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or brush to avoid tangling during the installation.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the desired locs' size. Make sure the sections are even and organized to avoid confusion during the installation.

Step 3: Start the Locs

Take a small section of hair and divide it into two parts. Twist the two parts together to form a basic twist. Add more hair to the twist as you continue twisting, making sure that the twist is tight and secure. Keep twisting until you reach the desired length of the loc. Repeat the process on all sections of your hair.

Step 4: Set the Locs

After twisting all the sections, you can set the locs using a hot water method. Dip each loc into hot water for a few seconds, then squeeze out the excess water and let it dry. You can also use a hair dryer to speed up the drying process. Once the locs are dry, you can style them as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when doing faux locs with your own hair:

  • Use a good-quality hair gel or styling cream to keep the twists in place.
  • Wrap the ends of the locs with hair tape or rubber bands to prevent them from unraveling.
  • Use a crochet hook to add extensions to your locs for a fuller look.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils that can weigh down your hair and cause buildup.
  • Take care when removing the locs to avoid damaging your hair.

FAQ

Can I do faux locs with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can do faux locs with relaxed hair. However, relaxed hair may be more fragile and prone to breakage, so you need to be gentle during the installation and take extra care when detangling and styling.

How long do faux locs last?

Faux locs can last up to several weeks depending on how well you take care of them. You should avoid excessive manipulation and keep your scalp clean and moisturized to prolong the style's lifespan.

Hair Care

After installing the faux locs, you need to take care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain healthy hair growth. Here are some hair care tips for faux locs:

  • Wash your scalp and locs regularly to prevent buildup and maintain scalp health.
  • Moisturize your hair and scalp using a water-based moisturizer or oil to prevent dryness and itching.
  • Avoid excessive manipulation, which can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin bonnet or scarf to prevent tangling and friction.
  • Take breaks between protective styles to allow your hair to rest and recover.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve the faux locs look with your own hair and enjoy a trendy and protective hairstyle that will turn heads.
