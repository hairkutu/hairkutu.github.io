Can Damaged Hair Turn Curly Exploring The Possibilities

Written by Daniel Aug 16, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you someone who has been experiencing hair damage lately and wondering whether it can turn curly? If yes, then you are not alone. Many people are curious about this topic but are often left confused due to conflicting information on the internet. In this article, we will explore the possibility of damaged hair turning curly and provide you with some tips to take care of your hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Hair damage can be caused due to various reasons such as excessive heat styling, chemical treatments, lack of proper hair care, and many more. When hair gets damaged, it loses its natural texture, and the strands become weak and brittle. However, in some cases, damaged hair can turn curly due to the change in the hair structure.

How to

The process of hair damage turning curly is not entirely under our control. However, there are some things that you can do to encourage your hair to become curly.

Use Curl-Enhancing Products

Using curl-enhancing products such as mousse, curl-defining cream, or gel can encourage the formation of natural curls in damaged hair. These products work by adding moisture to the hair, which helps in reviving the curls.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can cause significant damage to your hair. It is best to avoid using these tools and let your hair air dry naturally.

Get a Perm

If you want to speed up the process of getting curly hair, you can consider getting a perm. A perm is a chemical treatment that helps in setting the hair in curls. However, it is essential to get it done by a professional hairstylist to avoid any damage to your hair.

Step by Step

Here are some steps that you can follow to encourage your damaged hair to turn curly:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo
  2. Apply a curl-enhancing conditioner
  3. Detangle your hair gently using a wide-tooth comb
  4. Apply a curl-defining cream or mousse
  5. Scrunch your hair using your hands to encourage the formation of curls
  6. Let your hair air dry naturally

Tips

Here are some tips that you can follow to take care of your damaged hair:

  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair as it can strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Use a satin pillowcase to avoid friction and prevent breakage
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to give your hair the required moisture

FAQ

Can heat damage turn straight hair curly?

Heat damage can change the hair structure, which can result in straight hair becoming curly. However, this is not a guaranteed outcome, and it may vary from person to person.

How long does it take for damaged hair to turn curly?

The time taken for damaged hair to turn curly may vary from person to person. It depends on the extent of the damage and the individual's hair type.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is crucial, especially if you want to encourage your damaged hair to turn curly. Here are some hair care tips that you can follow:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Avoid using hot tools and let your hair air dry naturally
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to give your hair the required moisture
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends

In conclusion, damaged hair can turn curly in some cases due to the change in the hair structure. However, it is essential to take care of your hair and avoid any further damage. By following the tips mentioned above, you can encourage your damaged hair to turn curly and enjoy your new look.
