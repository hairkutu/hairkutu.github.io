How To Train My Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 03, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing, but it can also be a curse if you don't know how to take care of it properly. Many people with curly hair struggle with frizz, dryness, and a lack of definition. That's why it's important to train your curly hair to look its best. In this guide, we'll go over the basics of how to train your curly hair, including tips, tricks, and frequently asked questions.

  • How to Train Your Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Train Your Curly Hair

The first step in training your curly hair is to understand its natural texture. Curly hair is typically more fragile and prone to breakage than straight hair, so it's important to treat it gently. Here are some tips for training your curly hair:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so it's important to use a shampoo and conditioner that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for products specifically designed for curly hair, and avoid sulfates and other harsh chemicals.

2. Don't wash your hair too often.

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it even drier. Try to wash your hair no more than twice a week, and use a dry shampoo in between washes if necessary.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

Curly hair is prone to tangling, but it's important to detangle it gently to avoid breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

4. Use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream.

After washing and detangling your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls and reduce frizz. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, and scrunch your curls as you go.

5. Avoid heat styling.

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it even frizzier. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and set the temperature to no more than 350 degrees.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to training your curly hair:

1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

Use products specifically designed for curly hair, and avoid sulfates and other harsh chemicals.

2. Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.

Starting at the ends, gently detangle your hair and work your way up to the roots.

3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream.

Distribute the product evenly throughout your hair, and scrunch your curls as you go.

4. Let your hair air dry.

Avoid heat styling tools if possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and set the temperature to no more than 350 degrees.

5. Refresh your curls with a spray bottle and curl cream.

If your curls start to look frizzy or lose their definition, refresh them with a spray bottle of water and curl cream.

Tips for Training Your Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for training your curly hair:

  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage.
  • Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best way to define my curls?

Use a curl cream or gel to define your curls, and scrunch your hair as you go. You can also try using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls.

How often should I deep condition my curly hair?

You should deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.

What's the best way to prevent frizz?

Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz. Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel, and avoid using heat styling tools too often.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and dyes on your hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause breakage, such as tight ponytails or braids.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as this can cause dryness and frizz.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

By following these tips and techniques, you can train your curly hair to look its best. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair.
