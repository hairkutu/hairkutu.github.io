How To Cut Hair Away From Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to give yourself a fresh new look or just want to trim your hair away from your face, you've come to the right place. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your hair away from your face with ease, using simple tools you probably already have at home. Whether you're a seasoned pro or a beginner, these tips will help you achieve the look you want without any fuss.

Description

Cutting your hair away from your face can be a great way to enhance your features and create a flattering frame for your face. It's also a practical choice for those who want to keep their hair out of their eyes, especially during the hot summer months. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your hair away from your face step-by-step, including tips, tricks, and frequently asked questions.

How to Cut Hair Away from Face

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your hair away from your face is to gather your tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to help you wet your hair as you cut.

Step 2: Wet Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to wet your hair. This will make it easier to comb and cut, and will also help you see the natural fall of your hair. Use your spray bottle to mist your hair until it's damp but not soaking wet.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, you'll want to section your hair. Use your comb to separate the hair at the crown of your head from the rest of your hair. Clip the top section of hair out of the way, so you can focus on the hair around your face.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Now it's time to start cutting. Take a small section of hair from around your face and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, and use your scissors to cut a small amount at a time. It's best to start with small cuts and work your way up, so you don't accidentally cut too much.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

As you cut, be sure to check your progress in the mirror. You want to make sure you're cutting your hair evenly and that it's framing your face the way you want it to. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim them up.

Step 6: Repeat

Once you've finished cutting the hair around your face, unclip the top section of hair and repeat the process. Take small sections of hair and cut them a little bit at a time until you're happy with the length and shape.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you cut your hair away from your face:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up
  • Check your progress in the mirror frequently
  • Don't be afraid to take breaks and step away from the mirror if you need to
  • Consider using hair clippers for a more precise cut
  • Be patient and take your time - cutting your hair too quickly can lead to mistakes

FAQ

How do I know how much hair to cut?

It's always best to start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut. Take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to step away from the mirror if you need to.

Can I cut my hair away from my face if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your hair away from your face whether it's curly or straight. Just be sure to cut small sections at a time and check your progress frequently in the mirror.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic if you make a mistake - it happens to the best of us. If you accidentally cut too much hair, you can always trim it up later. If you're really worried, consider going to a professional stylist to help fix the mistake.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting your hair away from your face, be sure to take good care of it. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny, and consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep it moisturized. Regular trims will also help keep your hair looking its best.
