How To Cut Your Own Hair Short Bob Style

Written by Aprilia Sep 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a fresh, new hairstyle, a short bob might be just what you need. This classic cut is easy to maintain and looks great on almost everyone. The best part is, you can even cut your own hair short bob style right at home! In this article, we'll walk you through the process step by step so you can achieve the perfect short bob without having to make a trip to the salon.

Description

A short bob is a timeless haircut that falls between the chin and the shoulders. It's a versatile style that can be customized to suit your face shape and hair type. A short bob is perfect for those who want a low-maintenance hairstyle that still looks polished and put-together.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Short Bob Style

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a handheld mirror so you can see the back of your head.

Step 2: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry. Wet hair can be misleading and may result in a shorter cut than you intended. So, shampoo and condition your hair, then let it air dry completely before you start cutting.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to part your hair down the middle, then clip one side up and out of the way. Take a section of hair at the back and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers and trim it to your desired length. Repeat this process for the remaining sections, working your way around your head.

Step 4: Cut the Bangs

If you want bangs, now is the time to cut them. Section off the front portion of your hair where the bangs will be. Comb the hair straight down and trim it to your desired length. Remember, you can always go shorter, so start with a longer length and work your way up.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting, use your handheld mirror to check the back of your head for any uneven areas. Make any necessary adjustments until your hair is even all the way around.

Tips

  • Remember to take your time and cut small sections at a time. You can always go shorter, but you can't add hair back.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, start with a longer length and work your way up to a shorter bob.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can result in split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Don't forget to clean and sanitize your tools before and after use.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair wet?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry to avoid any surprises. Wet hair can be misleading and may result in a shorter cut than you intended.

How do I know what length to cut my hair?

It's important to have a clear idea of what you want before you start cutting. Look at pictures of short bobs and consider your face shape and hair type. If you're unsure, start with a longer length and work your way up to a shorter cut.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! It's just hair and it will grow back. If you make a mistake, try to even out the length or style your hair in a way that disguises the mistake. If all else fails, visit a professional stylist to fix the issue.

Hair Care

To keep your short bob looking fresh, trim the ends every 6-8 weeks. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and always use a heat protectant spray.

Cutting your own hair short bob style can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and a little patience, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home.
