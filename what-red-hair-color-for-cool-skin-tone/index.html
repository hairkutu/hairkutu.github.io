What Red Hair Color Is Best For Cool Skin Tone

Written by Luffy Sep 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have a cool skin tone, you're probably wondering what red hair color will look best on you. Red hair is a bold and beautiful choice, but choosing the right shade can be tricky. In this article, we'll help you determine which red hair color will complement your cool skin tone and provide tips on how to care for your new hair color.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Choose the Right Red Hair Color
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Red Hair Color
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Red Hair

Description

Cool skin tones have pink, red, or blue undertones, and they look best with cool hair colors like platinum blonde, ash brown, and burgundy. When it comes to red hair, cool skin tones look best with shades that have blue or violet undertones. These shades will complement your cool skin tone and make your features pop.

How to Choose the Right Red Hair Color

When choosing a red hair color for cool skin tones, you want to look for shades that have cool undertones, like blue or violet. Avoid warm shades like copper, golden, and orange, as they will clash with your cool skin tone. Instead, opt for shades like:

  • Deep Burgundy
  • Crimson Red
  • Cherry Red
  • Plum Red

You can also consult with a professional hair colorist to determine which shade will look best on you.

Step by Step Guide

If you're ready to take the plunge and dye your hair red, here's a step-by-step guide to help you achieve the perfect shade:

  1. Consult with a professional hair colorist to determine which shade will look best on you.
  2. Prepare your hair by washing it with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  3. Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair to protect it from damage.
  4. Apply the hair dye according to the instructions on the package.
  5. Rinse the hair dye out of your hair and apply a color-safe conditioner.
  6. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Your Red Hair Color

Once you've achieved the perfect shade of red, you want to make sure it stays vibrant and beautiful. Here are some tips for maintaining your red hair color:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip the color from your hair.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to direct sunlight, as this can cause the color to fade.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause the color to fade.
  • Use a color-depositing shampoo and conditioner to refresh your hair color between dye jobs.

FAQs

Q: Can cool skin tones wear warm red hair colors?

A: While warm red hair colors can be beautiful, they may clash with cool skin tones. It's best to stick with cool shades like burgundy, crimson, and cherry red.

Q: Will red hair color damage my hair?

A: Any hair dye can cause damage to your hair if not used properly. Make sure to follow the instructions on the package and use a deep conditioning treatment before and after dyeing your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my red hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every other day or every few days to prevent the color from fading. Use a dry shampoo on the days you don't wash your hair to keep it looking fresh.

Hair Care for Red Hair

Red hair requires extra care to maintain its vibrant color. Here are some tips for caring for your red hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause the color to fade.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to direct sunlight, as this can cause the color to fade.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips, you can rock a beautiful shade of red hair that complements your cool skin tone. Remember to consult with a professional hair colorist if you're unsure which shade to choose, and always follow the instructions on the package when dyeing your hair.
