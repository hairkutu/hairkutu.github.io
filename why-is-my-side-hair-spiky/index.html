Why Is My Side Hair Spiky

Written by Frank May 12, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of waking up every morning to find your side hair spiky and unmanageable? You're not alone. Many people struggle with this common hair problem, but there are solutions. In this article, we will explore the reasons why your side hair may be spiky and provide tips on how to tame it.

Description

Side hair refers to the hair on the sides of your head, also known as the temple area. This hair can be prone to becoming spiky, especially if it is naturally curly or wavy. Spiky hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, weather, and hair care practices.

How to Tame Spiky Side Hair

If you're tired of dealing with spiky side hair, there are several things you can do to tame it. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Dry hair is more prone to becoming spiky, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or argan oil, which can help to hydrate and nourish your hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can damage your hair and make it more prone to becoming spiky. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and opt for heat-free styling methods instead.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent it from becoming spiky. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your side hair after washing and conditioning, and style as usual.

4. Try a Different Haircut

If your side hair is constantly spiky, it may be time to try a different haircut. A shorter hairstyle or one with more layers can help to distribute the weight of your hair more evenly, making it less prone to becoming spiky.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to tame spiky side hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to focus on the temple area, as this is where your side hair is located.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing and conditioning, apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your side hair. This will help to keep it hydrated and prevent it from becoming spiky.

Step 3: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as usual, using heat-free styling methods if possible. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Step 4: Use a Hair Oil

If your side hair is still spiky, try using a hair oil to smooth it down. Apply a small amount of hair oil to your fingertips and run them through your hair, focusing on the temple area.

Tips for Taming Spiky Side Hair

Here are some additional tips for taming spiky side hair:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it more prone to becoming spiky. Try to wash your hair no more than every other day.

2. Avoid Touching Your Hair Too Much

Touching your hair too much can cause it to become frizzy and spiky. Try to avoid touching your hair throughout the day, and use a soft-bristled brush to detangle it instead.

3. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent your hair from becoming spiky. Silk is gentle on your hair and can help to prevent breakage and frizz.

FAQ

Q: Why is my side hair always spiky?

A: There are several reasons why your side hair may be spiky, including genetics, weather, and hair care practices. Try using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools, and using a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.

Q: Can I use hair gel to tame spiky side hair?

A: Yes, hair gel can be used to tame spiky side hair. However, be sure to use a lightweight gel that won't weigh your hair down or make it look greasy.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if it's spiky?

A: Washing your hair every other day is usually sufficient if your hair is spiky. Washing too often can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to becoming spiky.

Hair Care for Spiky Side Hair

If you're struggling with spiky side hair, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

1. Use Gentle Hair Products

Use gentle hair products that are free of harsh chemicals, sulfates, and parabens. Look for products that contain natural ingredients and are designed to hydrate and nourish your hair.

2. Protect Your Hair from the Elements

Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and cold weather by wearing a hat or scarf. These elements can dry out your hair and make it more prone to becoming spiky.

3. Get Regular Haircuts

Getting regular haircuts can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent it from becoming spiky. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks, or as needed.

In conclusion, spiky side hair can be frustrating, but there are solutions. By using the right hair care products, avoiding heat styling tools, and using gentle styling methods, you can tame your spiky side hair and achieve a smooth, sleek look.
