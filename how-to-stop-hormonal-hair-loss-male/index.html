How To Stop Hormonal Hair Loss In Males A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're a male experiencing hair loss, you're not alone. According to the American Hair Loss Association, by the age of 35, two-thirds of American men will experience some degree of hair loss, and by the age of 50, approximately 85% of men have significantly thinning hair. Hormonal hair loss is a common cause of hair loss in men, and it can be frustrating to deal with. In this article, we'll discuss how to stop hormonal hair loss in males and provide tips and advice for maintaining healthy hair.

What is Hormonal Hair Loss?

Hormonal hair loss is a type of hair loss that is caused by changes in hormone levels. Androgenetic alopecia, also known as male-pattern baldness, is the most common form of hormonal hair loss in males. This type of hair loss is caused by a genetic predisposition to sensitivity to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone that shrinks hair follicles, leading to thinning hair and eventual baldness.

How to Stop Hormonal Hair Loss in Males

1. Use Hair Loss Shampoos

One way to stop hormonal hair loss is by using hair loss shampoos that contain ingredients such as ketoconazole, saw palmetto, or caffeine. These ingredients work to block DHT and promote hair growth.

2. Take Supplements

Taking supplements such as biotin, zinc, and vitamin D can help promote healthy hair growth. These supplements work to strengthen hair follicles and improve overall hair health.

3. Use Topical Treatments

Topical treatments such as minoxidil can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. Minoxidil works by increasing blood flow to the hair follicles, promoting hair growth.

4. Consider Prescription Medications

If over-the-counter treatments are not effective, prescription medications such as finasteride may be an option. Finasteride works by blocking the production of DHT, promoting hair growth and preventing hair loss.

Step-by-Step Guide to Preventing Hormonal Hair Loss

Step 1: Consult with a Doctor

If you're experiencing hair loss, it's important to consult with a doctor to determine the underlying cause. A doctor can help determine if your hair loss is due to hormonal imbalances or other factors such as stress, medication, or genetics.

Step 2: Use Hair Loss Shampoos

Using hair loss shampoos can help block DHT and promote healthy hair growth. Look for shampoos that contain ingredients such as ketoconazole, saw palmetto, or caffeine.

Step 3: Take Supplements

Taking supplements such as biotin, zinc, and vitamin D can help promote healthy hair growth. These supplements work to strengthen hair follicles and improve overall hair health.

Step 4: Use Topical Treatments

Topical treatments such as minoxidil can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. Minoxidil works by increasing blood flow to the hair follicles, promoting hair growth.

Step 5: Consider Prescription Medications

If over-the-counter treatments are not effective, prescription medications such as finasteride may be an option. Finasteride works by blocking the production of DHT, promoting hair growth and preventing hair loss. However, it's important to discuss the potential side effects with a doctor before taking any prescription medication.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid hairstyles that pull on the hair, such as tight braids, ponytails, or cornrows. These hairstyles can lead to hair breakage and damage.

2. Use Gentle Hair Products

Avoid using harsh hair products that can damage hair, such as those containing sulfates or alcohol. Use gentle, natural products that are free from harmful chemicals.

3. Practice Good Hair Care Habits

Practice good hair care habits such as washing your hair regularly, using a wide-tooth comb to detangle hair, and avoiding excessive heat styling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can hormonal hair loss be reversed?

A: While hormonal hair loss cannot be completely reversed, it can be slowed down or stopped with proper treatment.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss in males?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss in males. Stress can cause hormonal imbalances that can lead to hair loss.

Q: How long does it take to see results from hair loss treatments?

A: Results from hair loss treatments can vary, but it typically takes several months to see noticeable results.

Conclusion

Hormonal hair loss in males can be frustrating, but with proper treatment and hair care, it can be managed effectively. By using hair loss shampoos, taking supplements, using topical treatments, and considering prescription medications, you can slow down or stop hormonal hair loss. Remember to practice good hair care habits and consult with a doctor if you're experiencing hair loss.
