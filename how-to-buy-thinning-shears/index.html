How To Buy Thinning Shears A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 03, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to achieve a perfectly layered haircut, then investing in a good pair of thinning shears is a must. Thinning shears work by removing some of the hair's weight, creating a softer and more natural look. But with so many options available in the market, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps on how to buy thinning shears and provide some tips on how to care for them.

Description

Thinning shears, also known as texturizing shears, have a unique design that allows them to cut hair at different lengths. Unlike regular scissors, thinning shears have teeth on one or both blades, which helps to remove some of the hair's volume. They come in different sizes and shapes, and the number of teeth on the blade also varies. Thinning shears are commonly used in salons or by professional hairstylists, but they're also a great tool for at-home haircuts.

How to Buy Thinning Shears

Step 1: Determine Your Needs

The first step in buying thinning shears is to determine your needs. Consider the type of hair you're working with, the style you want to achieve, and your level of experience. If you're a beginner, then a simple pair of thinning shears with fewer teeth may be easier to handle. However, if you're a professional hairstylist, then you may want to invest in a more advanced pair with a higher number of teeth.

Step 2: Check the Blade Quality

The blade quality is an essential factor to consider when buying thinning shears. Look for shears made with high-quality stainless steel or titanium, as they're durable and long-lasting. Avoid buying shears with low-quality blades, as they can cause split ends and damage your hair.

Step 3: Consider the Blade Length and Teeth Count

Thinning shears come in different blade lengths and teeth counts. Longer blades and higher teeth counts are ideal for thicker hair, while shorter blades and fewer teeth are better suited for fine hair. Consider the hair type you'll be working with and choose a pair of shears that will work best for your needs.

Step 4: Look for Comfortable Handles

Comfortable handles are a must when it comes to thinning shears. Look for shears with ergonomic designs and cushioned grips, as they'll provide more comfort and control when using them. Avoid buying shears with plastic handles, as they can be uncomfortable and may slip out of your hands.

Step 5: Check for Adjustability

Some thinning shears come with adjustable tension screws that allow you to customize the shears' cutting power. This feature is excellent for professionals, as it allows them to tailor the shears to their specific needs. Look for shears with adjustable tension screws if you're a professional or plan on using them frequently.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears

Once you've bought your thinning shears, it's essential to take care of them to ensure they last longer. Here are some tips for using and maintaining thinning shears:

  • Always use the shears on dry hair.
  • Never use thinning shears on the hair's ends.
  • Don't overuse the thinning shears, as they can cause damage to the hair.
  • Clean your shears after each use with a soft cloth or brush.
  • Oil your shears regularly to keep the blades sharp.

FAQs

Can thinning shears be used on all types of hair?

Thinning shears can be used on all types of hair, but it's essential to choose the right pair for your needs. Thicker hair requires shears with longer blades and higher teeth counts, while fine hair needs shorter blades and fewer teeth.

Can I use thinning shears to cut my own hair?

Yes, thinning shears can be used to cut your hair at home. However, it's important to have some experience using them before attempting to cut your hair. If you're a beginner, it's best to practice on a mannequin head or seek professional help.

How often should I clean and oil my thinning shears?

You should clean and oil your thinning shears after each use to ensure they last longer. Use a soft cloth or brush to remove any hair or debris from the blades and apply a small amount of oil to the pivot point.

Hair Care

Using the right tools is essential when it comes to achieving a perfect haircut. Thinning shears are a great tool for creating a soft and natural look, but it's important to choose the right pair for your needs. Remember to take care of your shears by cleaning and oiling them regularly to ensure they last longer.
