How To Thin Out Medium Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 15, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have medium-length hair that’s thick and heavy, you might be wondering how to thin it out. Thinning out your hair can give it more movement, help it hold styles better, and make it easier to manage. In this article, we’ll go over how to thin out medium length hair and give you tips for maintaining your new style.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Out Medium Length Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Thinned-Out Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thinned-Out Hair

How to Thin Out Medium Length Hair

There are a few different methods you can use to thin out your medium length hair:

  • Thinning shears
  • Razor cutting
  • Texturizing scissors
  • Point cutting

Each method has its own benefits and drawbacks, so it’s important to choose the one that’s right for your hair type and goals.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide for thinning out medium length hair with thinning shears:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off the hair you want to thin out.
  3. Hold the section of hair straight up from the scalp.
  4. Use the thinning shears to make small, quick cuts in the hair, moving the shears down the section of hair.
  5. Repeat until you’ve achieved your desired level of thinness.
  6. Repeat with the other sections of hair you want to thin out.

For razor cutting, texturizing scissors, and point cutting, the process is similar but the tools and techniques used are different. It’s best to consult with a hairstylist to determine which method is right for you.

Tips for Maintaining Your Thinned-Out Hair

Here are some tips for keeping your new hairstyle looking its best:

  • Use a lightweight, volumizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair from getting weighed down.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can make your hair look greasy or flat.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and to prevent split ends.
  • Consider getting a deep conditioning treatment every few weeks to keep your hair moisturized.

FAQs

Q: Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner overall?

A: Thinning out your hair can actually make it look thicker because it gives it more movement and texture.

Q: Will thinning out my hair damage it?

A: Thinning out your hair shouldn’t damage it as long as you use the right tools and techniques and don’t overdo it.

Q: How often should I thin out my hair?

A: How often you thin out your hair depends on how quickly it grows and how thick it is to begin with. It’s best to consult with a hairstylist to determine the right frequency for your hair.

Hair Care for Thinned-Out Hair

When you have thinned-out hair, it’s important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, as this can cause breakage.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.

Thinning out your medium length hair can give it a whole new look and make it easier to manage. By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy your new style for months to come.
