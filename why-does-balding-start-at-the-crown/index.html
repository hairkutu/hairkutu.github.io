Why Does Balding Start At The Crown

Written by Christine May 16, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you starting to notice hair loss at the crown of your head? You're not alone. Many men experience balding in this area, and it's a common question to ask why it starts there. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind why balding starts at the crown and what you can do about it.

Description

Balding at the crown, also known as vertex baldness or male-pattern baldness, is the most common form of hair loss in men. It's characterized by a receding hairline and a bald spot at the top of the head. This type of hair loss is caused by a combination of genetics and hormones.

How to Identify Balding at the Crown

To identify balding at the crown, look for a circular area of hair loss on the top of your head. This area may be smooth and shiny or have very fine hair. You may also notice a receding hairline and thinning hair on the temples.

Step by Step Guide to Prevent Balding at the Crown

Step 1: Take Care of Your Scalp

A healthy scalp is essential for healthy hair growth. Keep your scalp clean by washing it regularly with a gentle shampoo. Avoid harsh chemicals and hot styling tools that can damage your hair.

Step 2: Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals in your diet. Foods that are good for your hair include eggs, nuts, fish, and leafy greens.

Step 3: Manage Your Stress

Stress can cause hair loss, so it's essential to manage your stress levels. Find ways to relax, such as meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises.

Tips to Prevent Balding at the Crown

Here are some additional tips to prevent balding at the crown:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair, such as ponytails or braids.
  • Avoid smoking, which can damage your hair and reduce blood flow to your scalp.
  • Avoid excessive sun exposure, which can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for your hair type.
  • Consider using a hair growth supplement or topical treatment to stimulate hair growth.

FAQ

Is balding at the crown reversible?

Balding at the crown is typically permanent, but there are treatments available that can slow down or stop the progression of hair loss. It's essential to start treatment as soon as possible for the best results.

What causes balding at the crown?

Balding at the crown is caused by a combination of genetics and hormones. It's more common in men and can be passed down from either side of the family.

Can stress cause balding at the crown?

Stress can contribute to hair loss, including balding at the crown. It's essential to manage your stress levels to prevent hair loss.

Hair Care for Balding at the Crown

If you're experiencing balding at the crown, it's essential to take care of your remaining hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid harsh chemicals and hot styling tools. Consider using a hair growth supplement or topical treatment to stimulate hair growth.

Remember, balding at the crown is a common condition that affects many men. By taking care of your scalp and hair and managing your stress levels, you can slow down or stop the progression of hair loss.
