How To Twist Natural Hair For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Twisting your natural hair is a popular protective style that can help promote hair growth and retain length. If you're new to twist-outs, don't worry! This beginner's guide will take you through the steps to achieve perfectly twisted natural hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Twist Natural Hair for Beginners
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Twisting Natural Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Twisted Natural Hair

Description

Twisting natural hair is a simple and effective way to protect your delicate strands from damage and breakage. The twists can be done on wet or dry hair, and can be worn for several days to weeks, depending on your preference. Twisted natural hair can be styled in various ways, including updos, buns, and twist-outs.

How to Twist Natural Hair for Beginners

To twist natural hair, you'll need a few basic supplies, including a wide-tooth comb, hair clips, a spray bottle filled with water, and your favorite natural hair products. Here are the steps to achieve perfectly twisted natural hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, detangled hair.
  2. Section your hair into small, manageable parts using hair clips.
  3. Take a small section of hair and apply a leave-in conditioner or curling cream.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the section of hair.
  5. Divide the section of hair into two equal parts.
  6. Twist the two sections of hair around each other, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  7. Use your fingers to smooth the hair as you twist.
  8. Secure the end of the twist with a hair clip or a small rubber band.
  9. Repeat these steps for each section of hair until your entire head is twisted.
  10. Allow your hair to air dry or sit under a hooded dryer until your hair is completely dry.
  11. Once your hair is dry, gently unravel each twist and fluff your hair to create volume.

Tips for Twisting Natural Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect twist-out on your natural hair:

  • Use a moisturizing leave-in conditioner or curling cream to help define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Make sure your hair is completely detangled before you start twisting. This will help prevent breakage and tangling.
  • Keep your twists small and tight for maximum definition.
  • Use a satin or silk scarf or bonnet at night to protect your twists and prevent frizz.
  • If you're short on time, try twisting your hair in larger sections to save time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about twisting natural hair:

How long should I keep my twists in?

You can keep your twists in for several days to weeks, depending on your preference. Some people prefer to wear their twists for a week, while others may keep them in for two or three weeks.

Can I twist my hair on dry hair?

Yes, you can twist your hair on dry hair. However, twisting on wet hair can help reduce frizz and create more defined twists.

Can I use gel to twist my hair?

Yes, you can use gel to twist your hair. However, using too much gel can cause buildup and make your hair feel sticky and crunchy. Use a light hold gel and apply it sparingly.

Hair Care Tips for Twisted Natural Hair

Here are some hair care tips to help you maintain healthy, moisturized hair while wearing twists:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or moisturizing spray.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or butters that can cause buildup on your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night with a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.
  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your scalp clean and healthy.
  • Avoid manipulating your twists too much, as this can cause frizz and tangling.

Twisting natural hair is a great way to protect your hair while also achieving a cute and stylish look. With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve perfectly twisted natural hair in no time!
