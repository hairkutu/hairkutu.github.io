How To Style 60S Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Mar 23, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle to try out? Why not go retro with a 60s-inspired hairdo? The 60s was a time of bold and experimental hairstyles, and many of them are still popular today. In this article, we’ll show you how to style 60s hair in a few easy steps. From the beehive to the bouffant, we’ve got you covered. Read on for our tips and tricks.

Description

The 60s was a decade of change, both socially and culturally. In terms of fashion and beauty, it was a time of experimentation and rebellion. Hairstyles from this era were characterized by volume, height, and boldness. Women’s hair was often styled in bouffants, beehives, and flipped-out ends. Men’s hair was cut short and often styled with gel or pomade. In this article, we’ll be focusing on women’s hairstyles from the 60s.

How to Style 60s Hair: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start styling, you’ll need to prep your hair. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would, then towel dry it. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots to add lift and texture. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to create volume.

Step 2: Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair is crucial for creating a 60s-inspired hairstyle. Take a small section of hair at the crown of your head and backcomb it using a fine-tooth comb. Repeat this process until you’ve teased the entire crown of your head.

Step 3: Create Your Style

Now it’s time to create your desired hairstyle. Here are a few popular 60s styles:

The Beehive:

To create a beehive, gather your teased hair and secure it with bobby pins at the crown of your head. Smooth out the sides of your hair and pin them in place. Finish with a strong-hold hairspray.

The Bouffant:

To create a bouffant, gather your teased hair and gently pull it back, creating a bump at the crown of your head. Secure with bobby pins and smooth out the sides of your hair. Finish with hairspray.

Flipped-Out Ends:

To create flipped-out ends, part your hair down the middle and blow-dry it straight. Use a curling iron to curl the ends of your hair outward. Finish with hairspray.

Tips for Styling 60s Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when styling your hair in a 60s-inspired look:

  • Use a fine-tooth comb for teasing your hair.
  • Invest in a strong-hold hairspray to keep your style in place.
  • If you’re using bobby pins, try to use ones that match your hair color.
  • Add a headband or scarf for a fun and flirty touch.

FAQs About 60s Hair

Q: What hair length is best for 60s hairstyles?

A: Most 60s hairstyles are best suited for medium-length to long hair. However, some styles, such as the flipped-out ends, can be achieved on shorter hair.

Q: Can I style 60s hair without teasing my hair?

A: Teasing your hair is a key component of 60s hairstyles, but you can achieve a similar look by using volumizing products and blow-drying your hair with a round brush.

Hair Care for 60s Hair

Styling your hair in a 60s-inspired look can be damaging to your hair if you’re not careful. Here are a few hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before blow-drying or curling your hair.
  • Avoid using too much hairspray, as it can dry out your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it healthy and moisturized.

Now that you know how to style 60s hair, it’s time to give it a try. Whether you’re going for a retro look or just want to try something new, these hairstyles are sure to turn heads.
