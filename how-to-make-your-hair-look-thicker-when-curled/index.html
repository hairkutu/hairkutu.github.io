How To Make Your Hair Look Thicker When Curled

Written by Bardi Jul 12, 2023 · 9 min read

Curled hair looks absolutely stunning, but if you have fine or thin hair, it can be challenging to make your curls look full and voluminous. Fortunately, there are a few tips and tricks you can use to make your hair look thicker when curled. In this article, we will discuss how to achieve that coveted fullness and bounce in your curls.

How to Make Your Hair Look Thicker When Curled

When it comes to making your hair look thicker when curled, there are a few techniques that can help you achieve the desired look. Here are some tips:

Choose the Right Curling Iron

The right curling iron can make all the difference in creating full and voluminous curls. A curling iron with a larger barrel will create bigger curls, while a smaller barrel will create tighter curls. If you have fine hair, go for a smaller barrel to create tighter curls that will add volume.

Use a Volumizing Product

Using a volumizing product before you curl your hair can help add volume and texture to your curls. Look for a volumizing mousse or spray that is specifically designed to add volume to fine hair. Apply the product to your roots and work it through your hair before you start curling.

Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow-drying your hair upside down can help create volume and lift at the roots. Flip your head upside down and blow-dry your hair until it is about 80% dry. Then, flip your head back up and finish drying your hair as usual.

Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair at the roots can help create volume and lift. Take small sections of hair and backcomb them at the roots using a teasing comb. This will create a cushion of hair at the roots, making your curls look fuller and more voluminous.

Curl Your Hair in Sections

Curling your hair in sections can help create more defined curls and add volume. Divide your hair into small sections and curl each section individually. This will help you create more uniform curls and add volume to your hair.

Finish with Hairspray

When you have finished curling your hair, finish with a light mist of hairspray to help hold your curls in place. Choose a hairspray that is specifically designed to add volume and hold your curls without weighing them down.

Step by Step Guide to Making Your Hair Look Thicker When Curled

Here is a step-by-step guide to making your hair look thicker when curled:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help add volume and texture to your hair, making it easier to curl.

Step 2: Apply a Volumizing Product

Apply a volumizing product to your roots and work it through your hair. This will help add volume and texture to your hair, making it easier to curl.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Flip your head upside down and blow-dry your hair until it is about 80% dry. Then, flip your head back up and finish drying your hair as usual.

Step 4: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into small sections and curl each section individually using a curling iron with a smaller barrel.

Step 5: Tease Your Hair

Tease your hair at the roots using a teasing comb to create volume and lift.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

Finish with a light mist of hairspray to help hold your curls in place and add volume.

Tips for Making Your Hair Look Thicker When Curled

Here are some additional tips for making your hair look thicker when curled:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before curling your hair to protect it from damage
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair
  • Consider using hair extensions to add more volume to your hair
  • Experiment with different curling techniques to find the one that works best for your hair

FAQs

Can I use a larger barrel curling iron to create volume?

No, a larger barrel curling iron will create bigger curls, but it won't necessarily add volume to your hair. If you have fine hair, it's best to use a smaller barrel curling iron to create tighter curls that will add volume.

How often should I curl my hair?

It's best to avoid curling your hair every day, as this can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Try to limit curling your hair to once or twice a week.

Hair Care Tips for Fine Hair

If you have fine hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some hair care tips for fine hair:

  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add volume and texture to your hair
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as this will help prevent breakage

By following these tips and techniques, you can create full and voluminous curls that will make your hair look thicker and more beautiful than ever before!
