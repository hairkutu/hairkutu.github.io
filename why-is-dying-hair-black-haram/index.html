Why Is Dying Hair Black Haram

Written by Christine Jul 06, 2023 Â· 8 min read

As Muslims, we are always in pursuit of following the teachings of our religion. One of the things that we need to be mindful of is the prohibition of dying our hair black. This article will discuss why dyeing hair black is haram and what alternatives are available for those who want to change their hair color.

What Does Haram Mean?

Haram is an Arabic term that means forbidden or prohibited. In Islam, certain things are considered haram because they go against the teachings of the Quran and the Sunnah of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). These things are prohibited because they can lead to harm or are considered sinful.

Why is Dying Hair Black Haram?

According to Islamic teachings, dyeing hair black is considered haram or prohibited. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said, "The Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) cursed the one who does tattoos and the one who has a tattoo done, the one who plucks hair from his face and the one who has it done, and the one who changes the color of his hair and the one who has it done." (Sahih Bukhari)

This hadith clearly states that dyeing hair black is prohibited in Islam. The reason for this is that dyeing hair black is associated with imitating non-Muslims or trying to appear younger than one's actual age. It is also considered a form of deception, which is not acceptable in Islam.

What Are the Alternatives?

For those who want to change their hair color, there are alternatives to dyeing hair black. Some of these alternatives include using henna, which is a natural plant-based dye that can be used to create a range of colors from red to brown. Another alternative is to use hair extensions or wigs, which can be styled to match your natural hair color or to create a new look.

How to Use Henna?

If you want to use henna to change your hair color, here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Choose Your Henna Powder

Make sure that you choose a good quality henna powder that is free from any chemicals or additives. You can purchase henna powder from your local health food store or online.

Step 2: Mix the Henna Powder

Take a bowl and mix the henna powder with water until you get a thick paste. You can also add other natural ingredients like lemon juice, tea, or coffee to enhance the color and nourish your hair.

Step 3: Apply the Henna Paste

Apply the henna paste to your hair, starting from the roots to the tips. Make sure that you cover all your hair evenly and leave it on for at least 2-3 hours. You can also wrap your hair in a towel or plastic wrap to keep the henna paste moist.

Step 4: Rinse Your Hair

After 2-3 hours, rinse your hair with lukewarm water until the water runs clear. Do not use shampoo or conditioner for at least 24 hours after applying henna as it can affect the color.

Tips for Hair Care

Whether you dye your hair or use natural alternatives like henna, it is important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often as they can damage your hair
  • Eat a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals to nourish your hair from the inside out
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated and prevent dryness
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth

FAQ

Is it haram to dye hair in other colors?

No, dyeing hair in other colors is not haram as long as it does not involve any prohibited or harmful ingredients. However, it is important to be mindful of the intention behind changing your hair color and to avoid imitating non-Muslims.

Can men dye their hair?

Yes, men can dye their hair as long as they avoid using black dye. It is also important to choose a hair color that is natural and suitable for their skin tone.

Can women dye their hair?

Yes, women can dye their hair as long as they avoid using black dye. However, it is important to choose a hair color that is natural and does not involve any harmful ingredients.

Conclusion

In conclusion, dyeing hair black is considered haram in Islam because it involves imitating non-Muslims and deception. However, there are alternatives like henna and hair extensions that can be used to change your hair color without going against Islamic teachings. It is also important to take care of your hair and make sure that any hair products you use are free from harmful ingredients.
