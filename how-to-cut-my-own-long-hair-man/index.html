How to Cut My Own Long Hair Man: A Step-by-Step Guide

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you're a man with long hair. But with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut your own long hair, including tips and tricks to make the process easier. So grab your scissors and let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors (preferably hair cutting scissors)
  • A comb
  • A handheld mirror (or two mirrors to see the back of your head)
  • Clips or hair ties to section off your hair

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut. And if possible, invest in a pair of hair cutting scissors for the best results.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. Comb out any tangles and make sure your hair is smooth and even. Then, section off your hair into manageable pieces using clips or hair ties.

Decide on the length you want to cut your hair and measure it out. Keep in mind that it's always better to cut less than you think you need, as you can always go back and cut more later.

Cutting Your Hair

Start with the bottom layer of your hair and work your way up. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across, being careful not to cut too much. Repeat this process for each section of hair, moving up towards the top layer.

For the top layer of your hair, you'll want to use a different technique. Comb the hair straight up and hold it between your fingers. Cut into the hair vertically, making small snips to create texture and movement. This will help prevent your hair from looking too blunt and give it a more natural appearance.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start small and work your way up. It's always better to cut less than you think you need.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming.
  • Use a handheld mirror (or two mirrors) to see the back of your head.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, start with a small trim and work your way up to a bigger cut.
  • Watch tutorials or ask a friend for advice if you're still unsure.

FAQ

Can I cut my own long hair if I've never done it before?

Yes, you can! Just start small and work your way up.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't worry! Hair grows back, and you can always go to a professional to fix any mistakes.

How often should I cut my long hair?

It depends on your hair and how fast it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care After Cutting

Once you've finished cutting your hair, make sure to wash and condition it thoroughly. Use a hair mask or deep conditioner to help restore any damage caused by cutting.

Continue to take care of your hair by using a heat protectant when styling, avoiding excessive heat and chemicals, and getting regular trims to maintain the length and shape of your cut.

Cutting your own long hair can be nerve-wracking, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Just remember to start small, take breaks if you need to, and don't be afraid to ask for advice or help if you're unsure. Happy cutting!
