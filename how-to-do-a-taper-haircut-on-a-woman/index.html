How To Do A Taper Haircut On A Woman Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 28, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you looking for a stylish and low-maintenance haircut for women? A taper haircut is a great option that can add a touch of sophistication to your look. This haircut features longer hair on top that gradually gets shorter towards the nape of the neck. If you want to learn how to do a taper haircut on a woman, keep reading this step-by-step guide.

Description of a Taper Haircut

A taper haircut is a popular style that suits women of different ages and hair types. This cut involves trimming the hair shorter at the back and sides while keeping the length on top. The result is a gradual fade that can be customized to your preference. You can also add layers, bangs, or texture to make the haircut more versatile.

How to Do a Taper Haircut on a Woman

Before you start cutting the hair, you need to gather the necessary tools and materials. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, clippers, comb, hair clips, water spray bottle, and a mirror. You can also use a hair trimmer to achieve a more precise and uniform cut.

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Start by washing and conditioning the hair to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup. Towel-dry the hair gently and comb it to detangle any knots. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to add moisture and manageability to the hair.

Step 2: Section and Clip the Hair

Divide the hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up to the crown. Clip the top section away from the lower section to make it easier to cut the hair evenly.

Step 3: Create the Taper Fade

Using the clippers, start at the base of the neck and trim the hair gradually upwards. Use a longer guard for the top section and a shorter guard for the lower section. Blend the hair by switching between guards and adjust the taper according to the desired length.

Step 4: Cut the Top Section

Unclip the top section and comb the hair straight up. Use the scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. You can add layers or texture by point-cutting or using thinning shears. Blend the top section with the sides and back by using a comb and scissors.

Step 5: Finish and Style the Hair

Once you have achieved the desired length and blend, use the scissors to refine any rough edges or stray hairs. Spray the hair lightly with water or a styling product and comb it into place. You can style the hair with a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling wand to create different looks.

Tips for Doing a Taper Haircut on a Woman

Here are some tips to keep in mind when doing a taper haircut on a woman:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a comb and scissors to blend the hair for a more seamless and natural look.
  • Be patient and take your time to achieve a precise and even cut.
  • Communicate with your client to understand their preferences and expectations.
  • Experiment with different styling products and techniques to enhance the haircut.

FAQs About Taper Haircuts for Women

1. Is a taper haircut suitable for all face shapes?

Yes, a taper haircut can be adapted to different face shapes by adjusting the length and angle of the fade. Consult with your stylist to find the best taper for your features.

2. How often do I need to get a taper haircut?

The frequency of getting a taper haircut depends on the individual's hair growth and maintenance routine. On average, a taper haircut can last 4-6 weeks before needing a touch-up.

3. Can I color my hair after getting a taper haircut?

Yes, you can color your hair after getting a taper haircut. However, it is recommended to wait a few days to let the hair settle and recover from the cut. Consult with your stylist for the best coloring options and aftercare tips.

Hair Care Tips for Taper Haircuts on Women

To maintain a healthy and vibrant hair after getting a taper haircut, here are some hair care tips to follow:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools on the hair too often as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Protect the hair from the sun, wind, and pollution by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Trim the hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of the taper.
  • Eat a balanced and nutritious diet to promote hair growth and strength.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and flattering taper haircut on a woman. Whether you prefer a classic or modern look, a taper can add dimension and texture to your hair without sacrificing length or volume. Consult with your stylist for the best taper for your hair type and lifestyle, and enjoy the benefits of a low-maintenance and versatile haircut.
