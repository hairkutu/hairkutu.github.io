How To Blow Dry A Choppy Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jun 23, 2023 · 8 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to blow dry a choppy bob! If you’ve recently chopped your locks into a trendy, layered bob, getting the perfect blowout can be a bit tricky. But don’t worry, we’ve got you covered! In this article, we’ll show you how to achieve a salon-worthy blow dry at home in just a few easy steps. Let’s get started!

Description

A choppy bob is a stylish and versatile haircut that can be worn by women of all ages. It features layers and texture that give the hair a edgy, modern look. To maintain the shape of your choppy bob, it’s important to blow dry it properly. Blow drying your hair will not only keep your locks in place, but it will also add volume and shine.

How to Blow Dry a Choppy Bob

Follow these simple steps to blow dry your choppy bob:

Step 1: Pre-Dry Your Hair

Before you begin blow drying, towel-dry your hair to remove any excess water. Gently pat your hair with a towel until it’s damp, but not dripping wet. Next, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and remove any knots or tangles.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to protect it from heat damage. This will also help your hair to retain moisture and prevent frizz. Be sure to distribute the spray evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips or hair ties. Start by sectioning your hair into two parts: the top half and the bottom half. Then, divide each section into smaller sections, depending on the thickness of your hair.

Step 4: Blow Dry the Bottom Section

Using a round brush, start blow drying the bottom section of your hair. Begin at the roots and work your way down to the ends, using the brush to smooth out any kinks or curls. Once the section is dry, move on to the next section.

Step 5: Blow Dry the Top Section

Once you’ve finished blow drying the bottom section, move on to the top section. Use the same technique as before, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Make sure to lift the hair at the roots to add volume.

Step 6: Finish with a Blast of Cool Air

Once your hair is completely dry, finish with a blast of cool air to set the style. This will also help to seal the hair cuticle and add shine.

Tips for a Perfect Blow Dry

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect blow dry:

  • Use a high-quality hair dryer with adjustable heat and speed settings.
  • Choose the right brush for your hair type.
  • Blow dry your hair in sections for a more polished finish.
  • Avoid using high heat on wet hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Use a cool shot button to set the style and add shine.

FAQ

How often should I blow dry my hair?

It’s best to limit blow drying to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your hair. You can use dry shampoo on non-wash days to keep your hair looking fresh.

Can I blow dry my hair without a heat protectant?

No, it’s important to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair. Heat protectant sprays help to shield your hair from the high temperatures of the blow dryer.

What kind of brush should I use to blow dry my choppy bob?

For a choppy bob, it’s best to use a round brush with boar bristles. This will help to smooth out your hair and add volume at the roots.

Hair Care for a Choppy Bob

To keep your choppy bob looking its best, it’s important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your choppy bob.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.

That’s it! We hope this guide on how to blow dry a choppy bob has been helpful. Remember to take good care of your hair and enjoy your new, trendy haircut!
