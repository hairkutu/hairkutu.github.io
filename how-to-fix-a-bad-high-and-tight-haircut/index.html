How To Fix A Bad High And Tight Haircut

Written by Frank Mar 31, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you've recently gotten a high and tight haircut and it didn't turn out the way you wanted, don't panic! There are several ways you can fix it without having to wait for it to grow out. In this article, we'll go over how to fix a bad high and tight haircut, step by step.

Description

A high and tight haircut is a popular men's hairstyle that involves cutting the hair on the sides and back of the head very short, while leaving the hair on top longer. It's a low maintenance style that's easy to maintain, but if it's not done correctly, it can look unbalanced or uneven.

How to Fix a Bad High and Tight Haircut

Step by Step

Here are the steps you can take to fix a bad high and tight haircut:

Step 1: Identify the Problem

The first step in fixing a bad high and tight haircut is to identify what the problem is. Is the hair too short on the sides or back? Is it uneven? Is the top too long or too short? Once you know what the problem is, you can start to work on fixing it.

Step 2: Wait it Out

If the problem is that the hair is too short, you may have to wait for it to grow out before you can fix it. In the meantime, try styling it in a way that hides the problem areas.

Step 3: Trim the Top

If the problem is that the top of the hair is too long, you can trim it yourself or go to a barber to have it trimmed. Be sure to bring a picture of what you want it to look like so the barber knows what you're going for.

Step 4: Blend the Sides and Back

If the problem is that the sides and back are too short or uneven, you can try blending them in with the longer hair on top. Use clippers or scissors to carefully blend the hair together, taking care not to cut too much off.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when fixing a bad high and tight haircut:

  • Be patient. It may take some time to fix the problem, especially if you have to wait for your hair to grow out.
  • Don't be afraid to go to a barber or stylist for help. They can often fix the problem more quickly and efficiently than you can on your own.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you're fixing your haircut.
  • Be careful not to cut too much off, as you don't want to make the problem worse.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing a bad high and tight haircut:

  • Q: Can I fix a bad high and tight haircut myself?
  • A: Yes, you can, but it may be easier and more efficient to go to a barber or stylist.
  • Q: How long will it take to fix a bad high and tight haircut?
  • A: It depends on the severity of the problem. Some issues can be fixed quickly, while others may take several weeks or even months to resolve.
  • Q: Will fixing a bad high and tight haircut damage my hair?
  • A: Not if you're careful and don't cut too much off. However, if you're not confident in your abilities, it may be best to go to a professional.

Hair Care

Once you've fixed your bad high and tight haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as from blow dryers or straighteners.
  • Get regular haircuts to maintain your style and keep split ends at bay.

By following these steps and tips, you can fix a bad high and tight haircut and keep your hair looking great.
