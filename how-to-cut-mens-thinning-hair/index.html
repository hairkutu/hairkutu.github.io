How To Cut Men s Thinning Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to cut men's thinning hair. Thinning hair can be a challenge to style, but with the right techniques, you can create a style that looks great and suits your face shape. In this article, we'll show you how to cut men's thinning hair in a way that is easy to follow and gives you the best results. We'll cover everything from the tools you'll need to the different techniques you can use. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents:

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. Preparation
  3. The Cut
  4. Tips for Cutting Thinning Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Tips for Men with Thinning Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Water spray bottle

You may also want to invest in a thinning shear, which can help create texture and volume in your hair. Thinning shears are scissors with teeth that remove some of the bulk from your hair without cutting it too short.

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to prepare it. Here's what you should do:

  1. Wash your hair and let it dry naturally.
  2. Comb your hair in the direction it naturally falls.
  3. Decide on the length you want for your hair.
  4. Put on your haircutting cape or towel to protect your clothes from hair clippings.

Once you've prepared your hair, you're ready to start cutting.

The Cut

Here's how to cut men's thinning hair:

  1. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward.
  2. Use your clippers to trim the sides and back of your head, leaving the top longer.
  3. Use your scissors to cut the top of your hair to the desired length, taking care not to cut too much off at once.
  4. If you want to add texture and volume, use your thinning shears to remove some of the bulk from your hair.
  5. Blend the sides and top together using your scissors, making sure to remove any sharp lines.
  6. Check your hair in the mirror, making sure it's even and the length is what you want.
  7. Trim any stray hairs or adjust the length as needed.

Congratulations, you've just cut your own hair!

Tips for Cutting Thinning Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting men's thinning hair:

  • Start with a longer length and work your way down if needed.
  • Use your thinning shears sparingly to avoid over-thinning your hair.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're not sure what you're doing.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting men's thinning hair:

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how long you want it to be. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

A: Yes, you can cut your hair when it's wet, but it's generally easier to cut when it's dry.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don't worry, hair grows back! If you make a mistake, you can always go to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care Tips for Men with Thinning Hair

Here are some tips to help you care for your hair if you have thinning hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't weigh down your hair.
  • Avoid using too much product, which can make your hair look greasy and flat.
  • Consider using a volumizing spray or mousse to add volume to your hair.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools, which can damage your hair.

With these tips, you can keep your thinning hair looking great and styled to perfection.

Conclusion

Cutting men's thinning hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and tools, you can create a style that looks great and suits your face shape. Whether you're cutting your own hair or going to a professional stylist, these tips and tricks will help you get the best results. So, go ahead and give it a try, and see how great your hair can look!
