How To Cut Little Girl Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 15, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you considering cutting your little girl’s hair at home? It’s a great way to save money and create a fun bonding experience with your child. However, cutting hair can be intimidating, especially if you’ve never done it before. In this article, we’ll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut little girl hair, as well as some tips and FAQ to help you along the way.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut Little Girl Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

How to Cut Little Girl Hair

Cutting little girl hair can seem daunting, but it doesn’t have to be. Here are some key steps to follow:

1. Gather your supplies

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary supplies on hand. This includes:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Cape or towel to protect clothing

2. Choose a style

Decide on a hairstyle before you begin cutting. Look through pictures and decide on a length and style that you and your child like.

3. Wash and dry hair

Wash your child’s hair and let it dry completely before cutting. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, so it’s important to cut hair when it’s dry.

4. Section hair

Section the hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to keep the sections separated.

5. Start cutting

Start cutting the hair in the back, working your way up to the crown of the head. Cut small sections at a time, using the comb to hold the hair in place and the scissors to cut. Cut the hair in a straight line, following the guide you created earlier.

6. Cut the sides

Move on to the sides, cutting small sections at a time. Use the cut hair in the back as a guide for the length. If you want to create layers, cut the hair at an angle.

7. Cut the bangs

If your child has bangs, cut them last. Use the comb to hold the hair in place and cut straight across. Cut the bangs longer than you think you need to, as you can always go back and trim them later.

Tips for Success

Here are some tips to help you successfully cut your little girl’s hair:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can’t put it back once it’s been cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Work in a well-lit area.
  • Take breaks if your child becomes restless or fidgety.
  • Be patient and have fun!

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my child’s hair?

A: It depends on the length and style of your child’s hair. Generally, it’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don’t panic! Mistakes happen. If you make a mistake, try to even out the hair or take your child to a professional stylist to fix the mistake.

Hair Care

After cutting your child’s hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for children’s hair.
  • Brush hair daily to prevent tangling.
  • Use a detangling spray if necessary.
  • Trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends.

Cutting your little girl’s hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. By following these steps and tips, you can successfully cut your child’s hair and create a style that she loves.
