How To Make Thinning Hair On Crown Look Thicker Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Apr 29, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Thinning hair on the crown can be a frustrating issue for both men and women. If you're experiencing hair loss, you're not alone. According to the American Hair Loss Association, two-thirds of men will experience some degree of hair loss by the age of 35. While it's a natural part of aging, there are ways to make thinning hair on the crown look thicker. In this article, we'll explore some tips and tricks to help you achieve a fuller look.

Description

Thinning hair on the crown is a common problem that can be caused by genetics, stress, hormonal changes, or medical conditions. It can make you feel self-conscious and affect your confidence. However, there are ways to make your hair look thicker and fuller without resorting to expensive treatments or hair transplants. With some simple styling tricks and hair care tips, you can create the illusion of thicker hair and boost your confidence.

How to Make Thinning Hair on Crown Look Thicker

1. Choose the Right Haircut

The right haircut can make a huge difference in the appearance of thinning hair. Consider getting a shorter haircut or a buzz cut to create the illusion of thicker hair. Avoid long hairstyles that can make your hair look thinner and weigh it down. A layered haircut can also add volume and texture to your hair.

2. Use the Right Products

Using the right hair products can also help make your hair look thicker. Look for volumizing shampoos and conditioners that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, or collagen. They can help strengthen your hair and add volume. Avoid heavy styling products like gels or waxes that can weigh your hair down. Instead, use lightweight mousses or sprays to add volume and texture to your hair.

3. Blow-Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow-drying your hair upside down can add volume and lift to your roots. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Then, flip your hair upside down and blow-dry it using a round brush. Focus on lifting your roots and creating volume. Once your hair is dry, flip it back and style it as desired.

4. Use Hair Fibers or Concealers

Hair fibers or concealers can also help make thinning hair on the crown look thicker. They are made of natural keratin fibers that bind to your hair and create the illusion of thicker hair. Simply sprinkle the fibers onto your hair and pat them down gently. They can last for up to 24 hours and are resistant to wind, rain, and sweat.

5. Consider Hair Extensions

Hair extensions can also help make your hair look thicker and fuller. They are available in a variety of styles and colors and can be clipped or sewn onto your natural hair. However, they can be expensive and require maintenance. Make sure to choose high-quality extensions and consult with a professional stylist to avoid damaging your hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to making thinning hair on the crown look thicker:

Step 1: Choose the right haircut

Consider getting a shorter, layered haircut or a buzz cut to create the illusion of thicker hair.

Step 2: Use the right products

Use volumizing shampoos and conditioners and lightweight mousses or sprays to add volume and texture to your hair.

Step 3: Blow-dry your hair upside down

Flip your hair upside down and blow-dry it using a round brush to create volume and lift at the roots.

Step 4: Use hair fibers or concealers

Sprinkle hair fibers or concealers onto your hair and pat them down gently to create the illusion of thicker hair.

Step 5: Consider hair extensions

If you want a more dramatic change, consider getting high-quality hair extensions that can be clipped or sewn onto your natural hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help make your hair look thicker:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons that can damage your hair.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that support hair growth, such as biotin, iron, and zinc.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause damage.
  • Consult with a professional stylist or a dermatologist if you're experiencing severe hair loss or thinning.

FAQ

Q: Can stress cause thinning hair on the crown?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss and thinning. Stress can disrupt the normal hair growth cycle and lead to hair loss. If you're experiencing stress-related hair loss, try to reduce your stress levels through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques.

Q: Can hair loss be reversed?

A: It depends on the cause of hair loss. In some cases, hair loss can be reversed with proper treatment, such as medication or hair transplant surgery. However, in many cases, hair loss is a natural part of aging and cannot be reversed completely.

Q: Are hair fibers or concealers safe to use?

A: Yes, hair fibers and concealers are safe to use and do not cause any damage to your hair. They are made of natural keratin fibers and are completely safe for daily use.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential to maintaining its health and preventing hair loss. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water, which can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel, which can cause breakage and damage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make thinning hair on the crown look thicker and boost your confidence.
