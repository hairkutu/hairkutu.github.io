What If You Cut Your Hair With Regular Scissors

Written by Christine Jul 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Have you ever found yourself in a situation where you desperately need a haircut but can't wait for an appointment with your hairstylist? In such cases, you might be tempted to cut your hair with regular scissors at home. However, before you pick up your scissors and start chopping away, there are a few things you should know.

Description

Cutting your hair with regular scissors can be a risky move, especially if you don't have any experience in hair cutting. It can lead to uneven and choppy haircuts, making it difficult to fix even by a professional stylist. In some cases, it can also result in hair damage and breakage, leading to hair loss.

How to Cut Your Hair with Regular Scissors?

If you are still determined to cut your hair with regular scissors, here are a few steps you can follow:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner, and dry it completely before you start cutting. Wet hair is harder to cut and can result in uneven cuts.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will make it easier for you to cut each section evenly.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Using your regular scissors, start cutting your hair section by section. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven cuts. Cut slowly and carefully, making sure that you're cutting each section evenly.

Step 4: Check Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, check it in the mirror to make sure that it's even. If you notice any unevenness, you may need to trim it a little more.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair with Regular Scissors

Here are a few tips to keep in mind if you decide to cut your hair with regular scissors:

  • Invest in a good quality pair of scissors that are sharp and easy to handle.
  • Make sure that you have good lighting when cutting your hair to avoid any mistakes.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once to avoid uneven cuts.
  • Take your time and cut slowly to ensure that you're cutting your hair evenly.
  • Be prepared to make mistakes and have a backup plan in case things don't go as planned.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

Yes, you can cut your hair with regular scissors, but it's not recommended, especially if you don't have any experience in hair cutting. It can lead to uneven and choppy haircuts, and in some cases, hair damage and breakage.

What happens if you cut your hair with regular scissors?

Cutting your hair with regular scissors can result in uneven and choppy haircuts, making it difficult to fix even by a professional stylist. In some cases, it can also result in hair damage and breakage, leading to hair loss.

How can I avoid making mistakes when cutting my hair with regular scissors?

To avoid making mistakes when cutting your hair with regular scissors, invest in a good quality pair of scissors that are sharp and easy to handle. Make sure that you have good lighting when cutting your hair and cut slowly to ensure that you're cutting your hair evenly.

Hair Care after Cutting Your Hair with Regular Scissors

After cutting your hair with regular scissors, it's essential to take care of your hair to avoid any damage. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair with a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy and avoid split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals to keep your hair healthy from the inside out.

Cutting your hair with regular scissors can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a decent haircut. However, if you're not confident in your abilities or don't want to risk damaging your hair, it's best to leave it to the professionals.
