Does Your Hair Fall Out More When It s Growing

Written by Robby Apr 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

As we all know, hair loss is a common problem that affects millions of people around the world. There are many factors that can contribute to hair loss, including genetics, age, and hormonal changes. However, there is another question that often comes up - does your hair fall out more when it's growing?

In this blog post, we will explore this question in detail and provide you with some tips on how to manage hair loss while your hair is growing.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description:

Many people believe that their hair falls out more when it's growing because they notice more hair strands in their brush or shower drain. However, this is simply a misconception. The truth is that hair falls out at a consistent rate of 50 to 100 hairs per day, regardless of whether it's growing or not.

So, why do people think that their hair falls out more when it's growing? The reason is that when hair is growing, it pushes out the old, dead hair strands from the follicles. This shedding process is known as "telogen effluvium," and it can make it seem like more hair is falling out than usual.

How to:

If you're experiencing hair loss while your hair is growing, there are a few things you can do to manage it:

1. Practice good hair care:

Make sure you're using a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type. Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage.

2. Eat a healthy diet:

Eating a balanced diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals can help keep your hair healthy and strong. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and biotin, as these nutrients are essential for healthy hair growth.

3. Manage stress:

Stress can have a negative impact on your hair health, so it's important to manage it effectively. Try practicing relaxation techniques like meditation or yoga, or consider seeing a therapist if your stress levels are high.

Step by step:

Here are some steps you can take to manage hair loss while your hair is growing:

Step 1:

Start by assessing your hair care routine. Are you using products that are too harsh for your hair? Are you using hot tools too often? If so, make some changes to your routine to help protect your hair.

Step 2:

Make sure you're eating a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals. Consider taking a daily multivitamin or supplement to ensure you're getting all the nutrients your hair needs.

Step 3:

Manage your stress levels by practicing relaxation techniques or seeing a therapist. This can help reduce the impact of stress on your hair health.

Tips:

Here are some additional tips to help manage hair loss while your hair is growing:

1. Be gentle:

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and try to be gentle when brushing or combing it. This can help prevent breakage and hair loss.

2. Get regular trims:

Getting regular trims can help prevent split ends, which can lead to hair breakage and loss.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb:

Using a wide-tooth comb can help detangle your hair without causing breakage or damage.

FAQ:

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can have a negative impact on your hair health and contribute to hair loss.

Q: Can hair products cause hair loss?

A: Yes, using products that are too harsh for your hair can contribute to hair loss. Make sure you're using products that are appropriate for your hair type.

Q: Can hair loss be reversed?

A: In some cases, hair loss can be reversed with the right treatment. However, it's important to see a doctor or dermatologist to determine the underlying cause of your hair loss and develop an appropriate treatment plan.

Hair Care:

Overall, taking good care of your hair is key to preventing hair loss while your hair is growing. Be gentle with your hair, eat a healthy diet, manage your stress levels, and use appropriate hair products to keep your hair healthy and strong.

Remember, while shedding may seem like more hair is falling out than usual, it's simply a natural part of the hair growth process. With the right care, you can keep your hair healthy and looking great!
