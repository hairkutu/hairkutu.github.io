How To Prevent Flyaways In Your Short Hair

Written by Petter Aug 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Do you struggle with flyaways in your short hair? This can be a frustrating and annoying problem to deal with, especially if you have a busy lifestyle. Fortunately, there are several tips and tricks you can use to prevent flyaways and keep your hair looking sleek and polished. In this article, we will discuss how to prevent flyaways in your short hair, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and FAQs.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prevent Flyaways
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Preventing Flyaways
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Prevent Flyaways

Preventing flyaways starts with proper hair care. This includes using the right products, such as a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoiding heat styling tools that can damage your hair. Additionally, you can use the following tips to prevent flyaways:

1. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to moisturize your hair and prevent it from becoming dry and brittle. Apply a small amount to your hair after washing and conditioning, and be sure to focus on the ends of your hair where flyaways tend to occur.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When detangling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. This will help to prevent breakage and minimize flyaways.

3. Use a Smoothing Serum

A smoothing serum can help to tame flyaways and keep your hair looking sleek and polished. Apply a small amount to your hair after styling, focusing on the areas where flyaways tend to occur.

4. Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, making it more prone to flyaways. Try to wash your hair every other day, or even every few days, to help maintain its natural oils.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these step-by-step instructions to help prevent flyaways in your short hair:

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to prevent product buildup.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing and conditioning, apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair. Be sure to focus on the ends of your hair where flyaways tend to occur.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When detangling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. This will help to prevent breakage and minimize flyaways.

4. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush to help smooth out any flyaways. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from heat styling tools.

5. Apply a Smoothing Serum

After blow drying, apply a smoothing serum to your hair. Be sure to focus on the areas where flyaways tend to occur.

6. Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired, using a light hold hairspray to help keep flyaways in place.

Tips for Preventing Flyaways

Here are some additional tips for preventing flyaways:

1. Protect Your Hair from the Elements

Wind, sun, and humidity can all contribute to flyaways. To prevent this, wear a hat or use a scarf to protect your hair from the elements.

2. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent flyaways and minimize friction on your hair, which can cause breakage.

3. Use a Clarifying Shampoo

If you have product buildup in your hair, it can make it more prone to flyaways. Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any buildup and keep your hair healthy.

FAQs

Q: Can I use hairspray to prevent flyaways?

A: Yes, you can use a light hold hairspray to help keep flyaways in place. Just be sure not to use too much, as this can make your hair look stiff and unnatural.

Q: Can I prevent flyaways without using products?

A: Yes, proper hair care can go a long way in preventing flyaways. This includes using the right shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools, and using a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help prevent flyaways:

1. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends, which can contribute to flyaways.

2. Use a Heat Protectant Spray

When using heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Avoid Over-Processing Your Hair

Chemical treatments, such as coloring and perming, can damage your hair and make it more prone to flyaways. Avoid over-processing your hair and be sure to use a deep conditioning treatment after any chemical treatments.

By following these tips and tricks, you can prevent flyaways in your short hair and keep your hair looking sleek and polished.
