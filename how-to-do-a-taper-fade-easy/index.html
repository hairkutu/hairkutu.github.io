How To Do A Taper Fade Easy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Sep 13, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the barbershop for a simple taper fade? Why not do it yourself? With a little practice and the right tools, you can easily create a smooth and stylish taper fade at home. In this article, we’ll show you how to do a taper fade easy, step-by-step. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents:

  • Step 1: Gather Your Supplies
  • Step 2: Start with a Longer Guard
  • Step 3: Switch to a Shorter Guard
  • Step 4: Blend the Sides and Top
  • Step 5: Define the Hairline
  • Tips for a Perfect Taper Fade
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for a Healthy Scalp and Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start, make sure you have the right tools. You’ll need:

  • Clippers with guards of different lengths
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Brush
  • Haircut cape or towel
  • Two mirrors
  • Styling gel or pomade (optional)

Once you have everything ready, it’s time to get started.

Step 2: Start with a Longer Guard

Attach the longest guard to your clippers and start cutting the hair on the sides and back of the head. Work your way up towards the temples, making sure to keep the clippers level with the head. You want to create a gradual fade, so don’t go too high up yet.

Remember to go slow and use short strokes. You can always go shorter, but you can’t put the hair back once you’ve cut it.

Step 3: Switch to a Shorter Guard

Once you’ve gone as high up as you want with the longer guard, switch to a shorter one and repeat the process. You want to create a smooth transition between the longer and shorter hair, so use a blending technique.

Start by using the clippers without a guard to create a guideline around the hairline. Then, use the shorter guard to blend the hair between the guideline and the longer hair.

Step 4: Blend the Sides and Top

Now it’s time to blend the sides and top. Use the same technique as before, but this time, use a comb to lift the hair and create a more natural blend. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one, blending the hair as you go.

Step 5: Define the Hairline

Use scissors to trim the hairline around the ears and neck. Use a comb to lift the hair and create a clean line. You can also use the clippers to create a sharper line if you prefer.

Finally, use styling gel or pomade to define the hairline and add some texture to the hair.

Tips for a Perfect Taper Fade

  • Practice makes perfect. Don’t expect to get it right the first time.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  • Use short strokes and go slow.
  • Make sure your clippers are oiled and clean.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help if you need it.

FAQs

Q: How long does a taper fade last?

A: A taper fade can last up to two weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows.

Q: Can I do a taper fade on curly hair?

A: Yes, you can. However, it’s important to use the right technique and tools to avoid damaging the hair.

Q: Can I use a razor instead of clippers?

A: We don’t recommend using a razor for a taper fade. Clippers are easier to control and create a smoother blend.

Hair Care Tips for a Healthy Scalp and Hair

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Massage your scalp to stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Stay hydrated and get enough sleep.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you should be able to do a taper fade easy and achieve a professional-looking result. Remember to take your time, practice, and have fun. Good luck!
