Can I Straighten My Hair After Japanese Straightening

Written by William Jun 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you've recently undergone Japanese straightening, you might be wondering if you can still straighten your hair the way you used to. The answer is yes, but there are certain things you need to keep in mind to avoid damaging your hair. In this blog post, we'll discuss everything you need to know about straightening your hair after Japanese straightening.

Description

Japanese straightening is a popular hair treatment that permanently straightens your hair. It involves the use of chemicals to break the bonds in your hair and then resetting them in a straight position. This treatment can leave your hair sleek and manageable, but it's important to note that it's a permanent treatment, meaning that once it's done, you can't undo it.

How to Straighten Your Hair After Japanese Straightening

If you want to straighten your hair after Japanese straightening, you can still do so, but you need to be careful not to damage your hair. Here are some tips:

1. Wait at least three days

After Japanese straightening, you need to wait at least three days before washing your hair or applying any heat to it. This will give your hair enough time to settle and prevent any damage.

2. Use a heat protectant

Before applying any heat to your hair, make sure to use a heat protectant. This will help prevent damage and keep your hair healthy.

3. Use a straightening tool with ceramic plates

Ceramic plates are gentler on your hair than metal plates. They distribute heat evenly and prevent hot spots, which can cause damage to your hair.

4. Use a lower heat setting

When straightening your hair after Japanese straightening, use a lower heat setting. This will prevent your hair from getting too hot and becoming damaged.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to straighten your hair after Japanese straightening:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Wait at least three days after Japanese straightening before washing your hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

Step 2: Apply a heat protectant

Apply a heat protectant to your hair before applying any heat to it. This will help prevent damage and keep your hair healthy.

Step 3: Section your hair

Section your hair into small sections using clips. This will make it easier to straighten your hair and prevent any tangles.

Step 4: Straighten your hair

Use a straightening tool with ceramic plates to straighten your hair. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends. Use a lower heat setting to prevent damage.

Step 5: Finish with a serum

Apply a serum to your hair to add shine and keep it healthy. Use a small amount and apply it evenly throughout your hair.

Tips for Straightening Your Hair After Japanese Straightening

Here are some tips to keep in mind when straightening your hair after Japanese straightening:

1. Avoid washing your hair too often

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair no more than two to three times a week.

2. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner

Sulfates can be harsh on your hair and strip it of its natural oils. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

FAQs

Can I straighten my hair immediately after Japanese straightening?

No, you need to wait at least three days before straightening your hair to prevent damage.

Can I use any straightening tool after Japanese straightening?

No, you should use a straightening tool with ceramic plates to prevent damage to your hair.

How often should I straighten my hair after Japanese straightening?

You should avoid straightening your hair too often to prevent damage. Try to limit it to once a week.

Hair Care After Japanese Straightening

After Japanese straightening, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid coloring your hair

Coloring your hair can be damaging, especially after Japanese straightening. If you must color your hair, wait at least two weeks after the treatment and use a gentle, ammonia-free hair color.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair to prevent damage.

3. Avoid tying your hair too tightly

Tying your hair too tightly can cause breakage and damage. Use a loose hair tie or clip to keep your hair in place.

Overall, you can still straighten your hair after Japanese straightening, but you need to be careful not to damage it. Follow these tips and take care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful.
