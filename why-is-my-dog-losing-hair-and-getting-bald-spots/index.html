Why Is My Dog Losing Hair And Getting Bald Spots

Written by Luffy Aug 27, 2023 · 10 min read

As a dog owner, noticing that your furry friend is losing hair and getting bald spots can be concerning. There are several reasons why this may be happening, ranging from allergies to parasites to underlying health issues. In this article, we’ll explore some of the most common causes and offer tips on how to care for your dog’s hair.

Allergies

Allergies are one of the most common reasons why dogs lose hair and develop bald spots. These can be caused by environmental factors such as pollen, dust mites, and mold, or by food allergies. If your dog is scratching and biting at their skin, this can lead to hair loss and bald spots.

Symptoms

Some common symptoms of allergies in dogs include:

  • Itching and scratching
  • Red, inflamed skin
  • Bumps or hives
  • Chewing or biting at paws
  • Ear infections

Treatment

If you suspect your dog has allergies, it’s important to take them to the vet for a proper diagnosis. Treatment may include antihistamines, steroids, or a change in diet. Your vet may also recommend special shampoos or topical treatments to soothe your dog’s skin.

Parasites

Another common cause of hair loss in dogs is parasites such as fleas, ticks, and mites. These pests can irritate your dog’s skin, causing them to scratch and bite at themselves, leading to hair loss and bald spots.

Symptoms

Some common symptoms of parasites in dogs include:

  • Itching and scratching
  • Biting at skin
  • Red, inflamed skin
  • Bumps or scabs
  • Visible fleas or ticks

Treatment

To treat parasites, your vet may recommend a topical or oral medication. You may also need to treat your home and yard to prevent re-infestation. Regular grooming and flea/tick prevention can also help prevent parasites from taking hold.

Skin Infections

Underlying skin infections can also cause hair loss and bald spots in dogs. These can be caused by bacteria, yeast, or fungus and can lead to itching, redness, and other symptoms.

Symptoms

Some common symptoms of skin infections in dogs include:

  • Itching and scratching
  • Red, inflamed skin
  • Bumps or scabs
  • Discharge or odor from skin
  • Hair loss or bald spots

Treatment

Treatment for skin infections can vary depending on the underlying cause. Your vet may recommend antibiotics, antifungal medication, or special shampoos to help soothe your dog’s skin. It’s important to follow your vet’s recommended treatment plan to prevent the infection from spreading or recurring.

Hormonal Imbalances

Hormonal imbalances can also cause hair loss and bald spots in dogs. This is especially true for breeds such as the Pomeranian or Chihuahua, which are prone to hormonal imbalances such as hypothyroidism or Cushing’s disease.

Symptoms

Some common symptoms of hormonal imbalances in dogs include:

  • Weight gain
  • Lethargy
  • Increase in appetite or thirst
  • Hair loss or bald spots
  • Changes in skin or coat texture

Treatment

If your dog has a hormonal imbalance, treatment may involve medication or dietary changes. Your vet may also recommend diagnostic tests to determine the underlying cause of the imbalance.

Diet

A poor diet can also contribute to hair loss and bald spots in dogs. If your dog is not getting the proper nutrients, their skin and coat can suffer. This is especially true if your dog is not getting enough protein or essential fatty acids.

Symptoms

Some common symptoms of a poor diet in dogs include:

  • Dull, dry coat
  • Flaky skin
  • Hair loss or bald spots
  • Weight loss or gain
  • Lethargy

Treatment

To improve your dog’s diet, it’s important to choose high-quality, nutrient-rich foods. Look for foods that are high in protein and essential fatty acids, and avoid foods that contain fillers or by-products. Your vet can recommend a diet that is tailored to your dog’s needs.

Stress

Stress can also play a role in hair loss and bald spots in dogs. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including changes in routine, separation anxiety, or other environmental stressors.

Symptoms

Some common symptoms of stress in dogs include:

  • Excessive licking or chewing
  • Shedding or thinning coat
  • Hair loss or bald spots
  • Changes in appetite or behavior
  • Aggression or anxiety

Treatment

To reduce stress in your dog, it’s important to identify and address the underlying cause. This may involve providing more exercise, establishing a routine, or working with a professional trainer or behaviorist. Your vet may also recommend medication to help your dog manage their stress.

Genetic Factors

Some breeds are more prone to hair loss and bald spots than others due to genetic factors. For example, breeds such as the Chinese Crested or the Xoloitzcuintli are naturally hairless or have very little hair. Other breeds may have a predisposition to certain skin conditions that can cause hair loss.

Symptoms

The symptoms of genetic hair loss can vary depending on the breed. Some breeds may have hair loss in specific areas, while others may be completely hairless.

Treatment

There may not be a cure for genetic hair loss, but there are ways to manage the symptoms. This may involve providing special skin care, such as moisturizing or sunscreen, or using clothing or other protective gear to protect your dog’s skin.

How to Care for Your Dog’s Hair

Regardless of the underlying cause of your dog’s hair loss, it’s important to care for their coat to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

Groom regularly

Regular grooming can help prevent tangles, matting, and other issues that can lead to hair loss. Brush your dog’s coat regularly to remove loose hair and distribute natural oils.

Bathe as needed

Bathing can help remove dirt and debris from your dog’s coat, but be careful not to overdo it. Too much bathing can strip the skin of natural oils, leading to dryness and irritation.

Provide a healthy diet

A healthy diet can help keep your dog’s skin and coat healthy. Look for foods that are high in protein and essential fatty acids, and avoid foods that contain fillers or by-products.

Tips for Hair Care

Here are some additional tips for keeping your dog’s hair healthy:

  • Protect your dog’s coat from the sun with a dog-safe sunscreen
  • Use a conditioning spray to keep your dog’s coat soft and shiny
  • Trim hair around your dog’s eyes to prevent irritation and hair loss
  • Avoid using human hair products on your dog’s coat
  • Consult with your vet before using any new hair care products
