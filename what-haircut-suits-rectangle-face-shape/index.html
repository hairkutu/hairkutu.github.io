What Haircut Suits Rectangle Face Shape

Written by Robby Jun 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have a rectangle face shape, finding the right haircut can be a challenge. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll explore the best haircuts for rectangle face shapes, as well as tips and tricks for maintaining your new 'do. Let's get started!

Description

A rectangle face shape is characterized by a longer, narrower shape, with a forehead, cheekbones, and jawline that are all relatively similar in width. The goal of a good haircut for this face shape is to create the illusion of more width, balancing out the proportions of the face.

How To

The key to a good haircut for rectangle face shapes is to add volume and width to the sides of the face. This can be achieved through a variety of techniques, including layering, texturizing, and adding bangs.

Layering

Layering is a great way to add volume and movement to your hair. For rectangle face shapes, consider a layered cut that falls just below the chin, with shorter layers framing the face. This will help to soften the angles of the jawline and add width to the sides of the face.

Texturizing

Texturizing is another technique that can be used to add volume and movement to your hair. Consider a choppy, layered cut with lots of texture and volume. This will help to create the illusion of more width and balance out the proportions of your face.

Bangs

Bangs can also be a great option for rectangle face shapes. Consider a side-swept bang that falls just above the eyebrows, or a wispy fringe that falls just below the cheekbones. This will help to add width to the forehead and balance out the proportions of the face.

Step By Step

1. Consult with your stylist. Before getting a new haircut, it's important to consult with your stylist to determine the best cut for your face shape and hair type.

2. Consider layering. A layered cut can add volume and movement to your hair, softening the angles of the jawline and adding width to the sides of the face.

3. Try texturizing. Texturizing can also help to add volume and movement to your hair, creating the illusion of more width and balance.

4. Experiment with bangs. Consider a side-swept bang or a wispy fringe to add width to the forehead and balance out the proportions of the face.

5. Maintain your new haircut. To keep your new haircut looking fresh and stylish, be sure to maintain it with regular trims and styling.

Tips

- Avoid hairstyles that are too long or too straight, as these can elongate the face even further.

- Consider adding highlights or lowlights to your hair to add dimension and depth.

- Experiment with different partings to see what works best for your face shape.

FAQ

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a rectangle face shape?

Avoid hairstyles that are too long or too straight, as these can elongate the face even further. Also avoid hairstyles that add too much height, such as a high ponytail, as this can make the face look even longer.

What hairstyles are best for rectangle face shapes?

Hairstyles that add volume and width to the sides of the face are best for rectangle face shapes. Consider a layered cut, texturizing, or bangs to achieve this look.

Should I consider hair color when choosing a haircut for my rectangle face shape?

Yes! Adding highlights or lowlights to your hair can help to add dimension and depth, creating the illusion of more width and balance.

Hair Care

To keep your new haircut looking fresh and stylish, be sure to maintain it with regular trims and styling. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated, and consider using a heat protectant spray when styling with heat tools.

Now that you know what haircut suits rectangle face shape, it's time to hit the salon and get a new 'do! With the right cut and some simple hair care tips, you'll be looking and feeling your best in no time. Good luck!
