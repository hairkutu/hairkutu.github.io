How To Thin Out Thick Hair With Thinning Shears

Written by Eveline Jun 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can be voluminous and look great, it can also be difficult to manage and style. If you have thick hair, you may have trouble getting it to lay flat, or you may find that it looks too heavy. One way to combat this problem is by using thinning shears. In this article, we'll explain how to thin out thick hair with thinning shears, step by step.

Description

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that are used to remove bulk from hair. They have teeth on one blade, which allow them to cut hair in a way that thins it out without removing length. Thinning shears are a great tool for anyone with thick hair, as they can help to reduce the weight and make hair easier to manage.

How to Thin Out Thick Hair with Thinning Shears

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Before you begin thinning your hair, you'll want to make sure it's clean and dry. This will make it easier to see where you need to thin it out, and will also help the shears to cut more smoothly.

Step 2: Section your hair

Next, you'll want to section your hair so that you can work on one area at a time. Use hair clips to separate your hair into sections, starting at the bottom and working your way up.

Step 3: Use the thinning shears

Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the thinning shears to cut into the hair, starting at the mid-length and working your way down to the ends. Be sure to only cut a small amount of hair at a time, and to keep the shears at an angle so that you're not cutting straight across.

Step 4: Blend the cut sections

Once you've thinned out a section of hair, use a comb to blend the cut sections with the uncut sections. This will help to create a more natural look and prevent any choppy or uneven sections.

Step 5: Repeat on other sections

Continue to work your way up your head, sectioning off and thinning out each area of hair. Be sure to take breaks if your hands get tired, and to step back and look at your progress to make sure you're not cutting too much.

Tips

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Use the thinning shears sparingly. It's easy to overdo it and end up with too much hair removed.
  • Be patient. Thinning out thick hair can take time, but the end result is worth it.

FAQ

Can anyone use thinning shears?

Thinning shears are best for people with thick hair. If you have thin or fine hair, using thinning shears can make it look even thinner.

Do thinning shears damage hair?

When used properly, thinning shears should not damage hair. However, if you use them too often or cut too much hair at once, you could end up with damaged or split ends.

How often should I thin out my hair?

This depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it becomes thick. For most people, thinning out hair every 6-8 weeks is sufficient.

Hair Care

Thinning out your hair can make it easier to manage, but it's important to take care of it properly to prevent damage. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay, and consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.
