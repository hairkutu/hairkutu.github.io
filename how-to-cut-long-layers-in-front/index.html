How To Cut Long Layers In Front A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine May 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, adding long layers in front can be a great way to freshen up your look. But before you grab a pair of scissors and start snipping away, it's important to know the proper technique to avoid any hair disasters. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to cut long layers in front, as well as provide some tips and tricks for hair care.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Long Layers in Front
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips and Tricks
  4. FAQs

How to Cut Long Layers in Front

Cutting long layers in front is a great way to frame your face and add dimension to your hair. Here are the basic steps:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair so you can accurately see the length and texture of your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

2. Determine the length of the layers

Decide how long you want your layers to be. If you're not sure, start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't add hair back once it's cut.

3. Section off the hair you want to cut

Using a comb, section off the hair you want to cut into a triangular shape. The point of the triangle should be at the center of your forehead, and the base should be at the sides of your head.

4. Cut the hair at a slight angle

Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at a slight angle, starting from the shortest point at the center of your forehead and gradually getting longer towards the sides of your head. This will create a soft, blended look.

5. Check for balance

Once you've finished one side, check for balance by comparing it to the other side. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure both sides are even.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed guide to cutting long layers in front:

Step 1: Gather your tools

You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to keep your hair in place.

Step 2: Wash and dry your hair

Start with clean, dry hair so you can accurately see the length and texture of your hair.

Step 3: Section off the hair

Using a comb, section off the hair you want to cut into a triangular shape. The point of the triangle should be at the center of your forehead, and the base should be at the sides of your head. Clip the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 4: Cut the hair at a slight angle

Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at a slight angle, starting from the shortest point at the center of your forehead and gradually getting longer towards the sides of your head. Cut small sections at a time to ensure a more precise cut.

Step 5: Check for balance

Once you've finished one side, check for balance by comparing it to the other side. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure both sides are even.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to keep in mind when cutting long layers in front:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut small sections at a time to ensure a more precise cut.
  • Check for balance by comparing both sides.
  • If you're not confident in cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQs

Can I cut long layers in front on curly hair?

Yes, you can cut long layers in front on curly hair. Just be sure to cut the hair when it's dry so you can see the natural curl pattern and how it will fall.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too frequently to prevent damage.
  • Use a heat protectant before using hot styling tools.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet to prevent breakage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can successfully cut long layers in front and maintain healthy, beautiful hair.
