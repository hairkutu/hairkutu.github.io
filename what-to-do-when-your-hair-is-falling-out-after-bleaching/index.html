What To Do When Your Hair Is Falling Out After Bleaching Tips And Hair Care

Written by Bardi May 05, 2023 · 8 min read

If you’re experiencing hair loss after bleaching, you’re not alone. Many people have experienced hair damage after bleaching, including hair loss, thinning, and breakage. However, there are things you can do to prevent further damage, promote hair growth, and restore your hair’s health. In this article, we’ll go over some essential tips and hair care routines to help you manage hair loss after bleaching.

Table of Contents

  • How to Know if Your Hair Is Falling Out After Bleaching
  • What Causes Hair Loss After Bleaching
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Loss After Bleaching
  • How to Restore Hair Health After Bleaching
  • FAQ

How to Know if Your Hair Is Falling Out After Bleaching

Hair loss after bleaching can be gradual or sudden. You may notice that your hair is thinning or breaking, or you may see clumps of hair falling out when you brush or wash your hair. Other signs of hair damage and breakage include split ends, dryness, and lack of elasticity. If you’re experiencing any of these symptoms, it’s essential to take action to prevent further damage and promote hair growth.

What Causes Hair Loss After Bleaching

Bleaching is a harsh chemical process that can weaken and damage your hair. When you bleach your hair, the chemicals strip away the natural oils and proteins that protect your hair and keep it healthy. As a result, your hair becomes dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. Additionally, the heat from blow-drying, straightening, and other styling tools can further damage your hair and contribute to hair loss.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss After Bleaching

Preventing hair loss after bleaching requires a combination of protective measures and hair care routines. Here are some tips to help you protect your hair and promote hair growth:

1. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help strengthen and repair your hair by restoring the natural proteins that are lost during bleaching. Look for products that contain keratin, collagen, or other proteins to help rebuild your hair’s structure and prevent further damage.

2. Moisturize Your Hair

Dry hair is more prone to breakage, so it’s essential to keep your hair moisturized after bleaching. Use a hydrating conditioner and deep conditioning treatment to help restore moisture and elasticity to your hair.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can further damage your hair and contribute to hair loss. Try to avoid using hot tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons, and let your hair air dry whenever possible.

4. Limit Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments like relaxers, perms, and color can further weaken and damage your hair after bleaching. Try to limit these treatments as much as possible, or wait until your hair has fully recovered before getting them.

How to Restore Hair Health After Bleaching

Restoring your hair’s health after bleaching requires a combination of hair care routines and lifestyle changes. Here are some tips to help you restore your hair’s health:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth and restore your hair’s health. Focus on eating foods that are high in protein, iron, vitamin C, and biotin, which are all essential for healthy hair growth.

2. Take Hair Supplements

Hair supplements like biotin, collagen, and omega-3 fatty acids can help promote hair growth and restore your hair’s health. Talk to your doctor or a hair care specialist to find the right supplements for your hair type and needs.

3. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day to help improve circulation and promote healthy hair growth.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can contribute to hair loss. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and strong.

FAQ

Q: Can hair loss after bleaching be reversed?

A: Yes, in most cases, hair loss after bleaching can be reversed with proper hair care and lifestyle changes. You may need to give your hair time to recover and grow back, but with the right care, your hair can become healthy and strong again.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after bleaching?

A: The rate of hair growth varies from person to person, but on average, hair grows about half an inch per month. It may take several months or even years for your hair to fully recover and grow back after bleaching, depending on the extent of the damage.

The key to preventing hair loss after bleaching is to take action as soon as you notice signs of damage. By following these tips and hair care routines, you can help restore your hair’s health and promote healthy hair growth.
