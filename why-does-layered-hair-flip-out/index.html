Why Does Layered Hair Flip Out

Written by Christine Jun 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Layered hair is a popular hairstyle that many people love. It adds volume, movement, and texture to your hair. However, sometimes layered hair can flip out, which can be frustrating. If you're wondering why your layered hair flips out, keep reading to find out.

Table of Contents:

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description:

Layered hair flips out because of the way the layers are cut. When you have layers in your hair, they are usually cut at an angle. This angle creates what hairstylists call "weight distribution." When the weight of the hair is distributed unevenly, it can cause the layers to flip out.

Additionally, layered hair can flip out if you have naturally curly hair or if you use heat styling tools like a curling iron or flat iron. Curly hair tends to have more volume, and the layers can flip out if they are not cut correctly. Heat styling tools can also cause the layers to flip out if they are not used properly.

How to:

If you're tired of your layered hair flipping out, there are a few things you can do to fix it. Here are some tips:

1. Get a haircut

If your layered hair is flipping out, it might be time for a haircut. You can go to a hairstylist and ask them to cut your layers differently. They can cut your layers shorter or at a different angle to distribute the weight of your hair more evenly. Alternatively, you can use hair scissors to trim the flipped-out layers yourself.

2. Use a styling product

Using a styling product like mousse, gel, or hairspray can help keep your layered hair in place. Apply the product to your hair before styling it with heat tools or air-drying it. This will help hold the layers in place and prevent them from flipping out.

3. Blow-dry your hair correctly

If you want to use a blow dryer to style your layered hair, make sure you're using it correctly. Use a round brush to smooth out the layers and direct them downwards. This will help distribute the weight of your hair more evenly and prevent the layers from flipping out.

Step by step:

If you're not sure how to fix your flipped-out layers, here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a mild shampoo and conditioner. This will help remove any excess oil or product buildup that might be weighing down your hair.

2. Apply a styling product

Apply a styling product like mousse or gel to your hair. This will help hold your hair in place and prevent the layers from flipping out.

3. Blow-dry your hair

Use a round brush to blow-dry your hair. Direct the brush downwards and away from your face. This will help smooth out the layers and distribute the weight of your hair more evenly.

4. Use a flat iron

If your hair is still flipping out, you can use a flat iron to straighten it. Use the flat iron to smooth out the layers and direct them downwards.

Tips:

Here are some additional tips to help you prevent your layered hair from flipping out:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools
  • Don't use too much product on your hair
  • Don't brush your hair too vigorously
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair

FAQ:

Q. Can I fix my flipped-out layers at home?

A. Yes, you can fix your flipped-out layers at home. You can use hair scissors to trim the flipped-out layers or use a styling product to hold them in place.

Q. Will getting a haircut fix my flipped-out layers?

A. Yes, getting a haircut can fix your flipped-out layers. You can ask your hairstylist to cut your layers at a different angle or to cut them shorter to distribute the weight of your hair more evenly.

Hair Care:

Proper hair care can help prevent your layered hair from flipping out. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth and strength

By following these tips and tricks, you can prevent your layered hair from flipping out and enjoy a beautiful, voluminous hairstyle!
