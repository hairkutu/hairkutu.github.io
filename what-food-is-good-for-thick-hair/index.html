What Food Is Good For Thick Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Christine May 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Having thick and healthy hair is a dream of many, but not everyone is blessed with it. While genetics plays a vital role in determining hair thickness, diet also has a significant impact. Consuming foods that are rich in nutrients can help promote healthy hair growth and make your hair thicker. In this blog post, we will discuss what food is good for thick hair and how you can incorporate them into your diet.

Table of Content

  1. What nutrients are good for hair thickness?
  2. Foods that promote hair thickness
  3. How to incorporate these foods into your diet
  4. Tips for maintaining thick hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Conclusion

What nutrients are good for hair thickness?

Before discussing the foods that promote hair thickness, let's take a look at the essential nutrients that are good for hair growth and thickness:

  • Protein: Hair is primarily made up of protein, so it's crucial to consume enough protein in your diet to promote hair growth and thickness.
  • Biotin: Biotin is a B-vitamin that helps to strengthen hair and nails and promote hair growth.
  • Vitamin D: Vitamin D helps to create new hair follicles, which can lead to thicker hair.
  • Zinc: Zinc helps to keep the oil glands around hair follicles working correctly, which can promote hair growth.
  • Vitamin E: Vitamin E is an antioxidant that helps to improve blood circulation and promote hair growth.

Foods that promote hair thickness

Here are some of the foods that are good for hair thickness:

  • Eggs: Eggs are an excellent source of protein, biotin, and vitamin D, all of which are essential for healthy hair growth.
  • Spinach: Spinach is a great source of iron, which helps to carry oxygen to the hair follicles. It also contains vitamin A, vitamin C, and folate, which are all good for hair growth.
  • Avocado: Avocado is rich in vitamin E, which helps to improve blood circulation and promote hair growth. It also contains healthy fats that can nourish the hair follicles.
  • Nuts: Nuts like almonds, walnuts, and cashews are rich in protein, biotin, and zinc, all of which are good for hair growth and thickness.
  • Sweet potatoes: Sweet potatoes are an excellent source of beta-carotene, which the body converts into vitamin A. Vitamin A is essential for healthy hair growth and can help prevent hair loss.

How to incorporate these foods into your diet

Now that you know what food is good for thick hair, it's time to incorporate them into your diet. Here are some simple ways to do that:

  • Add eggs to your breakfast.
  • Make a spinach salad for lunch.
  • Snack on nuts throughout the day.
  • Use avocado as a spread on toast or in a salad.
  • Roast sweet potatoes as a side dish for dinner.

Tips for maintaining thick hair

Aside from consuming the right foods, there are other things you can do to maintain thick hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping the hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons as much as possible.
  • Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause hair breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

FAQs

1. Can supplements help promote hair thickness?

Yes, supplements like biotin, vitamin D, and zinc can help promote hair thickness. However, it's important to talk to your doctor before starting any supplements.

2. Does hair thickness change with age?

Yes, hair thickness can change with age. As we get older, our hair can become thinner due to hormonal changes, genetics, and other factors.

3. Can stress affect hair thickness?

Yes, stress can affect hair thickness. Stress can cause hair to fall out and can also affect the rate of hair growth.

Conclusion

Consuming the right food is crucial for promoting healthy hair growth and thickness. By incorporating foods that are rich in protein, biotin, vitamin D, zinc, and vitamin E into your diet, you can help make your hair thicker and healthier. Remember to also follow hair care tips like using sulfate-free shampoo and avoiding heat styling tools to maintain your hair's thickness.
