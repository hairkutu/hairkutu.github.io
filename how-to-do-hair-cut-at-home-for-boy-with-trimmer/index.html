How To Do Hair Cut At Home For Boy With Trimmer

Written by Luffy Sep 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts for your son? Do you want to learn how to give him a haircut at home with a trimmer? Look no further! In this article, we will guide you through step-by-step instructions on how to do a hair cut at home for boy with trimmer.

Description

Doing a hair cut at home for your boy can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a simple process. Using a trimmer, you can achieve a neat and tidy haircut that will save you time and money in the long run.

How to

Before starting the haircut, make sure that your son's hair is clean and dry. If his hair is wet, it will be difficult to cut and may result in an uneven haircut.

Step 1: Choose the Right Trimmer

For a successful haircut, you need to choose the right trimmer. Look for a trimmer that has different length settings and comes with various guards. This will allow you to adjust the length of the hair and achieve different haircuts.

Step 2: Choose the Right Length

Decide on the length of the haircut you want to achieve. Start with the longest guard and work your way down to the shorter ones. It is easier to go shorter if needed than to fix a haircut that is too short.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start by trimming the sides and back of your son's head. Use the longest guard and move the trimmer upwards in a straight line. Repeat this process on the other side and back of the head.

Once you have finished trimming the sides and back, move on to the top of the head. Use a comb to part the hair, and trim it using the guard of your choice.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

After trimming the top of the head, use a shorter guard to blend the hair between the top and sides. Move the trimmer in an upward motion, blending the hair as you go.

Step 5: Clean up the Edges

Use a smaller guard or no guard to clean up the edges around the ears and neck. Make sure to use a steady hand and take your time to achieve a clean and crisp edge.

Tips

  • Use a mirror to check your progress and make sure you are achieving the desired haircut.
  • Take your time and be patient. Rushing the haircut can result in an uneven cut.
  • If you are unsure about a particular area, it is better to cut less hair than to cut too much.
  • Keep the trimmer clean and oiled for optimal performance.

FAQ

Q: Can I use scissors instead of a trimmer?

A: While it is possible to use scissors, a trimmer provides a more even and precise cut. It is also easier to use and less time-consuming.

Q: What should I do if I make a mistake?

A: If you make a mistake, try to fix it by blending the hair. If the mistake is too noticeable, consider taking your son to a professional barber for a corrective cut.

Hair Care

After the haircut, make sure to wash your son's hair to remove any loose hairs. Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to nourish the hair and prevent frizz. Encourage your son to brush his hair regularly to maintain the haircut's shape and prevent tangles.

With these simple steps and tips, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut for your son at home with a trimmer. Not only will it save you time and money, but it will also give you a sense of satisfaction knowing that you can do it yourself!
