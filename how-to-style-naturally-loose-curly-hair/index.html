How To Style Naturally Loose Curly Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Aug 27, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizz and unmanageable curls? If you have naturally loose curly hair, you may find it challenging to style your hair every day. However, with proper care and styling techniques, you can rock your curls and embrace your natural texture. In this guide, we will show you how to style naturally loose curly hair and share some tips and tricks to keep your curls healthy and beautiful.

Hair Care Tips for Naturally Loose Curly Hair

Before we dive into styling your hair, it's essential to take care of your curls. The right hair care routine will help keep your curls healthy, hydrated, and bouncy.

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving your curls dry and brittle. Look for a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that won't dry out your hair.

2. Deep condition regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help restore moisture to your curls and prevent breakage. Aim to deep condition your hair once a week or every other week.

3. Avoid heat styling

Heat can damage your curls and cause frizz. Try to avoid using hot tools like blow dryers, straighteners, and curling irons. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

How to Style Naturally Loose Curly Hair

Washing and Conditioning

The first step to styling your curls is to wash and condition your hair. Follow these steps:

  1. Wet your hair with warm water and apply a sulfate-free shampoo to your scalp.
  2. Gently massage your scalp to create a lather, then rinse thoroughly with warm water.
  3. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  4. Comb the conditioner through your hair with a wide-tooth comb.
  5. Leave the conditioner in your hair for 5-10 minutes, then rinse thoroughly with cool water.

Remember to avoid rubbing your hair with a towel after washing. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze excess water from your hair.

Drying Techniques

Drying your hair is an important step in styling your curls. Here are some techniques to try:

1. Air-drying

Letting your hair air-dry is the gentlest way to dry your curls. After washing, gently scrunch your hair with a microfiber towel or t-shirt to remove excess water. Then, let your hair air-dry until it's about 80% dry before styling.

2. Diffusing

If you're short on time, you can use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your curls. Set your dryer to low heat and low speed, and scrunch your hair with the diffuser as you dry. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid touching your hair with the dryer nozzle.

Styling Tips

Now that your hair is clean and dry, it's time to style your curls. Here are some tips:

1. Use a curl-defining product

Curl-defining products can help enhance your natural texture and define your curls. Look for a product that works well with your hair type and apply it evenly to your hair.

2. Scrunch your curls

Scrunching your curls can help enhance your natural texture and create volume. Gently scrunch your hair from the ends to the roots, using your hands or a microfiber towel.

3. Avoid brushing or combing your curls

Brushing or combing your curls can cause frizz and disrupt your curl pattern. Instead, use your fingers to detangle your hair, or use a wide-tooth comb when necessary.

Tips and Tricks for Natural Loose Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you style your curls:

1. Sleep on a silk pillowcase

Silk pillowcases can help reduce frizz and prevent breakage while you sleep.

2. Experiment with different hairstyles

There are many ways to style your curls, from braids and twists to buns and puffs. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

3. Embrace imperfection

Curls can be unpredictable, and that's okay. Embrace the natural texture of your hair and don't worry about achieving perfection.

FAQs

1. How often should I wash my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with curly hair wash their hair every day, while others wash their hair once a week. Experiment to find what works best for you.

2. Can I use regular shampoo on my curly hair?

Regular shampoos can be too harsh for curly hair, as they often contain sulfates. Look for a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that won't dry out your curls.

3. How do I prevent frizz in my curly hair?

Frizz can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, dryness, and damage. To prevent frizz, use a deep conditioner regularly, avoid heat styling, and use a curl-defining product.

With these tips and tricks, you can style your naturally loose curly hair with confidence. Embrace your natural texture and rock your curls!
