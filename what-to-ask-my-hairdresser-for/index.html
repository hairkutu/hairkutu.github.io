What To Ask My Hairdresser For A Beginner s Guide

Written by Christine Mar 17, 2023 Â· 9 min read

When you visit a hairdresser, it can be overwhelming to know what to ask for if you don't have much experience with haircuts or styling. In this guide, we'll go over what to ask for when you visit your hairdresser, how to communicate effectively, and some tips for maintaining your hair after the cut.

Table of Contents

  • Introduction
  • How to Communicate Effectively
  • What to Ask For in Terms of Cut
  • What to Ask For in Terms of Color
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Hair After the Cut
  • FAQs
  • Conclusion

Introduction

Before you visit your hairdresser, it's important to have a general idea of what you want. You don't have to be an expert in hair terminology, but a general idea of what you're looking for will help you communicate more effectively with your hairdresser. It's also important to keep in mind that not all hairdressers are created equal, so if you're looking for a specific style or color, make sure to choose a hairdresser with experience in that area.

How to Communicate Effectively

When you're at the hairdresser, it's important to communicate clearly and effectively. Here are some tips for doing so:

  • Bring pictures: If you have a specific style or color in mind, bring pictures to show your hairdresser. This will help them understand exactly what you're looking for.
  • Be honest about your hair history: If you've had any recent coloring or chemical treatments, make sure to let your hairdresser know. This will help them determine what products to use and how to best care for your hair.
  • Ask questions: If you're not sure about something, don't be afraid to ask. Your hairdresser is there to help you and answer any questions you may have.

What to Ask For in Terms of Cut

When it comes to getting a haircut, there are a few things you should consider before seeing your hairdresser:

  • Face shape: Your face shape should be taken into consideration when choosing a haircut. Your hairdresser should be able to recommend styles that will flatter your face shape.
  • Hair texture: The texture of your hair can also affect what styles will look best. If you have curly or thick hair, for example, you may want to consider a layered cut to help manage the volume.
  • Maintenance level: How much time and effort are you willing to put into styling your hair? If you're looking for a low maintenance style, make sure to let your hairdresser know.

When you're at the hairdresser, some questions to ask for a cut might include:

  • "What styles do you recommend for my face shape?"
  • "What styles will work best with my hair texture?"
  • "How can I style my hair at home?"

What to Ask For in Terms of Color

If you're looking to change up your hair color, there are a few things to consider:

  • Skin tone: Your skin tone can affect what colors will look best on you. Your hairdresser can recommend colors that will complement your skin tone.
  • Natural hair color: If you're looking to make a drastic change, your natural hair color may affect what colors are possible. Your hairdresser can help you determine what options are available to you.
  • Maintenance level: How often are you willing to come in for touch-ups? Some colors require more maintenance than others.

When you're at the hairdresser, some questions to ask for a color might include:

  • "What colors do you recommend for my skin tone?"
  • "What colors are possible with my natural hair color?"
  • "How often will I need to come in for touch-ups?"

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair After the Cut

After you've gotten your haircut or color, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the look. Here are some tips:

  • Use the right products: Your hairdresser can recommend products that will help keep your hair healthy and styled.
  • Protect your hair: If you're going to be spending time in the sun or swimming, make sure to protect your hair from damage.
  • Come in for touch-ups: Depending on your style or color, you may need to come in for touch-ups every few weeks or months.

FAQs

What if I don't like my haircut or color?

If you're not happy with your haircut or color, speak up! Your hairdresser wants you to be happy with the end result, so they'll likely be willing to make adjustments or suggest other options.

How often should I get my hair cut?

This depends on your hair type and style. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

What if I don't know what I want?

That's okay! Your hairdresser can offer suggestions based on your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Don't be afraid to ask questions or try something new.

Conclusion

Visiting a hairdresser can be a fun and exciting experience, but it's important to communicate effectively and have a general idea of what you're looking for. By following the tips in this guide, you'll be able to ask for the right cut or color and maintain your hair after the appointment.
