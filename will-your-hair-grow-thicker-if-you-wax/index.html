Will Your Hair Grow Thicker If You Wax

Written by Eveline Apr 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

When it comes to hair removal, many people wonder if waxing can make their hair grow thicker. It's a common misconception, but the truth is that waxing does not make your hair grow thicker. In fact, waxing can actually thin out your hair over time. Here's everything you need to know about waxing and hair growth.

Description

Waxing is a popular method of hair removal that involves applying hot or cold wax to the skin and then pulling it off, along with the hair. This method is effective in removing hair from the root, leaving your skin smooth and hair-free for several weeks. However, many people are concerned that waxing can make their hair grow back thicker and darker.

How to Wax

If you're new to waxing, it's important to know how to do it properly to avoid injury or irritation. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Prepare Your Skin

Before waxing, it's important to prepare your skin by exfoliating and cleaning it thoroughly. This will help to remove any dead skin cells and oils, allowing the wax to adhere better to the hair.

Step 2: Heat the Wax

If you're using hot wax, heat it up according to the instructions on the package. If you're using cold wax, no heating is required.

Step 3: Apply the Wax

Apply the wax in the direction of hair growth, using a spatula or applicator. Make sure to apply a thin layer, as a thick layer can be more difficult to remove.

Step 4: Remove the Wax

Once the wax has cooled and hardened, grab the edge of the wax and pull it off in one swift motion, against the direction of hair growth. Repeat until all the hair has been removed.

Tips for Waxing

Here are some tips to help you get the best results from waxing:

  • Wait until your hair is at least 1/4 inch long before waxing
  • Exfoliate your skin a few days before waxing to remove dead skin cells
  • Apply a numbing cream or spray to reduce pain and discomfort
  • Avoid waxing if your skin is sunburned, irritated, or has open wounds
  • Moisturize your skin after waxing to soothe and hydrate it

FAQs

Does waxing make hair grow back thicker?

No, waxing does not make hair grow back thicker. In fact, waxing can actually thin out your hair over time, as it removes the hair from the root.

How long does waxing last?

Waxing can last anywhere from 2-6 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain your skin.

Is waxing better than shaving?

Waxing and shaving are both effective methods of hair removal, but waxing typically lasts longer and results in smoother skin. However, waxing can be more painful and time-consuming than shaving.

Hair Care After Waxing

After waxing, it's important to take care of your skin to prevent irritation and ingrown hairs. Here are some tips to follow:

  • Avoid hot showers, saunas, and steam rooms for 24 hours after waxing
  • Avoid tight clothing and friction on the waxed area
  • Exfoliate the waxed area twice a week to prevent ingrown hairs
  • Avoid sun exposure for 24 hours after waxing
  • Moisturize the waxed area daily to keep the skin hydrated

So, will your hair grow thicker if you wax? The answer is no. In fact, waxing can actually thin out your hair over time, making it a great option for those who want to reduce hair growth. Just be sure to follow the proper waxing techniques and take care of your skin afterwards to ensure the best results.
