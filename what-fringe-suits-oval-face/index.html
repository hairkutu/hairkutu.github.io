What Fringe Suits Oval Face The Ultimate Guide

Written by William Jun 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read
If you have an oval face shape, you're in luck! This face shape is considered to be the most versatile when it comes to hairstyles. However, choosing the right fringe can make all the difference. In this guide, we'll be discussing what fringe suits oval face and everything you need to know about it.

Description

An oval face shape is characterized by balanced proportions, with the forehead and jawline being of similar width, and the face being longer than it is wide. This face shape is considered to be the most versatile when it comes to hairstyles, as it can pull off almost any look. However, choosing the right fringe can enhance your features and make your face shape more symmetrical.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before we dive into what fringe suits oval face, it's essential to determine your face shape correctly. Here's how you can do it: 1. Pull your hair back and away from your face. 2. Take a measuring tape and measure the widest part of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. 3. Compare the measurements. If your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, and your forehead and jawline are of similar width, you have an oval face shape.

Step by Step: Choosing the Right Fringe for Oval Face

Now that we know what an oval face shape is let's take a look at what fringe suits oval face. Here's a step-by-step guide: 1. Consider the length of your face. If you have a longer face, go for a longer fringe that will cover your forehead and draw attention to your eyes. If you have a shorter face, go for a shorter fringe that will make your face appear longer. 2. Look at your hair type. If you have thin hair, go for a wispy fringe that will add volume and texture. If you have thick hair, go for a heavier fringe that will frame your face. 3. Think about your personal style. Do you prefer a more natural, effortless look? Opt for a side-swept fringe. Do you like a bold, statement look? Go for a blunt fringe. 4. Consider your hairline. If you have a high hairline, go for a longer fringe that will balance your forehead. If you have a low hairline, go for a shorter fringe that will create the illusion of a higher hairline.

Tips for Styling Fringe

Once you've chosen the right fringe for your oval face shape, here are some tips for styling it: 1. Blow-dry your fringe straight for a sleek look, or use a round brush for volume. 2. Use a small amount of hair serum or oil to keep your fringe in place and add shine. 3. Avoid cutting your fringe too short, as it can be difficult to style and may not suit your face shape. 4. Experiment with different styles, such as a center part or a deep side part, to find the look that suits you best.

FAQ

Q: Can I pull off a blunt fringe with an oval face shape? A: Yes, absolutely! A blunt fringe can create a bold, statement look that suits oval face shapes. Just make sure to consider the length of your face and your hair type before making the cut. Q: How often should I trim my fringe? A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your fringe grows out. Generally, you should trim your fringe every four to six weeks to keep it looking fresh and well-maintained.

Hair Care for Fringe

To keep your fringe looking its best, here are some hair care tips: 1. Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy. 2. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't weigh down your hair. 3. Avoid using too much heat styling, as it can damage your hair and make it harder to style. 4. Use a heat protectant spray before blow-drying or using a flat iron to protect your hair from damage. 5. Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

Conclusion

Choosing the right fringe can enhance your features and make your oval face shape more symmetrical. By considering the length of your face, your hair type, and your personal style, you can find the perfect fringe that suits you best. Remember to keep your fringe well-maintained and take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
