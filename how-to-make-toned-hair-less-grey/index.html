How To Make Toned Hair Less Grey Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Jul 28, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have recently toned your hair and noticed that it is looking a little too grey for your liking, don't worry - there are ways to fix it! In this article, we will go through some tips and tricks on how to make toned hair less grey. Whether you want to achieve a more natural look or simply tone down the grey, we've got you covered.

How to Make Toned Hair Less Grey: Step by Step

Step 1: Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a must-have for anyone looking to tone down the grey in their hair. This shampoo is specifically designed to neutralize yellow and brassy tones, which can make your hair look more grey than you intended. Simply wash your hair with purple shampoo once or twice a week to see a noticeable difference.

Step 2: Add Some Warmth with a Toner

If your hair is looking too cool-toned and grey, consider adding some warmth with a toner. A toner is a semi-permanent hair color that can be used to add depth and warmth to your hair. Look for a toner with warm undertones, such as gold or copper, to add some warmth to your hair and tone down the grey.

Step 3: Use a Demi-Permanent Hair Color

If you want to make a more significant change to your hair color, consider using a demi-permanent hair color. This type of hair color is less damaging than permanent hair color and can be used to add depth and warmth to your hair. Look for a color that is one shade warmer than your current hair color to add some warmth and tone down the grey.

Tips for Making Toned Hair Less Grey

Here are some additional tips for making toned hair less grey:

  • Use a moisturizing hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and nourished.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it look dull and lifeless.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add shine and moisture to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and make it look dry and brittle.

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular shampoo instead of purple shampoo?

A: While regular shampoo can help keep your hair clean, it will not have the same toning effect as purple shampoo. If you want to tone down the grey in your hair, it is best to use a purple shampoo specifically designed for this purpose.

Q: Can I use a toner and a demi-permanent hair color at the same time?

A: It is best to use either a toner or a demi-permanent hair color, as using both at the same time can result in an over-processed look. If you want to add warmth to your hair, try using a toner first and see if that achieves the desired effect. If not, you can then consider using a demi-permanent hair color.

Hair Care Tips for Toned Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of toned hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can make it look dry and frizzy.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you can make toned hair less grey and achieve the look you want. Remember to take care of your hair and avoid over-processing it to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
