How To Wear Your Hair In Your 50S A Guide To Looking Fabulous

Written by Robby Sep 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're in your 50s and wondering how to wear your hair, you're not alone. As we age, our hair tends to change in texture, thickness, and color. But that doesn't mean you can't still look fabulous! In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for wearing your hair in your 50s.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear Your Hair in Your 50s
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Wearing Your Hair in Your 50s
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Women in Their 50s

How to Wear Your Hair in Your 50s

There are so many ways to wear your hair in your 50s, depending on your personal style and hair type. Here are a few ideas:

1. Short and Sweet

Short haircuts can be chic and stylish for women in their 50s. A pixie cut, bob, or shaggy layers can all be great options. Short haircuts require less maintenance and can help you look younger and more vibrant.

2. Medium Length

If you're not ready to go super short, a medium-length haircut can be flattering and versatile. You can try a shoulder-length bob, layered cut, or even a lob (long bob).

3. Long and Lovely

Long hair can be beautiful at any age, but it does require more maintenance. If you have thick, healthy hair, consider keeping it long and adding layers for volume and texture. If your hair is thin or starting to thin, a shorter cut may be more flattering.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to wear your hair in your 50s:

1. Choose a Flattering Cut

The first step is to choose a haircut that flatters your face shape and hair type. Consult with a stylist to find the best cut for you.

2. Add Volume and Texture

If your hair is thin or starting to thin, consider adding layers for volume and texture. You can also use volumizing products like mousse or root lifter.

3. Embrace Your Natural Color

As we age, our hair often turns gray or white. Embrace your natural color and consider going gray gracefully. If you do decide to color your hair, choose a shade that complements your skin tone.

4. Experiment with Accessories

Hair accessories can be a fun way to switch up your look. Try adding a headband, scarf, or clip to your hair for a pop of color or texture.

Tips for Wearing Your Hair in Your 50s

Here are some additional tips for wearing your hair in your 50s:

1. Protect Your Hair from Damage

Protect your hair from damage by avoiding heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons. If you do use heat tools, use a heat protectant spray.

2. Get Regular Trims

Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends. Aim for a trim every 6-8 weeks.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eat a healthy diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote healthy hair growth. Foods like salmon, eggs, and leafy greens can all help keep your hair looking great.

FAQs

Q: Can I still have long hair in my 50s?

A: Yes, you can still have long hair in your 50s! Just make sure to keep it healthy and well-maintained.

Q: What's the best haircut for women in their 50s?

A: The best haircut for women in their 50s depends on their face shape and hair type. Consult with a stylist to find the best cut for you.

Hair Care Tips for Women in Their 50s

Here are some additional hair care tips for women in their 50s:

1. Use Gentle Products

Use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

2. Hydrate Your Hair

Hydrate your hair by using a deep conditioning treatment once a week. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to help tame frizz and add shine.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to wear your hair in your 50s with confidence and style. Remember, the key is to choose a haircut that flatters your face shape and hair type, and to keep your hair healthy and well-maintained.
