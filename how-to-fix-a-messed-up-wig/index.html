How To Fix A Messed Up Wig

Written by Aprilia Mar 29, 2023 · 7 min read

Wigs are a great way to change up your look, whether it’s for a special occasion or just for fun. However, sometimes wigs can get messed up and need a little TLC. In this post, we’ll go over how to fix a messed up wig in relaxed English language. We’ll cover everything from how to detangle a wig to how to fix a wig that’s lost its shape. So, let’s get started!

How to Detangle a Wig

One of the most common issues with wigs is tangling. If your wig is tangled, don’t panic! Here’s how to fix it:

  1. Start by gently brushing out any knots or tangles with a wide-tooth comb.
  2. If the tangles are stubborn, you can use a detangling spray or a mixture of water and fabric softener to help loosen them.
  3. Work slowly and carefully through the tangles until the wig is completely detangled.

Once your wig is detangled, you can move on to fixing any other issues.

How to Fix a Wig That’s Lost Its Shape

If your wig has lost its shape, you can easily fix it with these steps:

  1. Fill a sink or basin with lukewarm water and add a small amount of wig shampoo.
  2. Gently swirl the wig around in the water for a few minutes, being careful not to rub or twist it.
  3. Rinse the wig thoroughly with lukewarm water.
  4. Gently squeeze out any excess water and blot the wig with a towel to remove even more moisture.
  5. Place the wig on a wig stand or mannequin head and use a wide-tooth comb to carefully comb out any tangles or knots.
  6. Allow the wig to air dry completely before styling or wearing it.

By following these steps, your wig should regain its shape and look like new again!

Tips for Maintaining Your Wig

Now that you know how to fix a messed up wig, it’s important to take steps to prevent it from happening again. Here are some tips for maintaining your wig:

  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to help it maintain its shape.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your wig, as they can cause damage.
  • Use a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your wig, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Wash your wig every 6-8 wears or as needed, using a wig shampoo and lukewarm water.
  • Avoid exposing your wig to harsh chemicals, such as chlorine or bleach.

By following these tips, you can help extend the life of your wig and keep it looking great!

FAQs About Fixing a Messed Up Wig

Q: Can I use regular shampoo on my wig?

A: No, it’s important to use a wig shampoo specifically designed for synthetic or human hair wigs. Regular shampoo can damage the fibers and cause tangling.

Q: Can I use a blow dryer to dry my wig?

A: No, it’s best to allow your wig to air dry completely. Using a blow dryer or other heat styling tools can damage the fibers and cause frizzing or tangling.

Q: How can I prevent tangling in my wig?

A: To prevent tangling, be sure to store your wig properly on a wig stand or mannequin head, and avoid exposing it to harsh chemicals or heat styling tools. You can also use a detangling spray or a mixture of water and fabric softener to help keep the fibers soft and smooth.

Hair Care Products for Wigs

When it comes to maintaining your wig, there are a few products that can be especially helpful:

  • Wig shampoo and conditioner
  • Detangling spray
  • Wig stand or mannequin head
  • Wide-tooth comb or wig brush

By having these products on hand, you’ll be able to keep your wig looking great for longer!

Conclusion

Fixing a messed up wig doesn’t have to be difficult. By following these simple steps, you can detangle, reshape, and maintain your wig with ease. Be sure to use the right products and take care when handling your wig, and it will look great for many wears to come!
