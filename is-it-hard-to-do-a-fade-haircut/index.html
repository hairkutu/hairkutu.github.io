Is It Hard To Do A Fade Haircut

Written by Aprilia Jul 20, 2023 · 7 min read

In this article, we will tackle the question: is it hard to do a fade haircut? We will discuss the process of fading hair, tips on how to do it, and some frequently asked questions about fade haircuts. If you’re planning on doing a fade haircut to yourself or to someone else, read on to learn more.

Description

Fade haircuts have been in trend for a while now, and it is not going away anytime soon. A fade haircut is a style where the hair gradually shortens from the top to the sides and back of the head, creating a fading effect. It is a versatile haircut that can be done in different lengths, from short to long. Fading hair requires precision and skill, making some people wonder if it’s hard to do.

How to Do a Fade Haircut

Before starting a fade haircut, you need to prepare the necessary tools such as clippers, guards, scissors, comb, and a mirror. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to do a fade haircut:

Step 1: Choose the length of the fade.

Decide on how short or long the fade will be. Start with a longer guard and gradually work your way down to the shorter ones.

Step 2: Cut the top of the hair.

Use scissors or clippers to cut the hair on top to the desired length.

Step 3: Create a guideline.

Using a clipper, create a guideline around the head where the fade will start. This will serve as a reference for the next steps.

Step 4: Use the longer guard.

Start with the longer guard and cut the hair around the guideline. Work your way up, leaving a little bit of hair at the top. Be sure to blend the hair as you go.

Step 5: Use the shorter guard.

Switch to a shorter guard and repeat the process, starting below the guideline. Blend the hair as you go up.

Step 6: Fade the edges.

Use a trimmer to clean up the edges and create a smooth transition from the fade to the skin.

Step 7: Style the hair.

Use a comb and scissors to style the hair on top to your desired look.

Tips

Here are some tips to make fading hair easier:

  • Practice on a mannequin head or a friend before doing it on yourself.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Take breaks and step back to check the progress.
  • Use a mirror to see the back of the head.
  • Use a spray bottle to wet the hair for easier cutting.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fade haircuts:

Q: Can I do a fade haircut on my own?

A: Yes, you can do a fade haircut on your own, but it will require practice and skill.

Q: How long does a fade haircut last?

A: A fade haircut can last for 2-4 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows.

Q: What is the difference between a fade and a taper?

A: A fade is a gradual shortening of the hair from the top to the sides and back, while a taper is a gradual shortening of the hair from the back to the front.

Hair Care

After getting a fade haircut, it is important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a hair moisturizer to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as blow dryers and straighteners too often.
  • Visit a barber every 2-4 weeks for touch-ups.

In conclusion, doing a fade haircut requires skill and precision, but it is not impossible to do. With practice and the right tools, you can achieve the perfect fade haircut. Always take care of your hair after getting a fade haircut to maintain the style and keep it looking fresh.
