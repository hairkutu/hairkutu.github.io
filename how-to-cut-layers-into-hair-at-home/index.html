How To Cut Layers Into Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Apr 25, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of your long, one-length hair? Layers can add dimension, volume, and style to your hair. But going to a salon to get layers can be expensive. Don't worry, you can cut layers into your hair at home! In this guide, we'll show you how to cut layers into your hair step by step. Let's get started!

Description

Layered hair is a popular hairstyle among women. It's a versatile hairstyle that can suit any hair type, length, and face shape. Layers can add movement and texture to your hair, making it look fuller and healthier. You can choose different types of layers, such as long layers, short layers, choppy layers, or face-framing layers, depending on your preference.

How to Cut Layers into Hair at Home

Step 1: Gather the Right Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. It's best to use professional hair cutting scissors, as regular scissors can damage your hair and create uneven layers.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's easier to cut layers into clean, dry hair. So, wash and dry your hair before you start. Don't use any styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it harder to cut.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections to make it easier to cut. Use a comb to create a center parting, and then divide your hair into two sections, left and right. Clip the top section away, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer. Decide how long you want your layers to be and cut the bottom layer accordingly. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers, and point-cut into the hair with the scissors. Point-cutting means cutting into the hair at an angle, instead of straight across. This will create softer, more natural-looking layers. Repeat this process all around the bottom layer.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Once you've cut the bottom layer, release the top layer and comb it straight down. Cut the top layer to match the length of the bottom layer. Use the same point-cutting technique to create layers. Take small sections of hair and cut into them at an angle, blending the layers with the bottom layer.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Blend the layers by taking small sections of hair from the top layer and cutting them at an angle, so they blend in with the bottom layer. Use the comb to guide your scissors and make sure the layers are even.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Check your work by looking in the mirror and inspecting your layers. Make sure they're even and blended. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim them.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you cut layers into your hair at home:

  • Start with small sections of hair, and cut less than you think you need. You can always cut more later, but you can't undo a bad haircut.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from all angles. Make sure your layers are even and blended.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will look longer when it dries.
  • If you have curly hair, cut your hair when it's dry and in its natural state, so you can see the curls' pattern.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting hair can be challenging, but with practice, you'll get better.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut layers into my hair if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can cut layers into curly hair. However, you should cut your hair when it's dry and in its natural state, so you can see the curls' pattern. Use the point-cutting technique to create soft, natural-looking layers.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

A: It's best to use professional hair cutting scissors, as regular scissors can damage your hair and create uneven layers. If you don't have professional scissors, you can buy them online or at a beauty supply store.

Q: What type of layers should I choose?

A: You can choose different types of layers, such as long layers, short layers, choppy layers, or face-framing layers, depending on your preference. Consider your hair type, length, and face shape when choosing layers.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, take care of it by using the right hair products and tools. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type, and avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. Get regular trims to maintain your layers and keep your hair healthy and beautiful.
