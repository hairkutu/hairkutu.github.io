How To Keep Frizz Out Of Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby May 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it's beautiful and unique, it can also be prone to frizz. If you're tired of dealing with frizzy curls, there are several steps you can take to keep them smooth and defined. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for keeping frizz out of curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Keep Frizz Out of Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Frizz-Free Curls
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Products for Curly Hair

How to Keep Frizz Out of Curly Hair

If you're looking for ways to prevent frizz in your curly hair, here are some tips:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that's specifically formulated for curly hair.

2. Condition, condition, condition

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's important to use a hydrating conditioner every time you wash your hair. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to dry your hair

Regular towels can be too rough on curly hair, causing frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to gently squeeze out the excess water.

4. Apply a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help keep your curls hydrated and defined. Apply it to damp hair, working it through from roots to ends.

5. Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair

If you're blow-drying your hair, use a diffuser attachment to distribute the heat evenly and prevent frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping frizz out of curly hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo.

Massage the shampoo into your scalp and hair, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Apply a hydrating conditioner.

Work the conditioner through your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave it in for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to dry your hair.

Gently squeeze out the excess water, being careful not to rub your hair too vigorously.

Step 4: Apply a leave-in conditioner.

Distribute the leave-in conditioner through your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.

Step 5: Use a diffuser attachment when blow-drying your hair.

Attach the diffuser to your blow dryer and use it to dry your hair on a low heat setting.

Tips for Maintaining Frizz-Free Curls

Here are some additional tips for maintaining frizz-free curls:

1. Avoid touching your hair too much.

The oils from your hands can transfer to your hair, causing frizz. Try to avoid touching your hair too much, especially when it's dry.

2. Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase.

Cotton pillowcases can cause friction, leading to frizz. Switch to a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent this.

3. Use a hair serum or oil.

A hair serum or oil can help smooth down frizz and add shine to your curls. Apply it to your hair when it's dry.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a regular towel to dry my curly hair?

A: No, regular towels can be too rough on curly hair and cause frizz. Use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt instead.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it gets oily. Some people can go a few days without washing their hair, while others need to wash it every day. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Hair Care Products for Curly Hair

Here are some hair care products that are specifically formulated for curly hair:

  • Sulfate-free shampoo
  • Hydrating conditioner
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Microfiber towel
  • Cotton t-shirt
  • Diffuser attachment for blow dryer
  • Hair serum or oil

By following these tips and using the right hair care products, you can keep frizz out of your curly hair and enjoy smooth, defined curls.
