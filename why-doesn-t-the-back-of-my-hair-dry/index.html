Why Doesn t The Back Of My Hair Dry

Written by Bardi Apr 15, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're someone who has long hair, you've probably experienced this before - your hair is wet, you've dried the front and sides, but the back of your hair just won't dry. It's frustrating, isn't it? In this article, we'll explore the reasons why this happens and what you can do to fix it.

Description

The back of your hair doesn't dry for a number of reasons. It could be due to the way you're washing your hair, the type of hair products you're using, or simply because of the way your hair is cut. Understanding the cause of this issue can help you get to the root of the problem and find a solution.

How to

If the back of your hair doesn't dry properly, there are a few things you can do to fix it. Here are some steps you can take:

1. Change the Way You Shampoo Your Hair

If you're not shampooing your hair properly, this could be the reason why the back of your hair is not drying. Make sure you're thoroughly wetting your hair before applying shampoo. Then, massage the shampoo into your scalp and work it through your hair. Rinse your hair out thoroughly with warm water.

2. Use Lightweight Hair Products

Heavy hair products can weigh your hair down and make it harder to dry. Use lightweight products that won't leave a residue on your hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type.

3. Use a Hair Dryer

If you're air-drying your hair, it may not be drying evenly. Use a hair dryer on a low setting to dry your hair, making sure to focus on the back of your head.

Step-by-Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you dry the back of your hair:

1. Start with a Towel

Start by using a towel to blot away as much moisture as possible from the back of your hair. Use a gentle patting motion rather than rubbing your hair as this can cause damage.

2. Use a Hair Dryer on a Low Setting

Using a hair dryer on a low setting, start at the nape of your neck and work your way up. Use a round brush to help lift the hair away from your scalp as you dry it.

3. Finish with a Blast of Cool Air

Finish off by blasting the back of your hair with cool air. This will help to set your style and add shine.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you dry the back of your hair:

1. Use a Microfiber Towel

A microfiber towel is gentler on your hair than a regular towel and will help to absorb moisture more quickly.

2. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner will help to detangle your hair and make it easier to dry.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends, which can make it harder for your hair to dry.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about why the back of your hair doesn't dry:

Q: Why is the back of my hair still wet after blow-drying?

A: This could be due to a number of reasons, such as the thickness or length of your hair, or the type of hair dryer you're using. Try using a higher heat setting or a different hair dryer to see if this helps.

Q: Can using too much conditioner cause the back of my hair to not dry?

A: Yes, using too much conditioner can weigh down your hair and make it harder to dry. Use a small amount of conditioner and make sure to rinse it out thoroughly.

Q: Should I brush my hair while it's wet?

A: No, brushing your hair while it's wet can cause damage and breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair instead.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to keeping your hair healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

How often you should wash your hair depends on your hair type and lifestyle. However, most people should aim to wash their hair every 2-3 days.

2. Use the Right Products

Using the right products for your hair type can help to keep your hair healthy and looking its best. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat Damage

Heat damage can cause your hair to become dry and brittle. Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools and avoid using high heat settings.

In conclusion, there are a number of reasons why the back of your hair doesn't dry. By following the tips and steps outlined in this article, you can help to prevent this issue and keep your hair looking its best.
