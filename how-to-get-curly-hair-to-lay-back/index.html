How To Get Curly Hair To Lay Back A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Do you have curly hair that always seems to stick out or defy gravity? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people struggle with getting their curly hair to lay back and look neat and tidy. Luckily, there are some simple steps you can take to tame your curls and achieve the look you want. In this article, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about how to get curly hair to lay back.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Curly Hair to Lay Back
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Products for Curly Hair

How to Get Curly Hair to Lay Back

Getting curly hair to lay back can be a challenge, but it's not impossible. The key is to use the right products and techniques to tame your curls and keep them in place. Here are some tips for getting curly hair to lay back:

  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your curls and make them easier to manage.
  • Apply a curl-defining cream to your hair to help define your curls and keep them in place.
  • Blow-dry your hair with a diffuser attachment to help set your curls in place.
  • Sleep with a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz and keep your curls looking neat.
  • Use a hair oil or serum to add shine and help keep your curls looking healthy and hydrated.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get curly hair to lay back:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your curls.
  3. Apply a curl-defining cream to your hair, focusing on the ends and mid-lengths.
  4. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow-dryer to dry your hair. Use a low heat setting and scrunch your hair with your hands as you dry it.
  5. Once your hair is dry, apply a hair oil or serum to add shine and keep your curls looking hydrated.
  6. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently comb through your curls and help them lay back.
  7. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to help set your curls in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Curly Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining your curly hair and keeping it looking its best:

  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons on your hair, as they can damage your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your curls when they're wet.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your curls hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for sulfate-free shampoos that are designed for curly hair.
  • Try not to touch your hair too much throughout the day, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your curls.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting curly hair to lay back:

Q: Can I use gel instead of curl-defining cream?

A: Yes, you can use gel instead of curl-defining cream, but be careful not to use too much, as this can make your hair look crunchy and stiff.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp is. Some people with curly hair can go several days between washes, while others need to wash their hair every other day. Experiment to see what works best for you.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair if I want to?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair if you want to, but be sure to use a heat protectant and avoid doing it too often, as this can damage your hair.

Hair Care Products for Curly Hair

Here are some hair care products that are great for curly hair:

  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Curl-defining cream
  • Diffuser attachment for blow-dryer
  • Silk or satin pillowcase
  • Hair oil or serum
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Light-hold hairspray
  • Sulfate-free shampoo
  • Deep conditioning treatment

By following these tips and using the right products, you can get your curly hair to lay back and look neat and tidy. Remember to be patient and experiment with different techniques to find what works best for you. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at taming your curls in no time!
