How Can I Tell What Haircut Suits Me

Written by Bardi Aug 07, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of getting haircuts that don't quite suit your face shape or personal style? It can be a frustrating and discouraging experience. But don't worry, there are many ways to determine what haircut is best for you. In this article, we will explore how you can tell what haircut suits you best.

  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your New Haircut

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in determining what haircut suits you best is to determine your face shape. Your face shape can play a significant role in determining which hairstyles will flatter you the most. There are several face shapes, including:

  • Oval
  • Round
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond
  • Pear

To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and stand in front of a mirror. Use a washable marker to trace the outline of your face onto the mirror. Then, compare the shape to the six face shapes listed above.

Tip:

If you're having trouble determining your face shape, take a photo of yourself facing forward and upload it to a face shape calculator online.

Analyzing Your Hair Type

Once you have determined your face shape, it's time to analyze your hair type. Consider the following factors:

  • Texture (straight, wavy, curly, or kinky)
  • Thickness (thin, medium, or thick)
  • Density (how much hair you have)

These factors can affect how a haircut will look on you. For example, if you have thin hair, you may want to avoid haircuts that require a lot of volume. If you have curly hair, a shorter haircut may be easier to manage.

Considering Your Lifestyle

Another important factor to consider when choosing a haircut is your lifestyle. Consider the following:

  • Do you have a busy schedule?
  • Do you have time to style your hair every day?
  • Are you active and need a style that can withstand physical activity?

It's important to choose a haircut that fits your lifestyle. If you're always on the go, you may want a low-maintenance style that doesn't require a lot of styling. If you're active, you may want a shorter style that can withstand sweat and movement.

Reflecting on Your Personal Style

Your personal style is another important factor to consider when choosing a haircut. Consider the following:

  • Do you prefer a classic or trendy style?
  • Do you have any hair color preferences?
  • Do you prefer a certain length or shape?

It's important to choose a haircut that aligns with your personal style. If you prefer classic styles, you may want to stick with a simple, low-maintenance haircut. If you're more adventurous, you may want to try a trendy, bold haircut.

Haircut Ideas for Different Face Shapes

Now that you've determined your face shape, hair type, lifestyle, and personal style, it's time to choose a haircut. Here are some haircut ideas for different face shapes:

Oval Face Shape:

Almost any haircut will look good on an oval face shape, but try to avoid styles that add too much height to the top of the head. Consider a sleek bob or long layers.

Round Face Shape:

Long, layered haircuts can elongate a round face shape. Avoid blunt cuts and short bobs. Consider side-swept bangs to add length to your face.

Square Face Shape:

Soft, layered haircuts can soften a square face shape. Avoid blunt cuts and straight bangs. Consider a chin-length bob or long layers.

Heart Face Shape:

Long, layered haircuts can balance out a heart face shape. Avoid blunt cuts and short bobs. Consider side-swept bangs to soften your forehead.

Diamond Face Shape:

Short haircuts can complement a diamond face shape. Avoid haircuts with too much volume on top. Consider a pixie cut or chin-length bob.

Pear Face Shape:

Balance out a pear face shape with long, layered haircuts. Avoid blunt cuts and short bobs. Consider side-swept bangs to add volume to the top of your head.

Tips for Choosing the Right Haircut

Here are some additional tips for choosing the right haircut:

  • Bring photos to your stylist to show them the style you want.
  • Consider the maintenance required for the haircut.
  • Ask your stylist for their professional opinion on what will look best on you.
  • Don't be afraid to try something new!

FAQs About Choosing the Right Haircut

Q: What if I have multiple face shapes?

A: It's possible to have a combination of face shapes. Consider the dominant shape when choosing a haircut.

Q: How often should I get a haircut?

A: It depends on your hair growth rate and the style you choose. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is recommended for maintaining a short haircut, while longer haircuts can go 6-12 weeks between cuts.

Q: What if I don't like my new haircut?

A: Don't panic! Talk to your stylist about what you don't like and see if they can make any adjustments. It's also important to remember that hair grows back, so it's not the end of the world.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your New Haircut

After you've chosen the perfect haircut, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some hair care tips for maintaining your new haircut:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suited for your hair type.
  • Don't over-wash your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut's shape.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to choose a haircut that suits you perfectly and maintain it with ease. Happy styling!
