How To Style Men s Medium Length Straight Hair

Written by Bardi May 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a man with medium length straight hair, you may be wondering how to style it in a way that looks polished and put-together. Fortunately, there are a variety of hairstyles that work well for this hair type, whether you prefer a casual, messy look or a more structured style. In this article, we'll provide tips and step-by-step instructions for styling men's medium length straight hair.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Style Men's Medium Length Straight Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Styling Men's Medium Length Straight Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Tips for Men's Medium Length Straight Hair

How to Style Men's Medium Length Straight Hair

The first step in styling men's medium length straight hair is to determine what type of look you want to achieve. Some popular styles for this hair type include:

  • Slicked back
  • Messy and textured
  • Side-swept
  • Undercut
  • Man bun

Once you've chosen a style, you'll need to gather the necessary tools and products. For most styles, you'll need a comb, hair dryer, and styling product such as pomade or hair wax. You may also want to invest in a quality hairbrush to help distribute product evenly and keep your hair looking neat.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve your desired hairstyle:

  1. Wash your hair using a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Pat dry with a towel, being careful not to rub vigorously as this can cause damage to the hair.
  2. Apply a small amount of styling product to your hair, focusing on the roots and ends.
  3. Using a comb or brush, distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.
  4. Blow dry your hair using a low heat setting, using your fingers or a brush to style as desired.
  5. For a slicked back or side-swept look, use a comb to smooth your hair back or to the side.
  6. For a messy, textured look, use your fingers to create separation and volume.
  7. Finish with a small amount of additional product, if desired.

Tips for Styling Men's Medium Length Straight Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when styling men's medium length straight hair:

  • Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type and desired style.
  • Don't be afraid to try new hairstyles or modify existing ones to suit your individual preferences.
  • When blow drying, use a low heat setting to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Regular trims can help keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a heat protectant spray if you frequently use heated styling tools.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about styling men's medium length straight hair:

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This depends on your individual hair type and lifestyle. Some people may need to wash their hair daily, while others can go several days without washing. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Q: Can I straighten my hair if it's already straight?

A: Yes, but be careful not to apply too much heat as this can cause damage to your hair. Use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting on your straightening tool.

Hair Care Tips for Men's Medium Length Straight Hair

To keep your hair looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can cause damage and dryness.
  • Regularly trim your hair to prevent split ends and maintain a healthy appearance.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask to provide additional moisture and nourishment.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products that can cause damage or dryness.

With the right products and techniques, styling men's medium length straight hair can be easy and enjoyable. Whether you prefer a sleek and polished look or a more casual, messy style, there are plenty of options to choose from. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you and your hair type.
