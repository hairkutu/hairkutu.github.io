What Does The Bible Say About Keeping Beards

Written by Aprilia May 13, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you a man who is looking to grow out your beard, but wondering what the Bible says about it? Look no further! In this post, we will explore what the Bible has to say about keeping beards.

Table of Contents

  • History of Beards in the Bible
  • Biblical Verses on Beards
  • Pros of Keeping a Beard
  • Cons of Keeping a Beard
  • Hair Care Tips for Bearded Men
  • FAQ

History of Beards in the Bible

Beards have been a symbol of masculinity and wisdom throughout history. In biblical times, beards were commonly worn by men as a sign of respect and honor. The ancient Israelites saw beards as a sign of manliness and strength. The beard was also seen as a sign of God's favor, as it was a symbol of masculinity and virility.

Biblical Verses on Beards

The Bible has several verses that mention beards. Here are a few:

  • Leviticus 19:27 - "Ye shall not round the corners of your heads, neither shalt thou mar the corners of thy beard."
  • Psalm 133:2 - "It is like the precious ointment upon the head, that ran down upon the beard, even Aaron's beard: that went down to the skirts of his garments;"
  • Ezekiel 5:1 - "And thou, son of man, take thee a sharp knife, take thee a barber's razor, and cause it to pass upon thine head and upon thy beard: then take thee balances to weigh, and divide the hair."

These verses show that beards were a common and accepted part of biblical culture.

Pros of Keeping a Beard

There are many benefits to keeping a beard. Here are a few:

  • Protection from sun damage
  • Reduced risk of acne
  • Natural moisturizer
  • Increased confidence

Cons of Keeping a Beard

While there are many benefits to keeping a beard, there are also some downsides. Here are a few:

  • Requires maintenance
  • Can be itchy
  • Can be seen as unprofessional in certain industries

Hair Care Tips for Bearded Men

If you do decide to grow a beard, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your beard regularly
  • Use beard oil to keep it soft and moisturized
  • Comb or brush your beard daily
  • Trim your beard regularly to keep it looking neat

FAQ

Q: Can I grow a beard if I'm a Christian?

A: Yes! The Bible does not prohibit men from growing beards.

Q: Will growing a beard make me more manly?

A: While beards are often associated with masculinity, there is no guarantee that growing a beard will make you more manly.

Q: Will growing a beard make me more attractive?

A: That depends on the individual. Some people find beards attractive, while others do not.

Q: How long does it take to grow a beard?

A: That varies from person to person. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months to grow a full beard.

In conclusion, the Bible does not prohibit men from growing beards. If you do decide to grow a beard, be sure to take care of it properly with regular washing, oiling, and trimming. Remember, a beard is a sign of masculinity and wisdom, so wear it with pride!
