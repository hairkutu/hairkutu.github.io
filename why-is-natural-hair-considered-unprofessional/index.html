Why Is Natural Hair Considered Unprofessional

Description

For years, natural hair has been considered unprofessional in many workplaces. This is because of the Eurocentric beauty standards that have been imposed on us. These standards dictate that straight, silky hair is the only acceptable form of hair in professional settings. As a result, many people with natural hair have been forced to conform to these standards by relaxing their hair or wearing wigs that mimic straight hair. This is not only damaging to their self-esteem but also to their hair health.


How to

Overcoming this stereotype starts with the acceptance and celebration of natural hair in all its forms. This can be done by educating people on the different types of natural hair and how to care for them. It is also important to have policies in place that allow for natural hairstyles to be worn in the workplace. Companies can also provide resources for employees to learn about natural hair care and styles, as well as provide products for natural hair.


Step by step

Step 1: Educate yourself on natural hair types and care.
Step 2: Advocate for policies that allow for natural hairstyles in the workplace.
Step 3: Provide resources for employees to learn about natural hair care and styles.
Step 4: Provide products for natural hair.
Step 5: Celebrate and embrace natural hair in all its forms.


Tips

- Encourage diversity and inclusivity in the workplace.
- Provide a safe space for employees with natural hair.
- Celebrate and showcase natural hair in company marketing and advertising.
- Create a natural hair support group within the company.
- Offer natural hair styling services for employees.


FAQ

Q: Is it legal for an employer to ban natural hairstyles?
A: No, it is not legal for an employer to ban natural hairstyles, as this is considered discrimination based on race.


Q: Can natural hair be professional?
A: Yes, natural hair can be professional, just like any other hairstyle. It is important to focus on the neatness and appropriateness of the hairstyle rather than the texture of the hair.


Q: How can I protect my natural hair in the workplace?
A: You can protect your natural hair in the workplace by wearing styles that are appropriate for your job, such as braids or twists. You can also use protective styles such as wigs or weaves to give your hair a break from daily styling.


Hair Care

To care for natural hair, it is important to use products that are specifically designed for natural hair. These products should be free of harsh chemicals that can damage the hair. It is also important to properly cleanse and condition the hair on a regular basis, as well as protect it from heat and other environmental factors. Regular trims and protective styling can also help to maintain healthy natural hair.


In conclusion, natural hair should not be considered unprofessional. It is time to embrace and celebrate natural hair in all its forms. By educating ourselves and others, advocating for policies, and providing resources and products, we can create a more inclusive and accepting workplace for all hair types.
