How To Start Dreads Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you've been considering starting dreads in your long hair, you're not alone. Dreadlocks have been a popular hairstyle for centuries, and for good reason. They're unique, low-maintenance, and perfect for those who want to embrace their natural texture. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of starting dreads in long hair, including tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care advice.

How to Start Dreads Long Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, it's important to understand the basics of dreadlocks. Dreads are created by matting hair together over time, and the process can take anywhere from a few months to a few years to fully mature. There are a few different methods for starting dreads in long hair, including backcombing, twisting, and neglect. The method you choose will depend on your hair texture, lifestyle, and personal preference.

Backcombing

Backcombing is a popular method for starting dreads in long hair, and involves teasing the hair in small sections to create knots. This method can be done at home, but it's important to use the right tools and products to ensure the best results.

Twisting

Twisting is another common method for starting dreads in long hair, and involves twisting the hair in small sections to create knots. This method can be done at home, but it's important to use the right tools and products to ensure the best results.

Neglect

Neglect is a more natural method for starting dreads in long hair, and involves simply letting the hair mat together over time. This method requires little maintenance, but can take longer to fully mature.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're ready to start your dreadlock journey, follow these steps to get started:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you begin, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. Wash your hair thoroughly and allow it to air dry completely.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into small, manageable sections. The size of the sections will depend on your hair texture and the method you choose.

Step 3: Backcomb or Twist

Using a comb or your fingers, backcomb or twist each section of hair to create knots. Be sure to work from the roots to the ends, and use a small amount of product, such as beeswax or gel, to help the knots form.

Step 4: Palm Roll

After you've backcombed or twisted each section, use your palms to roll the hair between your hands. This will help to create a smoother, more uniform appearance.

Step 5: Maintain

Once your dreads have been started, it's important to maintain them regularly. This may include re-twisting or palm rolling, washing and conditioning, and using the right products to keep them healthy and strong.

Tips for Starting Dreads Long Hair

Starting dreads in long hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to do it right to ensure the best results. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Use the right tools and products for your hair texture
  • Be patient - the process can take time
  • Maintain your dreads regularly to keep them healthy
  • Experiment with different styles and accessories

Frequently Asked Questions

If you're considering starting dreads in your long hair, you may have a few questions. Here are some of the most common questions we hear:

How long does it take to start dreads in long hair?

The process can take anywhere from a few months to a few years, depending on your hair texture and the method you choose.

What products should I use to start dreads in long hair?

It's important to use the right products for your hair texture, including beeswax or gel to help the knots form.

Can I start dreads in long hair if my hair is relaxed or chemically treated?

Yes, you can start dreads in long hair even if your hair is relaxed or chemically treated. However, it's important to use the right products and take extra care to keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care for Dreads Long Hair

Once you've started your dreads, it's important to take good care of them to keep them healthy and strong. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Condition your dreads with natural oils, such as coconut or argan oil
  • Avoid using heavy products or over-manipulating your hair
  • Protect your hair from heat and sun damage

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be well on your way to starting dreads in your long hair. Remember to be patient, experiment with different styles, and most importantly, have fun!
