When Does Hair Stop Falling After Pregnancy

Written by Petter Aug 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you a new mom experiencing hair loss after giving birth? This is a common occurrence that affects many women. Known as postpartum hair loss, it is caused by hormonal changes that occur during pregnancy. In this article, we will discuss when does hair stop falling after pregnancy, how to manage it, and tips on hair care.

Table of Contents:

  • When does hair stop falling after pregnancy?
  • How to manage postpartum hair loss
  • Step-by-step guide to prevent hair loss
  • Tips for hair care after pregnancy
  • Frequently asked questions about postpartum hair loss

When does hair stop falling after pregnancy?

Postpartum hair loss typically starts around three months after delivery and can last up to a year. The good news is that it is a temporary condition, and your hair should start to grow back once your hormones stabilize.

Every woman is different, and the duration of hair loss can vary. Some women may experience it for a few months, while others may have it for up to a year. However, you should notice a decrease in hair shedding after six months, and your hair should return to its pre-pregnancy state by your baby's first birthday.

How to manage postpartum hair loss

While postpartum hair loss is a natural process, there are things you can do to manage it. Here are some tips:

  • Be gentle with your hair: Avoid hairstyles that pull or tug at your hair, such as tight ponytails or braids. Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling tools: Heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Consider air-drying your hair or using a low-heat setting on your styling tools.
  • Eat a balanced diet: Your hair needs nutrients to grow, so make sure you are getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals in your diet.
  • Take care of your scalp: A healthy scalp promotes healthy hair growth. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-washing your hair.

Step-by-step guide to prevent hair loss

Here is a step-by-step guide to prevent hair loss after pregnancy:

  1. Be gentle with your hair, and avoid hairstyles that pull or tug at it.
  2. Avoid heat styling tools, and use a low-heat setting if you must use them.
  3. Eat a balanced diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  4. Take care of your scalp by using a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  5. Consider taking a hair supplement containing biotin, iron, and zinc to promote hair growth.
  6. Get plenty of rest and manage your stress levels.
  7. Consult with your doctor if you are experiencing excessive hair loss or other symptoms.

Tips for hair care after pregnancy

Here are some tips for hair care after pregnancy:

  • Be patient: Your hair will grow back, but it may take some time.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles: Try out new hairstyles that work with your postpartum hair, such as braids or a messy bun.
  • Get regular trims: Trimming your hair every six to eight weeks can help prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment: These can help nourish and strengthen your hair.

Frequently asked questions about postpartum hair loss

Q: Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

A: No, postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition that should resolve on its own within a year.

Q: Can I prevent postpartum hair loss?

A: While you cannot prevent postpartum hair loss, you can take steps to manage it and promote healthy hair growth.

Q: How much hair loss is normal after pregnancy?

A: It is normal to lose up to 100 hairs per day. However, during postpartum hair loss, you may lose more than usual.

Q: When should I see a doctor about my hair loss?

A: If you are experiencing excessive hair loss or other symptoms, such as itching or pain, you should consult with your doctor.

Q: Can I dye my hair during postpartum hair loss?

A: It is generally safe to dye your hair during postpartum hair loss. However, it is best to wait until your hair has stopped shedding excessively and has returned to its pre-pregnancy state.

Postpartum hair loss can be a frustrating experience, but it is a common and temporary one. By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can help promote healthy hair growth and manage postpartum hair loss.
