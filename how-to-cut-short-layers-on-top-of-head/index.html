How To Cut Short Layers On Top Of Head A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 30, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Cutting short layers on top of your head can give you a stylish and trendy look. It's a great way to add volume, texture, and movement to your hair. However, it can be a bit tricky to do it yourself, especially if you're not experienced with hair cutting. In this article, we'll show you how to cut short layers on top of your head in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language. We'll cover everything from preparation to aftercare, so you can achieve the perfect short layered look.

  2. Tools You'll Need
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Cutting Your Hair

  6. Finishing Touches

  10. Conclusion

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have a clear idea of the style you want to achieve. Look for pictures or videos of short layered hairstyles that you like, and take note of the length and layering you want.

It's also important to have clean and dry hair before cutting. Wash and blow-dry your hair, and make sure it's tangle-free. Wet hair can be misleading, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.

Tools You'll Need

Here are the tools you'll need to cut short layers on top of your head:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Water spray bottle
  • Mirror (preferably a hand-held one and a larger one to see the back of your head)

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair properly is crucial to achieving even and symmetrical layers. Follow these steps to section your hair:

  1. Divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Use a comb to create a center parting, and then make a horizontal parting from ear to ear.
  2. Clip or tie the back sections out of the way.
  3. Take one of the front sections and comb it forward over your face. This will be your guide for the length of the layers.

Cutting Your Hair

Now that your hair is sectioned, it's time to start cutting. Follow these steps:

  1. Start with the front section that you combed forward. Hold it between your index and middle fingers, and point-cut the ends to create the desired length. Take small sections at a time to ensure evenness.
  2. Move to the other front section and repeat the process.
  3. Unclip or untie one of the back sections and comb it upward. Hold it between your fingers and point-cut the ends to match the length of the front sections.
  4. Repeat the process with the remaining back section.

Blending Your Layers

After cutting all sections, it's time to blend the layers together for a seamless look. Follow these steps:

  1. Take a small section of hair from the top of your head and comb it upward.
  2. Hold the hair between your fingers and point-cut the ends to blend the layers together. Be careful not to cut too much, as this can create choppy layers.
  3. Repeat the process with the remaining sections, working your way from the top of your head to the sides and back.

Finishing Touches

Once you've blended your layers, it's time to add the finishing touches. Follow these steps:

  1. Take a look in the mirror and check for any uneven sections or flyaways. Trim them as necessary.
  2. Style your hair as desired. You can blow-dry it straight or add some curls for a more textured look.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect short layered look:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure clean and precise cuts.
  • Take small sections at a time to ensure evenness.
  • Point-cut the ends for a softer and more natural look.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's easier to trim more later than to fix a mistake.
  • Use a hand-held mirror to check the back of your head.

FAQs

Can I cut my own short layers on top of my head?

Yes, you can cut your own short layers on top of your head. However, it's important to be careful and take your time. If you're not confident, it's best to seek the help of a professional hairdresser.

How often should I cut my short layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your layers lose their shape. On average, you should cut your short layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain their style.

Can I add color to my short layered hair?

Yes, you can add color to your short layered hair. However, it's best to consult a professional hair colorist to ensure the best results.

Hair Care

After cutting your short layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause breakage and split ends.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your layers regularly to maintain their shape and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Cutting short layers on top of your head can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and trendy look that suits your personality and style. Remember to take your time, be patient, and follow our step-by-step guide. Happy cutting!
