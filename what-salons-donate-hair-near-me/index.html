What Salons Donate Hair Near Me A Guide To Hair Donation

Written by Frank Jul 05, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking for a way to give back and make a difference in someone else's life? Donating your hair to make wigs for cancer patients is a great way to do just that. In this guide, we'll explore what salons donate hair near me, how to donate your hair, and some tips and FAQs to help you get started.

Table of Contents

  • Salons That Donate Hair Near Me
  • How to Donate Your Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Donating Your Hair

Salons That Donate Hair Near Me

There are many salons that participate in hair donation programs. Some of the most well-known programs include:

  • Locks of Love
  • Pantene Beautiful Lengths
  • Wigs for Kids
  • Children with Hair Loss

To find salons that participate in these programs near you, you can search online or check with your local hair salon. Many salons have information about hair donation programs posted in their waiting areas or on their websites.

How to Donate Your Hair

The process for donating your hair is fairly simple:

  1. Choose a hair donation program that you want to donate to.
  2. Make sure your hair meets the program's requirements (usually a minimum of 8-10 inches of hair that is not chemically treated).
  3. Visit a salon that participates in the program.
  4. Get your hair cut and styled according to the program's guidelines.
  5. Mail your hair donation to the program's headquarters.

Step-by-Step Guide to Donating Your Hair

Here are the steps to donate your hair:

  1. Research hair donation programs to find one that you want to donate to.
  2. Make sure your hair meets the program's requirements. Most programs require a minimum of 8-10 inches of hair that is not chemically treated.
  3. Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before getting it cut.
  4. Visit a salon that participates in the hair donation program. Let your stylist know that you want to donate your hair and ask them to style it according to the program's guidelines.
  5. Get your hair cut and styled according to the program's guidelines.
  6. Place your hair in a ponytail or braid and cut it off above the rubber band or hair tie.
  7. Put your hair donation in a plastic bag and mail it to the program's headquarters. Make sure to include your name and contact information.

Tips for Donating Your Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your hair donation:

  • Choose a program that you feel passionate about. Research the different programs and find one that aligns with your values.
  • Make sure your hair meets the program's requirements before getting it cut.
  • Bring a picture of the style you want to your stylist. This will help ensure that your hair is cut and styled correctly.
  • Consider donating money along with your hair. Most programs rely on donations to make wigs and support their mission.

FAQs About Hair Donation

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair donation:

How long does my hair need to be to donate?

Most programs require a minimum of 8-10 inches of hair that is not chemically treated.

Can I donate colored or highlighted hair?

It depends on the program. Some programs accept colored or highlighted hair, while others require hair that is not chemically treated.

What happens to my hair after I donate it?

Your hair will be made into a wig and given to a cancer patient who has lost their hair due to chemotherapy or radiation treatment.

Hair Care After Donating

After donating your hair, it's important to take care of your new haircut. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, like curling irons and flat irons.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to avoid tangles.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Donating your hair is a great way to make a difference in someone else's life. By following these tips and guidelines, you can ensure that your hair donation has the greatest impact possible.
