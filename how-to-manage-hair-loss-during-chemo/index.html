How To Manage Hair Loss During Chemo

Written by Eveline May 10, 2023 · 6 min read
How to Manage Hair Loss During Chemo - Tips and FAQ

Dealing with hair loss during chemotherapy can be a difficult and emotional experience. While it may not be possible to prevent hair loss during treatment, there are things you can do to manage it and help maintain your self-esteem. Here are some tips and frequently asked questions to help you navigate this challenging time.

Table of Contents:

  1. How to Manage Hair Loss During Chemo
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips for Managing Hair Loss
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care After Chemo

Step by Step Guide

Here are some steps you can take to manage hair loss during chemo:

  1. Speak with your healthcare team about the likelihood of hair loss and when it may occur
  2. Consider cutting your hair short or shaving your head before hair loss begins to help ease the transition
  3. Invest in a wig or other head covering that you feel comfortable wearing
  4. Explore scarves, hats, and other head coverings that can be worn in addition to or instead of a wig
  5. Protect your scalp from the sun and cold with a hat or scarf when outdoors
  6. Be gentle with your remaining hair when washing and styling it
  7. Avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons and hair dryers
  8. Consider using a satin or silk pillowcase to help reduce hair breakage

Tips for Managing Hair Loss

Here are some additional tips to help you manage hair loss during chemo:

  • Be patient with yourself and allow yourself time to adjust to your new appearance
  • Try out different head coverings and styles to find what works best for you
  • Consider talking to others who have gone through hair loss during chemo for support and advice
  • Experiment with makeup and accessories to draw attention to other features you like about yourself
  • Remember that hair loss is a temporary side effect of chemotherapy and that your hair will likely grow back once treatment is over

FAQ

Will all chemotherapy cause hair loss?

Not all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss, but many do. Your healthcare team can give you more information about the specific drugs you will be receiving and their potential side effects.

How soon will I begin to lose my hair?

Hair loss typically begins within a few weeks of starting chemotherapy. The rate and extent of hair loss can vary depending on the drugs used and the individual.

Can I color or perm my hair during chemotherapy?

It is generally recommended to avoid chemical treatments like coloring or perming your hair during chemotherapy, as they can be harsh on your hair and scalp. Ask your healthcare team for specific guidance.

Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

Yes, in most cases, hair will grow back after chemotherapy. It may take several months for your hair to grow back fully, and the texture and color may be different than it was before treatment.

Hair Care After Chemo

Once your hair begins to grow back after chemotherapy, it is important to take good care of it to promote healthy growth. Here are some tips for caring for post-chemo hair:

  • Be gentle with your hair when washing and styling it
  • Use mild, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons and hair dryers
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients that promote hair growth, such as iron, zinc, and vitamin B12
  • Consider using hair growth treatments like minoxidil or biotin supplements, but talk to your healthcare team first to make sure they are safe for you

Managing hair loss during chemo can be challenging, but with a little patience and the right tools and support, it is possible to navigate this difficult time with grace and confidence.
