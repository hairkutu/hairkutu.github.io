How To Cut Your Own Men s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Apr 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending money and time at the barbershop? Want to learn how to cut your own hair? With a few tools and some patience, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut in the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your own men's hair.

How to Cut Your Own Men's Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair cape or towel
  • Mirror

Make sure your clippers are fully charged and your scissors are sharp. You don't want to be halfway through your haircut and have your clippers die or your scissors become dull.

Step 2: Choose Your Haircut Style

Before you start cutting, decide what kind of haircut you want. Look up pictures or videos online to get an idea of what you like. Keep in mind your hair type and how easy or difficult it might be to achieve a certain style.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair and dry it completely before you start cutting. Wet hair can be difficult to cut and can cause uneven results. Use a towel or hair dryer to make sure your hair is fully dry.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair. Start with the sides and back, then move to the top. Use clips to hold your hair in place as you cut.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Start with the sides and back. Use your clippers with a guard to trim the hair to your desired length. Move the clippers against the direction of hair growth for a more even cut. Use scissors to blend the top and sides.

Step 6: Trim the Top

Use scissors to trim the top of your hair. Start at the front and work your way to the back. Use a comb to lift sections of hair and cut at an angle for a layered effect. Be careful not to cut too much at once.

Step 7: Blend and Finish

Use scissors to blend the top and sides of your hair. Check your work in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments. Use your clippers without a guard to trim around your ears and the back of your neck. Use a comb to make sure everything is even.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Men's Hair

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you want a shorter cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to uneven results.
  • Check your work in the mirror frequently to make sure everything is even.
  • If you're unsure about a certain section, err on the side of caution and cut less.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if you have curly hair. Just be careful not to cut too much at once and use scissors to blend the top and sides.

What if I mess up?

If you mess up, don't panic. You can always go to a professional for a fix or try again at home. Hair grows back, so it's not the end of the world.

How often should I cut my own hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, cutting your hair every 4-6 weeks is recommended.

Hair Care Tips

After you've cut your hair, make sure to take care of it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type. Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Cutting your own men's hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and some patience, you can achieve great results. Follow these steps and tips, and you'll be on your way to a professional-looking haircut in no time.
