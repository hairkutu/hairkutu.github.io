How To Cut Girl Hair Bangs Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Cutting girl hair bangs can be a daunting task, especially if you are doing it for the first time. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can create a beautiful, face-framing look that your little girl will love. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting girl hair bangs, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Girl Hair Bangs
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Girl Hair Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care after Cutting Girl Hair Bangs

How to Cut Girl Hair Bangs

Before you begin cutting girl hair bangs, you need to decide on the type of bangs you want to create. There are different types of bangs, including side-swept, blunt, choppy, and wispy. You should also consider the length and thickness of your girl's hair.

Once you have decided on the type of bangs, you need to gather the necessary tools. These include sharp scissors, a fine-tooth comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle filled with water. You should also make sure that your girl's hair is clean and dry.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair. Use the fine-tooth comb to create a straight part in the middle of the hair. Then, comb the hair forward and create another part that extends from the middle of the first part to the outside of each eyebrow. Clip the rest of the hair back.

Step 2: Wet the Hair

Use the spray bottle to wet the hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that the hair is even.

Step 3: Cut the Bangs

Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Make sure that you cut the hair slightly longer than the desired length. You can always go back and trim it later.

Step 4: Check the Length

Brush the bangs down and check the length. If they are too long, trim them a little at a time until they are the desired length. If they are too short, you will need to wait for them to grow out.

Step 5: Add Texture

If you want to add texture to the bangs, hold the scissors vertically and make small snips along the edge of the bangs.

Step 6: Style the Bangs

Finally, style the bangs as desired. You can blow-dry them straight or use a round brush to create a more voluminous look.

Tips for Cutting Girl Hair Bangs

  • Start by cutting the bangs a little longer than you think you want them. You can always go back and trim them later.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Make sure that the hair is wet before cutting. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that the hair is even.
  • If you are unsure about cutting the bangs yourself, consider taking your girl to a professional stylist.

FAQs

How often should I cut my girl's bangs?

This depends on how fast her hair grows and the type of bangs you have created. Generally, bangs need to be trimmed every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape.

What type of bangs are best for my girl?

The type of bangs that are best for your girl depends on her face shape and hair type. Consult with a professional stylist to determine the best type of bangs for your girl.

Can I cut bangs on curly hair?

Yes, you can cut bangs on curly hair. However, you need to be careful not to cut them too short since curly hair tends to spring up once it is dry.

Hair Care after Cutting Girl Hair Bangs

After cutting girl hair bangs, it is important to take good care of the hair. This includes washing it regularly, using a conditioner to keep it soft and manageable, and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible. You should also trim the bangs regularly to maintain their shape.

Cutting girl hair bangs can be a fun and rewarding experience, especially if you follow these simple steps and tips. With a little practice, you can create a beautiful, face-framing look that your little girl will love.
