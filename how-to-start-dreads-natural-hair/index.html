How To Start Dreads On Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Apr 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Starting dreadlocks on natural hair can be a daunting task, but with the right knowledge and tools, it can be a fun and rewarding journey. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads on natural hair, as well as tips on how to care for your hair during the process.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Start Dreads on Natural Hair
  2. Tips for Starting Dreads on Natural Hair
  3. FAQs About Starting Dreads on Natural Hair
  4. Hair Care for Your Dreads

How to Start Dreads on Natural Hair

Before you start the process of dreadlocking your hair, it's important to have healthy and well-moisturized hair. Here's how to get started:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove all the build-up and oils from your hair. This will help your hair to lock faster and better.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into the size of the dreads you want. You can use a comb or your fingers to section your hair. Make sure the sections are even in size.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Take one section of hair and backcomb it using a comb. Start from the middle of the section and work your way up to the roots. Repeat this process until all the sections are backcombed.

Step 4: Twist and Rip Your Hair

Take one section of backcombed hair and twist it in one direction. Then, grab the hair at the root and pull it in the opposite direction. This will help to create a knot in the hair, which will eventually turn into a dreadlock. Repeat this process for all the sections of hair.

Step 5: Use a Dread Wax

Apply a small amount of dread wax to each section of your hair. This will help to hold the hair together and make it easier to maintain your dreads.

Step 6: Maintain Your Dreads

Once your dreads are formed, it's important to maintain them properly. This involves washing them regularly and using a special shampoo designed for dreadlocks.

Tips for Starting Dreads on Natural Hair

Here are some tips to help you start your dreads on natural hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo before starting the process.
  • Section your hair evenly to get a uniform look.
  • Use a comb or your fingers to backcomb your hair.
  • Twist and rip your hair in the same direction to create uniform dreadlocks.
  • Use a dread wax to help hold your hair together.
  • Be patient and give your hair time to lock.

FAQs About Starting Dreads on Natural Hair

Q: Can I start dreads on short hair?

A: Yes, you can start dreads on short hair. However, it may take longer for the hair to lock.

Q: Can I start dreads on relaxed hair?

A: Yes, you can start dreads on relaxed hair. However, it's important to note that relaxed hair may take longer to lock than natural hair.

Q: How often should I wash my dreads?

A: You should wash your dreads once a week or every two weeks with a special shampoo designed for dreadlocks.

Hair Care for Your Dreads

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and beautiful dreadlocks. Here are some tips to help you care for your dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a special shampoo designed for dreadlocks.
  • Avoid using regular conditioner on your dreads, as it can cause buildup.
  • Avoid using a blow dryer on your dreads, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a silk scarf or pillowcase to protect your dreads while sleeping.
  • Be gentle when styling your dreads to avoid pulling and breakage.

Starting dreadlocks on natural hair can be a fun and rewarding journey. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve beautiful and healthy dreadlocks that you will love for years to come.
