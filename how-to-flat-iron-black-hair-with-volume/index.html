How To Flat Iron Black Hair With Volume

Written by William Apr 11, 2023 · 7 min read
How to Flat Iron Black Hair with Volume | Step-by-Step Guide

Do you have black hair with volume and want to achieve a sleek, straight look? Flat ironing your hair is a great option. Flat ironing is a straightforward process, but it can be tricky to get the right results, especially with black hair that has volume. In this article, we’ll show you the steps you need to follow to flat iron black hair with volume.

How to Flat Iron Black Hair with Volume

Step 1: Cleanse and Condition

The first step to flat ironing black hair with volume is to cleanse and condition your hair. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner that moisturizes your hair. Dry your hair thoroughly before starting the flat ironing process.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a detangling brush or comb to remove tangles and knots from your hair. This step is crucial to ensure that the flat iron glides smoothly through your hair.

Step 3: Apply Heat Protectant

Before using a flat iron, it’s essential to apply a heat protectant product to your hair. Heat protectant helps to prevent heat damage and keep your hair healthy.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using clips. This step will make the flat ironing process more manageable and ensure that you don’t miss any sections.

Step 5: Flat Iron Your Hair

Start flat ironing your hair one section at a time, working from the roots to the ends. Use the flat iron in a smooth, fluid motion, and don’t leave it in one place for too long to avoid heat damage. Repeat the process until you’ve flat ironed all of your hair.

Step 6: Apply Finishing Products

After flat ironing your hair, you can apply finishing products, like hair oil or serum, to add shine and reduce frizz. However, be careful not to use too much product, as this can weigh your hair down.

Tips for Flat Ironing Black Hair with Volume

Here are some additional tips to help you flat iron black hair with volume:

  • Use a high-quality flat iron with adjustable temperature settings
  • Don’t flat iron wet or damp hair, as this can cause heat damage
  • Use a heat-resistant glove to protect your hands while flat ironing
  • Avoid flat ironing your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage

FAQ About Flat Ironing Black Hair with Volume

Can I flat iron my hair every day?

No, it’s not recommended to flat iron your hair every day. Frequent heat styling can cause damage and breakage, so it’s best to limit your flat ironing to once or twice a week.

What temperature should I use for flat ironing black hair?

The temperature you use depends on your hair type and the flat iron you’re using. For black hair with volume, use a temperature between 350 and 400 degrees Fahrenheit.

How can I prevent heat damage while flat ironing my hair?

Use a heat protectant product before flat ironing, avoid leaving the flat iron in one place for too long, and don’t use too much heat. If your hair starts to smoke or smell burnt, lower the temperature or stop flat ironing immediately.

Hair Care for Flat Ironed Black Hair with Volume

After flat ironing your hair, it’s essential to maintain its health and prevent damage. Here are some tips for caring for flat ironed black hair with volume:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh your hair down
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage

Flat ironing black hair with volume can be challenging, but by following these steps and tips, you can achieve a sleek, straight look while keeping your hair healthy and strong.
