Short Hairstyles For Round Fat Faces Over 50 A Comprehensive Guide

As we age, our hair tends to lose its thickness and volume, making it harder to style. This can be especially challenging for those with round fat faces over 50. Luckily, there are plenty of short hairstyles that can flatter your features and make you look and feel your best.

Description

Round fat faces can be difficult to style, but with the right haircut, you can accentuate your best features and look your best. Short hairstyles are a great option for those over 50 because they're easy to maintain and can make you look years younger. Plus, they're perfect for those with thinning hair, as they can add volume and thickness to your locks.

How to Choose the Right Short Hairstyle

Choosing the right short hairstyle can be a daunting task, but there are a few things to keep in mind when making your decision. First, consider your face shape. Those with round faces should opt for hairstyles that add height and length to their features, such as pixie cuts or layered bobs. Next, think about your hair texture. If you have thin hair, a blunt bob can add thickness and volume, while those with thick hair can pull off a choppy, layered cut. Finally, don't forget about your personal style. Your haircut should make you feel confident and comfortable, so choose a style that suits your personality.

Best Short Hairstyles for Round Fat Faces Over 50:

  • Pixie cut
  • Layered bob
  • Blunt bob
  • Asymmetrical bob
  • Cropped curly cut

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Short Hair

Styling short hair can be quick and easy, especially if you have the right tools and products. Follow this step-by-step guide to achieve a flawless look every time:

  1. Start with clean, towel-dried hair.
  2. Apply a small amount of mousse or styling gel to your hair, focusing on the roots.
  3. Blow dry your hair, using a round brush to add volume and lift to your locks.
  4. Use a flat iron or curling wand to add texture and shape to your hair.
  5. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

Tips for Maintaining Short Hair

Short hair requires minimal maintenance, but there are a few tips to keep in mind to ensure your style stays looking great:

  • Visit your stylist every 4-6 weeks for a trim to keep your haircut looking fresh.
  • Invest in high-quality styling tools, such as a flat iron or curling wand, to achieve your desired look.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before styling your hair to prevent damage.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip your locks of natural oils and cause dryness.

FAQs

Q: Will short hair make my face look even rounder?

A: Not necessarily. In fact, the right short haircut can actually elongate your features and make your face appear slimmer. Opt for styles that add height and volume to your hair, such as pixie cuts or layered bobs.

Q: Can I still style my short hair if I have thinning hair?

A: Absolutely. In fact, short haircuts can add volume and thickness to your locks, making them a great option for those with thinning hair. Consider a blunt bob or layered cut for added fullness.

Q: How often should I wash my short hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid over-drying and damaging your locks. You can also use a dry shampoo in between washes to refresh your hair and absorb excess oil.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Short hair is easy to maintain, but it still requires some TLC to keep it looking its best. Follow these hair care tips to keep your locks healthy and strong:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools every day to prevent damage and breakage.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to nourish and hydrate your locks.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage and damage.

With the right short hairstyle and a little bit of TLC, you can look and feel your best at any age. Whether you opt for a pixie cut or a layered bob, be sure to choose a style that makes you feel confident and comfortable. Happy styling!
