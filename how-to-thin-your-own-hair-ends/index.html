How To Thin Your Own Hair Ends A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thick hair, you know that it can be a blessing and a curse. While you may have enviable volume, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One way to make your hair more manageable is by thinning out the ends. But you don't have to go to a salon to get this done - you can do it yourself at home. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to thin your own hair ends.

What Does it Mean to Thin Your Hair?

Thinning your hair means removing some of the bulk from the ends. This can be done with scissors or a razor, and it's a great way to make your hair look more textured and less heavy.

How to Thin Your Own Hair Ends

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin your own hair ends:

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start, make sure you have everything you need. You'll need a pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a mirror.

Step 2: Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair. Shampoo and condition your hair as you normally would, and let it air dry.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use a comb to detangle it. Make sure it's completely smooth and free of knots.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Use the comb to create a part down the center of your head. Then, create two more parts on each side of the center part. You should have a total of four sections.

Step 5: Start Thinning

Take one of the sections and hold it straight up in the air. Take your hair cutting scissors and make small snips along the ends of the hair. Be careful not to cut too much at once - you can always go back and cut more if you need to.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've thinned out the first section, use the scissors to blend the layers together. This will create a more natural look.

Step 7: Repeat

Repeat this process on all of the sections of your hair. Take your time and be patient - it's better to cut a little bit at a time than to cut too much at once.

Step 8: Check Yourself Out

Once you've finished thinning your hair, check yourself out in the mirror. If you're happy with the results, you're done! If you need to make any adjustments, go back and do so.

Tips for Thinning Your Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you thin your own hair ends:

  • Use sharp scissors - dull scissors can damage your hair
  • Cut a little bit at a time - you can always cut more later
  • Don't cut too close to the scalp
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet - it will be harder to control
  • Take breaks if you need to - this can be a time-consuming process

FAQ

Can you thin your hair with a razor?

Yes, you can use a razor to thin your hair. However, this method should only be used by professionals, as it can be dangerous if not done correctly.

How often should you thin your hair?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, you should thin your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Will thinning your hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning your hair will not make it grow back thicker. This is a myth.

Hair Care

Thinning your hair can be a great way to make it more manageable, but it's important to take care of your hair afterwards. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week
  • Avoid using heat tools too often
  • Use a hair mask once a month
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking and feeling healthy.
