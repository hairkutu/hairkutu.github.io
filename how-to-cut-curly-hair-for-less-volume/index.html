How To Cut Curly Hair For Less Volume A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 25, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how difficult it can be to manage. Curly hair tends to have a lot of volume, which can be overwhelming and difficult to style. Fortunately, there are ways to cut curly hair to reduce volume and make it more manageable. In this article, we will show you how to cut curly hair for less volume.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Curly Hair for Less Volume
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Description

Cutting curly hair for less volume involves using specific cutting techniques that help to create a more defined shape and reduce bulk. This type of haircut is best done by someone who is experienced in cutting curly hair, as it requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. By following the steps in this guide, you can achieve a more manageable and stylish haircut.

How to Cut Curly Hair for Less Volume

The first step to cutting curly hair for less volume is to start with dry hair. This will allow you to see the natural shape of the curls and make more precise cuts. You will also want to use styling products that help to define the curls, such as a curl cream or gel.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by sectioning the hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back.

2. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and cut at an angle, following the natural shape of the curls. Only cut a small amount of hair at a time to avoid cutting off too much length.

3. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way up to the top of the head. Be sure to cut at an angle to create layers and reduce bulk.

4. Once you have cut all the sections, use your fingers to separate and define the curls. You may want to use a diffuser to dry the hair and enhance the curls.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

- Use sharp scissors to make clean cuts and avoid damaging the hair.

- Cut the hair in small sections to avoid cutting off too much length.

- Cut at an angle to create layers and reduce bulk.

- Take your time and be patient. Cutting curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

A: It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink and make it difficult to see the natural shape of the curls.

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your curls.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

In addition to cutting your hair for less volume, it's important to take care of your curls to keep them healthy and defined. This includes using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, using a deep conditioner once a week, and avoiding heat styling tools that can damage the hair. You may also want to consider using a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz and breakage while you sleep.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a more manageable and stylish haircut for your curly hair. Remember to be patient and take your time, and don't be afraid to seek the help of a professional stylist if you're unsure about cutting your own hair.
