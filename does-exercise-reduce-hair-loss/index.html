Does Exercise Reduce Hair Loss The Truth Behind The Myth

Written by Christine May 01, 2023 Â· 8 min read

As we age, hair loss becomes a common concern for many people. While there are various factors that contribute to hair loss, ranging from genetics to stress, some people believe that regular exercise can help reduce hair loss. In this blog post, we will investigate whether exercise really can reduce hair loss and provide you with some tips on how to care for your hair during your workout routine.

Table of Contents

  • How to Exercise to Reduce Hair Loss
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Care During Exercise
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair While Exercising
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Exercise to Reduce Hair Loss

While there is no direct scientific evidence linking exercise to hair loss, regular exercise can help reduce stress levels, which is a known contributing factor to hair loss. When we are stressed, our bodies produce a hormone called cortisol, which can damage hair follicles and lead to hair loss. Exercise releases endorphins, which can help reduce cortisol levels in the body and improve overall well-being.

Additionally, exercise increases blood flow and oxygen to the scalp, which can stimulate hair growth. The increased blood flow helps to deliver essential nutrients and oxygen to the hair follicles, promoting healthy hair growth.

What Types of Exercise are Best for Hair Health?

Any form of exercise that gets your heart rate up and increases blood flow can be beneficial for hair health. Cardiovascular exercises such as running, cycling, and swimming are great options. Resistance training, such as weightlifting, can also be beneficial as it increases blood flow to the scalp and can help promote hair growth.

Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Care During Exercise

While exercise can promote healthy hair growth, it is important to take care of your hair during your workout routine to prevent damage.

Step 1: Choose the Right Hairstyle

Choose a hairstyle that keeps your hair off your face and neck during exercise. A high ponytail or bun can be a great option. Avoid tight hairstyles, as they can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

Step 2: Use a Sweatband

A sweatband can help absorb sweat and prevent it from running down your face and onto your hair. This can help prevent sweat from drying out your hair and causing damage.

Step 3: Use a Mild Shampoo

After your workout, use a mild shampoo to cleanse your hair. Avoid using hot water, as it can dry out your hair and scalp. Instead, use lukewarm water to rinse your hair.

Step 4: Condition Your Hair

Use a conditioner to help restore moisture to your hair after washing it. Focus on the ends of your hair, as this is where damage is most likely to occur.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair While Exercising

Here are some additional tips to help maintain healthy hair while exercising:

Tip 1: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair from the sun. The sun can dry out your hair and cause damage.

Tip 2: Avoid Chemical Treatments

Avoid chemical treatments, such as coloring or perming, as they can damage your hair. If you must color your hair, use a gentle, ammonia-free dye.

Tip 3: Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth. Foods such as salmon, avocados, and sweet potatoes are great options.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can exercise cause hair loss?

A: While there is no direct link between exercise and hair loss, excessive exercise can lead to physical stress on the body, which can contribute to hair loss. It is important to balance your workout routine with rest and recovery to prevent physical stress.

Q: Can wearing a helmet while cycling cause hair loss?

A: Wearing a helmet while cycling can cause friction on the hair and scalp, which can lead to breakage. However, the benefits of wearing a helmet far outweigh the risk of hair loss. To prevent damage, wear a sweatband under your helmet to absorb sweat and prevent it from drying out your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my hair after exercising?

A: It is best to wash your hair after every workout to remove sweat and prevent buildup. However, if you have dry hair, you may be able to wash your hair every other day.

In conclusion, while exercise may not directly reduce hair loss, it can help reduce stress levels and promote healthy hair growth. By taking care of your hair during your workout routine and maintaining a healthy diet, you can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent damage.
