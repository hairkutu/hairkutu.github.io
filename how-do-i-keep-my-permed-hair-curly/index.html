How Do I Keep My Permed Hair Curly

Written by Luffy May 19, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Permed hair has been a popular hairstyle for decades, and it remains just as trendy today. However, many people struggle with keeping their permed hair curly, especially as the days go by. If you're one of them, don't worry! Here are some tips and tricks to help you keep your permed hair curly for longer.

Description

A perm is a hairstyle that involves using chemicals to change the structure of your hair. The process creates permanent curls or waves that last for several months. However, permed hair needs special care to maintain its shape and texture. Without proper care, your permed hair may become frizzy, dry, or lose its curl.

How to Keep Your Permed Hair Curly

To keep your permed hair curly, you need to follow a hair care routine that includes the right products and techniques. Here's how:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Regular shampoos contain sulfates that strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to breakage. Sulfate-free shampoos, on the other hand, are gentler on your hair and help retain moisture. Look for a shampoo that's specifically formulated for permed hair.

2. Condition regularly

Permed hair needs regular conditioning to keep it soft and manageable. Use a deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair and restore its moisture. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to detangle your hair and prevent breakage.

3. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your permed hair and make it lose its curl. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize the damage. Alternatively, you can air dry your hair or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls.

4. Sleep on a silk pillowcase

Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and tangle your permed hair, leading to breakage and frizz. Silk pillowcases, on the other hand, are gentle on your hair and reduce friction. They also help retain moisture and prevent frizz.

5. Don't overwash your hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to breakage. Aim to wash your hair once or twice a week, and use a dry shampoo in between to refresh your hair.

6. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer

A diffuser attachment on your blow dryer can help enhance your curls and prevent frizz. To use it, apply a styling product to your hair and use the diffuser on low heat and air speed. Cup the diffuser around your curls and lift it up to your scalp, holding it there for a few seconds.

7. Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry

Brushing your permed hair when it's dry can cause it to lose its curl and become frizzy. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet. If you must brush your hair when it's dry, use a boar bristle brush to minimize frizz.

8. Use a curl-enhancing product

A curl-enhancing product like a mousse or gel can help define your curls and keep them in place. Apply the product to your damp hair and scrunch it with your hands to enhance your curls.

9. Protect your hair from the sun

Exposure to the sun can dry out your permed hair and make it lose its curl. Wear a hat or use a scarf to protect your hair from the sun's rays. You can also use a UV protectant spray to shield your hair from the sun.

FAQ

Q. How long do permed curls last?

A. Permed curls usually last for three to six months, depending on your hair's texture and the type of perm you get.

Q. Can I color my permed hair?

A. Yes, you can color your permed hair, but it's best to wait at least two weeks after getting a perm to color your hair.

Q. Can I swim with permed hair?

A. Yes, you can swim with permed hair, but you need to protect your hair from chlorine and saltwater. Wear a swim cap or apply a leave-in conditioner before swimming.

Hair Care Tips for Permed Hair

Keeping your permed hair curly requires a bit of effort, but it's worth it. Here are some additional tips to help you maintain your permed hair:

  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your permed hair curly and healthy. Remember to be gentle with your hair and give it the care it deserves!
