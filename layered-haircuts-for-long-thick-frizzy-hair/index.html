Layered Haircuts For Long Thick Frizzy Hair Tips And Techniques

Written by William Jul 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of dealing with long, thick, and frizzy hair? Do you want to find a haircut that will make it easier to manage without sacrificing style? Look no further than layered haircuts for long thick frizzy hair! In this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about this trendy and practical hairstyle.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get a Layered Haircut for Long Thick Frizzy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Layered Haircut
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Long Thick Frizzy Hair

Description

Layered haircuts for long thick frizzy hair involve cutting the hair at different lengths to create layers. This technique adds dimension and movement to the hair while reducing bulk and frizz. The layers can be long or short, and can be cut at different angles to create a variety of styles. This haircut is perfect for those with long, thick, and frizzy hair who want a low-maintenance style that still looks stylish and put-together.

How to Get a Layered Haircut for Long Thick Frizzy Hair

The best way to get a layered haircut for long thick frizzy hair is to go to a professional hairstylist who has experience with this technique. They will be able to assess your hair type and texture and recommend the best layering technique for you. However, if you prefer to do it yourself, there are a few things you should keep in mind.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products designed for your hair type.

2. Towel dry your hair until it is damp but not soaking wet.

3. Comb your hair to remove tangles and knots.

4. Part your hair in the middle or on the side, depending on your preference.

5. Divide your hair into sections, starting from the back and working your way to the front.

6. Cut each section of hair at a slight angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up. The length of each layer will depend on your hair length and the style you want to achieve.

7. Blend the layers by using scissors to cut into the hair at different angles.

8. Once you have cut all the sections, check for any unevenness and trim as needed.

9. Style your hair as desired using a blow dryer, straightener, or curling iron.

Tips for Maintaining Your Layered Haircut

To keep your layered haircut looking its best, follow these tips:

  • Use products designed for your hair type, such as anti-frizz serums or volumizing sprays.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your layers.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Consider getting a keratin treatment or other hair smoothing treatment to reduce frizz and add shine.

FAQ

Q: Will a layered haircut make my hair look thinner?

A: No, a layered haircut can actually make your hair look thicker by adding volume and movement.

Q: How often should I get a layered haircut?

A: It depends on your hair growth rate and the length of your layers. As a general rule, you should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain your haircut.

Q: Can I get a layered haircut if I have thin hair?

A: Yes, a layered haircut can add volume and texture to thin hair. However, you should consult with a hairstylist to determine the best layering technique for your hair type.

Hair Care for Long Thick Frizzy Hair

Aside from getting a layered haircut, there are other things you can do to care for your long, thick, and frizzy hair.

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can cause frizz. Use lukewarm or cool water instead.
  • Pat your hair dry with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt instead of rubbing it with a regular towel, which can cause frizz.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can cause it to become dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a layered haircut that is perfect for your long thick frizzy hair. Whether you go to a professional hairstylist or try it yourself at home, a layered haircut can add dimension and movement to your hair while reducing frizz and bulk. Give it a try and see the difference it can make!
