How To Take Care Of Bleached Hair Black Girl A Complete Guide

Written by Christine May 21, 2023 ¬∑ 7 min read

Bleached hair is a trend that has been on the rise in recent years, and Black girls are not left out. However, taking care of bleached hair can be quite challenging, and it requires a lot of effort and time. In this article, we will provide you with a complete guide on how to take care of bleached hair black girl.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Bleaching hair involves removing the natural pigment from your hair strands. The process is harsh on your hair, and it can lead to dryness, breakage, and split ends. Black girls need to take extra care of their bleached hair because their hair is naturally drier and more delicate.

How to

If you are a black girl with bleached hair, you need to follow these steps to take care of your hair:

1. Deep Condition Your Hair

Bleached hair needs to be deeply conditioned regularly to restore moisture and prevent breakage. You can use a protein-based conditioner to strengthen your hair and a moisture-based conditioner to hydrate your hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can further damage your bleached hair. You should avoid using hot tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow-dryers. If you must use heat, apply a heat protectant to your hair before styling.

3. Use Sulfate-Free Shampoos

Sulfate-free shampoos are gentle on your bleached hair and do not strip your hair of its natural oils. Using a sulfate-free shampoo will help keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

4. Limit Chemical Treatments

Bleached hair is already chemically treated, so you should avoid using any other chemical treatments such as relaxers, perms, or hair dyes. These treatments can further damage your hair and lead to breakage and split ends.

Step by Step

Here are the steps you should follow to take care of your bleached hair as a black girl:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Use a sulfate-free shampoo to wash your hair. Massage your scalp gently, and rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Deep Condition Your Hair

Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and cover your hair with a plastic cap. Leave the conditioner on for at least 20 minutes, then rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.

Step 3: Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help lock in moisture.

Step 4: Air-Dry Your Hair

Avoid using heat to dry your hair. Instead, gently towel-dry your hair and let your hair air-dry naturally.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you take care of your bleached hair:

1. Protect Your Hair at Night

Use a satin bonnet or sleep on a satin pillowcase to protect your hair at night. This will help prevent breakage and split ends.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly will help get rid of split ends and prevent breakage.

3. Avoid Chlorine

Chlorine can be harsh on your bleached hair. Avoid swimming in chlorinated pools or wear a swim cap.

FAQ

Q: How Often Should I Deep Condition My Hair?

A: You should deep condition your hair at least once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

Q: Can I Still Straighten My Hair?

A: Yes, you can still straighten your hair, but you should avoid using heat often. Apply a heat protectant to your hair before straightening, and use a low heat setting.

Q: Can I Use Oils on My Bleached Hair?

A: Yes, you can use oils on your bleached hair to moisturize and protect it. Coconut oil, jojoba oil, and argan oil are great options.

Hair Care

Taking care of your bleached hair as a black girl requires effort and patience. You need to deep condition your hair regularly, avoid heat styling, and use sulfate-free shampoos. Follow the steps outlined in this article, and you'll have healthy and beautiful bleached hair.
