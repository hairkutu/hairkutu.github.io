What Makes Your Hair Puffy

Written by Petter May 13, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're struggling with puffy hair, you're not alone. Many people deal with this issue, and it can be frustrating to try and tame your hair every day. But what causes puffy hair, and how can you fix it? In this article, we'll go over what makes your hair puffy and give you some tips on how to handle it.

Causes of Puffy Hair

There are a few different things that can make your hair puffy:

Humidity

One of the main culprits of puffy hair is humidity. When there's a lot of moisture in the air, your hair can absorb it and expand, causing it to become frizzy and puffy.

Damaged Hair

If your hair is damaged, it can become puffy. This is because damaged hair is more porous and can absorb more moisture than healthy hair. If you've been using a lot of heat tools or chemical treatments on your hair, it may be damaged and more prone to puffiness.

Product Buildup

If you're using too much product in your hair, it can build up and make your hair puffy. This is because the product can weigh your hair down and make it appear more voluminous than it actually is.

How to Fix Puffy Hair

If you're dealing with puffy hair, there are a few things you can do to fix it:

Use Anti-Frizz Products

Anti-frizz products can help to smooth your hair and prevent it from puffing up. Look for products that contain ingredients like silicone, which can help to seal the cuticle and prevent moisture from getting in.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to puffiness. Try to avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons, and opt for heat-free styling methods like air-drying or using rollers.

Trim Your Hair

If your hair is damaged, getting a trim can help to remove the damaged ends and make your hair healthier. This can help to prevent your hair from becoming puffy in the future.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to taming puffy hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to help smooth your hair.
  3. Blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to avoid roughing up the hair cuticle.
  4. Avoid using a brush, and instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  5. Apply an anti-frizz serum or oil to your hair to help seal the cuticle and prevent moisture from getting in.
  6. Style your hair using heat-free methods, like air-drying or using rollers.

Tips for Managing Puffy Hair

Here are a few additional tips for managing puffy hair:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can create more frizz.
  • Use a silk pillowcase to help prevent your hair from getting tangled and frizzy while you sleep.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it more prone to puffiness.
  • Try using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

FAQs

Q: Can puffy hair be genetic?

A: Yes, some people are more prone to puffy hair due to their genetics.

Q: Can I use a hair straightener to fix puffy hair?

A: While a hair straightener can help to smooth your hair, it's not the best solution for puffy hair. Heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz and puffiness in the long run.

Q: Can I use a hair mask to fix puffy hair?

A: Yes, a hair mask can help to moisturize your hair and prevent it from becoming puffy. Look for masks that contain ingredients like argan oil, which can help to smooth and nourish your hair.

Hair Care for Puffy Hair

If you have puffy hair, it's important to take good care of it to prevent further damage and puffiness. Here are a few tips for hair care:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, like bleach or relaxers.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, or use a heat protectant spray before styling.
  • Get regular trims to remove any damaged ends and keep your hair healthy.

By following these tips, you can help to prevent your hair from becoming puffy and keep it healthy and beautiful.
