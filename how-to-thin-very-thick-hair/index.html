How To Thin Very Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 14, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have thick hair, you know that it can be a blessing and a curse. While it can look voluminous and healthy, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One solution to this problem is to thin your hair. Thinning your hair can make it more manageable and easier to style. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to thin very thick hair.

Description

Thinning hair is the process of removing or reducing the thickness of your hair. This can be done in various ways, such as using thinning shears, razor cutting, or texturizing scissors. Thinning your hair can help remove excess bulk and make your hair more manageable. It can also help your hair look more voluminous and healthy.

How to Thin Very Thick Hair

Step 1: Wash your hair

The first step in thinning your hair is to wash it. This will help remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup that may be weighing your hair down. Use a clarifying shampoo to ensure that your hair is completely clean.

Step 2: Towel dry your hair

After washing your hair, towel dry it until it is damp. Do not use a blow dryer, as this can cause your hair to become frizzy and difficult to manage.

Step 3: Section your hair

Section your hair into four equal parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to secure each section in place.

Step 4: Use thinning shears

Take one section of your hair and use thinning shears to remove some of the bulk. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can result in an uneven cut.

Step 5: Repeat on all sections

Repeat steps 4 and 5 on all sections of your hair until you have thinned out your entire head of hair.

Step 6: Style your hair

After thinning your hair, style it as desired. You may find that your hair is more manageable and easier to style after thinning it.

Tips

  • Use sharp scissors or thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Start by removing a small amount of hair and work your way up to a more significant cut.
  • Be careful not to thin your hair too much, as this can make it look stringy and unhealthy.
  • Consult a professional hairstylist if you are unsure about how to thin your hair.

FAQ

Can thinning your hair cause damage?

Thinning your hair can cause damage if not done correctly. It is important to use sharp scissors or thinning shears and to start by removing a small amount of hair at a time.

How often should you thin your hair?

The frequency with which you should thin your hair depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Consult a professional hairstylist for advice on how often to thin your hair.

Hair Care

After thinning your hair, it is essential to take care of it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. It is also essential to get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Thinning your hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easier to style. Follow the steps outlined in this article to thin your very thick hair and enjoy a more voluminous and healthy-looking mane.
