How To Fade Your Own Hair Youtube A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy May 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to save some money and learn a new skill, fading your own hair at home might be the perfect solution for you. With the help of YouTube tutorials, you can master the art of fading your own hair in no time. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to fade your own hair YouTube style, as well as some helpful tips and FAQs to ensure your at-home haircut is a success.

How to Fade Your Own Hair YouTube Style

Before getting started, you'll need some basic tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A towel or cape to protect your clothes

Once you have your tools ready, you can follow these steps:

Step 1: Start with a Clean Cut

Before you start fading your hair, you should have a clean cut. If your hair is too long, start by trimming it with scissors or clippers. Make sure to clean up any stray hairs around the ears and neckline.

Step 2: Create a Guide Line

Using your clippers, create a guide line around the bottom of your hairline. This will help you know where to start your fade. Make sure the guide line is straight and even on both sides.

Step 3: Choose Your Guard Size

Depending on how short you want your hair to be, choose the appropriate guard size for your clippers. Start with a larger size and work your way down as needed.

Step 4: Start Fading Your Hair

Starting at the bottom of your hairline, use the clippers to create a fade by gradually moving up the sides and back of your head. Make sure to blend the hair as you go, using shorter guard sizes as you move up. Use the comb to help blend the hair and ensure it's even.

Step 5: Blend the Top

Once you've faded the sides and back, it's time to blend the top. Use the scissors to trim any longer hairs and create a smooth transition between the top and sides.

Step 6: Clean Up the Neckline and Ears

Using the clippers and scissors, clean up any stray hairs around the neckline and ears. Make sure to blend the hair so it looks natural.

Step 7: Finishing Touches

Take a final look in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments. If you're happy with the result, clean up any hair clippings and enjoy your new haircut!

Tips for Fading Your Own Hair YouTube Style

Here are some tips to help ensure your at-home haircut is a success:

  • Watch multiple YouTube tutorials to get a better understanding of the process.
  • Start with a larger guard size and work your way down as needed.
  • Blend the hair as you go to ensure a natural look.
  • Take breaks if needed to avoid getting tired or frustrated.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from different angles.

FAQs About Fading Your Own Hair YouTube Style

Q: Is it difficult to fade your own hair?

A: Fading your own hair can be challenging, especially if you're new to cutting hair. However, with practice and patience, you can master the skill.

Q: What should I do if I make a mistake?

A: If you make a mistake, try not to panic. Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may be able to fix it by blending the hair or trimming it shorter. If you're really struggling, it's always a good idea to seek the help of a professional.

Q: How often should I fade my hair?

A: How often you fade your hair depends on your personal preference and hair growth rate. Some people may need to fade their hair every few weeks, while others may only need it once a month.

Hair Care After Fading Your Hair YouTube Style

After fading your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or straighteners.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat.

Fading your own hair YouTube style can be a fun and rewarding experience. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home.
