How To Cut Side Swept Bangs For Fine Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having the same hairstyle day in and day out? If you have fine hair, you may feel like your styling options are limited. However, there is one simple change you can make that will instantly transform your look: side swept bangs. Not only do they add volume and texture to fine hair, but they are also versatile and easy to maintain. In this article, we will show you how to cut side swept bangs for fine hair in a few simple steps.

Table of Contents

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Side Swept Bangs

How to Cut Side Swept Bangs for Fine Hair

Cutting side swept bangs for fine hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, you can do it yourself at home. Here are the steps you need to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing and drying your hair. Comb it out so that it is free of tangles.

Step 2: Section off the hair you want to cut into bangs. Use a comb to create a triangle shape, with the point of the triangle at the top of your head and the base of the triangle at the hairline.

Step 3: Twist the section of hair you want to cut into a tight ponytail. Hold the ponytail between your fingers, about an inch away from the ends.

Step 4: Use hair cutting scissors to cut the ends of the ponytail. Start by cutting a small amount, then gradually cut more until you reach the desired length.

Step 5: Release the ponytail and comb through your new side swept bangs. If necessary, trim any stray hairs with your scissors.

Step 6: Style your bangs as desired. You can blow dry them with a round brush for extra volume, or use a flat iron for a sleeker look.

Tips for Cutting Side Swept Bangs

Now that you know how to cut side swept bangs for fine hair, here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Invest in a pair of quality hair cutting scissors. Using dull scissors can result in uneven and jagged cuts.
  • Start by cutting a small amount of hair. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting bangs can be nerve-wracking, but rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • Consider the shape of your face when cutting your bangs. Side swept bangs are flattering on most face shapes, but you may need to adjust the length and angle to suit your features.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my own side swept bangs?

A: Yes, you can cut your own side swept bangs at home. However, if you're not confident in your skills, it's always best to visit a professional stylist.

Q: How often do I need to trim my side swept bangs?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs lose their shape. On average, you may need to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Fine Hair

In addition to cutting your side swept bangs, here are some tips to keep your fine hair looking healthy and vibrant:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to create volume at the roots.
  • Use a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and give your hair a fresh look between washes.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage fine hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily cut side swept bangs for your fine hair at home. Give it a try and see how a simple change can make a big difference in your overall look!
