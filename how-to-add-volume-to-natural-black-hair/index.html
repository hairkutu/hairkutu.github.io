How To Add Volume To Natural Black Hair

Written by Robby May 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having flat, lifeless hair? Do you want to add volume to your natural black hair? You're not alone. Many people with natural black hair struggle with adding volume. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll show you how to add volume to your natural black hair in easy steps.

Table of Contents

  • How to Add Volume to Natural Black Hair
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

How to Add Volume to Natural Black Hair

There are several ways to add volume to your natural black hair. Here are a few methods:

1. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner can help add volume to your hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, biotin, and collagen, which help strengthen and thicken your hair.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow drying your hair upside down can help add volume. Start by flipping your hair over and blow drying the roots. Use a round brush to lift your hair at the roots while blow drying. Once your roots are dry, flip your hair back over and continue blow drying as usual.

3. Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair can help add volume, but be careful not to damage your hair. Use a fine-tooth comb to tease the roots of your hair. Spray a bit of hairspray on the teased area to help hold the volume.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to add volume to your natural black hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Massage your scalp to stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water.

Step 3: Apply a Volumizing Product

Apply a volumizing product, such as mousse or a root-lifting spray, to your hair. Use a comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Flip your hair over and blow dry the roots. Use a round brush to lift your hair at the roots while blow drying. Once your roots are dry, flip your hair back over and continue blow drying as usual.

Step 5: Tease Your Hair

Tease your hair at the roots to add volume. Use a fine-tooth comb to tease the roots of your hair. Spray a bit of hairspray on the teased area to help hold the volume.

Tips

Here are a few tips for adding volume to your natural black hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair to avoid breakage
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid hair breakage and frizz
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth

FAQs

Q: Can I add volume to my hair without using heat?

A: Yes, you can add volume to your hair without using heat. Try using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, and blow drying your hair upside down.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or work out regularly, you may need to wash your hair more often. If you have dry hair, you can wash your hair less frequently.

Hair Care

Caring for your natural black hair is important for maintaining healthy hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent breakage and dryness
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as relaxers and perms, as they can damage your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors, such as wind and pollution

By following these tips and methods, you can add volume to your natural black hair and maintain healthy hair. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine for the best results.
