Do Dreads Look Bad At First Everything You Need To Know

Written by Frank Mar 16, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you thinking of getting dreadlocks but worried about how they might look at first? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people wonder if dreads look bad at first, and it's a valid concern. In this article, we'll answer that question and provide tips on how to care for your dreads.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get Dreads
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Dreadlocks
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dreads

Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are made by matting or braiding hair together to form long, ropelike strands. Dreads can be worn by people of all hair types and lengths, and they can be styled in many different ways.

However, when you first get dreads, they may not look like the finished product you had in mind. This is because it takes time for the hair to fully mat and form into the desired shape. Some people may also experience some discomfort during the early stages of dreadlock formation.

How to Get Dreads

There are several ways to get dreads. The most common methods include:

  • Backcombing: This involves teasing the hair backwards to create tangles and knots.
  • Crochet hook: This method involves using a crochet hook to pull the hair into knots.
  • Twist and rip: This method involves twisting sections of hair and pulling them apart to create knots.

It's important to note that getting dreads is a time-consuming process that requires patience and dedication. It can take several months or even years for the hair to fully mat and form into the desired shape.

Step-by-Step Guide to Dreadlocks

Here is a step-by-step guide to getting dreadlocks:

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly and let it dry completely.
  2. Section your hair into the desired size and shape of your dreads.
  3. Backcomb, twist and rip, or use a crochet hook to create knots in each section of hair.
  4. Continue to maintain your dreads by washing them regularly and re-twisting them as needed.

It's important to note that the process of creating and maintaining dreads can be time-consuming and may require professional help.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Here are some tips for maintaining your dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Retwist your dreads as needed to maintain their shape.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your dreads.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your dreads from getting tangled while you sleep.
  • Be patient and allow your dreads to fully mat and form into the desired shape.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about dreads:

Do dreads look bad at first?

Yes, dreads may not look like the finished product you had in mind at first. It takes time for the hair to fully mat and form into the desired shape.

Can I get dreads if I have short hair?

Yes, you can get dreads if you have short hair. However, the process may take longer and require more maintenance.

Can I wash my dreads?

Yes, you can and should wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.

Hair Care for Dreads

Here are some tips for caring for your hair with dreads:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup in your dreads.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your dreads.
  • Retwist your dreads as needed to maintain their shape.
  • Protect your dreads while sleeping by using a silk or satin pillowcase.

In conclusion, dreads may look bad at first, but with patience and proper care, they can become a beautiful and stylish hairstyle. Remember to be patient and take good care of your dreads, and they will look great in no time!
