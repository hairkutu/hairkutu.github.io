Best Eyeshadow For Over 60 How To Choose And Apply

As we age, our skin changes and so do our makeup needs. When it comes to eyeshadow, finding the right formula and shades can be challenging. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll explore the best eyeshadow for over 60 and share tips on how to choose and apply it.

Table of Content:

  • How to Choose Eyeshadow for Over 60
  • How to Apply Eyeshadow for Over 60
  • Tips for Applying Eyeshadow for Over 60
  • Frequently Asked Questions about Eyeshadow for Over 60
  • Hair Care Tips for Over 60

How to Choose Eyeshadow for Over 60

When choosing eyeshadow for mature skin, it's important to look for formulas that are creamy, blendable, and long-lasting. Matte and satin finishes are also recommended as they don't emphasize fine lines and wrinkles.

Neutral shades such as taupe, beige, and brown are universally flattering and can be used for both daytime and evening looks. If you prefer some color, opt for soft shades of pink, peach, and lavender. Metallics can also be used sparingly to add some sparkle.

Don't forget to consider your eye color when choosing eyeshadow shades. For example, purples and plums complement green eyes, while blues and grays enhance blue eyes. Brown eyes can wear almost any shade, so feel free to experiment!

How to Apply Eyeshadow for Over 60

Before applying eyeshadow, make sure to prep your eyelids with a primer or concealer. This will help the eyeshadow stay in place and prevent creasing.

Use a fluffy brush to apply a neutral shade all over the lid, blending it upwards towards the brow bone. Then, apply a slightly darker shade to the crease, blending it outwards for a softer look.

If you want to add some depth, apply a darker shade to the outer corner of the eye and blend it towards the middle of the lid. This will create a subtle smoky effect.

Tips for Applying Eyeshadow for Over 60

  • Less is more - avoid applying too much eyeshadow, as it can look heavy and emphasize wrinkles.
  • Use a light hand when blending to create a seamless, natural look.
  • Avoid shimmery or glittery shades, as they can enhance fine lines and wrinkles.
  • For a more dramatic look, add eyeliner and mascara to define the eyes.
  • Consider using a magnifying mirror to help with precision and detail.

Frequently Asked Questions about Eyeshadow for Over 60

Q: Can I still wear bold eyeshadow colors?

A: Yes, you can! Just keep in mind that bright or neon shades may not be as flattering, so opt for softer versions of your favorite colors.

Q: Should I use a primer or concealer before applying eyeshadow?

A: Yes, using a primer or concealer can help the eyeshadow stay in place and prevent creasing. It also creates a smooth base for the eyeshadow to adhere to.

Q: How do I prevent eyeshadow from settling into fine lines and wrinkles?

A: Using a matte or satin finish eyeshadow can help minimize the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles. Applying a small amount of eyeshadow and blending it well can also help prevent settling.

Hair Care Tips for Over 60

As we age, our hair also changes and requires different care. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and vibrant:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as curling irons and straighteners, as they can damage the hair.
  • Consider getting regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish and hydrate the hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip the natural oils from the scalp.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful at any age!
