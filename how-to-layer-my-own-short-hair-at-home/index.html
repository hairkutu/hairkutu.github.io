How To Layer My Own Short Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon and spending a fortune on haircuts? Do you want to learn how to layer your own short hair at home? Look no further! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to layer your own short hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Layer My Own Short Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Layering short hair is a great way to add texture and dimension to your hair. It can also give the illusion of volume and movement. However, going to a salon to get your hair layered can be expensive. That's why we've put together this guide on how to layer your own short hair at home.

How to Layer My Own Short Hair at Home

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's go over some of the tools you will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Water spray bottle

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start layering.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Using a comb, brush your hair straight down. Make sure it is completely tangle-free.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip the front sections forward and the back sections backward.

Step 4: Start with the Back

Start with the back sections. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, and using the scissors, cut the hair at a slight angle. Repeat this process with small sections of hair until you have reached your desired length.

Step 5: Move to the Front

Once you have finished layering the back sections, move on to the front. Take a small section of hair, comb it straight down, and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at a slight angle, just like you did with the back sections. Continue this process until you have layered all of the front sections.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

After you have finished layering all sections of your hair, comb through your hair to blend the layers. Make sure there are no noticeable lines between each layer.

Step 7: Check for Any Unevenness

After you have blended the layers, check for any unevenness. If you notice any, simply trim the hair until it is even.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's better to take off too little than too much.
  • When cutting the hair, make sure the scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can cause split ends.
  • Don't be afraid to make mistakes. Hair grows back, and you can always fix any mistakes later.

FAQ

Can I layer my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can layer your own curly hair. However, it's important to remember that curly hair shrinks when it dries. It's best to cut your curly hair when it's dry so you can see how much it will shrink.

How often should I layer my hair?

It's recommended to layer your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care

After you have finished layering your hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suited for your hair type.
  • Don't wash your hair every day. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a heat protectant when using heat tools on your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to layer your own short hair at home, you can save money and have fun experimenting with different styles. Remember to take your time, start with small sections of hair, and don't be afraid to make mistakes. Happy layering!
