Why You Shouldn t Get A Pixie Cut A Guide To Hair Care In 2023

If you're considering a drastic change in your hairstyle, you might be thinking about getting a pixie cut. Pixie cuts have been popular for years, but before you make the decision to chop off your locks, there are a few things you should consider.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that typically features short hair on the back and sides of the head, with longer hair on top. This style has been popularized by celebrities like Emma Watson, Jennifer Lawrence, and Halle Berry.

While pixie cuts can be a bold and stylish choice, they're not for everyone. Here are a few reasons why you might want to think twice before getting a pixie cut:

1. It Might Not Suit Your Face Shape

One of the biggest concerns with getting a pixie cut is that it might not suit your face shape. This style tends to look best on people with oval or heart-shaped faces. If you have a round or square face, a pixie cut might not be the most flattering choice.

2. It Can Be High-Maintenance

While short hair can be easier to manage in some ways, a pixie cut can actually be quite high-maintenance. You'll need to get it trimmed regularly to maintain the shape, and you may need to style it every day to keep it looking polished.

3. It Can Take a While to Grow Out

If you decide that you don't like your pixie cut or you want to try a different style, it can take a while to grow your hair out again. Depending on how short you go, it could take months or even years to get back to your original length.

How to

If you're still considering a pixie cut, here are a few tips on how to make it work:

1. Do Your Research

Before you head to the salon, do some research on different pixie cut styles and find one that you like. Bring photos to your stylist so they can get an idea of what you're looking for.

2. Consider Your Face Shape

As mentioned earlier, different pixie cuts work best on different face shapes. Consider your face shape and talk to your stylist about what style would be most flattering for you.

3. Be Prepared for Maintenance

As we mentioned earlier, pixie cuts can be high-maintenance. Be prepared to spend time styling your hair every day and getting regular trims to maintain the shape.

Step by Step

If you're ready to take the plunge and get a pixie cut, here's what you can expect:

1. Consultation

Before your stylist starts cutting your hair, they'll likely have a consultation with you to discuss what you're looking for and assess your hair's texture and thickness.

2. Cutting

Once you've agreed on a style, your stylist will start cutting your hair. They'll likely use clippers to create the short back and sides, and scissors to create the longer top section.

3. Styling

Once your hair is cut, your stylist will style it to your liking. They may use a blow dryer and a round brush to add volume to the top section, or they may use a styling product to create a more tousled look.

Tips

If you decide to get a pixie cut, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

1. Use the Right Products

A pixie cut requires different hair products than longer hairstyles. Look for products that will add volume and texture to your hair, like a volumizing mousse or a texturizing spray.

2. Be Gentle When Styling

Short hair can be more delicate than longer hair, so be gentle when styling your pixie cut. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and be careful not to pull or tug on your strands.

3. Get Regular Trims

To maintain the shape of your pixie cut, you'll need to get regular trims every 4-6 weeks. This will help keep your hair looking polished and prevent split ends.

FAQ

1. Will a pixie cut make my hair look thicker?

Yes, a pixie cut can make your hair appear thicker because the shorter length adds volume to your hair.

2. Can I still style my hair if I get a pixie cut?

Absolutely! There are many styling options for pixie cuts, including slicked-back styles, tousled looks, and textured cuts.

3. Will a pixie cut suit my face shape?

Pixie cuts tend to look best on people with oval or heart-shaped faces. However, there are many different styles of pixie cuts, so talk to your stylist about what style would be most flattering for you.

Hair Care

Whether you decide to get a pixie cut or not, taking care of your hair is important. Here are a few tips for maintaining healthy hair:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Choose a shampoo that's gentle on your hair and scalp. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain natural ingredients like tea tree oil or argan oil.

2. Condition Regularly

Conditioner helps keep your hair soft, smooth, and healthy. Use a conditioner that's designed for your hair type, and apply it from mid-length to the ends of your hair.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Using heat tools like blow dryers and flat irons can damage your hair over time. Use a heat protectant spray before using these tools, and avoid using them every day.

In conclusion, while a pixie cut can be a bold and stylish choice, it's important to consider whether it's right for you. If you do decide to get a pixie cut, be prepared for high maintenance and regular trims. And don't forget to take care of your hair, no matter what style you choose!
