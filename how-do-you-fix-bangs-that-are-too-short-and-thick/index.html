How To Fix Bangs That Are Too Short And Thick

Written by Daniel Mar 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you struggling with bangs that are too short and thick? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people have experienced the frustration of a bad haircut or DIY bang-trim gone wrong. But fear not, there are ways to fix your bangs and get back to looking your best. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks on how to fix bangs that are too short and thick.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To Fix Bangs That Are Too Short and Thick
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

When bangs are too short and thick, they can be difficult to style and can look unflattering. It's important to first identify why your bangs are too short and thick. Did you cut them yourself and go too short? Did your stylist cut them too short? Or did they simply grow out and become thicker than you wanted? Knowing the cause will help you determine the best course of action to fix them.

How To Fix Bangs That Are Too Short and Thick

There are a few ways to fix bangs that are too short and thick:

1. Let Them Grow Out

If your bangs are just too short, the easiest solution may be to let them grow out. This will give you more hair to work with and allow you to style them in a way that is more flattering.

2. Trim Them

If your bangs are too thick, you can try trimming them to thin them out. It's important to be careful when doing this, as you don't want to make them even shorter. Use sharp scissors and only take off small amounts at a time until you achieve the desired thickness.

3. Style Them Differently

If you're having trouble styling your short, thick bangs, try parting them differently or pinning them back. You can also use styling products like mousse or hairspray to help control them and make them more manageable.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fix bangs that are too short and thick:

Step 1:

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will give you a clean slate to work with.

Step 2:

Part your hair where you normally would, and then section off your bangs.

Step 3:

If your bangs are too short, you can try letting them grow out or using a clip-in extension to add length.

Step 4:

If your bangs are too thick, use sharp scissors to trim them. Only take off small amounts at a time until you achieve the desired thickness.

Step 5:

Style your bangs as desired using a styling product like mousse or hairspray.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to keep in mind when fixing bangs that are too short and thick:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid making your bangs look jagged or uneven.
  • Only take off small amounts at a time when trimming your bangs.
  • Try different styling products to find the one that works best for your hair type.
  • Consider consulting with a stylist for professional advice and assistance.

FAQ

Q: Can I fix my bangs myself?

A: Yes, you can fix your bangs yourself, but be sure to use sharp scissors and take your time.

Q: How long will it take for my bangs to grow out?

A: The length of time it takes for your bangs to grow out will depend on how short they are and how fast your hair grows. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is important for maintaining healthy and manageable hair. Be sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat tools like straighteners and curling irons too often. Regular trims can also help keep your hair looking its best.

In conclusion, fixing bangs that are too short and thick can be frustrating, but there are ways to fix them. Whether you let them grow out, trim them, or style them differently, you can achieve a flattering look that makes you feel confident and beautiful.
