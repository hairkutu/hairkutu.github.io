How To Pick The Perfect Haircut For Your Face Shape

Written by Daniel Jul 04, 2023 · 9 min read

When it comes to getting a new haircut, it can be overwhelming to decide what style will look best on you. One important factor to consider is your face shape. By choosing a haircut that complements your face shape, you can enhance your natural features and create a flattering look. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of how to pick the perfect haircut for your face shape.

Table of Contents

  • What is Face Shape?
  • How to Determine Your Face Shape
  • Haircuts for Different Face Shapes
  • Tips for Choosing Haircuts for Face Shapes
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

What is Face Shape?

Your face shape is the overall shape of your face, including your jawline, cheekbones, and forehead. There are several different face shapes, including round, oval, square, heart, and diamond.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing a haircut that flatters your face shape is to determine what your face shape is. One way to do this is to look in the mirror and trace the outline of your face with a bar of soap or a washable marker. Another way is to take a selfie and draw the outline of your face on the photo using an app or editing software.

Once you have an outline of your face, compare it to the different face shape categories to see which one it fits into. Here are some general guidelines for each face shape:

Round

A round face has soft, curved lines with a width and height that are roughly equal. The jawline is rounded and the cheekbones are the widest part of the face.

Oval

An oval face has balanced proportions, with a slightly curved jawline and a forehead that is slightly wider than the chin.

Square

A square face has a strong jawline and a forehead that is roughly the same width as the jawline. The face is angular and has sharp lines.

Heart

A heart-shaped face has a wider forehead and a narrow, pointed chin. The cheekbones may be the widest part of the face.

Diamond

A diamond-shaped face has a narrow forehead and jawline, with wide cheekbones that are the widest part of the face.

Haircuts for Different Face Shapes

Now that you know your face shape, it’s time to choose a haircut that flatters your features. Here are some general guidelines for each face shape:

Round

If you have a round face, you want to create the illusion of length and slimness. A long, layered haircut that falls below the chin or a short, layered pixie cut can both be flattering. Avoid blunt cuts that end at the chin, as they can make your face appear wider.

Oval

If you have an oval face, you’re lucky – almost any haircut will look good on you! Experiment with different lengths and styles to find what you like best.

Square

If you have a square face, you want to soften the angles of your face. A layered, shoulder-length cut or a bob with soft, side-swept bangs can be flattering. Avoid blunt cuts and center parts, as they can accentuate the squareness of your face.

Heart

If you have a heart-shaped face, you want to draw attention away from your forehead and create balance. A chin-length bob or a layered cut that falls at the shoulders can be flattering. Avoid short, blunt cuts and full bangs, as they can emphasize your forehead.

Diamond

If you have a diamond-shaped face, you want to balance the width of your cheekbones with the rest of your face. A long, layered cut with side-swept bangs or a shoulder-length bob can be flattering. Avoid short, blunt cuts and center parts, as they can make your face appear longer.

Tips for Choosing Haircuts for Face Shapes

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a haircut for your face shape:

  • Consider your hair type and texture. Some haircuts may not work well with curly or fine hair.
  • Think about your lifestyle and how much time you’re willing to spend styling your hair each day. Some cuts require more maintenance than others.
  • Bring pictures of hairstyles you like to your stylist to help communicate your preferences.
  • Ask your stylist for their opinion on what haircut would look best on you.

FAQ

Q: Can I still get a cut I like if it doesn't match my face shape?

A: Of course! These are just general guidelines – ultimately, you should choose a haircut that makes you feel confident and happy.

Q: How often should I get my hair cut?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips

Once you’ve found the perfect haircut for your face shape, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suited to your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and choosing a haircut that flatters your face shape, you can create a gorgeous, flattering look that highlights your natural beauty.
