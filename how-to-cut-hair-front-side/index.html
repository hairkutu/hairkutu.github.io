How To Cut Hair Front Side A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 09, 2023 · 10 min read

Are you tired of constantly going to the salon to get your bangs trimmed or your front layers tidied up? Learning how to cut hair front side can save you time and money in the long run. Plus, it can be a fun and creative way to experiment with your hair. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of cutting your hair front side and provide some tips for achieving a professional-looking cut.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut Hair Front Side
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Professional-Looking Cut
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting

How to Cut Hair Front Side

Cutting your hair front side can be a bit tricky, especially if you’re not used to handling scissors. However, with some practice and patience, you can achieve a great-looking cut. Here are the basic steps:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can shrink up, making it difficult to get an accurate cut. If your hair is naturally curly or wavy, you may want to straighten it before cutting to get a better idea of the length.

2. Decide on the length

Decide how much you want to cut off. It’s always better to start with less and work your way up than to cut too much at once. Use a ruler or measuring tape to get an accurate measurement.

3. Section off your hair

Using a comb, section off the hair you want to cut. If you’re cutting bangs, section off the hair from temple to temple. If you’re cutting layers, section off the hair at the crown of your head.

4. Use sharp scissors

Invest in a good pair of hair-cutting scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends. Make sure to keep the blades sharp by getting them professionally sharpened every six months.

5. Cut in a straight line

When cutting, hold the hair between your fingers and cut in a straight line. Use the comb as a guide to keep the hair even. For bangs, start in the center and work your way out to the sides.

6. Check your work

Once you’ve finished cutting, check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments and trim any stray hairs.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide:

1. Wash and dry your hair

Start with clean, dry hair. It's important to have a clean canvas to work with to get an accurate cut that looks great.

2. Part your hair

Part your hair where you want to create the front side cut. Use clips to hold the rest of your hair back and out of the way.

3. Decide on the length

Decide how much you want to cut off. Measure the length with a ruler or measuring tape. It's better to start with less than to cut off too much and have to wait for it to grow back.

4. Use a comb and scissors

Take your hair between your fingers and use the comb to guide your scissors. Cut in a straight line to achieve an even and professional-looking cut. For bangs, work from the middle and gradually work your way out to each side.

5. Trim any stray hairs

Once you’ve finished cutting, check your work in the mirror. Trim any stray hairs or make any necessary adjustments to achieve your desired look.

Tips for a Professional-Looking Cut

To achieve a professional-looking cut, here are some tips:

1. Invest in good scissors

Sharp scissors make a big difference in achieving a clean cut. Invest in a good pair of hair-cutting scissors and keep them sharp by getting them professionally sharpened every six months.

2. Take it slow

It's better to take your time and cut less than to cut too much at once. You can always go back and trim more if needed.

3. Use a comb as a guide

Using a comb as a guide will help you achieve an even cut. Make sure to hold the hair taut and cut in a straight line.

4. Practice

Practice makes perfect. Don't be discouraged if you don't get it right the first time. Keep practicing and soon you'll be able to cut your hair front side with ease.

FAQ

1. How often should I cut my hair front side?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain your look. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good time frame to keep your cut looking fresh.

2. Can I cut my hair front side if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair front side. However, keep in mind that curly hair shrinks up when it's dry, so you may need to cut less than you think. It's also a good idea to cut your hair when it's wet to get an accurate measurement.

3. Should I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

If you're cutting bangs or layers, it's best to cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can shrink up, making it difficult to get an accurate cut. However, if you're cutting your hair straight across, it's best to cut it when it's wet.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair front side, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking great. Here are some tips:

1. Use a good shampoo and conditioner

Use a good shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type.

2. Avoid heat styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like hair dryers and straighteners. Heat can damage your hair and create split ends.

3. Get regular trims

Getting regular trims every 4-6 weeks will help maintain your cut and keep your hair looking healthy.

Learning how to cut hair front side can be a bit intimidating at first, but with some practice and patience, you can achieve a great-looking cut. Remember to invest in a good pair of scissors, take it slow, and use a comb as a guide. And don't forget to take care of your hair after cutting to keep it looking great!
