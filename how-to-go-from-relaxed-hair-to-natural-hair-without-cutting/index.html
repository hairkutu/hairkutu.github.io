How To Go From Relaxed Hair To Natural Hair Without Cutting

Description

Going from relaxed hair to natural hair can be a daunting task, especially if you don't want to cut off your relaxed ends. It is possible to transition to natural hair without doing a big chop, but it requires patience and a lot of TLC for your hair. In this article, we will share with you tips on how to go from relaxed hair to natural hair without cutting, and how to take care of your hair during this process.

How to

The first step to going from relaxed hair to natural hair without cutting is to stop getting relaxers. This means no more chemical treatments on your hair. You will need to let your natural hair grow out while still maintaining your relaxed hair, which can be a bit of a challenge. You will also need to change your hair care routine to cater to both your natural and relaxed hair.

Transitioning Styles

During the transitioning phase, it is important to find hairstyles that blend both your natural and relaxed hair. This can include twist outs, braid outs, bantu knot outs, and roller sets. These styles will help to camouflage the two different textures and minimize breakage.

Protective Styles

Another option during the transitioning phase is to wear protective styles such as braids, twists, weaves, or wigs. These styles will help to protect your hair and minimize manipulation, which can lead to breakage.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to transitioning from relaxed hair to natural hair without cutting:

  1. Stop getting relaxers
  2. Find transitioning styles that blend your natural and relaxed hair
  3. Use protective styles to minimize breakage
  4. Deep condition your hair regularly
  5. Moisturize your hair daily
  6. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair
  7. Avoid heat styling tools
  8. Trim your ends gradually as your natural hair grows out
  9. Be patient and gentle with your hair
  10. Stay consistent with your hair care routine

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you transition from relaxed hair to natural hair without cutting:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Wear a satin scarf or bonnet at night to protect your hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause tension on your hairline
  • Drink plenty of water and eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to transition from relaxed hair to natural hair?

A: The length of time it takes to transition from relaxed hair to natural hair varies for everyone. It can take anywhere from 6 months to 2 years or more, depending on how fast your hair grows and how much of your relaxed hair you want to keep.

Q: Can I still use heat on my hair during the transitioning phase?

A: It is best to avoid heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers during the transitioning phase as they can cause heat damage and breakage. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant and keep the temperature low.

Hair Care

During the transitioning phase, it is important to take extra care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Deep condition your hair once a week
  • Moisturize your hair daily with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil
  • Detangle your hair gently with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair
  • Trim your ends gradually as your natural hair grows out

Transitioning from relaxed hair to natural hair without cutting can be a challenging process, but with patience and proper hair care, it is possible. Remember to be gentle with your hair and stay consistent with your hair care routine. Before you know it, you'll have a head full of healthy, natural hair!
