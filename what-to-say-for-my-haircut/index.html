What To Say For My Haircut A Guide For Relaxed English Speakers

Written by Daniel Jun 12, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're someone who gets anxious about getting a haircut, you're not alone. Many people struggle with finding the right words to describe what they want. This guide is for those who want to feel confident and relaxed when asking for a haircut that suits them. Here's what you need to know about what to say for your haircut.

Description:

When you're describing the type of haircut you want, it's important to be specific. You don't want to leave the salon feeling disappointed because your hair didn't turn out the way you wanted it to. Here are some keywords you can use to describe your desired haircut:

  • Length: short, medium, long
  • Layers: choppy, blended, textured
  • Bangs: side-swept, blunt, wispy
  • Style: bob, pixie, shag, undercut
  • Color: highlights, balayage, ombre

Use these keywords to create a clear picture of the haircut you want. For example, you might say, "I want a shoulder-length haircut with long layers and side-swept bangs."

How to:

When you arrive at the salon, the stylist will likely ask you what you want. Here are some phrases you can use to communicate your desired haircut:

  • "I'm looking for a new hairstyle."
  • "I want to keep my length but add some layers."
  • "I'm thinking about getting bangs."
  • "Can you help me choose a style that will flatter my face shape?"
  • "I want to change up my hair color."

Don't be afraid to ask questions or ask for advice. A good stylist will be happy to help you find the perfect haircut for you.

Step by Step:

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you communicate your desired haircut:

  1. Research different hairstyles and determine what you like.
  2. Use the keywords above to describe your desired haircut.
  3. Practice saying your desired haircut out loud.
  4. When you arrive at the salon, tell the stylist what you want.
  5. Provide pictures if you have them.
  6. Ask for advice if you're unsure.
  7. During the haircut, communicate any changes you want.
  8. Be open to the stylist's suggestions.
  9. Ask for styling tips so you can recreate the look at home.
  10. Tip the stylist if you're happy with the results.

Tips:

Here are some additional tips to help you get the perfect haircut:

  • Bring pictures of hairstyles you like.
  • Consider your face shape and hair texture when choosing a style.
  • Be realistic about what will work for your hair.
  • Don't be afraid to speak up if you're unhappy with the results.
  • Book a consultation appointment if you're unsure about what you want.

FAQ:

Q: What if I don't know what kind of haircut I want?

A: Don't worry! The stylist can help you choose a style that will work for you. Bring pictures of hairstyles you like and be open to the stylist's suggestions.

Q: Should I wash my hair before the haircut?

A: It's up to you. If you're getting a haircut and a blowout, don't wash your hair. If you're just getting a haircut, you can wash your hair beforehand.

Q: How much should I tip the stylist?

A: It's customary to tip your stylist 15-20% of the total cost of the service.

Hair Care:

Once you've gotten your new haircut, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine.
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth.

With these tips in mind, you should feel confident and relaxed when asking for your next haircut. Remember to be specific about what you want, ask for advice, and take care of your hair afterwards.
