How Much Chemo Makes You Lose Hair Everything You Need To Know

Written by Petter Mar 31, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Dealing with cancer is a tough journey, and chemo is one of the most common treatments for cancer patients. While chemo is effective in fighting cancer, it can have some unpleasant side effects, including hair loss. In this article, we will discuss how much chemo makes you lose hair and provide tips on how to deal with hair loss during your treatment.

Table of Contents

  • How Much Chemo Makes You Lose Hair?
  • Why Does Chemo Cause Hair Loss?
  • When Does Hair Loss Occur During Chemo?
  • How to Deal with Hair Loss During Chemo
  • Hair Care Tips During Chemo
  • FAQs

How Much Chemo Makes You Lose Hair?

The amount of hair loss you experience during chemo depends on the type of drugs you are taking, the dosage, and the duration of your treatment. Some drugs cause more hair loss than others, and hair loss can range from mild thinning to complete baldness.

According to the American Cancer Society, most chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss, but the extent of hair loss can vary from person to person. Some people may lose only a small amount of hair or experience thinning, while others may lose all their hair, including eyebrows, eyelashes, and body hair.

Why Does Chemo Cause Hair Loss?

Chemotherapy drugs target rapidly dividing cells, including cancer cells, but also affect other rapidly dividing cells in the body, such as hair follicle cells. The drugs damage the hair follicles and prevent them from producing new hair, resulting in hair loss.

When Does Hair Loss Occur During Chemo?

Hair loss usually begins within 2-3 weeks of starting chemotherapy, but the timing can vary depending on the drugs you are taking. Hair loss can continue throughout your treatment and may take several months to grow back after the treatment is over.

How to Deal with Hair Loss During Chemo

Hair loss can be emotionally distressing for many cancer patients, but there are ways to cope with it. Here are some tips:

  • Consider cutting your hair short before starting chemo. Short hair is easier to manage and can make hair loss less noticeable.
  • Try wearing a wig, hat, or scarf to cover your head. There are many options available, and you can choose what makes you feel comfortable.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles and accessories to enhance your look. You can try using hair extensions, headbands, or turbans to add volume and style to your hair.
  • Take care of your scalp. Use a gentle shampoo and avoid using hot tools like hair dryers or flat irons. You can also use a scalp cooling cap during chemo to reduce hair loss.

Hair Care Tips During Chemo

While you may lose your hair during chemo, it's important to take care of your scalp and remaining hair to keep it healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and avoid using hot water.
  • Use a soft brush or comb to avoid pulling your hair.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol.
  • Protect your scalp from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQs

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemo?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back after chemo, but it may take several months. The new hair may have a different texture or color than your previous hair.

Q: Can I color or perm my hair during chemo?

A: It's recommended to avoid coloring or perming your hair during chemo, as the chemicals can be harsh on your scalp and hair. Wait until your hair has grown back and consult with your doctor before making any changes to your hair.

Q: Can men experience hair loss during chemo?

A: Yes, men can experience hair loss during chemo, although it's less common than in women. Men may lose hair on their scalp, face, or body.

Q: Is there any way to prevent hair loss during chemo?

A: Unfortunately, there is no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during chemo. However, using a scalp cooling cap during chemo may help reduce hair loss for some patients.

In conclusion, hair loss is a common side effect of chemo, but it's important to remember that it's temporary and your hair will grow back. By taking care of your scalp and remaining hair, and experimenting with different hair accessories, you can feel confident and beautiful during your treatment.
