How To See If A Hairstyle Suits You A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Mar 21, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Choosing a new hairstyle can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. Whether you're looking for a drastic change or just a subtle update, it's important to find a hairstyle that suits your face shape, hair type, and personal style. To help you find the perfect look, we've put together a step-by-step guide on how to see if a hairstyle suits you.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape
  • Step 2: Consider Your Hair Type
  • Step 3: Look for Inspiration
  • Step 4: Try on Wigs or Use Virtual Try-On Tools
  • Step 5: Consult with a Hairstylist
  • Tips for Choosing a Flattering Hairstyle
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your New Hairstyle

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding a hairstyle that suits you is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back from your face. Look at the overall shape of your face, paying attention to the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline.

If you're having trouble determining your face shape, there are several online resources and apps that can help. Once you've determined your face shape, you can start looking for hairstyles that flatter your features.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Type

In addition to your face shape, it's important to consider your hair type when choosing a new hairstyle. If you have fine hair, for example, you may want to avoid heavy layers that can weigh your hair down. If you have thick, curly hair, on the other hand, you may want to opt for a style that embraces your natural texture.

Consider your hair's length, texture, and thickness when browsing hairstyle options. This will help you narrow down your choices and find a style that works for you.

Step 3: Look for Inspiration

Before committing to a new hairstyle, it's a good idea to look for inspiration. Browse magazines, social media, and celebrity hairstyles to get an idea of what styles you like. Don't forget to consider your personal style and lifestyle when choosing a new hairstyle. If you're low-maintenance, for example, you may want to opt for a style that requires little upkeep.

Make a list of your favorite hairstyles, and bring them with you to your hairstylist appointment.

Step 4: Try on Wigs or Use Virtual Try-On Tools

If you're still unsure about a new hairstyle, try on wigs or use virtual try-on tools. This will give you a better idea of how a hairstyle will look on you without committing to a cut or color.

Many online retailers and beauty supply stores offer virtual try-on tools that allow you to upload a photo of yourself and "try on" different hairstyles. This can be a fun and low-risk way to experiment with different looks.

Step 5: Consult with a Hairstylist

Once you've narrowed down your hairstyle options, it's time to consult with a hairstylist. A professional hairstylist can help you determine which styles will work best with your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle.

During your appointment, be sure to bring your list of favorite hairstyles and any inspiration photos you've collected. Your hairstylist can provide recommendations and help you choose a style that suits you.

Tips for Choosing a Flattering Hairstyle

  • Consider your lifestyle and maintenance preferences when choosing a new hairstyle.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and colors.
  • Take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
  • Consider consulting with a hairstylist or image consultant for personalized recommendations.

FAQs

Q: How do I know if a hairstyle will look good on me?

A: Consider your face shape, hair type, and personal style when choosing a new hairstyle. Look for inspiration and consult with a hairstylist for personalized recommendations.

Q: How can I try on different hairstyles without committing to a cut or color?

A: Try on wigs or use virtual try-on tools to experiment with different hairstyles.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your New Hairstyle

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat tools on a daily basis.
  • Visit your hairstylist regularly for trims and touch-ups.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine.

By following these steps and tips, you can find a hairstyle that flatters your features and suits your personal style. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
