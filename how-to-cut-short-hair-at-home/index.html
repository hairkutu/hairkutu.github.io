How To Cut Short Hair At Home A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Apr 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a way to save money on haircuts or just want to try something new, cutting your own short hair at home can be a great option. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without ever leaving your house. In this guide, we'll take you through everything you need to know about how to cut short hair at home, from preparation to styling. Let's get started!

How to Prepare for Cutting Your Short Hair at Home

Before you begin cutting your hair, it's important to prepare properly. Here are some steps you can take to get ready:

Gather Your Tools

You'll need a few basic tools to cut your hair at home, including:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers (if you want a shorter cut)
  • A comb
  • Sectioning clips (optional)

Make sure your scissors are sharp and your clippers are charged and ready to go.

Clean Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair before you start cutting. This will help you see any uneven areas and make it easier to work with.

Choose Your Style

Decide on the length and style you want before you start cutting. Look at pictures online or in magazines for inspiration, and consider your face shape and hair texture.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Short Hair at Home

Now that you're prepared, it's time to start cutting! Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Use sectioning clips to divide your hair into manageable sections. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward.

Step 2: Cut the Back

Starting at the nape of your neck, use your scissors or clippers to cut your hair straight across. Work your way up to the crown of your head, making sure to cut evenly.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Next, move on to the sides of your head. Use your scissors to cut your hair at an angle, following the shape of your head. Keep your cuts consistent and even on both sides.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Finally, move to the top of your head. Use your scissors to cut your hair at an angle, following the shape of your head. Keep your cuts consistent and even.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting, check your hair for evenness. Use your comb to comb through your hair and look for any areas that need to be trimmed.

Tips for Cutting Short Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best possible results:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you go.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will look shorter when it dries.
  • Use your comb to guide your cuts and keep them even.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring!

FAQ: Cutting Short Hair at Home

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short hair at home:

Q: Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can! Just make sure to cut your hair dry and take your time to avoid any uneven cuts.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. As a general rule, aim to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your style.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don't panic! If you make a mistake, you can always go to a professional stylist to fix it. Alternatively, you can try styling your hair in a different way to hide any uneven areas.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Hair at Home

After cutting your hair at home, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and fresh. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain your style.

And there you have it - everything you need to know about how to cut short hair at home! With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a great-looking cut without ever leaving your house.
