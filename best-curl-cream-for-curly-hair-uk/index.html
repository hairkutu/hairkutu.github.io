Best Curl Cream For Curly Hair Uk The Ultimate Guide

Written by Christine Apr 14, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizz and unruly curls? Look no further, because we have compiled a list of the best curl cream for curly hair in the UK. Say goodbye to bad hair days and hello to gorgeous, defined curls!

Description

Curl creams are a must-have for anyone with curly hair. They help to define curls, reduce frizz, and provide much-needed moisture. However, with so many options on the market, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one for your hair type. In this article, we will be discussing the best curl creams for curly hair in the UK.

How to Choose the Right Curl Cream

Before we dive into our top picks, let's talk about how to choose the right curl cream for your hair. Here are some things to consider:

Hair Type

Not all curl creams are created equal. Some are designed for fine hair, while others are better suited for thick, coarse hair. Make sure to choose a curl cream that is specifically formulated for your hair type.

Curl Pattern

The curl cream you choose should also depend on your curl pattern. If you have loose curls, you may want a lighter formula, while tighter curls may benefit from a heavier cream.

Ingredients

Look for curl creams that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil. These ingredients are great for moisturizing and nourishing curly hair.

Top Picks for the Best Curl Cream for Curly Hair UK

1. Shea Moisture Coconut & Hibiscus Curl Enhancing Smoothie

This curl cream is a fan favorite for a reason. It's formulated with shea butter, coconut oil, and hibiscus flower extract to define curls and reduce frizz. It's also free of sulfates, parabens, and phthalates.

2. Cantu Moisturizing Curl Activator Cream

This curl cream is perfect for those with dry, damaged hair. It's formulated with shea butter, jojoba oil, and glycerin to provide intense moisture and definition.

3. Bounce Curl Light Creme Gel

This curl cream is ideal for those with fine, wavy hair. It's a lightweight formula that provides definition without weighing down your curls.

How to Use Curl Cream

Now that you've chosen the right curl cream for your hair, here's how to use it:

Step 1: Wash and Condition

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would. Pat your hair dry with a towel, but don't rub it, as this can cause frizz.

Step 2: Apply Curl Cream

Apply a small amount of curl cream to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up. Make sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Scrunch

Scrunch your hair to encourage curl formation. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls.

Step 4: Let Your Hair Air Dry

Let your hair air dry or use a hair dryer on a low heat setting. Avoid touching your hair while it's drying to prevent frizz.

Tips for Using Curl Cream

Here are some additional tips for using curl cream:

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When applying curl cream, use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly and prevent tangles.

Don't Overdo It

A little goes a long way with curl cream. Start with a small amount and add more if needed.

Refresh Your Curls

If your curls start to lose definition throughout the day, you can refresh them by applying a small amount of curl cream to your ends and scrunching.

FAQ

What is curl cream?

Curl cream is a styling product that helps to define curls, reduce frizz, and provide moisture.

Can curl cream be used on all hair types?

Curl cream can be used on all hair types, but it's important to choose a formula that is specifically designed for your hair type.

How often should I use curl cream?

You can use curl cream as often as needed, but be careful not to overdo it, as this can weigh down your curls.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Using the right products is just one part of taking care of your curly hair. Here are some additional tips:

Don't Wash Your Hair Too Often

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and frizz. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and breakage.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage curly hair and lead to frizz. Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.

With the right products and care, you can achieve beautiful, defined curls. Try out one of the curl creams on our list and see the difference it can make!
