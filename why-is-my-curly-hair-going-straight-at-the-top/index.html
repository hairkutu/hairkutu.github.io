Why Is My Curly Hair Going Straight At The Top

Written by Frank Jul 13, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can be beautiful and unique, it can also be difficult to manage. One common problem people with curly hair face is that it can go straight at the top. This can be frustrating, but there are ways to deal with it. In this article, we will explore the causes of curly hair going straight at the top and what you can do to prevent it.

Causes of Curly Hair Going Straight at the Top

There are several reasons why curly hair can go straight at the top. One of the most common causes is heat damage. If you use hot tools like flat irons, curling irons, or blow dryers on your hair, it can damage the hair cuticle and cause it to lose its curl pattern. This can lead to straight hair at the top and curly hair at the bottom.

Another cause of curly hair going straight at the top is genetics. Some people are born with hair that is naturally straighter at the top and curlier at the bottom. This is often referred to as "bell-shaped" hair.

Finally, product buildup can also cause curly hair to go straight at the top. If you use too much product or don't wash your hair often enough, it can weigh down your curls and make them appear straighter.

How To Prevent Curly Hair From Going Straight at the Top

The best way to prevent curly hair from going straight at the top is to avoid heat damage. This means limiting your use of hot tools and opting for air drying instead. If you must use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment to help maintain your curls.

Another way to prevent curly hair from going straight at the top is to choose the right products. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair and avoid heavy products that can weigh down your curls.

Finally, make sure to wash your hair regularly to prevent product buildup. If you have very dry hair, you may want to consider co-washing (using conditioner to wash your hair) instead of using shampoo every time you wash your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Curly Hair From Going Straight at the Top

1. Limit heat styling

2. Choose products designed for curly hair

3. Wash your hair regularly

4. Consider co-washing if you have very dry hair

5. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer

6. Avoid heavy products that can weigh down your curls

7. Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz

8. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush

9. Avoid pulling your hair back tightly into ponytails or braids

10. Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage

Tips to Keep Curly Hair Healthy and Bouncy

1. Deep condition your hair once a week

2. Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair

3. Apply a curl cream or gel to your hair when it's wet to help define your curls

4. Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz

5. Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel

FAQs

Q: Can I still straighten my hair if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can still straighten your hair if you have curly hair. Just be sure to use a heat protectant and limit your use of hot tools to prevent heat damage.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much product you use. Generally, it's recommended to wash your hair every 2-3 days.
