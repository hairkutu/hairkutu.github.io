How To Train Your Hair To Stay Slicked Back

Written by Frank Jun 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of constantly having to fix your hair throughout the day? Do you want to achieve a sleek and polished look that stays put all day long? Look no further, as we have the solution for you! Follow these steps to train your hair to stay slicked back.

Description

Slicked-back hair has been a timeless hairstyle for men that exudes confidence and sophistication. However, achieving this look can be a challenge, especially if you have stubborn hair that tends to fall out of place. Training your hair to stay slicked back takes time and patience, but the result is worth it!

How to Train Your Hair to Stay Slicked Back

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before you begin training your hair, make sure to start with clean hair. Wash your hair using a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup of products or oils that may prevent your hair from staying in place.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Apply a styling product, such as a pomade or gel, to damp hair. Use a comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair, making sure to focus on the roots.

Step 3: Brush Hair Back

Using a brush, brush your hair back in the direction you want it to stay. Make sure to use a brush with firm bristles to help train your hair to stay in place.

Step 4: Secure with a Hair Tie

Once your hair is slicked back, secure it with a hair tie or clip. This will help train your hair to stay in place and prevent it from falling forward or to the sides.

Step 5: Repeat Daily

Repeat this process daily until your hair begins to naturally stay slicked back. Over time, your hair will begin to adapt to this style, and you will no longer need to use as much product or a hair tie to keep it in place.

Tips

  • Use a hairdryer on a low heat setting to set the product and help train your hair to stay in place.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find the one that works best for your hair type.
  • Be patient! It may take a few weeks for your hair to adapt to this style.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to train your hair to stay slicked back?

A: It varies depending on your hair type and how often you repeat the process. It can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to train your hair to stay slicked back.

Q: Can I still use other hair products while training my hair?

A: Yes, but try to avoid using products that may weigh your hair down or make it greasy.

Q: Do I need to use a hair tie every day?

A: No, once your hair begins to adapt to this style, you may no longer need to use a hair tie to keep it in place.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair to promote healthy hair growth and prevent damage. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly using a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as hair straighteners or curling irons, too often.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.

Now that you know how to train your hair to stay slicked back, it's time to give it a try! Follow these steps, be patient, and soon you'll be rocking a sleek and polished look that stays put all day long.
