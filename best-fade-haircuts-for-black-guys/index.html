Best Fade Haircuts For Black Guys

Written by Petter Sep 04, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Looking for a new haircut that will make you stand out? Fade haircuts have been popular for decades, but they're especially trendy for black guys. In this article, we'll go over the best fade haircuts for black guys, how to get them, and how to take care of your hair after getting a fade.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get a Fade Haircut
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Fade
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

Description

Fade haircuts are characterized by gradually fading hair length from the top of the head down to the neck. They are a popular choice for black guys due to their versatility and ability to complement different face shapes and hair textures. Whether you have curly, kinky, or straight hair, there's a fade haircut that will work for you.

How to Get a Fade Haircut

If you're getting a fade for the first time, it's best to go to a professional barber. They will be able to assess your hair type and determine the best fade for you. When you go in for your appointment, bring a picture of the haircut you want to help your barber understand your vision.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get a fade haircut:

  1. Choose the type of fade you want (low, medium, or high).
  2. Decide on the length you want on top.
  3. Ask your barber to start cutting your hair at the desired length on top.
  4. As your barber moves down your head, the hair will gradually get shorter. The fade should be seamless and gradual.
  5. Make sure you communicate with your barber throughout the process to ensure you're happy with the length and style.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fade

Once you have your fade, you'll want to maintain it to keep it looking fresh. Here are some tips:

  • Visit your barber every 2-4 weeks for touch-ups.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • If you have curly or kinky hair, consider using a leave-in conditioner or oil to define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Invest in a good quality hair clipper to touch up your fade at home between appointments.

FAQ

What's the difference between a low, medium, and high fade?

A low fade starts just above the ears and gradually gets shorter towards the neck. A medium fade starts at the temples and gradually gets shorter towards the neck. A high fade starts at the top of the head and gradually gets shorter towards the neck.

Will a fade work with my hair texture?

Yes, fades work with all hair textures. Your barber will be able to recommend the best type of fade for your hair type.

How do I choose the right length for my fade?

It's best to ask your barber for their recommendation based on your face shape and hair texture.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and looking great after a fade, follow these tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a good quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons that can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

By following these tips and getting regular touch-ups from your barber, you'll be able to rock a fresh fade haircut all year round.
