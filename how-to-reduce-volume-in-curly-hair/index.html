How To Reduce Volume In Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Aug 03, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. While curly hair can be voluminous and beautiful, it can also become unruly and difficult to manage. If you're looking to reduce the volume in your curly hair, there are a few simple tips and tricks that can help.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Reduce Volume in Curly Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Reducing Volume in Curly Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

How to Reduce Volume in Curly Hair

Reducing the volume in curly hair can be a tricky task, as curly hair tends to naturally have more volume than straight hair. However, with the right techniques and products, you can tame your curly locks and achieve a sleeker, more manageable look.

1. Use a Diffuser Attachment

One of the easiest ways to reduce volume in curly hair is to use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. A diffuser helps to distribute heat evenly throughout your hair, which can help to tame frizz and reduce overall volume.

2. Try a Keratin Treatment

Keratin treatments can be a great option for reducing volume in curly hair. Keratin is a protein that is naturally found in hair, and a keratin treatment can help to smooth and straighten your hair, resulting in a sleeker, more manageable look.

3. Use a Hair Straightener

If you're looking for a quick fix, using a hair straightener can be a great way to reduce volume in curly hair. However, be sure to use a heat protectant spray beforehand to prevent damage to your hair.

4. Opt for a Shorter Haircut

A shorter haircut can help to reduce volume in curly hair, as shorter hair tends to be less voluminous than longer hair. If you're looking for a low-maintenance option, a shorter haircut may be the way to go.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for reducing volume in curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair, being careful not to rub it vigorously.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair.
  4. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair.
  5. If necessary, use a hair straightener to further reduce volume.
  6. Finish with a hairspray or other styling product, if desired.

Tips for Reducing Volume in Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for reducing volume in curly hair:

  • Avoid using a brush on your hair, as this can cause frizz and make your hair more voluminous.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Instead, use lukewarm or cool water.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Try to wash your hair no more than every other day.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a curling iron to reduce volume in my curly hair?

A: No, using a curling iron will typically add more volume to curly hair. Instead, try using a hair straightener or a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer.

Q: Will a keratin treatment damage my hair?

A: While keratin treatments can be damaging to hair if done improperly, most reputable salons use safe and effective formulas that are gentle on hair.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and make your hair more prone to frizz.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair.
  • Try sleeping with your hair in a loose bun or braid to prevent tangles and frizz.

Reducing volume in curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, it is possible to achieve a sleeker, more manageable look. Whether you opt for a keratin treatment, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer, or try a shorter haircut, there are many ways to tame your curly locks and achieve the look you want.
