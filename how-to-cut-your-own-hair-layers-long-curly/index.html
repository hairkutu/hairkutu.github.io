How To Cut Your Own Hair Layers Long Curly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Mar 18, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of paying for expensive salon appointments to keep your long curly hair looking fabulous? Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve salon-worthy layers at home. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair layers long curly, along with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Layers Long Curly
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Layers Long Curly
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Long Curly Hair

How to Cut Your Own Hair Layers Long Curly

Cutting your own hair layers long curly requires some preparation and the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb or hairbrush
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror

Once you have your tools, it's time to start cutting your own hair layers long curly.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own hair layers long curly:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, which can lead to uneven layers.
  2. Part your hair down the middle and separate it into two sections, left and right.
  3. Starting with one section, take a 1-2 inch section of hair from the front and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Cut the hair at a slight angle, and use this section as a guide for the rest of your hair.
  4. Continue to cut small sections of hair, using the previously cut section as a guide. Keep the scissors at a slight angle to create long layers.
  5. Repeat these steps on the other side.
  6. Once both sides are complete, use a mirror to check for any uneven layers or missed spots. Make adjustments as needed.
  7. Style your hair as desired and enjoy your new long curly layers!

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Layers Long Curly

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind as you cut your own hair layers long curly:

  • Start with small sections of hair, and cut less than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more, but you can't undo a bad cut.
  • Use hair ties or clips to keep hair out of the way while you work on each section.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is, which can lead to uneven layers.
  • Consider using hair shears specifically designed for cutting curly hair. These scissors have a serrated edge that can help prevent the hair from slipping while you cut it.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair layers long curly:

Can I cut my own hair layers long curly if I have never done it before?

Yes, you can! Just be sure to start with small sections of hair and cut less than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more, but you can't undo a bad cut.

Do I need to cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

You should cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is, which can lead to uneven layers.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my own hair layers long curly?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim your hair to even it out, or visit a professional stylist to fix any major mistakes.

Hair Care Tips for Long Curly Hair

Cutting your own hair layers long curly is just the first step in achieving beautiful, healthy hair. Here are some tips to help you care for your long curly locks:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can lead to breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair to keep it moisturized and prevent frizz.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help enhance your natural curls and prevent frizz.

Cutting your own hair layers long curly can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve salon-worthy results at home. Remember to start with small sections of hair, cut less than you think you need to, and use these tips to care for your long curly locks. Happy cutting!
