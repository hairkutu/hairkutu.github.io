How To Cut Your Own Hair Men s Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Apr 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're going for a fade. But with the right tools and a little bit of patience, it's possible to give yourself a fresh cut without leaving the house. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a men's fade haircut at home.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • The Cut
  • Blending
  • Clean Up
  • Tips for a Successful Cut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Your Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers with guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Hair spray or gel (optional)

Preparation

The first step to cutting your own hair is to make sure it's clean and dry. Wash your hair and allow it to air dry or use a blow dryer. Once your hair is dry, comb it out to remove any tangles.

Step 1: Start with the Sides

To start the fade, attach the appropriate guard to your clippers. Begin by cutting the sides of your head, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Use the clippers to create a straight line around the sides, using the guard to create a blended look.

Step 2: Move to the Back

Once you've finished the sides, move to the back of your head. Use the same guard you used on the sides and begin at the bottom, working your way up. Use the clippers to create a straight line around the back of your head.

Step 3: Cut the Top

Now it's time to move to the top of your head. Use scissors to trim the top, cutting no more than half an inch at a time. This will create a gradual fade from the sides to the top of your head.

Blending

To create a seamless fade, you'll need to blend the different lengths of hair. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the clippers to slowly blend the hair together. Be sure to use a guard that's one size shorter than the one you used on the sides and back of your head.

Clean Up

Once you've finished blending, it's time to clean up any stray hairs. Use scissors to trim any hairs that are longer than the rest.

Tips for a Successful Cut

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down, rather than starting with a shorter guard and trying to fix mistakes.
  • Take breaks and step away from the mirror to avoid overcutting.
  • Use a spray bottle or damp towel to keep hair in place while cutting.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. A friend or family member can help with hard-to-reach areas.

FAQs

Is it possible to give yourself a fade haircut?

Yes, it's possible to give yourself a fade haircut with the right tools and a little bit of practice.

How do I know which guard to use on my clippers?

The number on the guard corresponds to the length of the hair it will cut. The smaller the number, the shorter the cut.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. Take a break and step away from the mirror. You can always go back and fix any mistakes later.

Hair Care After Your Cut

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. You may also want to use a styling product, such as hair spray or gel, to help keep your new cut in place.

Cutting your own hair can be a challenging task, but with the right tools and some patience, it's possible to achieve a professional-looking fade at home. Follow these steps and tips for a successful cut, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
