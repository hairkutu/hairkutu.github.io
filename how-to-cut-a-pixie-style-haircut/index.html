How To Cut A Pixie Style Haircut

Written by Bardi May 12, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, a pixie cut is a bold and daring option. This trendy haircut features short layers that frame the face, creating a dramatic look that's both edgy and chic. But before you start chopping away at your locks, it's important to know how to cut a pixie style haircut properly. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about creating a pixie cut, from the tools you'll need to the step-by-step process.

Description

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that typically features short layers on top and longer layers on the sides and back. The hair is usually cut close to the scalp, creating a sleek and modern look. This style is perfect for women who want a low-maintenance hairstyle that's both stylish and versatile.

How to Cut a Pixie Style Haircut

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle filled with water.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: the top, both sides, and the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Starting with one of the side sections, comb the hair straight down and cut it to your desired length. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Next, move on to the back section. Comb the hair straight down and cut it to your desired length. Make sure to keep the hair at the nape of the neck slightly longer than the rest.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Finally, it's time to cut the top section. Comb the hair straight up and cut it to your desired length. Use the scissors to add texture and volume to the top section.

Step 6: Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting your hair, use the comb to blend the layers together. Use the scissors to make any final adjustments, and then style your hair as desired.

Tips

  • Start with longer hair and gradually cut it shorter to avoid any mistakes.
  • Keep the hair at the nape of the neck slightly longer for a more natural look.
  • Use texturizing scissors to add volume and texture to the top section.
  • Consider getting your hair professionally cut if you're not comfortable doing it yourself.

FAQ

Q: Can anyone pull off a pixie cut?

A: While a pixie cut can look great on anyone, it's important to consider your face shape and hair texture before making the cut. If you have a round face or thick hair, a pixie cut may not be the best option for you.

Q: How often do I need to get a pixie cut?

A: A pixie cut typically needs to be trimmed every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair into a pixie style, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too many styling products. Consider using a heat protectant spray if you plan on using heat tools on your hair. Finally, make sure to get regular trims to keep your pixie cut looking fresh and stylish.
