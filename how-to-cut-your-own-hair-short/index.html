How To Cut Your Own Hair Short In 2023 A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Apr 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of shelling out money at the salon every few weeks to maintain your short haircut? Why not try cutting your own hair? It may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and technique, you can give yourself a great short haircut in the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair short, as well as some tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Short
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs About Cutting Your Own Hair
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

How to Cut Your Own Hair Short

Before we get started, let's make sure you have the right tools. You will need:

  • Scissors - it's important to use hair-cutting scissors, as regular scissors can cause split ends.
  • Comb - a wide-toothed comb is best for detangling your hair.
  • Mirror - you'll need to be able to see the back of your head, so make sure you have a mirror set up behind you.
  • Hair ties or clips - you'll need to section your hair off, so have some hair ties or clips on hand.

Now that you have your tools, let's get started.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. It's easier to cut your hair when it's dry, so wash and blow-dry your hair before you start.

Step 2: Section your hair. Use your comb to create a part in the middle of your hair, then section your hair into four parts - two in the front and two in the back.

Step 3: Start with the back. Take a section of hair from the back and comb it straight up. Use your scissors to cut off the desired length. Make sure to cut in a straight line and avoid jagged edges.

Step 4: Move to the sides. Take a section of hair from one of the front sections and comb it straight out from your head. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length. Repeat on the other side.

Step 5: Trim the top. Take a section of hair from the front and comb it straight up. Use your scissors to trim the length as desired. Repeat until you have trimmed the entire top section of your hair.

Step 6: Check your work. Use your mirror to check the back and sides of your head to make sure everything is even.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with small snips. It's easier to cut more hair off than to try to fix a mistake.
  • Don't rush. Take your time and be patient.
  • Use hair clips or ties to keep hair out of the way while you work.
  • Check your work often to make sure everything is even.
  • When in doubt, seek professional help.

FAQs About Cutting Your Own Hair

Q: Is it really possible to cut my own hair?

A: Yes, it is possible. With the right tools and technique, anyone can cut their own hair.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: It's okay! Hair grows back. If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go to a professional to fix it or wait for it to grow out.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, short haircuts need to be maintained every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Now that you've cut your own hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain your short haircut.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a great short haircut in the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
